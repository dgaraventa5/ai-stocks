## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim, written before any data pull)

> "EMCOR is a US electrical and mechanical construction + facilities services contractor (segments: US Electrical Construction, US Mechanical Construction, US Building Services, US Industrial Services, UK Services). Data center / AI exposure comes through its 'network and communications' market sector — roughly mid-to-high teens percent of construction revenue and climbing — plus high-tech manufacturing (semi fabs); it closed the ~$865M Miller Electric acquisition in early 2025 to deepen Southeast data center electrical exposure. As of my training (most recent quarter I'm confident on: Q3 2025, reported late October 2025), RPOs were at a record in the ~$11-12B range, revenue running ~$4.0-4.3B/quarter with operating margins around 9-10% in the construction segments, and full-year 2025 EPS guidance had been raised repeatedly to roughly $24-25. Versus Comfort Systems (FIX): EMCOR is larger and more electrical-weighted with steadier but lower growth, while FIX has higher data-center revenue share and modular prefab momentum; EMCOR's moat is execution scale and skilled-labor depth, not AI-specific IP."

## 6. Implications for ratings (flags only — no rating changes made here)

- **AI Thesis (directness/exposure): direction UP.** ~30% of revenue and the dominant growth/backlog driver is data center construction; "limited AI-specific moat" prior understates directness, and the labor-scarcity environment (45-70% of DC cost is electrical; ~499K worker shortfall) makes incumbent skilled-labor scale itself moat-like. Re-examine D1/D2 against the Layer-1-style directness lens.
- **Growth: direction UP.** +19.7% reported / +16.8% organic, RPO +32.9%, two guidance raises. Objective growth inputs should be refreshed per rule 9 (8-K Item 2.02 on 4/29 already crossed the trigger; this briefing satisfies the research side).
- **Value: inputs STALE, direction unresolved.** EPS base materially higher (FY26 guide $28.25-29.75) but the stock has re-rated as an AI name — Fwd P/E/PEG must be recomputed before concluding anything.
- **Momentum (EPS Revisions subjective): direction UP.** Consecutive guidance raises; refresh 50DMA % alongside (rule 11).
- **Quality/margins: WATCH, mild negative pressure on margin %.** Mechanical mix shift (CM/prime, GMP/cost-plus, new geographies) trades markup for volume; management explicitly prioritizes margin dollars. Not a deterioration in execution — gross margins in Electrical held — but the margin-percentage trajectory is no longer monotonic.
- **Risk: MIXED.** Rising DC concentration (~30% of revenue, still about half of FIX's) increases AI-capex-cycle sensitivity; ~30% of FY26 work still to be booked means guidance has booking risk; working-capital intensity rising with growth; supervision (foreman) capacity is the binding internal constraint. Offsets: pure-US footprint, $916M cash, diversified non-DC backlog (water, institutional, healthcare also at records).

**Thesis-break signals: none found.** Direction of travel is the opposite — acceleration. The main caution is that the market narrative has caught up to (or passed) the watchlist's conservative framing, which is a valuation question, not a thesis break.
