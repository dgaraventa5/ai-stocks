## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim)

What I currently believe about ADBE before research:
- Adobe is the dominant creative-software franchise (Photoshop, Illustrator, Premiere, Acrobat) on a subscription (Creative Cloud / Document Cloud / Experience Cloud) model, ~$20B revenue, high-80s% gross margin, ~45% non-GAAP operating margin, strong FCF.
- AI thesis is double-edged: Firefly (generative AI) is the offensive product, but the central bear case is that gen-AI-native tools (Midjourney, OpenAI image/video, Google) disrupt the creative-software moat — "AI eats Adobe."
- Reporting historically split into Digital Media (Creative + Document Cloud) and Digital Experience.
- Stock has de-rated meaningfully on disruption fear; trades cheap vs. its own history (~mid-teens to low-20s fwd P/E).
- Net-new ARR (Digital Media) is the key KPI; growth decelerating into low-teens.

**The diff that matters most:** how big the AI-first ARR actually is relative to total ARR, and whether the disruption fear is in the price or still building.

## 4. WebSearch findings

### Finding 1: Beat-and-raise, but stock FELL ~6% — and was already down ~40% YTD
Despite beating and raising, ADBE dropped ~6% after the print, and had already declined ~40% YTD into the report. The decline is **company-specific** (gen-AI disruption fear), not sector — IGV (software ETF) was roughly flat-to-up over the same window. Sources: [Investing.com — beat-and-raise but slips on CFO exit](https://www.investing.com/news/earnings/adobe-delivers-beatandraise-quarter-but-stock-slips-after-hours-on-cfo-exit-4738231), [Yahoo/Benzinga — why ADBE falling after Q2 beat](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/why-adbe-stock-falling-easy-073117025.html), [ChartMill](https://www.chartmill.com/news/ADBE/Chartmill-49766-ADBE-Slips-After-Record-Q2-Despite-Beat-and-Raise-Results).

### Finding 2: The real reason for the drop — defensive pivot to freemium + deferred price increases
Management is "choosing to lower second-half ARR from individual subscribers to go all-in on a **freemium funnel for Firefly, Express and Acrobat**" and **deferring Creative Cloud price optimizations** (delaying planned price hikes). Patrick Moorhead framed this as "a defensive position … investment protection" — prioritizing MAU/user growth over near-term ARR. This is the market reading it as Adobe conceding pricing power is at risk from AI-native competition. Source: [Yahoo/Benzinga](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/why-adbe-stock-falling-easy-073117025.html).

### Finding 3: Ten consecutive quarters of growth deceleration; opex outgrowing revenue
Ex-Semrush, organic ARR growth was ~10.5% — implying ten consecutive quarters of decelerating growth. Opex grew ~17% vs revenue ~13%, raising efficiency concerns. Source: [Seeking Alpha — "Continued Deceleration"](https://seekingalpha.com/article/4914994-adobe-q2-2026-earnings-update-continued-deceleration), [Yahoo — Q2 CY2026 sales top estimates but stock drops](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/adobe-nasdaq-adbe-q2-cy2026-201523074.html).

### Finding 4: Firefly became a model aggregator — tacit admission its own models trail leaders
Firefly now offers **30+ third-party models** — Google Nano Banana 2 & Veo 3.1, Runway Gen-4.5, OpenAI GPT Image, Black Forest Labs Flux, Ideogram, Luma — alongside Adobe's own **Firefly Image Model 5** (native 4MP, NL editing). Reviews note Adobe's own video generation "trails market leaders like Veo 3.1 and Runway Gen-4.5 for purely creative applications," but Firefly's differentiation is **commercial-safety** (trained only on licensed/Adobe Stock/public-domain content → IP-indemnified output) plus **workflow integration** (Generative Fill/Expand inside Photoshop) and **enterprise governance** (GenStudio, Firefly Foundry custom models). The strategic bet: own the *interface and workflow*, not the frontier model. Sources: [Adobe — Firefly partner models](https://www.adobe.com/products/firefly/partner-models.html), [Feisworld guide 2026](https://www.feisworld.com/blog/adobe-firefly-video-generation-partner-models), [Adobe blog — Firefly expands video/image Mar 2026](https://blog.adobe.com/en/publish/2026/03/19/adobe-firefly-expands-video-image-creation-with-new-ai-capabilities-custom-models).

### Finding 5: Analyst PTs being cut; rating drifting to Hold; but PTs still well above price
Piper Sandler $280→$240 (Neutral, 2026-06-12); DA Davidson $300→$250 (Buy); Bernstein/Mizuho/BMO/Citi all trimmed. Consensus ~Hold, avg PT ~$288 (≈+39% above price). So the Street has cut targets (negative revision pressure) yet still sees material upside vs. a price that's priced for franchise erosion. Sources: [GuruFocus — Piper $240](https://www.gurufocus.com/news/8913797/adbe-maintains-by-piper-sandler-price-target-lowered-to-240), [Investing.com — DA Davidson cut](https://ng.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/da-davidson-cuts-adobe-stock-price-target-on-subscription-outlook-93CH-2555809), [public.com forecast](https://public.com/stocks/adbe/forecast-price-target).

### Finding 6: 10-K/10-Q risk language explicitly names gen-AI competition
Adobe's own filings now cite "increasing competition from companies offering generative and agentic AI solutions, including prompt-based and multi-modal creation and editing … purpose-built AI agents and AI-enabled workflows," and that competitors "may use AI, different data training strategies or proprietary access to data" to compete more rapidly. Adobe counters with "decades of proprietary data … purpose-built models, trusted partnerships, and deep enterprise relationships … precision, brand governance, and seamless integration." Source: [10-Q FY2026 Q2](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000796343/000079634326000112/adbe-20260529.htm), [DEF 14A FY2026](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000796343/000079634326000043/adbe-20260227.htm).

**Key tension (the crux for ADBE):** This is a quality cash machine (89% GM, 41% FCF margin, 13% FCF yield, net-neutral B/S) trading at a multi-year-low multiple **because** the gen-AI disruption thesis is actively playing out in the numbers — decelerating organic growth, a *deliberate* sacrifice of pricing power (freemium + deferred hikes), and Firefly conceding frontier model quality to third parties. The Value and Quality categories will score high; the AI Thesis (especially D3 Moat) and Risk categories must carry the disruption risk. **Do not let the cheap multiple and fat margins halo into a high Moat score** — the moat is exactly what's in question.

## 6. Implications for ratings (flags only — not pre-deciding)

- **D1 AI Revenue %:** AI-first ARR >$500M is <2% of total — but the *entire* franchise is now an AI story (offense + defense). Direct AI rev is small; thesis exposure is high-but-defensive. Lean 2 on strict % disclosure.
- **D2 Supply Chain Position:** Layer-10 app vendor with deep enterprise install base and workflow lock-in, but in a category where AI-native substitutes are proliferating and Adobe itself is deferring price increases — mid-chain with eroding pricing power. Lean 3.
- **D3 Moat — THE crux:** Decades of workflow lock-in, enterprise governance, IP-safe content, install base = real. But pricing power is being *voluntarily surrendered*, Firefly trails frontier models, and competitors attack the core. Halo-check hard. Lean 3 (down from a reflexive 4–5).
- **D4 Capacity Expansion:** N/A in the hardware sense; for software read as product/AI investment — heavy AI R&D + freemium funnel investment + Semrush M&A. Lean 4.
- **D5 Hyperscaler/AI-infra commitment:** Adobe is a *consumer* of AI compute, not a hyperscaler; minimal AI-infrastructure capex commitment. Lean 1–2 (supplier framework: no hyperscaler revenue).
- **M1 EPS revisions:** PTs and ests being cut (Piper, DA Davidson, others). Lean 2.
- **M2 Relative strength vs IGV:** −~40% YTD vs roughly flat sector → severe underperformance. Lean 1.
- **M3 Insider:** No open-market buys; routine sells. Default 3.
- **R1 Customer concentration:** Highly diversified (millions of subs, broad enterprise). Lean 5.
- **R2 Geographic/export:** US-HQ, global diversified SaaS; no single >10% geographic shock, minimal China/policy exposure. Lean 4–5.
- **R3 Balance sheet:** Net-neutral (ND/EBITDA 0.15x), $10B+ FCF, large buyback. Lean 5.
- **R4 Regulatory/litigation:** FTC consumer-cancellation suit history; no current existential antitrust, but gen-AI/IP and consumer-protection overhangs exist. Lean 3–4.
