## Pre-research mental model

(Written before searching.) NNE is a pre-revenue microreactor developer (KRONOS stationary HTGR design, ZEUS solid-core battery reactor, plus acquired ALIP pump / fuel-transport assets), with ~$0 revenue and a large cash pile from repeated equity raises (~$560-570M as of fiscal Q2 2026 per the existing news log). I believed KRONOS moved from pre-application into a formally docketed NRC construction permit application in May 2026 for a demonstration unit at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, ZEUS earlier stage, first operation plausibly 2030-31. I was not aware of any signed, binding datacenter power contract — datacenter linkage assumed narrative/MOU-level. Most recent quarter known: fiscal Q2 2026 (ended ~3/31/2026), EPS -$0.18.

2. **NRC licensing — no longer pre-application.** Construction Permit Application (Part 50) for a full-scale KRONOS MMR prototype at U. of Illinois campus submitted to NRC 2026-03-31 (by the university, NNE's partner); NRC **formally accepted the CPA for review 2026-05-20** (8-K Item 8.01, accession 0001493152-26-024659; GlobeNewswire 2026-05-20). Company expects ~12-month NRC review, initial construction activities mid-to-late 2027. Company claims KRONOS is "the first commercially-ready microreactor to progress to the CPA stage" of NRC licensing. Note: this is a *construction permit* (Part 50 two-step), not a combined license — an operating license application would still follow.

3. **Datacenter linkage — ALL non-binding as of 2026-06-10.**
   - **Supermicro MOU (2026-05-06):** explore integrating KRONOS with Supermicro AI server racks/cooling for "self-powered, grid-independent AI data centers." Explicitly an exploratory framework toward possible future definitive agreements; no financial or timing terms. (GlobeNewswire 2026-05-06; StockTitan.)
   - **BaRupOn feasibility study (completed, per Q2 PR 2026-05-14):** evaluated 1 GW of KRONOS power for BaRupOn's AI Data Center & Manufacturing Campus in Texas; study recommends staged deployment; parties "jointly advancing the project toward initiation of licensing process." A completed paid/joint study, not a power contract. Counterparty is a small private company — 1 GW claim unverified.
   - **Blockfusion MOU:** explore microreactors for remote AI datacenters (company PR; date not re-verified this pass — pre-existing, believed 2025).
   - **EHC Investment MOU** (Gulf region JV) and **DS Dansuk MOU** (Korea localization) — strategic/geographic, not datacenter power contracts. (Q2 PR 2026-05-14.)
   - **No PPA, no signed power-supply or deployment contract with any datacenter operator found.**

4. **STS acquisition — first revenue-generating operations (closed 2026-05-22; 8-K 2026-05-29, Items 1.01/2.01/3.02, accession 0001493152-26-025709).** Acquired Secured Transportation Services LLC (nuclear materials logistics; holds approvals on >90% of active NRC-approved U.S. spent-fuel routes) for up to $13.0M: ~$6.0M cash (~$5.19M net paid at close) + $1.0M stock at close (38,581 shares) + $6.0M stock over 5 anniversary installments (cap 10,364,476 total shares). 6. **Competitive position (microreactor cohort, as of mid-2026).**
   - **Oklo:** COLA (combined license) for Aurora-INL accepted for NRC review — further along in *scope* of license (construction + operation) and a larger reactor class (NRC.gov; GAIN/INL).
   - **X-energy:** Dow Seadrift CP review accelerated; possible CP approval end-2026 (Utility Dive) — SMR class, not microreactor.
