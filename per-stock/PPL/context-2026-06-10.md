# PPL — pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-10)

Prepared for AI Thesis re-rating of Layer 1 power names. Session lens: how DIRECTLY does PPL own the AI power bottleneck (disclosed datacenter load share, rate structures capturing growth, signed electric service agreements) vs. a general "load growth" narrative — and how much upside actually flows through given regulated ROE caps.

---

## Pre-research mental model

PPL Corporation is a regulated utility holding company: PPL Electric Utilities (Pennsylvania, T&D only — PA generation is deregulated), Louisville Gas & Electric / Kentucky Utilities (vertically integrated Kentucky), and Rhode Island Energy. The AI angle as I understood it: one of the largest disclosed data center interconnection queues in the country in PA (tens of GW requested, ~10 GW "advanced" as of mid-2025), plus a 51/49 JV with Blackstone Infrastructure (announced July 2025) to build gas generation in PA for data centers under long-term energy supply agreements — with zero ESAs signed at announcement. Kentucky had its own nascent pipeline (first 400 MW Louisville campus) driving new generation builds under KPSC-approved mechanisms. Key tension: PA T&D earns capped allowed ROE on rate base — datacenter load drives rate-base growth, not price upside. Most recent quarter I had solid knowledge of: ~Q3 2025.

Reaffirmed 2026 ongoing EPS guidance $1.90–$1.98 (midpoint $1.94) and 6–8% annual EPS growth through at least 2029, "near top end" of range, off 2025 actual ongoing EPS of $1.81, "with stronger growth beginning in 2027." Includes a new **large-load tariff**: applies at ≥50 MW single-site / ≥75 MW aggregate (69 kV+); minimum initial contract term ≥10 years; load ramp up to 5 years; minimum load guarantees, exit fees, security deposits equal to transmission upgrade costs; $11M/yr from large-load customers to the low-income program. Settlement language: without protections, large-load growth "poses a risk of stranded assets, unrecovered costs, and cross-subsidization" (Utility Dive, 2026-03 — utilitydive.com/news/ppl-electric-rate-case-settlement-data-center-tariff/814760/). Settlement is black-box: allowed ROE not disclosed in coverage reviewed.

LG&E/KU told KPSC in March 2026 it had 29 potential DC projects, 11 of them (~3.5 GW) at ≥50% probability (LPM/WKYT coverage). Rate base $29.0B (2025) → $42.9B (2029), 10.3% CAGR. 2026 capex $5.1B (PPL release via PR Newswire/investors.pplweb.com, 2026-02-20; Q1 2026 8-K).

## DIFF (what changed vs. mental model)

- **Pipeline is ~3x bigger than my prior:** advanced-stage PA pipeline is 28.3 GW (was ~10 GW in my model from mid-2025), and Kentucky's 11.9 GW pipeline is now material on its own — my model treated KY as a single 400 MW campus.
- **The 28.3 GW is interconnection/wires commitments, not supply contracts.** Press coverage loosely calls these "signed agreements"; they are signed developer agreements in the interconnection process for T&D service — they monetize at capped regulated ROE on wires rate base, not at energy margins.
- **Blackstone JV is still at zero signed ESAs** — unchanged from announcement 10 months ago, despite turbine reservations and PJM queue entries. The merchant-like upside leg of the thesis remains an option, not an asset.
- **New since my model:** $275M PA rate settlement with a dedicated large-load tariff (10-yr minimums, exit fees, security = transmission upgrade cost — de-risks stranded-asset scenario, also confirms DC costs are ring-fenced rather than margin-accretive); KY rate case resolved at 9.775% ROE with GCR rider; capital plan raised to $23B with rate base CAGR 10.3%; EPS growth guided to "near top end" of 6–8%.
- **Negative surprise:** FERC's ISO-NE ROE cut hit the RI segment — a reminder the regulatory-cap risk runs both directions.

## Evidence for D1 (% of revenue datacenter-tied TODAY)

- **Not disclosed by the company.** No DC revenue or margin contribution figure in the Q1 2026 release or coverage reviewed. FLAG.
- Best available math: data center load actually online in PA in 2026 ≈ 0.6 GW (ramp schedule, Utility Dive 2026-05-11) vs PPL Electric peak ~7.8 GW (~8% of peak), and KY's first 130 MW arrives Oct 2026. At ~85–90% load factor, 0.6 GW ≈ 4.5–4.7 TWh/yr vs PA retail delivered ~41 TWh annualized (Q1 2026: 10,315 GWh, 8-K) ≈ ~11% of PA *volumes at full-year run-rate* — but 2026 is the ramp year, and transmission-voltage large-load delivery revenue per MWh is a fraction of blended retail (PA is T&D-only; no energy margin).
- Defensible estimate: **datacenter-tied revenue TODAY is low single digits of consolidated revenue** (PA segment is only $0.25 of $0.63 ongoing EPS; DC load began materially ramping only in 2025-26; large-load tariff rates effective 2026-07-01). This is an estimate, not a disclosure — see FLAGS.
- Forward, the picture inverts: 28.3 GW PA + 11.9 GW KY pipelines vs ~7.8 GW PA peak means DC becomes the dominant marginal growth driver of the $23B capex / 10.3% rate-base CAGR plan.

## Evidence for D2 (regulated return capture vs pricing power)

- **Kentucky (vertically integrated):** authorized ROE 9.775% base / 9.675% riders (KPSC Feb 2026); GCR rider gives timely return ON and OF new DC-driven generation, and the Excess Facilities Rider makes customer-specific build customer-funded. Clean capture of growth, zero pricing power above the cap; stay-out until Aug 2028 fixes base rates.
- **Pennsylvania (T&D only):** DC growth monetizes as transmission/distribution rate base. Distribution: $275M black-box settlement (ROE undisclosed — FLAG), rates frozen ≥2 years after approval. Transmission: FERC formula rate — annual true-ups capture DC-driven capex without rate-case lag, but PA-specific FERC allowed ROE not located in this pass (FLAG). PPL earns the wires return only; energy/capacity upside from PA's deregulated market goes to merchant generators (TLN, VST, CEG cohort), not PPL.
- **Large-load tariff terms cut both ways:** 10-year minimums + exit fees + security deposits = downside protection (no stranded-asset risk), but the same structure confirms there is no price-escalation lever — DCs pay cost-of-service like any other class.
- **The only uncapped vehicle is the Blackstone JV** (unregulated, "regulated-like risk profiles" intended via long-term ESAs) — and it has signed nothing yet.
- **Cap risk is two-sided:** FERC just cut ISO-NE ROE 100bps retroactively (RI segment hit). Allowed ROEs can be revised down.
- Net: PPL converts the AI bottleneck into **volume of rate base** (10.3% CAGR, EPS growth near top of 6–8%) rather than price. Upside flow-through per GW is structurally a fraction of a merchant generator's; downside is commensurately protected.

## Evidence for D5 (named hyperscaler commitments, contracted GW, signed agreements)

- **Named counterparties (PA):** QTS, Amazon Web Services, PowerHouse Data Centers, CoreWeave — named as customers with contracts in PPL territory (Utility Dive 2026-05-11). AWS and CoreWeave are direct AI-buildout names; one true hyperscaler (AWS) named.
- **Named counterparties (KY):** PowerHouse Data Centers / Poe Companies JV — 400 MW Louisville campus, first 130 MW Oct 2026 (LG&E PR 2025-01-16). Developer, not hyperscaler; end tenant undisclosed.
- **Contracted GW:** 28.3 GW PA "advanced-stage" (signed developer agreements; up from 25.2 GW QoQ); ~5 GW of advanced-stage projects reportedly under construction (Investing.com/Utility Dive Q1 coverage — see FLAGS). KY: 11.9 GW pipeline, of which ~3.5 GW at ≥50% probability per KPSC filing (March 2026).
- **Signed energy supply agreements (the merchant-like leg): ZERO.** Blackstone JV has executed turbine reservation agreements and PJM queue submissions only; CEO signals first ESA announcement possible in 2026 (Q1 8-K; Utility Dive 2026-05-11).
- **Distinction that matters for the rating:** PPL's "signed GW" are interconnection/wires commitments — strong evidence of direct bottleneck ownership on the T&D side, fully inside the regulated cap. The supply-side commitment (named hyperscaler ESA with the JV) does not exist yet.
