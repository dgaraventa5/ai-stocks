## 1. Pre-research mental model

NOW is the dominant enterprise-workflow SaaS platform (ITSM/HRSD/CSM/ITOM) running a subscription model with a ~97% renewal rate and RPO of ~$27.7B. The investable AI angle is whether agentic-AI products (Now Assist, AI Agents, the Pro/Pro Plus → token-tier monetization) convert the installed base into a large incremental ACV stream — management's stated target was ~$1B+ (raised toward $1.5B) Now Assist ACV. The open question (from the 2026-05-18 sensitivity lock at 73.5 ✓✓): the stock was down ~50% with heavy current-quarter EPS down-revisions, and the April 9 AI pricing restructure (Pro Plus seat add-on → Foundation/Advanced/Prime token pools) signaled real enterprise friction monetizing AI. Two live debates: (a) is D1 AI-revenue-mix overrated at 4 when AI is <10% of revenue, and (b) does the workflow moat survive agentic-AI commoditization (rule-16 R5).

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not finalize; collaborative session required)

- **rule-10 EV/FCF (Value):** Use statement-based FCF, ~$1.5B Q1 / ~35% FY-guided margin (≈ ~$5.5B FY26 FCF). With the stock down ~50%, the EV/FCF input is materially friendlier than at the 5/18 lock — Value likely improves. Recompute with current EV; do NOT use yfinance `info.freeCashflow` (rule-2 trap).
- **rule-14 expectations:** CLEAN — `expectations_flag.py NOW` returns no flag (P/S at 2.7th percentile of its own 3-year range). This is the opposite of the "peak multiple, decelerating growth" pattern; the de-rating has already happened. No red flag for the briefing.
- **D1 (AI revenue %):** Prior note flagged D1=4 as likely too high vs literal rubric. AI mix still <10% of total ($1B+ Now Assist ACV vs ~$15.75B FY sub) → strict rubric reads **2–3, not 4**. Now Assist >$1M-ACV customers +130% YoY and Pro Plus 2–4× support strong *momentum* but not a high *mix*. **D1 downgrade candidate (4 → 3, possibly 2 on strict read).**
- **D3 (Moat):** 97% renewal (6th straight qtr) keeps the core-platform moat intact; the unresolved question is AI-premium durability (token-pool ROI). Hold D3=5 unless Q2 token consumption disappoints; watch.
- **D2/D4/D5 (other AI-thesis dims):** No new evidence to move; D4 "capacity" remains a loose fit for software.
- **Momentum (M / rule-11 50DMA%):** Stock fell ~17% on a beat; persistent underperformance vs software peers. Refresh `momentum_50dma.py NOW` — likely still weak. Subjective EPS-revisions/relative-strength ratings stay pressured.
- **Risk R1–R4:** New **$4B short-dated term loan + Armis/Veza integration** is a modest R3 (balance-sheet) watch — first real debt, due Oct 2026; not alarming for a ~$5.5B-FCF business but note it. Customer concentration low (renewal 97%, broad base).
- **rule-16 R5 (Disruption Risk) — explicit assessment, DO NOT finalize:** NOW is *both* a leading agentic-AI monetizer (Now Assist/Pro Plus) and a theoretical victim if agents commoditize workflow tooling. Against the absolute lens: the moat is the **workflow data fabric + system-of-record integrations + 97% renewal + regulated/enterprise switching costs** — agents need NOW's structured workflow/CMDB data to act, and NOW is positioning as the *orchestration + security layer* for agents rather than a thin UI that agents replace. That argues the core is **contested but structurally moated**, and NOW is currently a net AI beneficiary, not an erosion victim. **Proposed R5 = 3 (contested-but-moated), with a credible case for 4** (durable; AI is a net tailwind, threatens only peripheral seat-pricing). I would NOT propose R5=2: there is no visible active erosion (renewal flat at 97%, ACV accelerating). Lands **3–4; lean 3** until token-pool ROI/consumption data confirms the AI-premium pricing holds. Stress-test the *whole* Layer-10 cohort on this lens before finalizing (rule-16 method).
- **rule-15:** GAAP EPS YoY is muted (+2%) but appears tax/opex/integration-driven, not one-time-gain-inflated → no blanking; flag that GAAP EPS understates the operating story (non-GAAP op income +26%). Growth category should lean on revenue + cRPO.
