# CRWD (CrowdStrike) -- Context Briefing 2026-05-25

## 1. Pre-research mental model

What I currently believe about CRWD:
- CrowdStrike is a leading endpoint security / cybersecurity platform (Falcon platform)
- AI thesis: dual -- CrowdStrike both USES AI extensively (behavioral threat detection, Charlotte AI) and PROTECTS AI infrastructure (as attack surface grows with GPU deployment)
- Revenue ~$4-5B, growing 30%+ YoY, strong recurring revenue model
- High gross margins (~75-80%), reaching profitability/FCF positive
- Market cap ~$95B -- large cap, well-followed
- Competes with SentinelOne, Palo Alto Networks, Microsoft Defender, CarbonBlack
- Had the massive July 2024 outage (faulty content update crashed millions of Windows systems) -- reputational/legal overhang may still be a factor
- Key products: Falcon platform, Charlotte AI assistant, cloud security (CNAPP), identity protection
- Customers: enterprises, government, critical infrastructure

## 2. WebSearch findings

### Finding 1: FY2026 full-year results stronger than expected, ARR past $5B

CrowdStrike reported Q4 FY2026 (ended Jan 31, 2026) with total revenue of $1.31B (+23% YoY), ending ARR of $5.25B (+24% YoY), and record net new ARR of $331M (+47% YoY). Full-year FY2026 revenue was ~$4.8B. Non-GAAP subscription gross margin was 81%, non-GAAP operating margin 25%, and free cash flow of $376M in Q4 (29% of revenue). The company returned to GAAP operating profitability in Q4 after six consecutive quarters of losses. Full-year FCF reached $1.24B (26% margin).

Long-term target model: subscription gross margins 82-85%, operating margins 28-32%, FCF margins 34-38%.

## 4. Implications for ratings (scoring dimensions affected)

### Value
- At ~29x FY2027E revenue and ~$169B market cap, this is a premium cybersecurity valuation
- FCF yield: ~$1.24B FCF / $169B MCap = ~0.7% -- expensive by any measure
- P/E on FY2027E non-GAAP EPS ($4.78-4.90): ~133-137x
- The quality of the business supports a premium, but the Value score will be low
- **Likely drag on Total Score**

### Quality
- 81% non-GAAP subscription gross margin, improving toward 82-85% target
- 98% gross retention rate
- $1.24B FY2026 FCF (26% margin), targeting 34-38% long-term
- Net new ARR reaccelerating (+47% YoY in Q4)
- GAAP profitability achieved
- **Should score well -- high quality metrics across the board**

### Growth
- Revenue growth decelerating: 23% in Q4 FY2026, guiding 22-23% for FY2027
- ARR growth 23-24% guided for FY2027
- Net new ARR growth reaccelerating (47% YoY in Q4, guided 20-25% for FY2027 full year)
- At $5.9B revenue scale, 22-23% growth is still strong but no longer hypergrowth
- **Moderate-to-good; will depend on how methodology calibrates 22-23% growth**

### AI Thesis
- **Dual AI exposure**: (a) Charlotte AI / AgentWorks -- AI-native product line with frontier model partnerships; (b) protecting AI infrastructure as attack surface expands
- Charlotte AI AgentWorks ecosystem with Anthropic, OpenAI, NVIDIA, AWS -- credible AI product, not just branding
- ISO 42001 AI governance certification -- differentiated
- AI-driven threat detection is a core selling point, not a bolt-on
- However: CRWD is an AI **user/enabler** more than an AI **infrastructure** company. Revenue is cybersecurity, not AI compute/chips/networking
- **The AI thesis connection is real but indirect** -- CrowdStrike benefits from AI infrastructure buildout (more infrastructure = more attack surface to protect) and uses AI in its products, but it's not in the core AI supply chain the way semis, networking, or cloud providers are
- **This is the key scoring question**: how much credit does CRWD get under the AI Thesis dimension for being a cybersecurity layer on top of AI infrastructure?

### Momentum
- Stock at all-time high ($674.84 intraday, May 22, 2026)
- More than doubled from 52-week low
- Named Overall Champion in Omdia 2026 Cybersecurity Platform Ecosystems Leadership Matrix
- Major partnership announcements (Anthropic, OpenAI, NVIDIA, AWS, Salesforce)
- Q1 FY2027 earnings due June 3, 2026 -- potential catalyst
- **Strong momentum; should score well**

### Risk
- Outage litigation dismissed; retention held -- risk has largely dissipated
- Concentration risk: no evidence of unusual customer concentration
- Competition: Palo Alto Networks, SentinelOne, Microsoft Defender remain competitors, but CRWD gaining market share
- Insider selling is routine/pre-planned -- not a red flag
- Key risk remaining: valuation -- at 29x revenue and 130x+ P/E, any growth miss gets punished severely
- Potential regulatory risk around AI in cybersecurity (emerging area)
- **Moderate risk profile; valuation is the primary vulnerability**

### Net assessment before scoring
CRWD is a high-quality cybersecurity platform with strong competitive position, improving profitability, and genuine (if indirect) AI exposure through Charlotte AI and infrastructure protection. The July 2024 outage is no longer a material headwind. The main challenge for the scoring framework is: (a) the AI thesis connection is real but secondary to the core supply chain, and (b) the valuation is extremely rich, which will drag down Value and create asymmetric downside risk on any growth miss.
