## Strategic Customer Agreements (SCAs) — Major Business Model Shift

The single most significant development in this quarter. Micron announced completion of **16 take-or-pay Strategic Customer Agreements**.

| SCA Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Total SCAs signed | 16 |
| Minimum contracted revenue (14 of 16 agreements) | ~$100B |
| Customer upfront cash deposits | $18B |
| Customer letters of credit | $4B |
| Total financial commitment from customers | $22B |
| DRAM volume covered | ~20% |
| NAND volume covered | ~1/3 |
| Customer mix | 4 very large, 3 medium, 9 automotive |
| Primary term | 5 years (2026–2030); automotive ~3 years |

**Pricing structure:** Ceiling at current (Q2 CY2026) market levels; floor price throughout the term, with floor designed to guarantee gross margins **above any level Micron has ever achieved historically**.

**Structural significance:** This converts ~20% of DRAM and 33% of NAND from spot/contract cycle exposure to locked, take-or-pay cash flows. The $22B in upfront commitments is held during performance, returned back-half weighted. This is the most concrete evidence that hyperscalers and automotive customers view memory as a strategic infrastructure input, not a commodity.

## HBM4 Update

- HBM4 in high-volume production for NVIDIA's Vera Rubin platform
- 2,048-bit data bus width (2× prior generation)
- >2.8 TB/s bandwidth per stack
- 2× faster ramp than HBM3E
- Full CY2026 HBM capacity committed under long-term supply agreements
- CEO: no line of sight on when supply will catch up to demand

## Competitive Landscape

- **SK Hynix**: ~43–62% HBM market share, first to demo 16-layer HBM4, primary NVIDIA HBM supplier
- **Micron**: ~24% HBM share, overtook Samsung; fastest DRAM node ramp in company history
- **Samsung**: Still struggling with HBM quality; material share loss to both SK Hynix and Micron

**Key competitive risk flagged by analysts:** Micron losing incremental HBM share on NVIDIA's Rubin platform if SK Hynix accelerates HBM4 qualifications ahead of Micron's yield ramp.

---

## New Risks Raised

1. Coordinated capacity additions (Micron Idaho + NY + SK Hynix + Samsung) could normalize pricing ~2028–2029
2. Export control escalation: Micron's geographic advantage (US-HQ, CHIPS Act recipient) also creates regulatory dependency
3. SCA deposit return liability: $22B in deposits are returned to customers over the back half of the agreements — not a permanent capital improvement
