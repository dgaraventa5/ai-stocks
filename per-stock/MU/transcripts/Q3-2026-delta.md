# Micron Q3 FY2026 vs Q2 FY2026 — Delta Summary
**Written:** 2026-06-26
**Source quarters:** Q2 FY26 (March 2026 call) vs Q3 FY26 (June 24 2026 call)

---

## Management Tone: AI Demand Confidence

**Q2:** "Demand exceeds supply across AI memory." First LTA signed. Confident but one data point.

**Q3:** No qualification, no hedging. Mehrotra: *no line of sight on when supply catches up to demand*. New fab output not until fiscal 2028+. SCAs expanded from 1 to 16 agreements in one quarter. Data center revenue annualized at >$100B.

**Delta: Materially more confident.** The tone moved from "demand is strong" to "we cannot physically satisfy demand." This is a ceiling-on-growth problem, not a demand problem. The 16-SCA announcement confirms customers are making multi-year take-or-pay bets — they believe this too.

---

## Customer Concentration Changes

**Q2:** First LTA signed (one large AI customer, undisclosed). Rest on contract pricing.

**Q3:** 16 SCAs — 4 very large customers, 3 medium, 9 automotive. Combined: ~$100B contracted revenue. Customers deposited $22B in cash upfront. ~20% of DRAM volume and ~33% of NAND volume now take-or-pay through ~2030.

**Delta: Massive concentration improvement through diversification.** Counterintuitively, moving from "lots of contract customers" to "16 take-or-pay customers" reduces concentration risk because:
- 9 of 16 are automotive (diversified end-market)
- Minimum revenue obligations mean cyclical demand drops can't pull volumes below floor
- $22B in deposits absorbs the financial hit even if a customer scales back

Remaining ~80% of DRAM (not under SCA) still has traditional contract-cycle exposure.

---

## AI Revenue Mix Change

**Q2:** Cloud Memory ($7.7B) + Core Data Center ($5.7B) = $13.4B out of $23.9B = 56%. AI/data center majority confirmed.

**Q3:** Data center >$25B out of $41.5B = >60% of total. SSD revenue more than doubled sequentially. HBM4 >$1B for second consecutive quarter.

**Delta: Mix continues shifting toward AI infrastructure.** At >$25B/quarter, data center is now at a $100B+ annualized run rate as a single segment — larger than Micron's total company revenue was in FY2022. The business has structurally re-rated.

---

## Capex / Capacity / Lead Times

**Q2:** FY2026 capex raised to $25B. Idaho and New York fabs under construction. Q3 capex guided at $6.4B.

**Q3:** Q3 actual capex $7.1B. Q4 guided capex ~$10B. Full-year FY2026 capex now >$25B (on track). Idaho meaningful volume by 2028; New York meaningful volume 2028–2030. Lead times extended: no line of sight on supply/demand balance through 2027.

**Delta: Capex accelerating into Q4 ($10B single-quarter step-up).** Lead times lengthening, not compressing — demand growth outrunning supply additions despite record capex. Fab output timelines extended vs Q2 (Idaho moved from "meaningful" to "not until 2028").

|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $41.5B (+24% vs $33.5B guide) | $50B |

The pattern is consistent: Micron has beaten its own guidance by 20-30%+ for two consecutive quarters. The guidance regime appears deliberately conservative (or, more likely, genuinely uncertain in both directions). Either way, consensus estimates after each beat have been insufficient.

## New Risks Raised in Q3

**Not raised in Q2, first mentioned in Q3 or post-Q3 analyst coverage:**

1. **SCA deposit return liability.** $22B in customer deposits is a genuine liability — it must be returned over the back half of the agreements. Net cash appears higher than it is. If customers fail, credit risk materializes. (Not raised by management, flagged by analysts.)

2. **HBM share risk on Vera Rubin.** SK Hynix has first-mover on 16-layer HBM4, is NVIDIA's primary supplier (~43–62% HBM share), and has deeper qualification history on Rubin. If Micron's HBM4 yield ramp lags or fails Vera Rubin qualification criteria, it could lose incremental share to SK Hynix on the highest-volume AI GPU in 2027. (Flagged by several sell-side analysts post-earnings.)

3. **Coordinated capacity normalization ~2028–2029.** All three HBM producers (Micron, SK Hynix, Samsung) are adding capacity that won't meaningfully ship until 2027–2029. If demand moderates or hyperscaler capex cycles down, coordinated supply additions could flip the tight-market dynamic. The SCAs provide partial protection but only on 20% of DRAM.

## Thesis-Relevant Summary

The Q3 call strengthens the structural thesis on every dimension:
- Business model shift: 16 take-or-pay SCAs = partially de-cyclicalized, not commodity anymore
- HBM lead: 2× faster HBM4 ramp than HBM3E confirms Micron has closed the technology gap to SK Hynix
- Pricing power: Floor-priced agreements lock in margins "above any level Micron has ever achieved"
- AI mix: Data center >60% of revenue, trending higher
- Demand: CEO sees no end to the supply/demand imbalance through at least 2027

**No thesis-changing negative developments in Q3.** New risk #2 (SK Hynix HBM share on Rubin) is the most concrete downside scenario and is worth watching at the Q4 call.
