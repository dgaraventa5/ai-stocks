## 1. Pre-Research Mental Model (verbatim)

> Micron is a major beneficiary of AI infrastructure buildout via HBM (High Bandwidth Memory). HBM3E is the key product, supplying NVIDIA's H100/H200/B100/B200 GPU packages. AI/data center revenue has been growing rapidly but Micron still has large DRAM commodity and NAND segments that are cyclical. Competes with Samsung and SK Hynix in HBM — SK Hynix has had a lead in HBM3E yields, but Micron has been closing the gap. Samsung has struggled with HBM quality. Fiscal Q1 FY2026 (reported ~Dec 2025) showed strong data center/AI revenue growth, but guidance may have disappointed on margins due to NAND oversupply and consumer weakness. HBM3E ramping into volume production; HBM4 development underway.

## 7. Open Question: Is It Still a Value?

At $903:
- 15.2x FY2026E EPS, 8.8x FY2027E EPS
- FCF yield ~1.0% TTM but rapidly improving
- PEG 0.26 (yfinance)

Bull case: 8.8x FY27 is cheap for a structural growth story. At even 15x FY27 EPS ($103), implied price is $1,538 (+70%).

Bear case: If HBM demand normalizes and memory reverts to cyclical, peak earnings get a trough multiple. At 7x $54 (low-end FY27E), that's $376 (-58%).

The entry decision depends entirely on conviction in the structural thesis.
