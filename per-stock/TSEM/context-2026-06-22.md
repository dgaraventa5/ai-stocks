## 1. Pre-research mental model

TSEM is a specialty analog foundry (Layer 05 — Fabs/Foundry), Israeli-domiciled but reporting in USD, filing 20-F as a foreign private issuer. The legacy business is specialty analog/RF — RF/SiGe, power, sensors, CMOS image sensors — a slow-growth, cyclical foundry niche. The AI angle is a genuine but (in my prior mental model) still-small pocket: silicon photonics for optical interconnect in AI data centers. Expectation entering this pass: AI/SiPho is a real growth optionality layered on a mature, GM-constrained analog base, with net cash on the balance sheet. Key questions: has SiPho moved from optionality to a material, contracted revenue driver, and is gross margin inflecting?

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not pre-decide)

Per rule 12, subjective ratings are not changed here; these are flags for a collaborative rating session. Objective inputs (rule 9) should be refreshed off the Q1 2026 / Q2-guide / TTM figures above when next scoring.

- **D1 (AI thesis directness):** Strong upward pressure to revisit. SiPho is no longer peripheral — +3× YoY, $1.3B contracted 2027, named AI-interconnect partners (Marvell, NVIDIA, Coherent). BUT apply the absolute-lens discipline (rule 12): the **bulk of revenue is still specialty analog/RF**, and SiPho off a 2025 base is still a minority of the $1.6–1.7B run-rate. Directness rating should weigh the contracted ramp trajectory against the current revenue mix — flag for explicit discussion, do not pattern-match to "optical = high D1."
- **D2 (AI revenue exposure / customer):** Revisit upward — concrete AI-DCI design wins and shipped volume, not just exposure claims.
- **D5 (moat/durability within AI thesis):** Revisit — claimed leading SiPho share, production-ready 400Gbps/lane process, CPO roadmap, and now-secured InP supply (IQE) support a process/know-how moat in SiPho. Counter-consideration: competitors (e.g., foundry SiPho ramps, plus integrated players) are ramping — Ellwanger acknowledged it. Durable but contested.
- **Growth:** Refresh objective Growth inputs — revenue +15% YoY actual, +22% YoY guide. Per **rule 15**, check whether EPS YoY (+62%) is operationally driven (it appears so — operating profit ~2×, margin mix, not one-time) → likely keep EPS YoY, but verify no large one-offs in the primary release before scoring.
- **Quality:** GM inflection (20.4%→26.8% YoY) is the headline. Per rule 9 TTM caveat: TTM GM will understate the current 26.8% run-rate during this margin ramp — **flag the TTM-vs-MRQ divergence** so Quality isn't scored on stale trailing margins. Also flag that TTM FCF/OCF is inflated by the $285M Q1 customer-advance inflow.
- **M (Momentum):** Refresh **50DMA %** via `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` (rule 11) — not computed here. Subjective momentum (EPS revisions likely positive post-beat-and-raise) for discussion.
- **R1–R4 (Risk):** Customer concentration worth checking — SiPho revenue concentrating around a few large optical/networking customers (Marvell named) is a growing concentration vector; geography = Israel (geopolitical) is a standing R2 consideration. Balance sheet R3 strong (net cash). No change pre-decided.
- **R5 (Disruption, rule 16):** **Defaults to 5** — Layer 05 physical specialty-foundry capacity, not a Layer-10 agentic-AI-disruptable software model. No sub-5 R5.
- **Rule 14 (expectations red flag):** Run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py TSEM` before scoring — note the script self-skips on foreign filers / unmapped XBRL revenue tags, which **may misfire on TSEM (20-F filer)**; if it skips, flag the gap rather than fabricate.

**THESIS-BREAK watch (none triggered this pass):** Watched for SiPho ramp slip, end-market softness, guidance cut, margin compression — all four moved the *favorable* direction (5× capacity on track, broad-based + record guide, +640bps GM). No thesis-break.
