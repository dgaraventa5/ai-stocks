## 1. Business Overview

Knowles Corporation is a specialty electronic components manufacturer headquartered in Itasca, Illinois, founded in 1946. The company completed a multi-year transformation from a consumer MEMS microphone company into an **industrial technology pure-play** focused on defense, medtech, electrification, and industrial end markets.

**Key transformation milestones:**
- **Sep 2023:** Announced strategic transformation; acquired **Cornell Dubilier** for $263M cash, adding film, electrolytic, and mica capacitors to the Precision Devices segment ([BusinessWire, 2023-09-18](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20230918922458/en/Knowles-Announces-Next-Phase-of-Transformation-to-an-Industrial-Technology-Company))
- **Dec 2024:** Completed sale of **Consumer MEMS Microphone business to Syntiant** for $150M ($70M cash + $80M Syntiant preferred stock). The divested CMM business had generated $256M revenue in 2023 ([ainvest.com](https://www.ainvest.com/news/knowles-completes-consumer-mems-microphone-business-sale-to-syntiant-24121010b2a88f27dc7008bb/))

**Current two-segment structure:**

| Segment | Q1 2026 Revenue | YoY Growth | Products |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Precision Devices (PD)** | $85.1M | +17.4% | Ceramic capacitors (MLCC), film capacitors, electrolytic capacitors, mica capacitors, supercapacitors, RF filters, EMI filters, microwave devices |
| **MedTech & Specialty Audio (MSA)** | $68.0M | +13.9% | MEMS microphones for hearing aids, balanced armature speakers, audio processors |

## 3. AI Infrastructure Relevance

### Direct AI exposure: Limited but emerging

Knowles does **not** have a direct, material AI data center revenue stream today. The Q1 2026 earnings call contained **no mention** of AI, data centers, servers, or networking applications. Management's growth narrative centers on defense, medtech, industrial, and electrification -- not AI infrastructure.

However, there is a **product-level connection** worth monitoring:

**Knowles published a blog post** ([blog.knowlescapacitors.com](https://blog.knowlescapacitors.com/blog/the-role-of-capacitors-in-powering-ai-at-scale)) detailing how their capacitors address AI data center power delivery:

| Application | Knowles Product | Role |
|---|---|---|
| DC Link (PSU) | CDE 944L film capacitor | Bulk energy storage, voltage smoothing, hold-up time in high-density power supplies |
| LLC Resonant Conversion | C0G high-voltage MLCC | Stabilize LC tank frequency at 98% efficiency targets |
| EMI Filtering | X1/Y2 safety-certified MLCC | Noise suppression in faster-switching environments |
| GPU load buffering | Supercapacitors | Buffer synchronized GPU initialization demands |
| Near-GPU smoothing | Aluminum polymer capacitors | Localized smoothing near GPU loads |

**Context on the broader MLCC-AI connection:** Each AI server (e.g., NVIDIA GB300, AMD MI300) requires 20,000 to 440,000 MLCCs. The 2025-2026 period is when AI server capacitor demand is visibly stressing high-end MLCC capacity ([passive-components.eu](https://passive-components.eu/mlcc-manufacturers-consider-price-increase-as-ai-demand-outpaces-supply/); [barronmlcc.com](https://www.barronmlcc.com/high-capacity-mlcc-for-ai-servers--data-centers-2026-selection--supply-guide.html)). But this market is dominated by Murata, TDK, Samsung Electro-Mechanics, and Yageo -- not Knowles.

### Where Knowles' AI thesis could emerge
1. **Electrification / power infrastructure:** AI data center power demands are driving investment in grid equipment, where Knowles' film and electrolytic capacitors (acquired via Cornell Dubilier) serve power conversion and energy storage applications. The $75M+ large energy order could be adjacent.
2. **Semiconductor equipment:** Management mentioned "semiconductor equipment" as a PD end market with growing demand. Semi-equipment is upstream of AI chip manufacturing (Layers 4-5 in our supply chain map).
3. **Defense electronics:** AI-enabled defense systems (radar, EW, missile guidance) require high-reliability capacitors and RF filters -- Knowles' core competency.
4. **800 VDC data center architectures:** Knowles claims product relevance for the 800 VDC distribution trend in AI racks, but no disclosed revenue.

**Bottom line on AI thesis:** Knowles is a specialty capacitor company with *potential* indirect AI exposure through power infrastructure, semi-equipment, and defense electronics. But this is speculative -- management is not pitching an AI story, and revenue attribution to AI-related end markets is not disclosed. This is a defense/medtech/industrial story first.

## 4. Cornell Dubilier Acquisition Integration

- **Acquired:** 2023, $263M cash consideration
- **What it added:** Film capacitors, aluminum electrolytic capacitors, mica capacitors, supercapacitors -- complementing Knowles' existing ceramic MLCC and RF product lines
- **Integration costs:** $8.6M in FY2025, declining to $5.5M in FY2026 (through Q1), indicating integration is winding down
- **Key product from CDE:** The **specialty film capacitor line** for "pulse power" applications (defibrillators, railgun systems, radiotherapy, downhole oil & gas). This line experienced production challenges in Q4 2024/Q1 2025 during facility consolidation but is now ramping
- **Large energy order:** A $75M+ specialty film contract (with substantial prepayment) is the signature integration win. Revenue ramping from ~$25-30M in 2025 to $55-60M in 2026, with full ramp by end of Q2 2026. Currently a margin headwind (startup costs) but expected to become a tailwind in H2 2026
- **Strategic logic confirmed:** The acquisition expanded Knowles from a ceramic-only capacitor company to a full-spectrum capacitor platform serving higher-power applications in defense and electrification

(Sources: [BeyondSPX/EverTicker analysis](https://everyticker.com/quote/KN/knowles-corporation-specialty-film-ramp-signals-margin-inflection-at-industrial-tech-pure-play-nyse-kn); [Motley Fool Q1 2026 Transcript](https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/04/23/knowles-kn-q1-2026-earnings-call-transcript/))

## 5. End Markets & Customer Concentration

### End Market Exposure (Precision Devices)

All four PD end markets grew YoY in Q1 2026:
- **Defense:** Core growth driver. Elevated orders driven by geopolitical demand (Iran conflict cited as inventory replenishment driver into 2027). Multi-year discussions emerging with defense OEMs. Knowles has 40+ years in mil-spec components.
- **MedTech/Wearables:** Hearing health product cycles driving MSA segment; PD ceramic capacitors also serve medical device power systems.
- **Industrial:** Downhole (oil & gas), semiconductor equipment demand. Broad-based recovery.
- **Electrification:** SiC/GaN power conversion systems, EV applications, grid infrastructure. Growing but early.

### Customer Concentration
- **MSA segment:** Described as having a "very limited customer base" -- likely concentrated in the top hearing aid OEMs (Sonova, Demant, WS Audiology, Starkey). High margins (53.5% gross) suggest strong pricing power / captive position, but concentration risk is real.
- **PD segment:** More diversified across defense primes, industrial OEMs, and distribution partners. No specific concentration metrics disclosed.
- **Book-to-bill:** 1.19x in Q1 2026, marking the **sixth consecutive quarter above 1.0**. 97% of backlog ships within 12 months.

### Pricing Power
Management characterized pricing as "becoming a big part of our opportunity" -- targeting 2%-4% annual price increases in PD through customer-specific negotiations. This is unusual for a component supplier and suggests differentiated/sole-source positions.

## 8. Key Risks

### Structural Risks
1. **Unclear AI thesis:** No disclosed AI infrastructure revenue. The AI connection is speculative and primarily based on product capability, not customer pull. If scoring this name for the AI supply chain watchlist, the AI Thesis score would be low.
2. **Small company in a giants' market:** The MLCC market is dominated by Murata, TDK, Samsung Electro-Mechanics (~60% combined share). Knowles competes on specialization (mil-spec, high-reliability, custom design) rather than volume. Any pricing war in commodity segments would hurt.
3. **Thin analyst coverage and liquidity:** Only ~4 analysts cover the name. Small-cap stocks have wider bid-ask spreads and less institutional sponsorship, though this is improving (130% 1-year return has attracted attention).
4. **MSA customer concentration:** The MedTech & Specialty Audio segment serves a "very limited customer base" of hearing aid OEMs. Loss of a major OEM relationship would be material.

### Execution Risks
5. **Specialty film ramp:** The $75M+ energy order is a high-profile execution test. Any production issues or delays would hit both revenue and credibility. The line already had challenges in Q4 2024/Q1 2025.
6. **Cornell Dubilier integration tail:** Integration costs are declining but not zero ($5.5M in FY2026 YTD). Full margin realization depends on completing facility consolidation and optimizing the combined product portfolio.
7. **Defense spending cyclicality:** Defense is a core growth driver, but defense budgets are subject to political cycles. Current tailwinds (Iran conflict, inventory replenishment) may not persist.

### Valuation Risk
8. **Stock has run significantly:** +130% in the past year, from ~$15 to ~$35. Forward P/E of ~18x is not extreme for the growth rate (~15-20% revenue growth, 50% EPS growth), but a lot of the transformation story is now priced in. Any execution miss would be punished disproportionately.

## 9. Preliminary Assessment for AI Supply Chain Watchlist

**Supply chain layer fit:** Closest to **Layer 2 (Grid Equipment)** or **Layer 4 (Semi Equipment)** as an upstream component supplier. Capacitors go into power conversion, grid infrastructure, and semiconductor manufacturing equipment. Could also be classified as a cross-layer passive component supplier.

**AI Thesis strength:** Weak to moderate. Knowles has relevant products (capacitors for power delivery, RF filters) but no disclosed AI-specific revenue. The company is not marketing itself as an AI play. The investment case is defense/medtech/electrification, which may benefit from AI infrastructure buildout indirectly but is not dependent on it.

**What would strengthen the AI thesis:**
- Management commentary specifically quantifying data center or AI-related revenue
- Design wins with server PSU or networking equipment OEMs
- Disclosure of semiconductor equipment revenue as a % of PD segment
- Participation in 800 VDC data center power architecture programs

**Comparison to existing watchlist names:** More comparable to a KEYS (test & measurement, indirect AI exposure) than a direct AI infrastructure play like NVDA or ANET. The defense/medtech moat and margin profile are attractive independent of AI.
