## 1. Pre-research mental model

POET is a small/micro-cap, essentially pre-revenue / early-commercialization photonics company whose Optical Interposer platform (alignment-free, wafer-level optical engines for 800G/1.6T transmit and receive, CPO-adjacent) targets the optical-interconnect bottleneck in AI/datacenter compute. The entire equity story is design-wins → future production revenue; valuation is narrative-driven, not cash-flow-anchored. As a chronic cash-burner, the dominant risk is funding/dilution, and Value/Quality/Growth fundamentals are effectively not-meaningful (n.m.) because there is no revenue base to score. AI directness is real (optical engines are squarely an AI-infrastructure enabler) but unproven until volume revenue actually lands.

## 4. Results/update highlights

- **The story is shifting from demos to (small) deliveries.** Q1 revenue is still a rounding error ($0.5M), but three commercial signals landed in May 2026: a $50M Lumilens EOI purchase order (with a $500M+ five-year framework), a >$5M POET Infinity 800G production order shipping 2H 2026, and management's >30,000-engine 2026 shipment color. These are design-win-to-revenue conversion datapoints — exactly the milestones the thesis hinges on — but they are orders/frameworks, not recognized revenue, and the dollar amounts are heavily back-end-loaded and contingent.
- **Funding risk is materially de-risked near-term but at heavy dilution cost.** The $400M MMCAP raise on top of $429M cash gives ~$829M pro-forma vs. ~$35M/yr operating burn. Runway is multi-year. The cost: 19.0M new shares + 19.0M warrants (MMCAP) plus up to 22.9M Lumilens warrants — substantial overhang. Stock reportedly fell on the raise.
- **No going-concern flag, no gross-margin disclosure, no audited 2026 guidance.** Margin economics of the engines at volume remain unknown — a key unanswered question for any future Quality/Value framing.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do NOT pre-decide; collaborative rating session required)

- **CRITICAL — Value / Quality / Growth inputs are garbage / n.m.** Pre-revenue (~$0.5M Q1), deeply negative operating income and FCF, no meaningful margins. Forward P/E, EV/EBITDA, FCF yield, P/S (on $2M-ish annualized revenue vs. a multi-hundred-$M+ market cap), PEG, ROIC, gross/FCF margin, EPS YoY — all are not-meaningful for this name. Same garbage-input principle as the negative-EBITDA / pre-revenue blanking conventions (CLAUDE.md rules 10/13/15). Recommend blanking these inputs rather than letting them score; flag in any scoring pass. P/S in particular would be an absurd outlier (and the foreign-ADR/USD trap does NOT apply — POET reports in USD — but the pre-revenue trap does).
- **CRITICAL — R3 (balance sheet) cash-burn/dilution is the dominant risk axis, but is bimodal.** Near-term funding risk is LOW post-raise (~$829M pro-forma, multi-year runway, no going-concern). The offsetting negative is severe, ongoing dilution (38M+ shares/warrants issued/grantable in one month). Whatever R3 lands on, the rationale must capture both sides: liquidity strong / dilution structural.
- **D1 / D5 (AI directness / demand) — real but still unproven.** Optical engines for AI/datacenter interconnect are a genuine AI-infrastructure play, and there are now named customer commitments (Lumilens EOI for AI networks; an unnamed transceiver integrator). But recognized AI revenue is still ~zero; the $50M/$500M framework and >30k-engine color are forward/contingent. Rate the directness of the *exposure* high but separate it from the (still-low) *proven* demand conversion — and do not let order announcements be double-counted as realized demand. Per CLAUDE.md rule 14 (expectations), this is a narrative-priced name where consensus is buying the framework, not the financials — flag the gap between priced-in optimism and de-minimis current revenue.
- **D2 / moat — partially evidenced.** Wafer-level alignment-free interposer, Lightwave award, customer co-development (Lumilens JDA). Genuine differentiation claims, but durability unproven at volume; treat award/JDA as supporting, not dispositive.
- **R5 (Disruption Risk) — defaults to 5.** POET is Layer 07 (Optical/Networking), not Layer 10. Per rule 16, R5 = 5 (no sub-5 R5 outside Layer 10). The relevant tail risks here are execution/dilution/competition (Coherent, Marvell, Broadcom CPO, etc.), not AI-software disruption of a revenue model — those belong in Risk R1–R4 and the AI-thesis dims, not R5.
- **Momentum (rule 11, 50DMA %)** — objective; refresh via `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` if scored. Expect high short-term volatility around the raise (gap down on dilution) and the May PR cluster; the 50DMA% will capture trend but is noisy for a speculative micro-cap. Subjective momentum sub-ratings (EPS revisions, insider, rel-strength) need their own sourcing — not done here.
