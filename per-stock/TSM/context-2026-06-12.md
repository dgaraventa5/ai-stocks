## 1. Pre-research mental model

> TSMC is the dominant pure-play foundry (~2/3 of global foundry revenue, >90% of leading-edge capacity), with HPC/AI as the largest platform (~50%+ of revenue) and key customers Apple (~20-25% of revenue), NVIDIA, AMD, Broadcom, Qualcomm, MediaTek. As of my training, the most recent reported quarter was Q4 2025 (reported mid-January 2026): record revenue around US$32-33B with ~+35-40% YoY growth, gross margin around 59-60%, and 2026 capex guided up materially (roughly US$52-56B range) on AI demand. N2 (2nm) entered volume production in late 2025 at Hsinchu/Kaohsiung with ramp through 2026 led by Apple and then HPC customers; N3 remains tight; CoWoS advanced-packaging capacity was roughly doubling annually and still the AI-accelerator bottleneck. Arizona has Fab 1 (N4) in volume production and an accelerated multi-fab buildout under the enlarged ~$165B US investment plan; main risks are Taiwan geopolitical concentration, potential US tariffs/export controls on AI chips, and overseas-fab gross-margin dilution (~2-3pp/year guided).
