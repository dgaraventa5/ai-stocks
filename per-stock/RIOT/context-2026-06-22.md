## 1. Pre-research mental model

RIOT is a large US bitcoin miner (flagship Corsicana TX with ~1 GW ERCOT-approved power + Rockdale ~700 MW + Kentucky) sitting on a big powered-land/grid position, now pivoting some capacity to HPC/AI hosting under activist (Starboard) pressure. The Value object per rule 13 is secured gross MW, not income-statement metrics — but the AI-conversion question (whether MW becomes AI revenue) belongs to AI Thesis, and as of the last data point RIOT had powered land but essentially one small proof-of-concept AI tenant (AMD ~50 MW), not the investment-grade hyperscale lease the thesis needs. Key watch items: AI-tenant signing slippage at Corsicana, financing/dilution stress, bitcoin-price exposure (large BTC treasury + BTC-collateralized credit), and power-cost economics.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not pre-decide)

- **Rule-13 EV/MW Value object (the key divergence point):** capacity-mw.json carries RIOT at **1,700 MW secured gross**, basis "1.7 GW fully ERCOT-approved gross across Rockdale + Corsicana; AMD lease 50 MW critical IT within it," asof **2026-01-16**, confidence High. Value should run off EV ÷ secured gross MW, NOT income-statement metrics (which are garbage here — fully-loaded mining losses, $(500M) GAAP net loss). RIOT may **score well on EV/MW** because the power base is large — but **D1–D5 (AI Thesis) must stay low until an investment-grade hyperscale tenant is actually signed.** As of now only AMD (50 MW, proof-of-concept) is contracted; ~1.65 GW of the secured base has zero AI/HPC contract. This is exactly the rule-13 design intent: EV/MW measures price per unit of capacity; conversion probability lives in AI Thesis (cf. CLSK precedent in capacity-mw.json).
- **capacity-mw.json staleness:** asof **2026-01-16** is **~5 months stale** and pre-dates the Q1 2026 print (2026-04-30). The headline 1.7 GW number still appears consistent with the Q1 10-Q (Corsicana ~1 GW + Rockdale ~700 MW + KY 162 MW ≈ 1.86 GW gross developed/approved), and the AMD basis moved from 25→**50 MW** critical IT — the json's "AMD lease 50 MW" note is current, but the **as-of date and source should be refreshed to the Q1 2026 filing** before the next scoring pass. Likely a small/no change to the MW denominator, but the date is stale per rule-13's "stale MW denominator is as bad as a stale margin."
- **AI Thesis D1–D5:** D2 (direct AI revenue) and D5/customer-proof remain weak — single small tenant (AMD), no hyperscale signing. Re-examine on any signed Corsicana lease. Do not let Starboard's $1.6B-EBITDA/$21B-EV upside narrative inflate D-dims without a contract.
- **Risk:** R3 balance-sheet — large BTC treasury (15,679 BTC) + **BTC-collateralized Coinbase credit** = direct bitcoin-price exposure both ways; $(500M) GAAP loss is non-cash BTC MtM (rule-15 logic: do not treat as operational deterioration). Dilution overhang from +15M share plan + ongoing equity-funded build-out. **R5 defaults to 5** (Layer 09 physical infra/power — not a Layer-10 disruption target). **THESIS-BREAK watch: financing/dilution stress + bitcoin-price exposure + power-cost squeeze (mining at 126.7% of production value).**
