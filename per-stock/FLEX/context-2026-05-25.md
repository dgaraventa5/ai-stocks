# FLEX (Flex Ltd) — Context Briefing 2026-05-25

## 1. Pre-research mental model

> What I currently believe about FLEX:
> - Flex Ltd is a global electronics manufacturing services (EMS) / contract manufacturer
> - Makes servers, networking equipment, power supplies, and other hardware for OEMs and hyperscalers
> - AI thesis: as GPU server demand explodes, someone has to physically build them — Flex is one of the major contract manufacturers alongside Foxconn and Jabil
> - Revenue likely $25-30B range but low margins typical of EMS businesses
> - Competes with Jabil (JBL), Foxconn, Celestica, Sanmina
> - Market cap ~$15B — under-followed relative to AI theme
> - Has been restructuring/focusing portfolio in recent years
> - Key segments likely include cloud/data center, automotive, industrial, health solutions

### Finding 3: CPI spin-off announced — SpinCo targeting 65-80%+ revenue growth

Flex announced it will **spin off its Cloud and Power Infrastructure segment** into a new independent publicly traded company ("SpinCo"). Key details:
- **Tax-free** distribution to shareholders, targeted to close **Q1 calendar 2027**
- Flex retains **up to 19.9%** ownership at time of spin
- SpinCo targets **65%-75% revenue growth in FY2027**, accelerating to **80%+ in FY2028**
- **Revathi Advaithi** (current CEO) will lead SpinCo; **Michael Hartung** becomes Flex CEO
- SpinCo focuses on end-to-end power and thermal management for AI data centers
- Margin expansion of **100+ bps** expected in CPI as FY2026 capacity investments are recouped
- Capex peaks at **$1.4B-$1.6B in FY2027** to support new hyperscaler contracts, then normalizes

**Bottom line on mental model accuracy:** My prior was materially wrong on multiple dimensions. I had FLEX mentally filed as a low-margin commodity contract manufacturer with a ~$15B market cap. The CPI business is about integrated power and thermal systems for gigawatt-scale data centers — structurally higher-value than commodity server assembly.

## 4. Implications for ratings

### Value (15% weight)
- Market cap has tripled to ~$50B. At $3.30 adjusted EPS FY2026, the stock trades at ~40x trailing earnings. At FY2027 midpoint guidance of $4.36 EPS, forward P/E is ~30x.
- For an EMS company, even one with a high-growth segment, this is a premium valuation. The spin-off complicates valuation — you're buying two businesses in one.
- FCF of $1.06B on a $50B market cap = ~2% FCF yield. Below-average.
- **Flag:** Value score likely pressured by the run-up. Need to think about whether to score pre-spin or post-spin.

### Quality (20% weight)
- Gross margin 9.9% and operating margin 6.7% are record levels and well above EMS industry norms (~5-6% gross, 3-4% operating historically).
- Six consecutive quarters at 6%+ operating margin suggests structural improvement, not one-time.
- Free cash flow positive despite heavy capex investment phase.
- **Flag:** Margin trajectory is genuinely impressive. Quality score should reflect this is not a commodity EMS business anymore.

### Growth (15% weight)
- FY2026 revenue +8%, FY2027 guided +18% at midpoint. CPI segment +38% in Q4.
- SpinCo guiding 65-80%+ revenue growth in FY2027-2028.
- EPS growth +25% FY2026, guided +32% FY2027.
- **Flag:** Growth profile is very strong. The question is how much is already priced in at 30x forward.

### AI Thesis (30% weight)
- This is the core of the FLEX story. CPI segment (~$6.5B, 25% of revenue) is directly building the physical infrastructure for AI data centers.
- Not just server assembly — integrated power, thermal, and modular solutions for gigawatt-scale facilities.
- Named partnerships with Google (multiyear), AMD (MI355X manufacturing in Austin), NVIDIA (Omniverse DSX).
- AI infrastructure platform announced for 30% faster deployment.
- **Flag:** AI thesis score should be strong. The spin-off creates a pure-play AI infrastructure company. The question is whether the remaining Flex (automotive, industrial, health, consumer) still has AI relevance post-spin.

### Momentum (10% weight)
- Stock tripled from $41 low to $132 current. 52-week high $147.
- Multiple guidance raises during FY2026.
- Spin-off announcement is a positive catalyst.
- **Flag:** Momentum is clearly strong, but the stock is near its 52-week high — some of the move may be behind us.

### Risk (10% weight)
- **Customer concentration:** Google multiyear contract is a major driver — losing it would be material. AMD partnership is newer.
- **Execution risk on spin-off:** Separating a high-growth segment while maintaining service levels is non-trivial. CEO going with SpinCo leaves RemainCo with new leadership.
- **Capex intensity:** $1.4-1.6B capex in FY2027 is a big number. If hyperscaler spending slows, Flex could be over-invested.
- **Valuation risk:** At 30x forward, any miss on CPI growth or spin-off timeline could cause a significant de-rating.
- **Post-spin RemainCo:** The remaining Flex business (automotive, industrial, health, consumer) may not sustain the current multiple without the CPI growth engine.
- **Flag:** Risk profile is moderate-to-elevated. The spin-off is a double-edged sword — it could unlock value or create two sub-scale entities.
