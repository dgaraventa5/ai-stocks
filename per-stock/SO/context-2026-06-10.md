# SO — Pre-rating context briefing — 2026-06-10

Prepared for AI Thesis re-evaluation of Layer 1 power names. Session lens: how DIRECTLY does SO own the AI power bottleneck (disclosed datacenter load share + rate structures capturing growth), and how much upside actually flows through given regulated ROE caps.

## Pre-research mental model

SO is the dominant Southeast regulated utility holdco (Georgia Power, Alabama Power, Mississippi Power, Southern Company Gas, plus mostly-contracted Southern Power). Georgia Power sits on one of the largest datacenter load pipelines in the country — by late 2025 management cited a ~50 GW "large load" pipeline with several GW committed, and the 2025 Georgia IRP process added large-load tariff protections (minimum-take contracts) approved by the Georgia PSC. Vogtle 3 & 4 (~2.2 GW new nuclear) entered service 2023/2024. Upside flows through rate-base growth at capped allowed returns, not merchant pricing. Last quarter I was confident on pre-research: Q3 2025; I did not have Q4 2025/FY2025 results, Q1 2026, the Dec 2025 PSC generation certification, or the DOE loan.

### Georgia regulatory architecture (the D2 core)
- **Retail ROE set point 10.50%, band 9.50%–11.90%, equity ratio 56%** under 2022 ARP + ARP Extension. Earnings above 11.90% shared: 40% to regulatory assets, 40% refunded to customers, **only 20% retained by Georgia Power**. No recovery of shortfall below 9.50%. (SO 10-K FY2025, Note 2, Georgia Power – Rate Plans)
- **Base rates frozen through 2028** (Georgia PSC order 2025-07-31). (Georgia PSC Data Center Fact Sheet, March 2026)
- Jan 2025 PSC rule for large-load (>100 MW) customers: **minimum billing requirements, longer contract terms**, all new datacenter contracts filed with PSC ≥30 days before execution. April 2025: revised price structures for large-load. (Georgia PSC Data Center Fact Sheet, March 2026)
- **Dec 19, 2025: PSC certified 9,985 MW of new generation, ~80% expected to power data centers** (fact sheet says 9,985 MW; Perkins Coie and Georgia Recorder report 9,885 MW — UNRESOLVED DISCREPANCY, check the PSC order itself). Estimated cost ~$16B over 5 years (georgiarecorder.com 2025-12-19); 10-K cites $19.5B certified cost for the Georgia Power projects in service through 2030 — different scopes, reconcile before citing.
- Dec 2025 stipulation: Georgia Power must allocate portfolio costs to large-load customers in the 2028 rate case sufficient to ensure **≥$8.50/month downward pressure on the typical 1,000 kWh residential bill for 2029–2031**, and ensure **$556M in annual revenue from large load customers** at the 2028 rate case. (thecentersquare.com / Perkins Coie, Dec 2025)
- **Georgia Power financially "backstops" the new generation costs through 2031** — if datacenter contracts don't materialize at expected levels, the company (shareholders), not ratepayers, eats the cost. If contracts for all ~9.9 GW aren't in place by end-2026, PSC has 5 years of remediation options (suspend projects, close plants early, end PPAs, force open-market sales). (Georgia PSC Data Center Fact Sheet, March 2026)
- PSC voted to keep large-load cost-of-service details **confidential until 2028**. (NRDC press release, Dec 2025)

## DIFF (what changed vs my mental model)

1. **Bigger and faster than modeled.** Contracted large load is now >11 GW across 28 projects with 6 GW more in final stages and a >75 GW prospective pipeline — my model had "~50 GW pipeline, several GW committed." Q1 2026 DC consumption +42% YoY is acceleration, not steady-state.
2. **The Dec 19, 2025 PSC certification (~9.9 GW, ~80% for datacenters, ~$16B) and the $26.5B DOE loan did not exist in my model.** Capex plan went from $45.2B/5yr to $78.1–81B/5yr — a ~73%+ step-up — and long-term EPS growth guidance is now 8–9% through 2028.
3. **The risk allocation flipped vs my assumption.** I assumed minimum-take contracts pushed risk onto datacenters. Partially true (minimum bills, termination payments), but the Dec 2025 stipulation makes Georgia Power *shareholders* the backstop for the new generation through 2031 if datacenter load doesn't materialize — downside is on SO, upside is capped at the ROE band.
4. **Pipeline quality is softer than headlines.** PSC staff documented 11.3 GW of datacenter exits since 2023 and called materialization "underperforming expectations"; only ~1.9 GW had executed contracts under the new framework as of Nov 2025 (vs 9.9 GW of generation being built).
5. **Rate freeze through 2028** means no base-rate true-up window until the 2028 case; near-term EPS growth must come from sales volume, riders, and the existing ARP mechanics.

## Evidence for D1 (% of revenue datacenter-tied TODAY)

**Hard disclosure: NONE.** Neither SO nor Georgia Power discloses datacenter sales as a % of retail KWh or revenue. FLAG: this is a disclosure gap, not a research gap — checked 10-K, Q1 2026 transcript coverage, PSC fact sheet.

Derived bound (estimate-grade, assumptions explicit):
- Q1 2026: DC consumption +42% YoY while weather-normal commercial class grew +4.5% (transcript). If non-DC commercial grew ~0.5–1%, DC share of commercial KWh a year ago ≈ (4.5 − 0.75) / (42 − 0.75) ≈ 9%; ≈ 12–13% of commercial class today. Commercial is roughly one-third of Southern's retail KWh (typical class mix; NOT verified from 10-K KWH tables this session) → **datacenters ≈ 3–5% of retail KWh today**. Revenue share likely similar or lower (large-load tariffs price below residential per-kWh).
- Cross-check: Dec 2025 stipulation guarantees $556M annual large-load revenue at the 2028 rate case vs $29.55B FY2025 total revenue ≈ **1.9%** — but that's incremental Georgia Power large-load revenue, not total DC revenue; floor, not estimate.
- Conclusion for rating: datacenter-tied revenue TODAY is small single-digit %. The thesis is forward rate-base growth, not current revenue mix.

## Evidence for D2 (regulated return capture vs pricing power)

- **No pricing power in the merchant sense.** Georgia Power retail ROE set point 10.50%, evaluated against 9.50–11.90% band; above 11.90%, only 20% of excess is retained (40% reg assets / 40% customer refunds) (10-K FY2025, Note 2). Base rates frozen through 2028 (PSC order 2025-07-31).
- Upside transmission mechanism is **rate-base growth at allowed ROE**: capex plan $78.1B 2026–2030 (10-K) vs $45.2B prior plan = the actual AI earnings lever; management guides 8–9% EPS growth through 2028 on it.
- Large-load tariff protections (Jan 2025 rule: minimum bills, longer terms, termination payments; Apr 2025 price structure changes) protect *ratepayers and the company against stranding* — they do not let SO capture premium pricing. Dec 2025 stipulation explicitly forces large-load revenue to push residential bills DOWN ($8.50/month by 2029–31): datacenter economics are being shared with ratepayers by design.
- **Asymmetry to price into the rating:** shareholders backstop ~9.9 GW of new generation through 2031 if DC load under-materializes; upside above the ROE band is 80% given away. A merchant (VST/CEG/TLN/NRG) keeps the price spike; SO keeps the volume-driven rate base at ~10.5% ROE on 56% equity.
- Mitigant: DOE loan at Treasury +37.5 bps lowers financing cost (savings flow to customers, $7B/30yr) and only $1.8B equity needed through 2030 — low dilution path for an $80B capex program.
