## 1. Pre-research mental model

TOELY is Japan's WFE leader — a near-monopoly in coater/developer (track) plus strong positions in etch, deposition, cleaning, and test. Long-thesis is AI-driven leading-edge logic (2nm) + memory (HBM/DRAM) + advanced packaging WFE, partly offset by a structural decline in its outsized China mature-node mix as export curbs and the post-2024 mature-node binge unwind. Prior expectation entering this pass: FY2026 a flattish/transition year (China rolling off) with a back-half-loaded re-acceleration into FY2027 on AI/HBM. Key watch items: China revenue %, WFE direction, and guidance.

## 2. Latest results highlights

All figures JPY unless labeled. Source: Tokyo Electron FY2026 (FY ended **March 31, 2026**) full-year results, announced ~April 2026; reported via Investing.com (2026-04, "FY2026 slides: Q4 beats fuel 40% growth outlook") and NineScrolls (2026-04). **JPY — not USD.** No USD conversions made below; where a USD figure is needed it must use the period-appropriate JPY/USD rate and be labeled.

- **Net sales FY2026:** ¥2,443.5B, +0.5% YoY — record high but essentially flat (Investing.com FY2026 slides, 2026-04; NineScrolls, 2026-04).
- **Operating income FY2026:** ¥624.9B, beating company's own Feb-2026 guidance of ¥593B (Investing.com, 2026-04).
- **Net income FY2026:** ¥574.4B, +5.6% YoY; **EPS ¥1,254.57** (Investing.com, 2026-04).
- **Gross margin FY2026:** 45.3%, **down 1.8pp YoY** on higher labor + materials costs (Investing.com, 2026-04). *Margin-strain flag — see globeandmail headline "Robust Start Despite Margin Strain."*
- **Free cash flow FY2026:** ¥433.2B (record); shareholder returns ¥437.4B (dividends + buybacks) (Investing.com, 2026-04).
- **Segment mix FY2026:** SPE (new equipment) sales ¥1,775.4B; **Field Solutions** (parts/service/mods) ¥626B, **+16.3% YoY** (Investing.com, 2026-04). Service growth is the resilient annuity leg.
- **New-equipment application mix FY2026:** non-memory 59%, DRAM 31%, non-volatile (NAND) 10% (Investing.com Q3/FY2026, 2026; NineScrolls, 2026-04).
- **China mix FY2026:** **34.1% of total sales for the full year, but fell to 26.8% in Q4 FY2026** (Investing.com / NineScrolls, 2026-04). Analysts project China settling ~30% going forward, down from 41–42% peak. *This is the China-roll-off playing out, not yet a cliff — see DIFF.*
- **Advanced chips** (2nm logic, leading-edge DRAM, HBM packaging into TW/KR/US/JP) rose to **~40% of total sales** in FY2026, the mix shift away from China mature-node tools (NineScrolls / TrendForce, 2025-12 to 2026-04).

## 4. Earnings/results commentary highlights

From Q4 FY2026 call coverage (Investing.com transcript article, 2026-04; NineScrolls, 2026-04):
- Management framed FY2026 as the **trough/transition** — flat headline masks a mix shift from China mature-node tools toward AI leading-edge (2nm logic, leading-edge/HBM DRAM, advanced packaging).
- FY2027 setup is the re-acceleration: 1H guided +33% revenue / +42% OI, with coater-developer (TEL's monopoly leg) >+50% — the highest-conviction growth driver.
- Margin pressure (labor + materials) is the explicit negative; bulls expect operating leverage to recover GM as volumes scale into FY2027.
- DRAM investment described as broadening beyond HBM into commodity DRAM, double-digit growth expected to continue. (PortersFiveForce summary of IR commentary, 2026.)
- *Caveat:* commentary sourced from secondary transcript aggregators, not yet cross-checked against the primary tel.com PDF — verify exact quotes before citing in thesis.
