# FORM (FormFactor, Inc.) — Refresh-Context Briefing

**Date:** 2026-06-05
**Layer:** 04 Semiconductor Equipment & Materials / Test & measurement
**Business:** Probe cards for wafer sort (Foundry & Logic, DRAM, Flash) + engineering/test Systems (probe stations, cryogenic, thermal, CPO/Triton test platform)

---

## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim)

> FormFactor is the dominant probe card supplier (~$700M-1B revenue range), with a near-duopoly/oligopoly structure in advanced probe cards alongside Japanese players (Technoprobe, MJC/Micronics). My understanding: DRAM is a large segment, Foundry & Logic growing, with HBM driving probe card intensity since HBM stacks require more wafer-sort test touchdowns. Last quarter I have any clarity on is roughly mid-2025; gross margins were in the low-to-mid 40s% with a long-term target near 47%. Net cash balance sheet, some China revenue exposure. AI angle is real but indirect — probe cards are consumables tied to DRAM/HBM and logic wafer volumes, so the company benefits from HBM unit growth and KGD (known-good-die) test intensity.

## 5. DIFF — what changed since my training

- **Revenue run-rate much higher than my mental model:** $226M/qtr (~$900M+ annualized) and guiding $240M — my "~$700M-1B range" understated the current pace; topline is +32% YoY and accelerating.
- **Gross margin materially above my prior:** non-GAAP GM now 49.0% (Probe Cards segment 50.5% GAAP). I anchored on low-40s% with a 47% long-term target — the company has blown through that target. This is the kind of staleness the earnings-refresh rule (CLAUDE.md §9) targets: GM moved +510bps sequentially.
- **DRAM/HBM mix bigger and faster:** DRAM +69.7% YoY to 36.7% of rev; HBM explicitly the driver. NVIDIA now a 10%+ direct customer (Foundry & Logic / networking).
- **Competition is a sharper threat than I modeled:** Technoprobe doubled share since 2018 and took ~30% of TSMC 2nm quals — moat is real but actively contested at the leading edge. FORM responded with a $120M MEMS acquisition.
- **New capex cycle:** Farmers Branch $140–170M 2026 build — capacity expansion is now "major buildout funded and underway," not steady-state.
- **CPO/Triton optionality:** Systems repositioning toward co-packaged optics test — a new AI-networking-linked product vector I hadn't weighted.
