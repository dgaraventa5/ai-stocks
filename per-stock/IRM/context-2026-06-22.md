## 1. Pre-research mental model

Iron Mountain is a records-storage cash cow (Global RIM) whose 38-year storage-rental annuity funds a deliberate pivot into higher-growth adjacencies: **data centers** (leased-MW ramp, hyperscaler demand), **ALM/Asset Lifecycle Management** (IT asset disposition + data-center decommissioning), and **digital solutions** (InSight digital transformation). Project Matterhorn is the multi-year transformation/cross-sell program driving this. The AI angle is genuine but **partial** — data center is still a minority of total revenue versus the legacy records base, so AI exposure is blended/indirect, not pure-play. I expect high leverage (net debt/EBITDA elevated for a REIT), AFFO growth in the low-to-mid teens, and a rich-ish multiple for a transitioning REIT that the market is increasingly valuing on the growth story rather than the legacy annuity.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — no rating numbers pre-decided)

**REIT-economics caveat (CRITICAL — applies across categories):**
- **Value:** EV/EBITDA is the right Value metric mechanically *but* read it knowing IRM is a high-leverage REIT — EV is inflated by ~$17B net debt, so EV/EBITDA will look elevated vs equity-light names; that is structural, not a quality signal. FCF-yield / P/S inputs should be sanity-checked against **AFFO yield and P/AFFO** (~$5.79–$5.86 FY2026 AFFO/share guide is the right denominator), since FCF and EPS understate REIT cash earnings. Flag for the rater: **don't penalize IRM on EPS-based or naive FCF inputs — AFFO is the correct lens.**
- **Quality / Growth:** EPS-based Quality and Growth inputs (ROIC, EPS YoY) are **unreliable** for a REIT — depreciation-heavy GAAP suppresses EPS and ROIC. Growth should lean on revenue (+22%) / AFFO growth, not EPS. Consider whether EPS YoY should be treated cautiously here (analogous to rule-15 garbage-input discipline, though this is a structural REIT issue, not a one-time item).
- **Rule 14 (expectations red flag):** Guidance raised, stock likely re-rated on the growth story — run `scripts/expectations_flag.py IRM` for the P/S-percentile + decel check before trusting Value. Note IRM is a domestic filer (script should run cleanly).

**AI Thesis (D1–D5):**
- **D1 (directness of AI exposure):** flag as **PARTIAL / blended.** Data center ($255M/qtr, +47%) + ALM AI-hardware-refresh decommissioning are real AI vectors, but the **majority of revenue is still legacy RIM records** (~$1.4B of ~$1.94B). D1 should reflect blended, indirect exposure — not pure-play data center. This is the single most important AI-thesis nuance for IRM.
- **D2 (demand visibility / backlog):** strengthened — 32 MW leased YTD, ≥100 MW target with stated upside, 400 MW 24-month pipeline, record 72 MW NoVa lease. Look here.
- **D5 (secular tailwind / moat):** ALM decommissioning + DC both ride the AI capex cycle; InSight FedRAMP High adds a federal moat angle. Re-examine.

**Momentum (M):**
- Strong: record results, guidance raise, all-time-record DC lease, 25-year-high organic growth — likely supports EPS-revision / relative-strength subjective ratings and the objective 50DMA % (refresh via `scripts/momentum_50dma.py IRM`). Flag M for an upward look.

**Risk (R1–R5):**
- **R3 (balance sheet) — KEY FLAG: HIGH LEVERAGE.** Net debt $16.97B, 4.8x net lease-adjusted (rising in absolute $ on DC capex build). Even though it's improving and in-target for a REIT, it is elevated vs the watchlist — R3 should carry this.
- **R1 (customer concentration):** DC/ALM lean on hyperscalers; growing hyperscaler dependence in the growth segments is worth a look, though RIM diversifies the overall base.
- **R5 (disruption risk):** **defaults to 5** — IRM is physical infrastructure / records / data centers (non-Layer-10). No agentic-AI disruption to the core revenue model; AI is a tailwind, not a threat. Per rule 16, default max.

**Earnings-triggered refresh (rule 9):** IRM reported Q1 2026 on 2026-04-30 — this briefing satisfies the subjective-review side; the **objective input refresh (yfinance: margins, FCF/AFFO, P/S, 50DMA) should still be run** before any scoring pass, with the REIT caveats above applied (AFFO over FCF/EPS).

**THESIS-BREAK signals checked — NONE triggered.** No DC leasing deceleration (accelerating), records volume stable/growing (not declining), AFFO guidance *raised* (not cut), leverage improving (no covenant stress evident), no dilutive funding or regulatory action surfaced. Watch-items only: rising absolute net debt on capex build, growth-segment hyperscaler concentration, and expectations/crowding given the re-rate.
