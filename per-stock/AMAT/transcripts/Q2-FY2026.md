## Prepared Remarks — Key Themes

### 1. WFE outlook raised to >30% growth (from >20%)

Gary Dickerson raised Applied's calendar 2026 semiconductor equipment (WFE) growth forecast from "more than 20 percent" to "more than 30 percent." This is the second consecutive quarter of upward revision.

Leading-edge foundry logic, DRAM, and advanced packaging together account for "more than 80% of year-on-year WFE growth." This is the first year leading logic spending exceeds ICAPS.

### 2. Advanced packaging — >50% growth in CY2026

Advanced packaging is AMAT's highest-growth business, expected to grow above 50% in calendar 2026. Key drivers:
- HBM stacking: die counts increasing from 12 to 16 to 20+
- HBM requires 3-4x more wafer starts per delivered bit vs standard DRAM
- 3D chiplet stacking and large-body AI accelerator packages
- NEXX acquisition (from ASMPT) adds panel-level advanced packaging ECD capability

Dickerson: Applied is "the leader in packaging" — positioned across etch, deposition, CMP, and now panel-level ECD.

### 3. Gate-All-Around (GAA) driving record foundry revenue

Sym3 conductor etch system described as "the fastest-ramping product in Applied's history" with "more than 250 chambers" shipped. GAA enablement products (Trillium ALD, precision PECVD) also contributing record foundry revenue.

GAA nodes expected to be "one of our fastest-growing businesses this calendar year."

### 4. DRAM — record $1.7B revenue

DRAM revenue hit $1.7B, up 18% YoY. AMAT is "the number 1 process equipment provider" for DRAM. Strength in:
- 6F-squared DRAM node transitions
- Periphery CMOS logic upgrades
- HBM packaging capacity additions
- Next-generation DRAM development accelerating

### 5. ICAPS — digestion phase, flat to slightly higher

ICAPS (IoT, Communications, Auto, Power, Sensors) spending expected "flat to slightly higher" in CY2026. Currently in a digestion period after recent capacity additions. Sub-segments showing strength: analog, photonics, power chips.

300mm fab utilizations described as "very healthy." ICAPS expected to resume growth at "mid to high single digits" once utilization catches up.

### 6. China — 24%, declining but factored into guidance

China represented 24% of Semi Systems + AGS revenue, down from ~27% in Q1. Management expects China/ICAPS business "flat to slightly higher in the calendar year." All current restrictions and conditions factored into guidance.

**BIS settlement:** $252.5M penalty paid in full during Q2 for unauthorized reexports of ion implanters to SMIC via South Korea (2020-2022). Second-highest BIS penalty ever. The charge hit Q1 GAAP operating income; cash payment in Q2.

### 7. Gross margin — 25-year high, still expanding

Non-GAAP gross margin of 50.0% is the highest in more than 25 years. Semi Systems segment gross margin reached 54.8%. Drivers:
- Value-based pricing on differentiated products
- Portfolio mix enrichment (more leading-edge, less commodity)
- Manufacturing cost innovations
- 800 bps cumulative expansion since Gary Dickerson became CEO (2013)

Guided 50.1% for Q3, implying continued gradual expansion.

### 8. Applied Global Services — mid-teens growth

AGS revenue $1.67B, up 17% YoY (record). Growth revised upward from low-double-digit to mid-teens expectation. Over 35,000 chambers now connected to AIx software with AI-powered monitoring. Mix shifting toward higher-margin transactional and spare parts revenue.

### 9. EPIC Center launching fall 2026

Founding partners: TSMC, Micron, Samsung, SK Hynix. University partners: ASU, RPI, Stanford. Development partner: Advantest.

Positioned to accelerate technology commercialization and provide "multi-node technical and demand visibility." Strategic moat builder — embeds AMAT deeper into customers' R&D pipelines.

## Q&A Highlights

### Backlog and visibility (C.J. Muse, Cantor Fitzgerald)
Customers providing "rolling 8-quarter forecasts" for supply chain planning. Brice Hill: "Our supply chain needs that kind of lead time...to make their investments." Over 100 factory projects tracked globally; 10+ new projects added last quarter. Expected "continued growth across extended planning horizon into 2027 and beyond."

### China geopolitics (Timothy Arcuri, UBS)
Dickerson declined specific commentary: "All of that has been factored into our guide for the quarter and our viewpoint on growth."

### ICAPS recovery timing (Sidney Ho, Deutsche Bank)
Brice Hill: "ICAPS is still a very strong market...we just won't grow a lot year-over-year until we digest the capacity." Projected ICAPS to grow at "mid to high single digits" once utilization catches up.

### Conductor etch share gains (Joseph Moore, Morgan Stanley)
Dickerson: "Sym3 is the fastest-ramping product in Applied's history" with "more than 250 chambers, multi-hundreds of millions of dollars of growth." GAA nodes are key demand driver.

### AGS margin trajectory (Harlan Sur, JP Morgan)
Brice Hill: margins at "a healthy spot" driven by mix shift to transactional and spare parts. Mid-teens growth expected.

### 2027 outlook (Stacy Rasgon, Bernstein)
Brice Hill: "2027 still looks like a strong growth year." Dickerson: "We see a similar profile in 2027 to what we're seeing in 2026" driven by AI computing demand.

### Gross margin sustainability (Stacy Rasgon, Bernstein)
Hill: Q2 non-GAAP gross margin of 50% reflects "Semi Systems gross margin...54.8%." Improvement driven by portfolio mix, value-based pricing, and manufacturing efficiencies. Guided 50.1% for Q3.

### NEXX acquisition (Atif Malik, Citi)
Dickerson: addresses "large area packaging electroplating" for "large body size architectures" for AI accelerators.

### Agentic AI demand
Management flagged agentic AI as an emerging demand layer driving additional CPU, DRAM, and NAND requirements beyond traditional AI training/inference workloads.
