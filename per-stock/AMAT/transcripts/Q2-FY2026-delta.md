# AMAT Q2 FY2026 — Delta Summary

**Reported:** May 14, 2026 | **Quarter ended:** April 26, 2026
**Prior quarter:** Q1 FY2026 (reported Feb 12, 2026)

---

## What changed this quarter

### 1. AI equipment demand signal — strongly positive

AMAT raised its CY2026 WFE growth forecast from "more than 20%" (Q1 call) to "more than 30%" (Q2 call). This is the second consecutive upward revision and the most bullish WFE call from any major semi equipment company this cycle.

Leading-edge foundry logic, DRAM, and advanced packaging now account for "more than 80% of YoY WFE growth." This is the first year leading logic spending exceeds ICAPS — a structural shift reflecting the AI capex cycle's dominance over legacy end-markets.

**What it means for the thesis:** Semi capex is accelerating into mid-2026, not decelerating. The Q1 FY2026 revenue miss (which prompted some bearish takes) now looks like a timing blip, not a cycle turn. AMAT has 8-quarter rolling visibility from major customers and sees 2027 as "a similar profile to 2026."

### 2. China revenue — declining but manageable

China dropped to 24% of Semi + AGS revenue (from ~27% in Q1 FY2026). Management expects China/ICAPS business "flat to slightly higher" for calendar 2026. Export controls are baked into guidance.

The BIS settlement ($252.5M penalty for unauthorized SMIC reexports) is now paid and behind them. This removes a legal/reputational overhang.

**What it means for the thesis:** China revenue is a declining tailwind, not a rising risk. The mix shift toward leading-edge (where China is largely excluded) reduces future exposure organically. The BIS settlement closure removes the largest regulatory tail risk.

### 3. Advanced packaging — strongest growth vector

Advanced packaging revenue expected to grow "above 50%" in CY2026. AMAT is self-described "leader in packaging" with the broadest portfolio (etch, deposition, CMP, and now panel-level ECD via NEXX acquisition).

Key demand drivers:
- HBM stack counts rising 12 -> 16 -> 20+ dies, each requiring 3-4x more wafer starts per bit
- 3D chiplet architectures for AI accelerators driving new packaging complexity
- Large-body AI accelerator packages requiring panel-level processing (NEXX's niche)

**What it means for the thesis:** AMAT's advanced packaging position is directly correlated with HBM and AI accelerator buildout. This validates the "pick and shovel" thesis — AMAT benefits from every major AI chip architecture trend without needing to bet on a single customer or design win.

### 4. Guidance and backlog — accelerating into Q3

Q3 FY2026 guidance: $8.95B revenue (+13% sequential, +23% YoY), EPS $3.36 (+36% YoY). This implies meaningful acceleration from Q2's already-record numbers.

Supporting evidence:
- Customers increased orders in the prior 90 days
- 100+ global fab projects tracked, 10+ new projects added last quarter
- 8-quarter rolling forecast visibility from large customers
- Supply chain capacity (not demand) is the primary constraint

**What it means for the thesis:** The demand curve is steepening, not flattening. AMAT is capacity-constrained on the supply chain side, which typically supports pricing power and margin expansion. The 8-quarter visibility is unusually long for semi equipment — consistent with a multi-year AI capex super-cycle, not a normal capex pulse.

### 5. Margin trajectory — structural expansion continuing

Non-GAAP gross margin hit 50.0% (25-year high). Semi Systems segment at 54.8%. Guided 50.1% for Q3.

Drivers are structural, not cyclical:
- Portfolio enrichment toward differentiated, higher-margin products (GAA, advanced packaging)
- Value-based pricing on mission-critical tools
- Manufacturing efficiencies from doubled capacity in US, Europe, Singapore
- 800 bps of cumulative margin expansion since 2013 under Dickerson

**What it means for the thesis:** Margin expansion is not a one-off. The mix shift toward leading-edge and packaging (which carry higher ASPs and margins) should sustain expansion as these become a larger share of revenue.

### 6. Cash flow warning flag

FCF dropped to $210M from $1.06B YoY. Cash from operations fell to $845M from $1.57B. Capex rose to $635M. The company paid out $765M to shareholders (>3.5x FCF).

Causes: working capital consumption (likely receivables/inventory build ahead of accelerating shipments) and the BIS penalty cash payment.

**What it means for the thesis:** Likely a one-quarter anomaly driven by working capital timing and the one-off BIS payment. But worth monitoring — if FCF stays depressed in Q3 while revenue accelerates to $9B, that would flag a structural working capital problem. For now, the balance sheet can absorb it.

## Leading indicator reads for AI infrastructure thesis

AMAT is the broadest bellwether for semiconductor equipment spending. Their Q2 signals:

1. **The AI capex cycle is accelerating, not plateauing.** WFE raised from >20% to >30%. This contradicts the "peak capex" narrative that circulated after hyperscaler Q1 results.

2. **HBM and advanced packaging are the fastest-growing equipment segments.** >50% growth in packaging equipment confirms the bottleneck is shifting from logic to memory/interconnect. Names exposed to HBM (SK Hynix, Micron, Samsung) and packaging (ASE, Amkor) should see sustained demand.

3. **The cycle is multi-year, not single-year.** 8-quarter rolling forecasts and "2027 similar to 2026" suggest at least 2 more years of above-trend WFE growth. This is relevant for every name in Layers 4-6 on the watchlist.

4. **ICAPS is not dead, just digesting.** The weakness is in legacy end-markets, not AI. ICAPS should resume mid-to-high-single-digit growth once utilization normalizes. Names with pure ICAPS exposure (e.g., some analog semi companies) face a different cycle than leading-edge.

5. **Agentic AI is a new demand driver management is flagging.** If agentic AI drives incremental CPU/DRAM/NAND demand beyond training and inference, that extends the capex cycle further. Worth monitoring whether other management teams corroborate.
