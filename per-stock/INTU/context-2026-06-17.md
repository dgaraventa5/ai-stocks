## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim)

What I currently believe about INTU before research:
- Intuit is the dominant US small-business + consumer-finance software franchise: QuickBooks (SMB accounting/payroll/payments), TurboTax (DIY tax), Credit Karma (consumer finance/lead-gen), and Mailchimp (SMB marketing). ~$18-19B revenue, ~80% gross margin, mid-40s% non-GAAP operating margin, strong FCF.
- It is one of the most aggressive incumbent adopters of generative AI: "GenOS" is its in-house gen-AI operating system, and "Intuit Assist" is the AI assistant embedded across all four products. The thesis is double-edged — AI agents could deepen the moat (done-for-you tax/bookkeeping) OR commoditize it (LLMs can do simple tax prep; ChatGPT-style assistants disintermediate the TurboTax funnel).
- Historically a serial premium-multiple compounder (25-35x fwd P/E), high single-digit/low-teens organic growth, durable pricing power, big buyback + growing dividend.
- TurboTax faced a recurring policy overhang from IRS Direct File (free government filing); I believe that program's status was uncertain.

**The diff that matters most:** (a) how the 2026 tax season actually went and whether pricing power is intact, (b) how AI is being monetized vs. how much it threatens the core, and (c) where the multiple sits now relative to history.

## 4. WebSearch findings

### Finding 1: The crux — a disappointing 2026 tax season caused a ~20% one-day crash; INTU "lost on price" at the low end
On May 20, 2026 Intuit said it "did not have the overall tax season we expected" and "faced pressure among the most price-sensitive DIY filers" — i.e., it "lost on price." TurboTax online paying units were expected to grow only ~2% against an industry backdrop where total IRS filers were expected to *decline* ~30bps — "the most significant industry-wide contraction since the post-COVID tax season." The stock fell **$76.86 (-20%)**, from $383.93 (5/20) to $307.07 (5/21). It has since fallen further to ~$269 (-67% off the 52-wk high). Sources: [Seeking Alpha — FY26 rev guide + 17% workforce cut](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4595530-intuit-outlines-21_341b-21_374b-fy2026-revenue-as-it-cuts-workforce-17-percent), [TIKR — downgrades, "difficult 2026"](https://www.tikr.com/blog/intuit-stock-drops-6-after-sharp-downgrades-are-we-looking-at-a-difficult-2026).

### Finding 2: Securities-fraud investigation (plaintiff-firm stage) tied to the pricing miss
Bleichmar Fonti & Auld (and others) opened an **investigation into potential securities fraud** over whether Intuit misrepresented TurboTax's low-end **price positioning** ahead of/during the 2026 tax season — having told investors it understood the 2025 lesson ("being at the lowest price compared to alternatives") and that 2026 was "off to a strong start," then disclosing on 5/20 it "faced pressure among the most price-sensitive DIY filers." This is **plaintiff-firm solicitation, not a filed class action or DOJ/SEC enforcement action** as of this writing — material for R4 but not (yet) an existential regulatory event. Sources: [BFA Law case page](https://www.bfalaw.com/cases/intuit-class-action-lawsuit), [GlobeNewswire 2026-06-12](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/12/3310967/0/en/intu-stock-drop-reminder-intuit-pricing-issues-lead-to-20-stock-decline-bfa-law-is-investigating-the-company-for-securities-fraud-to-recover-losses.html).

### Finding 3: $100M+/yr OpenAI partnership (Nov 2025) — AI both deepens AND threatens the moat
Intuit signed a multi-year deal to pay **OpenAI $100M+/year** to embed OpenAI models into **GenOS** across TurboTax, QuickBooks, Credit Karma and Mailchimp — and, critically, to let **ChatGPT users take financial/tax actions via Intuit apps directly inside the ChatGPT experience** ("estimate a tax refund, find the right credit card, generate QuickBooks insights"). Bull read: Intuit becomes the financial-action layer for 100M+ users and inside ChatGPT. Bear read: surfacing tax/finance actions *inside ChatGPT* concedes the top of the funnel to OpenAI and accelerates commoditization of the DIY entry point Intuit just lost on price. Sources: [CNBC 2025-11-18](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/18/intuit-pays-openai-100-million-turbotax-integrates-chatgpt.html), [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/91444240/turbotax-gets-an-ai-upgrade-as-intuit-inks-major-deal-with-openai), [Intuit IR — Intuit & OpenAI join forces](https://investors.intuit.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1284/).

### Finding 4: GenOS + "done-for-you" agentic AI is the offensive strategy
Intuit positions **GenOS** as its proprietary gen-AI OS (GenStudio = model import; GenRuntime = orchestration/memory; agent orchestration module) and is shipping **"done-for-you" agentic AI** to ~100M consumers/businesses: QuickBooks AI agents for accounts receivable/payable, invoice/bill creation, form/receipt/email data extraction; tax-code-update automation feeding TurboTax for TY2025/2026; **Intuit Assist** embedded across all four products. The pitch is that agents move Intuit up the value chain (from tool to outcome) and justify higher ARPU. Sources: [Intuit IR — GenOS for done-for-you agentic AI](https://investors.intuit.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1254/), [Intuit IR — GenOS advances agentic AI at scale](https://investors.intuit.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1272/), [Futurum — Intuit Assist](https://futurumgroup.com/insights/intuit-assist-brings-generative-ai-to-consumers-and-smb-customers/).

### Finding 5: IRS Direct File (free government e-file) effectively ended for 2026 — a policy tailwind
The IRS's free **Direct File** program effectively died for the 2026 season ("over 296,000 users last year, 0 this year") after record tax-prep-industry lobbying (Intuit + H&R Block). This removes a structural free-competitor overhang at the low end — though it sits awkwardly against the same-season finding that Intuit STILL lost the price-sensitive DIY segment (to other paid/free competitors, not Direct File). Sources: [Fortune 2026-04-15](https://fortune.com/2026/04/15/tax-filing-lobbying-intuit-hr-block-irs-direct-file/), [Kiplinger](https://www.kiplinger.com/taxes/a-free-tax-filing-option-just-disappeared), [OpenSecrets](https://www.opensecrets.org/news/2026/04/intuit-and-hr-block-set-lobbying-records-the-year-direct-file-died).

### Finding 6: Sell-side cut to neutral; PTs still far above price
Wells Fargo downgraded INTU Overweight→Equal Weight; multiple cuts followed the tax miss. Yet yfinance **targetMeanPrice $491** sits ~+83% above the $269 price — the Street trimmed ratings/ests but still models large upside vs. a price now discounting franchise risk. Net revision direction over 90 days is clearly **negative** (downgrades + the guidance-quality concern), even though the company technically *raised* the FY26 revenue/EPS guide. Source: [TIKR — downgrades](https://www.tikr.com/blog/intuit-stock-drops-6-after-sharp-downgrades-are-we-looking-at-a-difficult-2026), yfinance targetMeanPrice.

**Key tension (the crux for INTU):** A high-quality, ~37% FCF-margin, net-cash, double-digit-growth franchise trading at a **3-year-low multiple** because two related fears are converging: (1) the 2026 tax season showed the DIY low end is **price-sensitive and contestable**, cracking the perceived pricing-power moat in Consumer; and (2) the AI strategy — while genuinely deep (GenOS + $100M/yr OpenAI) — includes letting **ChatGPT transact inside Intuit**, which is exactly the disintermediation vector the bears fear. The 17% layoff reads as management both harvesting AI efficiency AND bracing for a leaner growth regime. Value/Quality/Growth will score well; the live questions are **D3 Moat** (is TurboTax pricing power structurally impaired?) and **R4** (the securities-fraud investigation). **Do not let the cheap multiple + fat margins halo into a high Moat score** — the moat in Consumer is precisely what's under question, even as the QuickBooks/SMB moat looks intact.

## 6. Implications for ratings (flags only — not pre-deciding)

- **D1 AI Revenue %:** Intuit does not disclose a discrete "AI revenue" line. The entire platform is being re-architected around GenOS/Assist, but very little revenue is *directly attributable* to AI as a monetized product yet (TurboTax Live is "assisted," only partly AI). Strict-disclosure read is low. Lean 1-2.
- **D2 Supply Chain Position:** Layer-10 application/platform vendor with a massive SMB + consumer install base and workflow lock-in (esp. QuickBooks). But the 2026 tax miss shows the DIY entry point is contestable. Mid-chain with differentiated-but-pressured position. Lean 3.
- **D3 Moat — THE crux:** QuickBooks/SMB ecosystem (data, integrations, switching costs) = strong; TurboTax Consumer pricing power just visibly cracked at the low end; AI strategy cedes top-of-funnel to ChatGPT. Halo-check hard against the cheap multiple. Lean 3-4 (down from a reflexive 5).
- **D4 Capacity Expansion:** N/A as hardware; read as AI/product investment — heavy GenOS + $100M/yr OpenAI + agentic-AI rollout, but simultaneously CUTTING 17% of headcount (efficiency, not expansion). Mixed. Lean 3.
- **D5 Hyperscaler/AI-infra commitment:** Intuit is a *consumer* of AI compute (pays OpenAI), not a hyperscaler/infra supplier. Supplier framework: no hyperscaler revenue. Lean 1.
- **M1 EPS revisions:** Net negative — Wells Fargo and others downgraded/cut after the tax miss; "difficult 2026" framing; despite the technical FY26 guide raise the revision *tone* is down. Lean 2.
- **M2 Relative strength vs IGV:** −67% off high, near 52-wk low, 50DMA% = 4.2% — severe underperformance vs a roughly flat software sector. Lean 1.
- **M3 Insider:** Exactly one open-market discretionary buy (Prabhu ~$541K post-crash); routine sells otherwise. Per procedure → 4.
- **R1 Customer concentration:** Tens of millions of SMB + consumer customers; no customer remotely material. Highly diversified. Lean 5.
- **R2 Geographic/export:** ~95%+ US revenue, US-based assets; the single worst plausible shock (US tax-policy / IRS competition) is domestic, not geographic/export. No China/policy/asset leg. Lean 5. (Worst plausible shock: US DIY-tax disruption — but that's a moat/demand risk, not an R2 geographic shock.)
- **R3 Balance sheet:** Net-cash-neutral (ND/EBITDA 0.02x), ~37% FCF margin, ~$7.7B TTM FCF, trivial capex. New June 2026 senior-notes issuance is modest. Lean 5.
- **R4 Regulatory/litigation:** Active securities-fraud *investigation* (plaintiff-firm stage) over pricing disclosures + history of FTC/state AG settlements on TurboTax "free" advertising. Material overhang but not (yet) a filed class action or enforcement action. Lean 2-3.
