## 1. Pre-research mental model

Ciena is the optical-networking leader (Layer 07) — coherent optical transport (WaveLogic), routing/switching, and pluggables — historically a lumpy, carrier-capex-cyclical equipment vendor. The AI angle is data-center interconnect (DCI): hyperscalers connecting AI training clusters across and within data centers, which should shift Ciena's mix from telco carriers (slow, cyclical) toward direct cloud/hyperscaler demand (faster-growing, but concentrated). Pre-research priors: carrier softness has been the multi-year drag; the bull case is that cloud/DCI is now large enough to drive double-digit growth and margin mix-up; the key risks are carrier-spend cyclicality, lumpy ordering, and customer concentration in a handful of hyperscalers.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not pre-decide)

Do not change any rating from this file. Surfacing where the new data likely matters:

- **D1 (AI demand directness / driver):** Strong positive signal — cloud ~46% of rev (+70% YoY), DCI/scale-across explicitly tied to AI cluster interconnect, demand>supply. Revisit; this is now a direct AI-infrastructure beneficiary, not a peripheral one.
- **D5 (durability of AI exposure / runway):** Positive — WL6 ramp (110 customers), 1.6T ZR, multi-year backlog visibility. Revisit upward potential, but weigh against concentration.
- **D2 (moat/competitive position):** Claimed #1 DCI + coherent-optics IP lead (WaveLogic) supports moat; verify vs Nokia/Infinera-Nokia, Cisco, Marvell/Coherent pluggables before crediting.
- **Momentum (M / 50DMA %):** Big beat + raised guide is a momentum tailwind; refresh `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` for CIEN before scoring (rule 11).
- **Risk R1 (customer concentration):** Negative flag — **two customers = 34.0% of revenue**; this is genuine concentration risk and should weigh on R1.
- **Risk R2 (geography) / R4 (regulatory):** Check China/export exposure in 10-Q before crediting; not surfaced as material here.
- **Risk — cyclicality:** Legacy carrier-capex cyclicality is masked by current backlog/supply constraints; flag that the demand>supply condition is itself cyclical and could reverse (THESIS-BREAK watch).
- **R5 (disruption risk):** Defaults to **5** (non-Layer-10 physical infrastructure / optical hardware; no agentic-AI disruption to the revenue model). No change implied.
- **Rule 14 (expectations red-flag):** After a +40% quarter and raised guide, run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py CIEN` before scoring — check whether P/S is near its 3-yr high while growth is set to decelerate into FY27 (FY26 guide +32% is below the +40% Q2 run-rate). Qualitative flag only, not a scored metric.
- **Rule 15 (EPS YoY):** Q2/1H net-income surge looks operationally driven (restructuring only $0.8M), but confirm the adjusted-vs-GAAP bridge and any tax one-offs before using EPS YoY — do not blank mechanically, but verify it is not one-time-dominated.

**THESIS-BREAK watch (none triggered this pass):** monitor for (1) DCI/cloud order deceleration or backlog drawdown without replacement, (2) loss/cut from either >10% customer (concentration), (3) guidance cut, (4) gross-margin compression below ~42%. None present as of 2026-06-04; all current signals are positive/accelerating.
