## 1. Pre-research mental model

Digital Realty is a global data-center REIT (PlatformDIGITAL) operating the largest interconnection-dense colocation + hyperscale footprint, leasing to both hyperscalers (big-block build-to-suit) and enterprise/interconnection customers. The thesis: a core AI-datacenter landlord riding record AI-driven leasing/bookings, growing Core FFO/share, funding a large development pipeline via JVs and equity (with attendant dilution risk), and trading at a rich multiple on AI demand. Key watch-items: bookings/backlog momentum, hyperscaler concentration, churn, re-leasing spreads, same-capital cash NOI, and leverage (net debt/EBITDA).

Not a distressed raise; routine ATM shelf.

**Risk (R1–R5):**

- **R3 (balance sheet):** Improved — 4.7x net debt/EBITDA multiyear low. But pair with the ATM/forward equity program (ongoing equity funding = dilution watch, not a balance-sheet-weakness flag).
- **R5 (disruption risk):** Defaults to **5** — physical data-center infrastructure / power-and-real-estate landlord, no credible AI/structural disruption to the core revenue model (CLAUDE.md rule 16; non-Layer-10). No change expected.

**CRITICAL DATA-QUALITY CAVEAT (REIT economics):**
- DLR is a REIT. **GAAP EPS-based Quality and Growth inputs and EV/EBITDA-based Value inputs are unreliable for DLR** — REIT GAAP net income is depressed by large non-cash real-estate depreciation, and GAAP EBITDA/EPS do not reflect the cash earning power that FFO/AFFO/Core FFO capture. The correct lens is **Core FFO/share growth, AFFO, same-capital cash NOI, P/FFO (not P/E), and net debt/EBITDA (REIT-appropriate, ~4.7x)**. This is the same class of input-validity problem already handled by convention for other cohorts: SaaS EV/FCF (rule 10), Layer-9 capacity EV/MW (rule 13). DLR (and DC-REIT peers generally) warrant the analogous treatment — flag any EPS-driven Quality/Growth or EV/EBITDA Value cells as garbage-input and substitute the FFO-based equivalents, or blank per the negative/garbage-input blanking convention. **Surface to Dom before scoring; do not silently feed EPS/EBITDA into DLR's row.**
- **Rule-14 (expectations) angle:** DLR trades at a rich AI-infrastructure multiple after a beat-and-raise; the AI-landlord trade is crowded. Run `scripts/expectations_flag.py DLR` at scoring time (note: may self-skip on REIT XBRL revenue-tag mapping) and read the result as a qualitative "what's priced in" caution, not a scored metric.
