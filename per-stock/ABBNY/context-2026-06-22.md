## 1. Pre-research mental model

ABB is a Swiss diversified electrification, motion, and process-automation industrial that reports in USD. Its Electrification segment is a direct beneficiary of data-center power buildout (switchgear, UPS, medium-/low-voltage gear). I went in expecting solid but cyclical industrial growth, a known robotics spinoff in progress (announced 2025), and data-center electrification as the AI-thesis hook. Open questions: how big is the data-center order tailwind, what is the robotics divestiture status, and is 2026 guidance holding or being raised.

## 6. Implications for ratings (flags only — no rating numbers decided here)

- **D1 / D2 (AI demand directness & magnitude):** Strengthened. Data-center electrification is now an explicit, quantified driver (Electrification orders +44%, triple-digit DC order growth, VoltaGrid hyperscale-AI supply). Revisit whether the AI-thesis directness rating reflects this is a *named primary driver* rather than diffuse exposure — but apply the Layer-2 per-name research discipline (MEMORY: feedback_per_name_research), not sector pattern-match.
- **D5 (moat/structural):** Capacity capex ($200M EU MV manufacturing, $110M US) and the VoltaGrid synchronous-condenser/eHouse supply suggest a defensible position in grid-stability + medium-voltage gear for AI power; worth a fresh look.
- **Momentum (M / 50DMA %):** Record orders + raised guidance + reported ~5% stock pop on the print are momentum-positive; refresh the objective 50DMA% (`scripts/momentum_50dma.py ABBNY`) and re-examine EPS-revisions / relative-strength subjective inputs in light of the guidance raise.
- **Growth:** Comparable revenue +11% Q1 and FY guide raised to HSD–LDD; EPS +21% (caveat: Q1 EPS includes a real-estate gain — per CLAUDE.md rule 15, if a future EPS-YoY input is dominated by this non-operating gain, blank it and let Growth average the revenue metrics; cite the item). Flag for whoever scores Growth/Value next.
- **R1–R4 (risk):** Robotics divestiture changes segment mix (loses ~7% of revenue, a lower-margin 12.1% EBITA unit) — slightly margin-accretive to remaining group; not a balance-sheet concern (cash inflow ~$5.4B). **Robotics deal regulatory risk** (EU/China/US approvals, close mid-to-late 2026) is a near-term execution/regulatory watch item under R4 — not yet a thesis-break, but a deal-break or forced remedies would be material.
- **R5 (disruption risk):** Layer 02 physical infrastructure (grid/electrical equipment) — **defaults to 5** per CLAUDE.md rule 16 (no credible AI/structural disruption to the core revenue model; AI is a tailwind, not a threat). Nothing here changes that default.
- **Structural-change note:** The robotics SoftBank sale is a genuine portfolio/segment-mix change and should be logged in news-log.md and reflected in any segment-based analysis (post-close, the group is electrification + motion + automation, AI/power-weighted).
