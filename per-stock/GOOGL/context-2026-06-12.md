## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim, written before any data pull)

> "Alphabet's search/ads franchise has so far defended itself against AI disruption better than feared — AI Overviews and AI Mode have rolled out across Search without visible monetization damage, and ad revenue kept growing low-double-digits through 2025. Gemini (2.5/3.0 era as of my training) closed most of the gap with OpenAI on capability and has a massive distribution advantage via Search, Android, and Workspace, but ChatGPT still leads consumer chatbot mindshare. The TPU program (v7/Ironwood, co-designed with Broadcom) is Alphabet's structural cost edge and an emerging external revenue line — the Anthropic ~1GW TPU deal was the first marquee external win — while Google Cloud grows ~30%+ with an enormous backlog. Capex was guided to roughly $175-185B for 2026 (up from ~$91-93B in 2025), funded almost entirely from operating cash flow with minimal external financing; the most recent quarter I have confidence on is Q4 2025 (reported early Feb 2026), with Q1 2026 results around late April 2026 that I have limited detail on."

## 4b. FCF conversion check (rule from refresh-context Step 2b)

yfinance (pulled 2026-06-12): TTM FCF (Q2'25–Q1'26) = 5.301+24.461+24.551+10.116 = **$64.4B** (matches CFO's TTM figure on the call) vs TTM net income **$160.2B** → conversion **~40%**. FY2025: $73.3B/$132.2B = **55%**; FY2024: $72.8B/$100.1B = **73%**; FY2023: $69.5B/$73.8B = **94%**.

**Verdict: clean — no red flag.** The <60% readings span only FY2025+TTM (not 2+ full years below at the time the slide began), and both legs of the conditional fail anyway: (a) the denominator is inflated by non-cash equity-securities gains ($36.9B in Q1'26 alone, 10-Q MD&A), and (b) the FCF compression is **capex supercycle, not working-capital deterioration** — capex went $17.2B → $35.7B YoY in Q1 (10-Q cash flow stmt) while receivables grew roughly in line with revenue (AR +20.2% in FY2025 vs revenue +15.1%, yfinance balance sheet — slightly faster, consistent with Cloud enterprise mix shift, no inventory build). This is investment, not accounting. Worth stating plainly: declining FCF here is a deliberate capital-deployment choice, and the new $84.75B equity package exists precisely because capex now exceeds what OCF comfortably funds alongside dividends.

## 5. DIFF since training (what changed vs the mental model)

1. **Cloud is growing 2x my model:** I carried "~30%+"; actual is **+63% YoY** at a $80B run-rate with **32.9% segment margin** and a $462.3B backlog (10-Q). Magnitude, not direction, was wrong.
2. **The funding model flipped — this is the big one.** My model said "funded almost entirely from operating cash flow with minimal external financing." Reality: **$84.75B equity raise (first since IPO era), incl. $10B from Berkshire Hathaway**, 6.25% mandatory convertible preferred, a $40B ATM, $31.4B of Q1 debt, yen notes — and **buybacks fully halted** (zero in Q1 vs $15.1B yr-ago). Alphabet has crossed from capital-returner to capital-raiser.
3. **Capex higher than modeled:** $180–190B 2026 guide (raised), 2027 "significantly increase" — vs my $175–185B static view. Q1 actual $35.7B annualizes below guide, implying steep H2 ramp.
4. **TPU externalization went from one cloud deal to three channels:** (a) direct **hardware sales** agreements in backlog with revenue starting H2 2026 (10-Q MD&A — this did not exist in my model), (b) **Blackstone $5B JV** selling TPU compute outside Google Cloud, (c) Broadcom LTA through 2031 with AVGO acknowledging Google will have "diversity of sources" for TPUs (AVGO 8-K 2026-04-06, per scan notes). TPU is becoming a merchant silicon business, not just an internal cost edge.
5. **Gemini consumer share materially better than modeled:** ~27% chatbot web-visit share vs ChatGPT ~55% and closing (third-party estimates); 650M MAU; Gemini 3.5 shipped at I/O (per prior scan context).
6. **AI search monetization is now evidenced, not hoped:** ads in ~25% of AI Overviews monetizing at parity with classic search (Google VP statement via MonetizeMore); Search & other +19% YoY. The "AI kills search ads" bear case has no support in current prints.
7. **Earnings quality wrinkle (new):** Q1 net income nearly doubled YoY largely on **$36.9B unrealized non-marketable equity gains** (almost certainly incl. the Anthropic stake mark; 10-Q does not name it — flag, don't assume). NI-based metrics (P/E, PEG, FCF-conversion denominators) are distorted this year.
8. **Wiz closed** 2026-03-11, ~$33.6B cash out (10-Q) — was still pending-regulatory in my model.
9. **Dilution era begins, modestly:** ~58.6M new common shares issued June 2026 (~0.5% of shares out), preferred converts to ~43–54M more in 2029 (capped calls offset), $40B ATM pending, +200M Class C plan reserve approved 2026-06-05 (8-K 2026-06-11). Individually small; collectively a regime change from net shrinkage to net issuance.
10. **Structure simplification:** Verily deconsolidated Q1; GFiber deconsolidation expected ~Q4 2026 (call).

- **Momentum (subjective + 50DMA):** Likely upward — Q1 beat, stock +6% on print (CoinDCX/heygotrade), EPS revisions presumably positive. Refresh 50DMA % via scripts/momentum_50dma.py alongside any rescore.
- **Risk (subjective):** Mixed, needs genuine debate. New: preferred dividend obligation (~$1.2B/yr), buyback halt, dilution program, 2027 capex acceleration ahead of revenue recognition, depreciation/energy cost pressure flagged by CFO, and unresolved search-remedies + ad-tech appeals. Offset: Berkshire's $10B is a notable external validation, and the raise de-risks the balance sheet vs debt-only funding. Direction not obvious — this is the dimension where Claude/Dom divergence is most likely productive.
- **Quality (objective):** Margins up (op margin 36.1%), but ROIC/FCF-margin inputs will be whipsawed by capex and equity gains; apply rule-9 TTM-vs-MRQ flag when refreshing.

**Thesis-break check: none found on demand or monetization.** The structural watch-item is the funding flip (point 2): if Cloud backlog conversion or TPU external revenue disappoints while the equity/preferred stack grows, the bear case shifts from "AI kills search" to "capex outruns returns." Track: backlog conversion rate, TPU hardware revenue recognition starting H2 2026, ATM usage, any buyback resumption.
