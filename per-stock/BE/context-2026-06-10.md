## Pre-research mental model

As of the last rating (2026-05-17, basis Q4 2025): Bloom is a solid oxide fuel cell (SOFC) maker that pivoted hard into onsite AI datacenter power, with an Oracle partnership (announced Oct 2025), an AEP utility channel, and the May 2026 Nebius deal ($2.6B / 328 MW, news-log 2026-05-20). News log also captured Q1 2026 revenue $751M (+130% YoY), FY2026 guidance raised to $3.4-3.8B, and C-suite insider selling May 14 at $288-293. Open questions going in: what share of revenue is actually datacenter-tied, whether pricing/margins are holding as volume ramps, and how concentrated the datacenter book is. Thesis.md was never populated (still template) — this rating effectively builds the BE evidence base from scratch.

## Findings (sources)

1. **Q1 2026 results — growth and profitability inflection.** Revenue $751.1M, +130.4% YoY; product revenue $653.3M, +208.4% YoY; GAAP gross margin 30.0% (+2.8pp YoY); GAAP operating income $72.2M (9.6% margin) vs -$19.1M loss prior year; net profit to common $70.7M; operating cash flow +$73.6M; cash $2.49B (Q1 2026 press release, 2026-04-28, 8-K Ex-99.1, SEC accession 0001628280-26-027913; 10-Q filed 2026-04-29). FY2026 guidance raised to revenue $3.4-3.8B (~80% YoY at midpoint, from ~60%), non-GAAP GM ~34%, non-GAAP operating income $600-750M, non-GAAP EPS $1.85-2.25 (same PR; prior guidance $3.1-3.3B / ~32% / $425-475M / $1.33-1.48 from Q4 2025 PR, 2026-02-05).

2. **Oracle expanded to a 2.8 GW master agreement.** Bloom PR 2026-04-13: master services agreement for Oracle to procure **up to 2.8 GW** of fuel cell systems for US AI datacenters, with **1.2 GW initially contracted** and deployment underway "continuing into next year"; cites prior deployment completed in **55 days** vs 90-day target. No dollar value disclosed. Separately, **Project Jupiter**: "up to 2.45 GW power block" replacing previously planned gas turbines and backup diesel generators, with Bloom as **sole power provider** for Oracle's grid-independent AI campus in New Mexico (KR Sridhar, Q1 2026 earnings call, 2026-04-28, Motley Fool transcript; Oracle/BorderPlex PR dated 2026-04-27). Warrant economics: Bloom agreed (2025-10-28) to issue Oracle a warrant for 3,531,073 Class A shares at $113.28 strike, issued 2026-04-09, accounted as **consideration payable to a customer's customer — a reduction of revenue** as Oracle-arrangement servers deliver (10-Q Q1 2026, Note 3; 8-K 2026-04-13).

3. **Backlog ~$20B total / ~$6B product (2.5x YoY).** Q4 2025 PR (2026-02-05): "Total current backlog of ~$20 billion; current product backlog of ~$6 billion, up ~2.5x YoY" — remainder (~$14B) is long-term service. FY2025 revenue $2.02B (+37.3%), "driven by significant growth from the AI data center industry." NOT restated in the Q1 2026 PR — April media citing "~$20B" are referencing the February figure. Q1 call gave no updated number; KR Sridhar: "well more than half of our current data center backlog comes from other hyperscalers, neo clouds and colocation providers" (i.e., beyond Oracle).

4. **Nebius master fuel cell capacity agreement, up to $2.6B.** Signed 2026-05-14, announced 2026-05-20 (Businesswire joint PR; Nebius newsroom): three ten-year phases, total contract value up to $2.6B; phase 1 = 328 MW installed (~250 MW guaranteed capacity), first capacity live in 2026; Nebius purchases capacity + electricity, Bloom installs/operates/maintains. This is a capacity-as-a-service structure (recurring fees), not a one-time equipment sale.

5. **Customer concentration is extreme and intermediated.** Q1 2026 10-Q: two customers = ~50% and ~12% of total revenue; the 50% customer is the **related party Brookfield-affiliated financing structure** (Fund JVs / Bolt US JVCo) — related-party revenue was **$373.3M in Q1 2026 vs $2.8M in Q1 2025** (Note 11). The 10-Q explicitly notes "customer" may be "a project-finance affiliate rather than the ultimate end user." Product growth "largely resulting from multiple projects executed through the joint venture with Brookfield, including our major hyperscaler project" (MD&A). US revenue 91% of total vs 56% prior year. Brookfield partnership reported at up to $5B for AI datacenter deployments (Datacenter Dynamics).

6. **AEP utility channel.** AEP has an agreement for up to 1 GW of Bloom SOFCs "for data centers and other large energy users" — largest utility fuel cell initiative in the US (AEP newsroom story). Secondary sources (enkiai) describe a $2.65B, 20-year offtake finalized Jan 2026 — **dollar/tenor not verified against a primary source in this pass.**

7. **Capacity and competitive position.** Manufacturing footprint supports **5 GW of product annually**, expanding "hundreds of megawatts a quarter" (KR Sridhar, Q1 2026 call). Gas turbine alternative: GE Vernova reported sold out through 2028 with <10% of 2029 slots remaining (December statement cited in Seeking Alpha analysis) — vs Bloom's 55-day Oracle deployment. Project Jupiter is a disclosed head-to-head displacement of planned gas turbines + diesel backup. On pricing, KR declined cost comparison: "we do not compare our pricing with engines and turbines. It's apples and oranges" — selling time-to-power and total value, not $/kW parity.

## DIFF vs mental model

- **Oracle is much bigger and more structural than at last rating:** the Oct 2025 partnership is now a 2.8 GW MSA with 1.2 GW contracted, plus sole-provider status on a 2.45 GW grid-independent campus (Project Jupiter). The relationship between the 2.8 GW MSA and the 2.45 GW Jupiter block is **not clarified anywhere** — do not sum them.
- **Datacenter is now the majority of the business, not a growth vector:** related-party (Brookfield JV, incl. the Oracle hyperscaler project) alone = ~50% of Q1 revenue; US mix jumped 56%→91% in four quarters.
- **New: pricing concession mechanism.** The Oracle warrant is booked as a reduction of revenue — Bloom paid equity to land the anchor hyperscaler. Modest vs deal size, but it means headline product GM slightly understates the concession-free margin and signals hyperscalers extract terms.
- **New: revenue intermediation.** Half of revenue flows through a Brookfield project-finance affiliate, which obscures end-user identity in concentration disclosures and adds a financing-structure dependency that didn't exist a year ago (related-party revenue was $2.8M in Q1 2025).
- **Margin texture is mixed, not uniformly improving:** product GM 34% flat YoY at 3x volume (good — cost downs retained), but installation GM flipped to -35%, electricity GM fell 57%→24%, and Q4 2025 GAAP GM was down 7.5pp YoY. Total GM up on mix.
- **Insider selling continued/accelerated:** 57 Form 4s on file Aug 2025–May 2026, incl. C-suite sales 2026-05-14 at $288-293 (stock ~2.5x the Oracle warrant strike of $113.28 set Oct 2025). Per house principle: selling is an ambiguous signal — note, don't over-weight.

## Evidence for D1 (% revenue datacenter/AI-tied today)

- **No official datacenter revenue % is disclosed** — flag. Best supportable floor: ~50% of Q1 2026 revenue ran through the Brookfield-affiliated Fund JVs ($373.3M of $751.1M = 49.7%), which the 10-Q ties to "multiple projects... including our major hyperscaler project" (10-Q Q1 2026, Notes 1 & 11, MD&A).
- Corroboration: product revenue +208.4% YoY attributed to datacenter time-to-power demand; US revenue mix 91% vs 56% YoY (10-Q); FY2025 record revenue "driven by significant growth from the AI data center industry" (Q4 2025 PR, 2026-02-05).
- Second 12% customer is unnamed (could be AEP or a colo — unknown).
- Backlog skew: product backlog ~$6B up 2.5x YoY (2026-02-05) with Oracle 1.2 GW contracted + Nebius phase 1 + AEP 1 GW channel all datacenter-directed; ">half of data center backlog" is non-Oracle (Q1 call).
- **Working estimate: ≥50% of current revenue is datacenter/AI-tied, trending higher; treat 50% as the defensible floor, not a precise figure.**

## Evidence for D2 (pricing power, position vs gas turbines/grid)

- **Lead-time moat (the core advantage):** GE Vernova gas turbines sold out through 2028, <10% of 2029 slots left (Dec statement, via Seeking Alpha); grid interconnection 5-10 years. Bloom: 55-day Oracle deployment (Bloom PR 2026-04-13), 5 GW/yr manufacturing capacity (Q1 call). Project Jupiter directly displaced planned gas turbines + diesel backup (Q1 call; Oracle PR 2026-04-27).
- **Margins holding at volume:** product GM 34% in Q1 2026, flat YoY despite +208% product revenue (10-Q); non-GAAP GM guided UP to ~34% FY2026 from 31.9% Q4 2025 / 29.0% FY2025 (PRs 2026-04-28, 2026-02-05). Cost-downs from automation are being retained, not fully passed through.
- **But concessions exist:** Oracle warrant (3.53M shares, $113.28 strike) booked as revenue reduction (10-Q Note 3) — equity sweetener to the anchor customer. Installation GM -35% in Q1 (project timing/mix per MD&A).
- Management explicitly refuses $/kW comparison vs turbines ("apples and oranges," Q1 call) — pricing is value/time-to-power based, which holds only while turbine slots and grid interconnects stay scarce. Secondary claim of SOFC capex parity with gas turbines + 15-20% lower fuel consumption (Introl blog) — low confidence, unverified.

## FLAGS

1. **D1 is an inference, not a disclosure** — Bloom does not break out datacenter revenue %. The 50% floor rests on related-party (Brookfield JV) revenue being predominantly datacenter projects, which MD&A supports but doesn't quantify.
2. **AEP deal economics ($2.65B / 20 years) come from a secondary aggregator** (enkiai); only "up to 1 GW" is confirmed by AEP itself. Verify via AEP/BE filings before citing dollars.
3. **$20B backlog is a 2026-02-05 figure** (Q4 2025 PR); not updated at Q1 2026 — April-May media treating it as current are recycling it.
4. **Oracle 2.8 GW MSA vs 2.45 GW Project Jupiter overlap is undisclosed** — do not add them; total Oracle commitment is ambiguous between ~2.8 and ~5.25 GW.
5. **Concentration + intermediation risk:** ~50% of revenue through one Brookfield-affiliated counterparty; project-finance structure obscures end users and adds financing dependency (related-party AR was 41% of receivables at 2025-12-31).
6. **Margin soft spots:** installation GM -35%, electricity GM 57%→24% YoY in Q1 2026; Q4 2025 GAAP GM -7.5pp YoY. Product GM strength carries the P&L.
7. **Oracle warrant = revenue reduction** — small but real pricing concession; watch for warrant repeats in future hyperscaler deals (Nebius structure not yet detailed in a filing).
8. **Heavy insider selling** Aug 2025–May 2026 (57 Form 4s in /per-stock/BE/filings/), latest 2026-05-14 at $288-293. Per 2026-05-17 rating principles: selling is ambiguous, weight asymmetrically vs buying.
