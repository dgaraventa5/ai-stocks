# PLTR — Q1 2026 Earnings Delta Summary

**Earnings date:** May 4, 2026
**Prior quarter reviewed:** Q4 2025
**Sources:** Palantir Q1 2026 8-K press release, earnings call transcript (Motley Fool, Insider Monkey, Investing.com)

---

## 1. AIP Commercial Traction — ACCELERATING

The AIP flywheel is compounding faster than prior quarters suggested:

- **US commercial revenue +133% YoY** to $595M (up from ~$255M in Q1 2025). This is the highest growth rate in PLTR's history for this segment.
- **US commercial TCV bookings: $1.2B** in Q1 alone (+45% YoY), with 12-month TCV of $4.7B (+115% YoY). The backlog is building, not depleting.
- **Net dollar retention hit 150%**, up 1,100 bps sequentially. Existing customers are expanding aggressively — this is a top-decile NRR for enterprise software at any scale.
- **Customer count: 615 US commercial** (+42% YoY, +8% QoQ). Still adding logos, not just expanding existing ones.
- Management narrative shifted from "AIP boot camps drive adoption" to "governed systems of action" — agents executing work autonomously under ontology-based authorization. This positions PLTR for the agentic AI wave, not just copilot deployments.

**Delta vs. prior quarter:** Significant acceleration. Q4 2025 US commercial was already strong; Q1 raised the bar materially.

---

## 2. Government Contract Pipeline — BROADENING

Government revenue grew 84% YoY (US gov) to $687M with $2.4B in TCV bookings (+61% YoY).

Key developments:
- **Maven Smart System:** Usage doubled in 4 months, 4x over 12 months. Expanding beyond initial deployment to multiple services and combatant commands.
- **USDA $300M contract:** Agricultural support, farmland security, supply chain resilience. This is a NEW agency vertical for PLTR — not defense/intel.
- **Ship OS (Navy):** Dramatic operational gains (200 hours to 15 seconds for BOM approval). This is the kind of quantified ROI that drives expansion within DoD.
- **Apollo security:** Zero-day discovery capabilities "about to skyrocket." This is a potentially significant new revenue stream.

**Delta vs. prior quarter:** Government pipeline is broadening beyond defense/intel into civilian agencies (USDA). Maven's doubling in 4 months is a leading indicator for future revenue.

---

## 3. Revenue Acceleration — 11TH CONSECUTIVE QUARTER

Revenue growth trajectory: 17% (2023) -> 29% (2024) -> 50%+ (H2 2025) -> 85% (Q1 2026). This is historically unprecedented for a company at $6.5B+ ARR.

- Total revenue $1.633B beat consensus of $1.54B by 5.8%.
- Full-year guidance raised to $7.65-7.66B (71% YoY), a 10-point increase from prior guidance.
- Q2 guide of $1.80B implies ~10% QoQ growth, suggesting management is guiding conservatively relative to the Q1 run rate.
- RPO of $4.5B (+134% YoY) provides strong revenue visibility.

**Delta vs. prior quarter:** The growth acceleration narrative is now firmly established. The question is no longer "will PLTR grow fast?" but "how long can 70-85% growth sustain at this scale?"

---

## 4. Margin Trajectory — EXPANDING

- **Adjusted operating margin: 60%** (up from historical mid-30s just two years ago).
- **Adjusted FCF margin: 57%** ($925M on $1.633B revenue). Q1 2025 FCF was $370M at 42% margin — massive improvement.
- **GAAP net income margin: 53%** ($871M). PLTR is now a highly profitable GAAP company.
- **Rule of 40: 145** (85% growth + 60% margin). This is elite-tier, comparable to NVDA at peak.
- **SBC: $202M** (12.4% of revenue). SBC as a % of revenue continues to decline as revenue scales faster. This was a major bear concern historically; the dilution story is largely resolved at current growth rates.
- Expenses grew 32% YoY — well below revenue growth, demonstrating operating leverage.

**Delta vs. prior quarter:** Margin expansion continues. The combination of 85% revenue growth and 60% adjusted operating margin is almost unheard of in enterprise software.

---

## 5. Customer Count & Deal Size Trends — BOTH EXPANDING

- Total customers: 1,007 (+31% YoY, +6% QoQ). Crossed the 1,000 customer threshold.
- Large deal activity: 206 deals >= $1M, 72 >= $5M, 47 >= $10M. The number of $10M+ deals in a single quarter is remarkable for enterprise software.
- Top-20 customer average TTM revenue: $108M (+55% YoY). Whale accounts are getting bigger.
- Mix of logos: Airbus, Bain, GE Aerospace, Stellantis, Freedom Mortgage — these are large-cap industrials and financial services firms, not just defense/tech.

**Delta vs. prior quarter:** Customer base is both widening (new logos in new verticals) and deepening (existing accounts expanding via NRR of 150%).

---

## 6. Enterprise AI Competitive Dynamics — PLTR CLAIMS DOMINANCE

Management made unusually strong competitive claims:
- Karp: "Almost every single highlighted example of AI that actually is producing results in the U.S. is actually Palantir."
- Positioned as the only platform for "production AI" vs. "AI slop" from competitors.
- 70 salespeople vs. 7,000 at comparable-revenue companies — product-led growth, not sales-led.
- Sanker's "Jevons Paradox for tokens" framework: cheaper inference drives MORE demand for governance/orchestration (PLTR's core).

**My assessment:** The competitive claims are bold but directionally supported by the numbers. 133% US commercial growth at this scale requires pulling budget from somewhere — and the customer wins (GE Aerospace, Airbus, Bain) suggest PLTR is displacing or preempting legacy enterprise software/consulting engagements. The risk is that hyperscalers (MSFT Copilot, GOOGL Vertex, AMZN Bedrock) commoditize the orchestration layer over time. PLTR's defense is the ontology + security classification infrastructure, which is genuinely difficult to replicate.

---

## 7. International Segment — LAGGING

International commercial grew only 26% YoY to $179M (vs. 133% US commercial). International is now ~21% of total revenue and shrinking as a share. Management did not flag this as a concern — US demand is overwhelming capacity.

**Risk flag:** If US growth decelerates, the international segment is not yet positioned to pick up the slack. This is a concentration risk, though arguably a high-quality one (US enterprise + US government are the two best AI markets globally).
