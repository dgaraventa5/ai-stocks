## Prepared Remarks — Key Themes

### Alex Karp (CEO) — Competitive Positioning & Vision

- "Almost every single highlighted example of AI that actually is producing results in the U.S. is actually Palantir." Karp positioned PLTR as the only platform delivering production-grade AI at enterprise scale vs. "AI slop" from competitors.
- Sales efficiency: company operates with ~70 salespeople ("only seven really sell") vs. the 7,000 a comparable-revenue company would typically employ. Revenue per employee ~$1.5M annualized.
- Talent strategy: emphasized recruiting neurodivergent engineers and international talent. Claimed joining Palantir offers "the most important degree in the world."
- Stated the company is building "the platform that establishes a true AI no-slop zone" — governed, ontology-native agent deployment with security markings and cost attribution.

### Ryan Taylor (CRO/CLO) — Commercial Traction

- **GE Aerospace:** 26% engine performance increase via AIP; expanded partnership into military aviation supply chain.
- **Freedom Mortgage / Motor:** End-to-end mortgage process transformation using agentic AI workflows coordinated through the ontology.
- **Thomas Cavanagh Construction:** 97% daily employee adoption of Foundry.
- **Airbus, Bain, Stellantis** added as commercial customers — legacy industrial and consulting buyers.
- Described shift from "copilots to governed systems of action" — enterprise-grade authorization and provenance tracking for AI agents.

### Shyam Sankar (CTO) — Technology & Product

- **Jevons Paradox for tokens:** As inference costs drop, task volume explodes exponentially, requiring stronger governance infrastructure. Palantir benefits from this dynamic.
- New **Agent Engine SDK** and unified cost attribution for enterprise agent deployments.
- **Apollo security platform:** Mythos/SPUD models discovering thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across major operating systems and browsers. Zero-day discovery "about to skyrocket."

### David Glazer (CFO) — Margins & Expenses

- Adjusted gross margin held at 88%.
- Total expenses grew 32% YoY and 7% QoQ to $649M — reflecting AI platform investment and technical hiring.
- Expects continued expense growth through 2026 while maintaining GAAP profitability.
- SBC: $202M in Q1 (12.4% of revenue, down from higher historical ratios as revenue scales faster than comp expense).
- Equity-related employer payroll tax: $28M additional.

## Government Contract Highlights

- **Maven Smart System:** Usage doubled in 4 months, 4x over 12 months. Spreading across services and combatant commands.
- **USDA:** Awarded up to $300M contract for agricultural support, farmland security, and supply chain resilience.
- **Ship OS (Navy):** Manufacturing bill-of-materials approval reduced from 200 hours to 15 seconds. Contract review cycles 57-73% faster. Monthly material planning 94% reduction.
- **Ondas/World View:** Operational backbone for stratospheric launches.

---

## Q&A Highlights

Key topics from analysts:
- **International growth lagging US:** International commercial grew only 26% YoY vs. 133% US commercial. Management did not express concern; US demand is absorbing capacity. International is ~21% of revenue and shrinking as a share.
- **AIP boot camps:** Continue to drive land-and-expand motion in commercial accounts. Boot camp → production deployment cycle shortening.
- **Government pipeline:** Maven expansion, USDA win, and Ship OS scale all point to accelerating government adoption of AIP-style deployments beyond traditional Gotham use cases.
- **Valuation:** Not directly addressed by management. Stock dropped ~5.7% after hours despite the beat, suggesting the market was pricing in even stronger results or had concerns about the international segment.

---

## Stock Reaction

Despite the comprehensive beat (revenue +5.8% vs. consensus, EPS +17.9% vs. consensus), shares fell approximately 5.7% in after-hours trading on May 4. The stock has since recovered. As of late May 2026, PLTR trades around $137 with a forward P/E of ~97x and trailing P/E of ~155x.
