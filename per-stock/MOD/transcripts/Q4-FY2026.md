## Earnings Call (2026-05-27) — Prepared Remarks & Q&A
*(Source: Investing.com, "Earnings call transcript: Modine Manufacturing beats Q4 2026 expectations," https://www.investing.com/news/transcripts/earnings-call-transcript-modine-manufacturing-beats-q4-2026-expectations-93CH-4712690 — processed 2026-06-12)*

### CEO Neil Brinker — prepared remarks
- **Data Centers FY2026 revenue ~$1.1 billion, +73% YoY**; Q4 Data Centers sales exceeded $400 million.
- > "Our chiller production in North America increased fivefold as compared to the prior year" — achieved despite losing ~20 production shifts to severe weather.
- On the LTA: customer commitment of "more than $4 billion of data center cooling products during calendar years 2027 through 2029"; $165M upfront deposit funds capacity expansion. > "This agreement highlights the confidence our customers have in Modine and validates our need for our current investment in capacity expansion."
- Capacity expansion "firmly on schedule" ($100M U.S. program announced in Q2 FY2026): first chillers shipped from **Jefferson City, MO**, and first air handling units from **Franklin, WI**, during Q4.
- New product: "groundbreaking 3-megawatt chiller, which delivers a 50% increase in cooling capacity with only a 9% increase in footprint."

### CFO Mick Lucareli — financial highlights
- Q4: Climate Solutions revenue +87% YoY; Data Centers +$246M (+158%); company adjusted EBITDA +40%; Q4 free cash flow +$152.8M (includes the $165M LTA deposit received in March 2026).
- Weather cost "50 to 100 basis points on the climate business from a gross margin standpoint" in Q4.
- Component shortages emerged late in Q4; management will manage through a "shortage of certain components" during Q1 FY2027 but anticipates "no impact on our full-year outlook."
- FY2027 guidance: sales +20–35%; **Data Centers growth +60–80%** (raised from the prior 50–70% framing); adjusted EBITDA $650–680M (+38–44%); **free cash flow 4–6% of sales**.

### Q&A highlights
- **LTA customer/scope** (KeyBanc): Brinker — the customer is "an existing customer"; "the agreement is specific for our chillers."
- **LTA cadence** (same exchange): Lucareli — the $4B spreads over three years with "no more than $2 billion in any one year."
- **LTA margins** (D.A. Davidson, Matt Summerville): Lucareli — the LTA "would definitely be accretive to where we are today. Say it another way, it's absolutely within the target margins of where we want the data center business to be."
- **Is the LTA incremental to guidance?** (Oppenheimer, Noah Kaye): Lucareli — FY2027 Data Centers growth of "60%–80%," then "a 50%–70% growth rate" for the following year on top of FY2027.
- **Capacity allocation** (William Blair, Brian Drab): Brinker — a "high degree" of existing capacity will support the LTA, but "I can't give you a specific number because these are all ramping at different rates."

## The $4 Billion LTA — Full Detail

**Agreement type:** Long-term capacity agreement, entered March 2026 (10-K FY2026, contract balances note); 10-K risk factor caveat: "there can be no assurance that this customer will purchase the amounts projected"
**Customer:** Unnamed "major hyperscale customer" / "one of its strategic data center customers" under a confidentiality agreement; confirmed on the call to be an **existing** customer. Likely the "one global technology customer" at ~11% of FY2026 consolidated sales — first >10% customer in at least 3 years (10-K, Customer Dependence)
**Products:** Per the call, "the agreement is specific for our chillers" (Brinker) — narrower than the LTA press release's full Airedale suite framing
**Term:** Calendar years 2027–2029 (3 years)
**Total value:** >$4 billion
**Cadence:** "No more than $2 billion in any one year" (Lucareli, Q4 call) — not necessarily ratable at ~$1.3B/yr
**Upfront payment:** $165 million customer deposit received March 2026, booked as contract liability ($170.6M noncurrent portion at 3/31/26; 10-K) — funds capacity expansion, not revenue
**Pricing structure:** Not disclosed; capacity-reservation based. Margins "accretive to where we are today... within the target margins of where we want the data center business to be" (Lucareli, Q4 call)

### Strategic context
- Pre-LTA, MOD's entire Data Centers segment was on a run rate toward ~$2B by FY2028 (management target stated in Q3 FY2026 call)
- LTA alone represents ~$1.3B/year vs. the prior total segment target, suggesting either: (a) the LTA accelerates timing, (b) there is additional revenue from other customers on top, or (c) the $2B FY2028 target was conservative
- The procurement model shift is significant: hyperscalers are reserving manufacturing capacity years in advance, analogous to semiconductor capacity allocation — not normal project-by-project bidding
- U.S. chiller/AHU manufacturing footprint supporting the LTA: Franklin WI, Grand Prairie TX, Grenada MS, Jefferson City MO ($100M program); chiller capacity expected to double by end of FY2027 (corrected 2026-06-12 from "Franklin, OH")

### Customer identity analysis
The customer is not identified. No public hints in any filing or press release. Circumstantially, the 2027–2029 timeline aligns with hyperscaler capex plans disclosed by Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta (all have publicly committed multi-year AI infrastructure build programs). Without additional data points, no attribution is supportable.

## Margin Trajectory

### Headwinds (structural vs. temporary)

| Headwind | Type | Q4 FY2026 impact | Outlook |
|---|---|---|---|
| Data center capacity expansion costs | **Temporary** | Significant — 510 bps gross margin hit in CS | Should normalize as facilities ramp and fixed costs spread |
| Tariffs (materials) | **Ongoing** | ~100–150 bps (est.) | Partial recovery via surcharges; uncertain policy trajectory |
| Weather-related labor | **One-time** | Minor | No recurrence expected |
| Acquisition integration | **Temporary** | Dilutive (CDI, LBW, Scott Springfield) | Synergies expected to improve by FY2027 |

### Q3 FY2026 context
Management guided Climate Solutions margins to "200+ bps sequential improvement" in Q4, targeting "20%–21%." Q4 landed at 18.7% — a 130–230 bps shortfall vs. guidance. This is worth tracking. Either expansion costs ran higher than modeled or tariffs were worse than expected. The $165M upfront LTA payment may have partially offset this but flows differently (likely treated as deferred revenue or offset to capex, not gross margin).

## Spin-Off: Performance Technologies + Gentherm

**Structure:** Reverse Morris Trust — MOD spins PT, immediately combines with Gentherm (THRM)
**Valuation of PT:** ~$1 billion (6.8x TTM adj. EBITDA)
**MOD proceeds:** ~$210M cash + ~40% ownership of combined Gentherm/PT
**Gentherm shareholder vote:** Required; expected Q4 2026 close
**Credit agreement:** Amended April 2026 to permit separation; allows SpinCo to incur escrow debt
**Transaction costs:** $20.3M incurred FY2026; $30–40M more expected FY2027

### Post-spin Modine profile
Post-close, Modine retains only Climate Solutions (data centers + HVAC). This:
1. Eliminates PT's margin drag (PT EBITDA margin ~13.8% vs. CS ~18.3%)
2. Removes ~$1.1B in flat/declining revenue
3. Creates a pure-play data center thermal company
4. Will likely command a higher multiple (pure-play premium vs. conglomerate discount)
5. Cash from PT ($210M) reduces net debt from $362.8M

## Competitive Position in DC Thermal

- Airedale technology positioned as "most efficient on the market" per CEO (Q3 FY2026 call): "substantial savings from lower energy costs"
- Industry trend: cooling is now a strategic infrastructure constraint, not commodity procurement — evidenced by multi-year capacity reservations (cf. Schneider Electric's ~$2.3B in US DC agreements including a $1.9B Switch deal)
- MOD's LTA is the largest publicly disclosed single-customer cooling commitment by a non-hyperscaler supplier as of May 2026
- Liquid cooling transition: Airedale product line includes liquid cooling technologies alongside traditional air-based systems — positions MOD for the GPU-rack heat density increase driving liquid cooling adoption
