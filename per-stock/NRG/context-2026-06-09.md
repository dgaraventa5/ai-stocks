# NRG — pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-09)

Prepared for the Layer 1 AI Thesis re-rating session. Lens: how DIRECTLY does NRG own the AI power bottleneck (signed hyperscaler PPAs / behind-the-meter MW at premium prices) vs. indirect/second-derivative exposure.

## Pre-research mental model

NRG is the most retail-weighted of the Layer 1 IPPs — roughly 6 million retail electricity customers (Reliant, Direct Energy, Green Mountain) plus Vivint smart home, with a mostly gas-fired generation fleet and no nuclear, which puts it a tier behind VST/TLN/CEG on the "scarce 24/7 carbon-free capacity" version of the AI thesis. Its AI angle as I understood it: a 2025-announced partnership with GE Vernova and Kiewit to develop ~5.4 GW of new gas generation aimed at data centers (first units ~2029), LOIs with data center developers (Menlo Equities and one other) for potentially ~6.5 GW of co-located development at NRG sites, and the ~$12B acquisition of LS Power's ~13 GW gas portfolio announced May 2025 to roughly double generation capacity. Most recent quarter I was aware of: Q1 2026, reported May 2026, a large adjusted EPS miss (~$1.49 vs ~$2.78 consensus) per the news log. My prior: NRG's datacenter exposure is mostly announced pipeline and LOIs rather than signed, disclosed-MW deals with named hyperscalers — i.e., more indirect than TLN (AWS) or CEG (Microsoft).

- **New / corrected:** (1) LS Power has CLOSED (2026-01-30) — this is done, not pending; East segment is now NRG's largest revenue segment ($6.4B in Q1). ## Evidence for D1 (AI Revenue %)

Current datacenter/AI-tied revenue is approximately 0.01% of total revenue — effectively zero. Math: company-disclosed contracted datacenter capacity ramps from 5 MW in 2026 to 445 MW by 2032 at >$80/MWh and >80% load factor (Q1 2026 earnings slides via Investing.com, May 2026). 5 MW × 8,760 h × 0.8 × $80/MWh ≈ $2.8M annualized, against Q1 2026 revenue alone of $10,256M (10-Q, 2026-05-06) and a FY run-rate north of $30B. Even at the full 445 MW ramp in 2032: 445 × 8,760 × 0.8 × $80 ≈ $250M/yr ≈ <1% of revenue. The overwhelming majority of revenue is mass-market retail electricity/gas (≈6M customers), C&I retail, merchant generation, and Vivint smart home ($578M in Q1). The 10-Q contains no datacenter revenue line at all. By the session's "direct ownership of the AI bottleneck" lens, NRG's AI revenue today is a rounding error; the exposure is forward pipeline, not current P&L.

## Evidence for D2 (Supply chain position / pricing power)

Mixed, leaning price-taker today with credible premium-pricing evidence at small scale. For: the signed datacenter retail agreements carry disclosed target pricing >$80/MWh with retail margins >$25/MWh at >80% load factors (Q1 2026 slides via Investing.com) — far above typical mass retail margins, and the 295 MW Texas deal sits on NRG-owned land (co-location leverage). The GE Vernova/Kiewit lockup of 5.4 GW of turbine slots through ~2032 (Utility Dive, 2026) is genuinely scarce physical capacity in a sold-out turbine market — that is a form of bottleneck ownership, but it monetizes only from 2029. Against: the current ~25 GW fleet (post-LS Power) earns merchant ERCOT/PJM prices — Q1 2026 EPS missed by ~46% on nothing more than mild weather and low volatility (Investing.com, May 2026), which is the signature of a commodity price-taker, not a price-setter. Net: pricing power is demonstrated but on 445 MW of contracts vs ~25 GW of merchant exposure.

## Evidence for D5 (Hyperscaler exposure)

No signed, disclosed deal with any named hyperscaler as of 2026-06-09. The 295 MW Texas agreements' counterparty is undisclosed (DCD explicitly notes the developer was not named, 2025); the 150 MW PJM deals' counterparties are likewise undisclosed (Q1 2026 call). The Menlo Equities/PowLan agreements (potential 6.5 GW) are still letters of intent with data center DEVELOPERS, not hyperscalers (TradingView/Zacks, 2026). The anchor datacenter agreement for the GE Vernova 5.4 GW program is "expected to be announced in 2026" — i.e., unsigned (Utility Dive, 2026). Contrast with peers in this layer: TLN-AWS and CEG-Microsoft have named-counterparty, disclosed-term deals. On this dimension NRG screens clearly below VST/TLN/CEG: real signed MW (445) but anonymous counterparties, plus a large announced-but-unsigned pipeline (5.4 GW JV + 6.5 GW LOIs).

- The 295 MW deal's "expandable to 1 GW" and the 6.5 GW LOI figures are company-announced potentials, not commitments; no independent confirmation of conversion progress found.
