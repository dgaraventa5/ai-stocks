## Pre-research mental model

Vistra is the largest competitive IPP (~41 GW pre-Cogentrix, 6.6 GW nuclear) with the strongest hyperscaler contract book after Constellation: ~3,800 MW of nuclear under 20-year hyperscaler PPAs — Meta ~2,600 MW at the PJM nuclear sites (Perry, Davis-Besse, Beaver Valley uprates) announced early 2026, plus AWS ~1,200 MW at Comanche Peak. Q1 2026 (reported May 7) was a record: $1,494M adj. EBITDA (+20% YoY), revenue $5,640M (+43% YoY), with 2026 guidance reaffirmed at $6.8-7.6B EBITDA and the 5,500 MW Cogentrix gas acquisition pending H2 2026 close, both Meta PPAs and Cogentrix excluded from guidance. Versus peers: CEG has the largest nuclear fleet and the Microsoft Crane Clean Energy Center restart, TLN has the original AWS behind-the-meter deal at Susquehanna, NRG is more retail/gas-tilted with Powering America JVs — VST sits between CEG and TLN on directness, with diversification (retail + gas + nuclear) that dilutes the pure-play AI power story. Management is notably more measured than consensus on load growth (Burke: ERCOT 5-6%/yr through 2030, "the physical world takes much longer to develop").

## Evidence for D1 (AI Revenue %)

Current revenue from signed hyperscaler PPAs is effectively **zero**: Meta deliveries begin late 2026, AWS Comanche Peak Q4 2027 (Vistra IR 2026-01-09; Q1 2026 call). The AI link in TODAY'S P&L is indirect — PJM capacity revenue (≈$1.24B annualized = 10,314 MW × $329.17/MW-day × 365, vs Q1 revenue $5,640M / ~$20B+ run-rate, so roughly 6% of revenue) where data center demand is a major driver of the record cap-clearing price, plus broad ERCOT/PJM demand growth (mgmt: ERCOT 5-6%/yr, 10-15 GW data center through 2030). Forward math once both PPAs fully ramp (2028+): 3,809 MW × 8,760 h × ~92% CF × ~$100/MWh (analyst-estimated price, undisclosed) ≈ $3.1B/yr ≈ ~13-15% of revenue — signed and high-visibility, but not current revenue. D1 scored on current revenue should be low single digits (capacity-revenue attribution only); scored on contracted-and-signed it's ~13-15% pending ramp. Flag: actual PPA prices undisclosed, so the forward number is an estimate.

## Evidence for D2 (Supply chain position / pricing power)

Two concrete scarcity data points. (1) PJM 2026/27 capacity auction cleared at the $329.17/MW-day price cap, +22% YoY, with PJM short of its reliability target — Vistra cleared 10,314 MW at that record price (Utility Dive; Vistra 8-K via TipRanks). Capacity prices hitting administrative caps is direct evidence sellers have pricing power and the bottleneck is real. (2) Analyst-estimated hyperscaler PPA pricing of $85-115/MWh vs merchant prices in the $50s (~2x premium; Goldman via CNBC 2026-02-06, Morgan Stanley/Jefferies comps on CEG-MSFT) — hyperscalers are paying up for 24/7 firm carbon-free power, and Vistra owns 6.6 GW of it (second-largest competitive nuclear fleet after CEG). Offset: 98% hedged 2026 / 89% 2027 (8-K 2026-05-07), so near-term merchant upside is largely sold; the pricing power monetizes through the 20-year PPAs and 2028+ hedge roll, not current spot capture.

## Evidence for D5 (Hyperscaler exposure)

Strongest disclosed contract book in the coverage after CEG, and it is signed, not narrative: Meta — 2,609 MW, 20-year PPAs, named counterparty, unit-level MW disclosed (Perry 1,268 / Davis-Besse 908 / uprates 433), front-of-meter grid-connected structure supporting license renewals, deliveries late 2026 (Vistra IR 2026-01-09). AWS — 1,200 MW, 20-year PPA at Comanche Peak, deliveries Q4 2027 (Q1 2026 call/8-K). Total 3,809 MW ≈ 58% of nuclear fleet contracted to two named hyperscalers for 20 years. Beyond signed: gas colocation discussions with two large data center operators (named opportunity, no MW/terms — narrative stage). Vs peers: comparable directness to TLN-AWS (which is behind-the-meter at Susquehanna) and CEG-MSFT; clearly more direct than NRG. Gap: AWS deal's FOM-vs-BTM structure and both deals' prices undisclosed.
