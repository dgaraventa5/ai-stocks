## What changed this quarter

### 1. Record Q1 EBITDA signals structural step-up, not weather windfall

Adjusted EBITDA of $1,494M was a record calendar Q1 — up 20% YoY and 85% vs. Q1 2024. The East segment ($801M, +55.8% YoY) was the primary driver, boosted by higher PJM capacity revenues and three months of Lotus acquisition contributions. Texas ($586M, +19% YoY) grew despite extremely mild ERCOT weather, demonstrating the integrated gen/retail model's resilience. (Source: 8-K earnings release, May 7, 2026)

### 2. Meta nuclear PPAs crystallize the data center contracting thesis

Long-term (20-year) PPAs with Meta for ~2,600 MW at PJM nuclear sites (Perry 1,268 MW, Davis-Besse 908 MW, plus uprate capacity at Perry/Davis-Besse/Beaver Valley). These support subsequent license renewal for all four PJM nuclear units for an additional 20 years. Combined with the previously announced AWS Comanche Peak deal (~1,200 MW), Vistra now has ~3,800 MW of nuclear capacity under long-term hyperscaler contracts. Management noted opportunity for additional gas-based colocation deals. Neither Meta PPAs nor Cogentrix are included in current guidance. (Source: 8-K, 10-Q filings; earnings call Q&A)

### 3. Cogentrix acquisition adds 5,500 MW of gas dispatchable capacity

Agreed to acquire Cogentrix Energy for ~$2.3B cash + 5M shares at $185/share. Expected close H2 2026. Adds scale in markets where bridge power and dispatchable gas are increasingly sought by data center customers. Excluded from current guidance — upside when folded in. (Source: 8-K, earnings call)

### 4. Investment-grade credit at two agencies

Fitch upgraded to IG in Q1, joining S&P (late 2025). Triggered fallaway provisions releasing liens on senior secured debt. Enhances financial flexibility for the $4B+ growth investment plan. (Source: earnings call prepared remarks)

### 5. Capital return machine accelerating

~$600M returned to shareholders in first 4 months of 2026 ($525M buybacks + $75M dividends). Since Nov 2021: 169M shares retired at avg. $37/share — 30% share count reduction. $1.475B buyback authorization remaining, target completion by YE 2027. (Source: earnings call, Moldovan prepared remarks)

### 6. ERCOT load growth: management more measured than consensus

Burke guided 5-6% annual ERCOT load growth through 2030 (10-15 GW data center) and 2-3% PJM growth. Notably more conservative than some third-party forecasts. "The physical world takes much longer to develop than what people might imagine." This is a credibility signal — not overpromising. (Source: earnings call, Burke Q&A)

### 7. Hedging profile provides visibility but limits upside capture

98% hedged for 2026, 89% for 2027, 65% for 2028 (as of May 1). The Nuclear Production Tax Credit provides downside protection. High hedge ratio means near-term earnings are largely locked in, but spot price upside in ERCOT is mostly foregone for 2026-2027. (Source: 8-K, earnings press release)

## What did NOT change

- **Guidance reaffirmed** at $6.8B-$7.6B EBITDA and $3.925B-$4.725B FCFbG for 2026. No raise despite the beat — likely waiting for Cogentrix close to update.
- **2027 EBITDA midpoint opportunity range maintained** — no incremental detail.
- **Development pipeline** at ~4,500 MW largely unchanged from prior disclosures.
- **Retail business** remains steady but unspectacular ($68M EBITDA) — mild weather offset by good customer retention.
