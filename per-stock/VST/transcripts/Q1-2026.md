# Vistra Corp (VST) — Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript Summary

**Date:** May 7, 2026

**Source:** Compiled from Insider Monkey, Alpha Spread, Investing.com, MarketBeat, StockTitan SEC filing summaries

## Prepared Remarks — Jim Burke, President & CEO

### Opening

"2026 is off to a fast start. Within the first week of the year, we announced the acquisition of the 5,500-megawatt Cogentrix natural gas generation portfolio as well as long-term power purchase agreements with Meta for approximately 2,600 megawatts."

### Financial Performance

Vistra delivered approximately $1.5 billion of adjusted EBITDA, a record result for a calendar first quarter — up ~20% YoY and ~85% vs. Q1 2024.

### Operational Performance

- Natural gas fleet: 97% commercial availability
- Nuclear fleet: 100% availability during volatile winter weather
- Martin Lake Unit 1 returned from extended outage late in Q1 with good performance

### Demand Environment

"Within the geographies we serve, we are seeing a structurally improved demand environment. Load growth remains elevated."

### ERCOT Outlook

"Annual load growth of at least 5% to 6% through 2030 is reasonable. We believe ERCOT is looking at something in the order of probably 30 to 40 gigawatts of growth in total by 2030, with 10 to 15 gigawatts likely large data centers."

PJM load growth projected at 2-3% annually.

"The market had a view that load was going to compound a lot faster than this. We did not. The physical world takes much longer to develop than what people might imagine."

### Strategic Positioning

"With our large, diversified and flexible fleet, our development capabilities, innovative retail franchise and experienced commercial team, we believe Vistra is uniquely positioned to deliver on these initiatives."

## Q&A Highlights

### PJM Colocation & Regulatory

**On FERC colocation order:** "We're encouraged by the colocation recognition. FERC was providing direction to support it...We do think there's opportunity to do additional deals like the one we did with Meta — doesn't need to just be with nuclear. I think we have opportunities to do it with gas as well."

**On customer engagement:** "Customer engagement remains strong...They want bilateral contracts and are willing to be flexible on contract structure, but they need clarity on issues like backstop procurement, Connect and Manage and what flexibility products will look like."

**On Connect and Manage:** "Different products will probably have different attributes. Some products might actually be firm from a capacity standpoint. Some may not be. That could be a cheaper product."

### Data Center & Hyperscaler Demand

**On bridge power solutions:** "Bridge has become part of the workaround for these customers...More of our conversations have been leaning towards the use of gas in these bridge power solutions."

**On hyperscaler flexibility:** "Hyperscalers are learning the opportunities to be flexible. You've seen some hyperscalers really lean into that with a lot of public announcements."

**On technology agnosticism:** "We're not committed to one technology or another...We're able to help them identify what's available and what would serve their needs, and that can be a variety of different types of OEMs and technologies."

### ERCOT Market Dynamics

**On mild weather impact:** "One of the benefits of our business is a highly diversified business, both generation and retail. So we saw some offsets...that's why we had a good quarter."

**On batch process (ERCOT interconnection):** "Batch 0 could almost end up becoming on top of every estimate that we've provided...If we think the number is 10 to 15 of additional data center between 2025 and 2030, you don't even really need a whole lot of batch 0."

### Development & Growth Pipeline

- ~4,500 MW of organic development opportunities completed or in process (majority online by 2028)
- >200 MW uprate potential at Comanche Peak
- ~300 MW additional capacity at PJM gas sites
- ~3.2 GW of nuclear capacity at Beaver Valley and Comanche Peak available for long-term contracting
