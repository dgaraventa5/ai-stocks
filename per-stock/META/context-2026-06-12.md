1. **Capex regime is a full step-change beyond my model.** I had 2025 ~$70-72B + "notably larger" 2026; actual 2026 guidance is **$125-145B** (10-Q Q1 2026) — roughly double 2025 — raised once already (Apr 29), with 2027 explicitly unanchored and $237.67B of non-cancelable commitments (+$24B more in April). 2. **Meta is now a structural debt issuer.** $59B senior notes at 3/31/26 plus a **$25B six-tranche raise on 2026-05-04** at coupons up to 6.45% (2066s), plus $5B cash newly restricted under a purchase agreement. My model had Meta essentially self-funding with early off-balance-sheet experiments (Hyperion SPV); on-balance-sheet leverage is now scaling fast, while FCF conversion has compressed to ~36% TTM (yfinance).
3. **MTIA went from inference-side experiment to multi-GW, multi-generation roadmap.** Extended Broadcom partnership (2026-04-14): >1 GW initial, multi-GW from 2027 (AVGO call: ~3 GW through 2028, initial 1 GW order for 2H 2027), 2nm-first silicon, plus "a significant amount of AMD chips" (transcript). A shipped model ("Spark"/Muse Spark) reversed the Llama 4-era model-quality narrative at least partially; Llama-successor delay/closed-source reports persist (Digitimes, single source).
5. **Q1 2026 fundamentals came in stronger than my model implied:** revenue +33% (I'd have guessed mid-20s), impressions +19% with price +12% concurrently, FoA op income +24%. Optically, EPS $10.44 is inflated by the **$8.03B CAMT tax benefit** (underlying ~$7.31) — a reverse of the Q3 2025 one-time tax *charge* I had in my model; both distort y/y EPS comparisons.
6. **FTC antitrust risk flipped from "awaiting ruling" to "Meta won, FTC appealing":** judgment for Meta 2025-11-18; FTC appeal filed 2026-01-20, D.C. Circuit argument expected late 2026. New risks I did not have: New Mexico AG trial 2026-09-08 (up to $62.85B sought), IRS $15.89B deficiency notice (2017-2019 transfer pricing), FTC/state-AG scrutiny of AI chatbots.
