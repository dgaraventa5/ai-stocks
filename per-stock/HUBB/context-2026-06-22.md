## 1. Pre-research mental model

Hubbell is a ~$30B+ electrical & utility products company in two segments: **Utility Solutions** (grid T&D hardware — transmission/distribution components, substation/connectors, plus Aclara meters & grid-monitoring/communications) and **Electrical Solutions** (electrical connectors, wiring, enclosures; increasingly data-center power distribution). The bull case is a multi-year grid-modernization + electrification + AI-datacenter-power buildout tailwind, with Utility Solutions the larger/higher-margin engine and Electrical Solutions the most direct data-center play. Risk going in: it trades at a premium industrial multiple, has cyclical electrical end-markets (housing/light industrial), and the Aclara/grid-automation (AMI metering) sub-line has historically been a soft spot. Expected to be a "quality at a full price" name where the question is how much of the grid/DC story is already priced.

- **R1 (customer concentration):** No concentration red flag surfaced; utilities + diversified electrical. Low priority.
- **R5 (disruption risk):** Defaults to **5** — physical grid/electrical infrastructure, no AI/agentic disruption to the revenue model (per rule 16, non-Layer-10). No change.
- **Rule-14 expectations flag:** Multiple is full and the DC narrative is increasingly crowded consensus; when scoring, run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py HUBB` (P/S percentile vs revenue-growth-vs-median) as a qualitative counterweight. Value bands are absolute — peak-multiple + accelerating-growth is the opposite of the rule-14 trap pattern, but worth checking the multiple isn't 90th-percentile.

**No thesis-break signals.** Demand accelerating, guidance raised, orders >1, telecom/gas healthy, tariffs neutral. The only weak line (Aclara/AMI −7%) is small, bottoming, and disclosed. The watch-item is **leverage**, not demand.
