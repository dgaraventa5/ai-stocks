## What NXP actually is

A broad-line analog/mixed-signal and embedded-processing semiconductor company. Four reported end markets (Q1 2026, 8-K Ex-99.1 2026-04-28): **Automotive $1,782M (+6% YoY, ~56% of revenue)**, Industrial & IoT $628M (+24% YoY), Mobile $391M (+16% YoY), Communications Infrastructure & Other $380M (+21% YoY). This is an auto + industrial/IoT core. There is **no merchant datacenter/AI-accelerator content** — NXP does not sell into AI training/inference servers.

## AI exposure & directness

- **Genuine AI-supply-chain content: low, and it is EDGE AI, not datacenter.** NXP's AI angle is the Kinara NPU + eIQ software + crossover MCUs/applications processors enabling on-device inference in cars and industrial equipment, plus "physical AI" framing for automotive processing. This is downstream demand *enabled by* AI, not selling into the hyperscale buildout that this project tracks.
- **Zero datacenter/accelerator/HBM/optical content.** No exposure to the NVDA/AVGO/hyperscaler-capex bottleneck. Communications Infrastructure segment is RF/networking for telecom/basestations, not AI servers.
- **% genuinely AI-supply-chain (this project's definition — the hyperscale AI buildout): ~0–5%.** Edge-AI silicon is a real growth vector for NXP but sits outside the Aschenbrenner buildout thesis. Directness is **indirect/tangential**.

## DIFF vs typical Layer 6 mental model

- Unlike NVDA/AVGO/MRVL/MPWR, NXP has **no leverage to AI accelerator boards or datacenter power**. It is an auto/industrial cyclical that happens to add edge-AI features. Do not pattern-match it to the merchant-silicon AI names.
- Revenue has been **declining on a multi-year basis** (auto/industrial downcycle): FY2023 $13.28B → FY2024 $12.61B → FY2025 $12.27B (10-K). **3Y revenue CAGR is NEGATIVE (~-2.4%, 2022→2025).** The +12% Q1 2026 / +14–21% guided Q2 is a cyclical recovery off a 2024–25 trough, not secular AI growth.

## Red flags

1. **EPS YoY one-time-dominated (rule 15)** — $627M MEMS divestiture gain; blank the input. (HIGH confidence, documented.)
2. **TTM EBITDA / EV/EBITDA contamination:** TTM EBITDA ($4,717M, summing yfinance quarterly) includes the Q1 2026 gain-inflated quarter ($1,702M). A clean run-rate EBITDA is closer to ~$4.0–4.2B (yfinance `info.ebitda` = $4,161M). **EV/EBITDA on clean basis ≈ 20.5x vs 18.1x on contaminated TTM.** Flag: use the conservative (higher) clean number when scoring, or note the gain.
3. **Negative 3Y revenue CAGR** — Growth category will score poorly on the long-run metric even as near-term recovers. This is correct (NXP shrank 2023–2025), not an error.
4. **Cyclical, China/auto-exposed, tariff-sensitive.** Auto ~56% of revenue; recovery depends on auto/industrial inventory normalization holding.
5. **FCF yield modest (~3.2%)** for a mature cash generator — reflects the recent share-price run (+22–26% post-Q1) compressing the yield; price is near 50DMA highs.
