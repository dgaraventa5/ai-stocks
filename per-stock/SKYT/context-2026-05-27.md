## 1. Business Overview

SkyWater Technology is the **only solely US-owned and US-operated pure-play semiconductor foundry**. Headquartered in Bloomington, MN. DMEA Category 1A Accredited Trusted Foundry.

**Business model:** Technology as a Service (TaaS) — co-development with customers on specialty processes, not leading-edge logic. Revenue comes from:
- **ATS (Advanced Technology Services):** NRE/development revenue for custom process work — defense ASICs, quantum computing chips, biomedical, photonics, MEMS, rad-hard ICs, superconducting ICs
- **Wafer Services:** Production wafer revenue, primarily from Fab 25 (Texas) — embedded processors/MCUs, memory, mixed signal, RF, power
- **Tools:** Equipment procurement on behalf of customers (pass-through, lower margin)

**Process node:** 90nm and above on 200mm wafers. This is NOT leading-edge; this is specialty/analog/mixed-signal territory where differentiation comes from process customization, trusted access, and US-based manufacturing — not node shrinks.

**Certifications:** DMEA 1A Trusted Foundry, AS9100 (Aerospace), ISO13485 (Medical), IATF16949 (Automotive), ISO9001, ISO14001, REACH Compliant.

**Source:** [SkyWater corporate site](https://www.skywatertechnology.com/who-we-are/); [Aerospace & Defense page](https://www.skywatertechnology.com/aerospace-defense/)

## 4. AI Relevance

### Direct AI Chip Manufacturing
SkyWater does **not** manufacture leading-edge AI training or inference chips. Their 90nm+ / 200mm process is generations behind what NVIDIA, AMD, or Broadcom require (sub-5nm on 300mm at TSMC). SkyWater is not competing with TSMC, Samsung, or Intel Foundry on AI accelerators.

### Advanced Packaging for AI (Indirect/Emerging)
This is where the AI relevance exists, though it is early-stage and small-scale:

- **Silicon interposers:** All-copper TSVs (10um x 100um), multi-layer routing at 1um line/space. These are building blocks for 2.5D chiplet integration used in AI chip packaging — but SkyWater is on 200mm, whereas leading AI chip interposers (CoWoS for NVIDIA H100/B200) are on 300mm at TSMC
- **FOWLP (Fan-Out Wafer Level Packaging):** Partnership with Deca Technologies to build the first high-volume US FOWLP capability at the Florida facility. Targets "high-performance computing and networking, AI, and edge computing" per company language
- **Hybrid bonding:** Licensed ZiBond and DBI technologies from Xperi/Adeia for fine-pitch 3D stacking
- **DOD RESHAPE program:** $120M+ DOD contract (announced 2024-01) to build advanced packaging at Florida facility. Focused on secure heterogeneous integration for defense, not commercial AI volumes

**Assessment:** AI packaging relevance is real but **niche and defense-oriented**. SkyWater is not going to package NVIDIA GPUs. The AI angle is more about enabling US-sovereign advanced packaging for defense AI processors and edge inference chips, plus quantum computing chips (via the IonQ relationship). This is a policy/security play, not a volume AI play.

## 5. CHIPS Act & Government Funding

### CHIPS Act Award (December 2024)
- **Amount:** Up to $16M in proposed direct funding (preliminary memorandum of terms)
- **Status as of May 2026:** Still at PMT stage — NOT a final signed agreement. The Trump administration has been reviewing CHIPS Act awards; smaller awards like SkyWater's face uncertainty
- **Purpose:** Modernize and expand 200mm capacity at Minnesota facility by 30% (~70 new jobs)
- **Combined package:** $16M CHIPS + $19M Minnesota Forward Fund + $320M customer-funded capex = $350M+ total investment through this decade

### DOD Contracts
- **RESHAPE program (Florida):** $120M base / $190M with options over 5 years (announced 2024-01). Funds advanced packaging buildout at Osceola County facility with Deca Technologies
- **$36.5M Dept. of Commerce grant:** To Florida Semiconductor Coalition, portion flows to SkyWater Florida operations

### Assessment
SkyWater's CHIPS Act direct award ($16M) is modest compared to Intel ($8.5B), TSMC ($6.6B), Samsung ($6.4B), or even Microchip ($162M). The real government funding story is the **DOD contracts** for trusted foundry and advanced packaging services. SkyWater's value proposition to the government is sovereignty and security clearance, not scale.

**Source:** [NIST announcement, 2024-12](https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2024/12/biden-harris-administration-announce-preliminary-terms-coherent-skywater); [SkyWater CHIPS PR](https://www.skywatertechnology.com/skywater-announces-proposed-chips-funding-to-enhance-domestic-semiconductor-production-and-capabilities/); [DOD RESHAPE PR, 2024-01](https://ir.skywatertechnology.com/news/news-details/2024/SkyWater-Florida-and-Deca-Technologies-Announce-120M-DOD-Award-to-Expand-Advanced-Packaging-Capabilities-in-Osceola-County-Florida/default.aspx)

## 6. Customer Concentration & Key Programs

### Concentration Risk
Customer concentration was **78% of revenue** from top customers in Q1 2026, up from 53% in Q1 2025 (10-Q). The Fab 25 acquisition added a large single-customer wafer supply agreement that increased concentration.

### Key Customer Segments
- **Defense/Aerospace:** DMEA trusted foundry work — rad-hard ICs, readout ICs (ROICs) for infrared sensors, custom ASICs. Government is the anchor customer base
- **Quantum computing:** IonQ (now acquirer), D-Wave, Eeroq, Silicon Quantum Computing, PsiQuantum, Quamcore. Quantum ATS revenue grew 30%+ in FY2025
- **Infineon (via Fab 25 supply agreement):** Fab 25 came with a long-term wafer supply agreement — Infineon remains a major Wafer Services customer post-acquisition. This is the likely driver of the 78% concentration increase
- **Biomedical, industrial, automotive:** Smaller, diversified commercial customers

## 8. Key Risks

### Merger Risk (Dominant Near-Term)
- FTC Second Request creates timeline uncertainty; antitrust review of a quantum computing company acquiring a semiconductor foundry is unusual
- If deal breaks, SKYT likely drops significantly from current ~$37 level (deal price is $35)
- Deal uncertainty freezes strategic planning and may cause customer/employee attrition

### Execution Risk
- Fab 25 integration still pressuring margins (gross margin compressed from 23% to 20% after acquisition)
- Florida advanced packaging facility had $9.3M in cost overruns (inflation-related) in FY2025
- Legacy SkyWater revenue declined 22% in FY2025 — organic business is shrinking while Fab 25 grows total numbers

### Financial Risk
- Highly leveraged: $220M total debt vs. $22M cash (cash-to-debt 0.09)
- $182M drawn on $350M revolver with only $62M available
- FY2025 FCF was negative $53M; Q1 2026 FCF turned positive at $19M but sustainability is unproven
- Beta above 3.5 — extreme stock price volatility

### Scale & Competitive Position
- 200mm / 90nm+ only — cannot serve leading-edge AI chip demand
- Tiny vs. global foundry peers: TSMC (~$90B revenue), GlobalFoundries (~$7B), UMC (~$7B)
- SkyWater at $600M+ guided revenue is a niche player
- Advanced packaging capabilities are pre-revenue / early-stage vs. TSMC CoWoS, ASE, Amkor

### Government Dependence
- Defense contracts and CHIPS Act funding are subject to political risk and budget cycles
- $16M CHIPS award still at PMT stage — not finalized under current administration
- DOD RESHAPE contract is 5-year with options; execution risk on $120-190M program

### Customer Concentration
- 78% of Q1 revenue from top customers — loss of a major customer (especially Infineon supply agreement from Fab 25) would be material
