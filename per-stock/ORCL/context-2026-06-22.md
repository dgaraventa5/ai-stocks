## 1. Pre-research mental model

ORCL sits in Layer 09 (Cloud / Hyperscaler). The AI driver is OCI (Oracle Cloud Infrastructure), where an exploding RPO/backlog — driven by multi-hundred-billion-dollar AI-training-capacity deals including OpenAI/Stargate — is converting Oracle from a slow-growth legacy database/apps vendor into a hyperscale AI-capacity landlord. The watches are (a) backlog *quality* and whether it converts to profitable revenue, and (b) the cost side: aggressive datacenter capex funded by rising debt, driving deeply negative free cash flow. ORCL is explicitly NOT in the rule-13 EV/MW cohort (it is a hyperscaler, excluded). The single biggest unknown going in is how much of the headline RPO is concentrated in one customer (OpenAI) and whether the capex/debt build is sustainable if AI demand softens.

## 2. Latest filing highlights

Source unless noted: Oracle Q4 & FY2026 press release, Exhibit 99.1 to **8-K filed 2026-06-10** (fiscal Q4 ended **May 31, 2026**; fiscal year-end is May, so FY2026 just closed). Local copy: `per-stock/ORCL/filings/pr.htm` and `8-K_2026-06-10_0001193125-26-265848.htm`.

**RPO / backlog (the AI signal — lead item):**
- RPO ended Q4 FY2026 at **$638 billion, up 363% YoY**, up **$85 billion sequentially** from $553B at end of Q3 (8-K 2026-06-10, ex-99.1, RPO section).
- Management: "Most of the RPO increase in both Q3 and Q4 were large scale AI contracts where the customer prepaid Oracle for the purchase of the GPUs, or the customer bought and supplied the GPUs to Oracle. The prepaid and customer supplied hardware portions of our large AI contracts now total **$75 billion**. This substantially reduces the amount of capital Oracle must raise to build out our AI datacenters." (8-K 2026-06-10, ex-99.1).

**OCI / cloud growth:**
- Q4 Total Cloud Revenue **$9.9B, up 47%** USD; of which Cloud Infrastructure (IaaS) **$5.8B, up 93%** USD; Cloud Apps (SaaS) **$4.1B, up 10%** USD (8-K 2026-06-10, ex-99.1).
- Q4 Total Revenue **$19.2B, up 21%** USD (+20% cc). Services revenue $1.5B (+13%), Hardware $0.9B (+9%).
- FY2026 Total Revenue **$67.4B, up 17%**; FY cloud revenue **$34.0B, up 39%** (per WebSearch corroboration of release).

**Margins / EPS:**
- Q4 GAAP operating income **$6.1B, up 20%**; non-GAAP operating income **$8.6B, up 22%**. Q4 GAAP operating margin **32%**, non-GAAP **45%** (8-K 2026-06-10, ex-99.1 operating-income table).
- Q4 GAAP EPS **$1.45 (+21%)**, non-GAAP EPS **$2.11 (+24%)**. Q4 GAAP net income to common $4.2B (+23%).
- FY2026 GAAP EPS **$5.83 (+34%)**, non-GAAP EPS **$7.63 (+27%)** (8-K 2026-06-10, ex-99.1).

**Capex / cash flow / debt (the risk side):**
- FY2026 capital expenditures **$55.663B** (TTM capex table, ex-99.1) — up from **$21.215B** in FY2025 (~2.6x).
- FY2026 operating cash flow **$32.0B, up 54%** (record); but FY2026 **free cash flow NEGATIVE $23.7 billion** "as Oracle continued to execute on investments to support the growth of its Cloud Infrastructure business" (8-K 2026-06-10, ex-99.1).
- Cash & equivalents **$31.289B** at May 31, 2026 (vs $10.786B prior year) — balance sheet cash up but funded by financing.
- Financing FY2026: **$43B debt raised + $5B equity raised** (ex-99.1, Capital Investment Program section). FY2027 plan: **~$40B combined debt+equity**, including a previously-announced **$20B at-the-market equity issuance**; "Oracle does not expect to issue additional debt in calendar year 2026."
- NOTE (gap): the press release does NOT state a total-debt balance. WebSearch reports total debt ~$134.6B and net debt ~$95.5B (Sherwood/Investing.com, 2026-06) — **secondary source, not yet verified to the 10-K balance sheet; flag for confirmation.**

**Guidance (FY2027):**
- Q1 FY2027: Total revenue +27% to +29%; Total Cloud +57% to +63% cc; non-GAAP EPS $1.72–$1.76.
- Full FY2027: prior revenue guidance of **$90B total revenue confirmed**; non-GAAP EPS raised to **$8.05** (+18% after adjusting for one-time FY2026 events: sale of the Ampere chip business and Bloom Energy warrants) (8-K 2026-06-10, ex-99.1).

**Other (8-Ks since 2026-03-24):**
- 8-K 2026-06-10 also declared common dividend **$0.50/sh** (payable 2026-07-24) and a $1,625/sh Mandatory Convertible Preferred dividend (Item 8.01). The existence of **6.50% Series D Mandatory Convertible Preferred (ORCL-PRD)** confirms equity-side financing is already in the capital structure.
- 8-K 2026-05-12 (event 2026-05-06): board elected Tomislav Mihaljevic (Cleveland Clinic CEO) as director — Item 5.02, governance, not financial.
- 8-K 2026-04-06 filed (d132760dex991) — not parsed in detail; appears to be a prior corporate matter. **No 10-Q or 10-K filed since 2026-03-24** (10-K for FY ended May 31, 2026 expected within ~60 days, i.e. ~late July 2026 — balance-sheet debt/lease detail will land then).

## 6. Implications for ratings (flags only — do NOT pre-decide; collaborative session required per rule 12)

**Gate status (rule 12):** ORCL `thesis.md` is the untouched template. This `context-2026-06-22.md` briefing now satisfies the research-backed gate for a rating session — but ratings must be set collaboratively, not inferred here.

- **D1–D5 (AI Thesis):** The $638B RPO (+363% YoY, +$85B QoQ) and +93% IaaS growth are the strongest possible AI demand-signal — flag as the primary AI D-signal (likely lifts D1 demand-exposure / D2 revenue-visibility dimensions). Caveat for the session: backlog is contracted, not yet delivered revenue; quality hinges on OpenAI's ability to pay over 5 years. Directness is high (OCI is literal AI-training capacity), but ORCL is also ~half legacy DB/apps/SaaS (+10% only) — blended AI mix, not pure-play.
- **Momentum (M / 50DMA %):** ORCL has had a very large multi-year run-up on the AI narrative; the stock dipped on the 2026-06-10 print despite the beat (capex/FCF concerns). Refresh the objective 50DMA % via `python3 scripts/momentum_50dma.py ORCL` (rule 11) before setting Momentum — do not eyeball it.
- **R1 (customer concentration):** Flag HIGH. The OpenAI/Stargate ~$300B deal is the dominant RPO contributor; single-customer dependence on a cash-burning AI lab is the key R1 risk. Counterparty credit quality of OpenAI is the live question.
- **R3 (balance sheet):** Flag as the KEY RISK. FY2026 FCF **negative $23.7B**, capex $55.7B, $43B debt raised, reported total debt ~$134.6B / net debt ~$95.5B (verify to 10-K when filed ~July 2026), ongoing $20B+ equity dilution planned. The model is "borrow/dilute to build capacity against prepaid contracts." The $75B prepaid/customer-supplied GPU offset partially mitigates but does not eliminate funding risk.
- **R5 (Disruption Risk):** Defaults to **5** — ORCL is Layer 09 (Cloud/Hyperscaler), not Layer 10; the core revenue model (cloud compute capacity + database) is not an agentic-AI disruption target. No sub-5 R5 unless the session argues the legacy DB/apps lines (SaaS +10%) face erosion — but per rule 16 scope, non-Layer-10 defaults to 5.

- **Value category (objective):** ORCL is a hyperscaler — NOT in the rule-13 EV/MW cohort, so use standard Value bands (Fwd P/E, EV/EBITDA, FCF Yield, P/S, PEG).
