## Prepared Remarks — Key Excerpts

### Mac McFarland, CEO

Opened by emphasizing the "Talen flywheel" strategy: accretive M&A, data center contracting, spark spread expansion, and potential asset monetization. Described Talen as trading at "double-digit free cash flow yields" with significant upside if contracted portfolio ratios increase from 35% to 50%+.

Stated: "Our direction of travel remains the same," emphasizing commitment to leveraging existing and new generation for data center-driven growth.

### Terry L. Nutt, COO

- Susquehanna Unit 1 refueling outage "progressed well" and the unit was "synced back to the grid yesterday" (i.e., May 4, 2026).
- Fleet generated approximately 16 terawatt-hours at 55% fleet-wide capacity factor.
- Fleet intermediate/peaking assets (Montour, Martins Creek) showed "significantly higher run times" versus prior year Q1 — continuing a trend of higher runtimes from 2023-2026.
- Safety recordable incident rate: 0.37, below industry average.
- Freedom and Guernsey assets (acquired previously) onboarded in April post-closing.

### Cole Muller, CFO

**Q1 2026 Results:**
- Revenue: $1.13 billion
- EPS: $1.33
- Adjusted EBITDA: $473 million (more than doubled YoY)
- Adjusted Free Cash Flow: $350 million (quadrupled YoY)
- Drivers: recent acquisitions (Freedom, Guernsey), higher spark spreads, reduced cash tax payments.

**2026 Guidance (Reaffirmed, excludes Cornerstone):**
- Adjusted EBITDA: $1.75B–$2.05B
- Adjusted Free Cash Flow: $980M–$1.18B

**2027-2028 Preliminary Outlook (includes Cornerstone):**
- 2027 FCF per share: ~$34
- 2028 FCF per share: ~$36 (base case, flat share count)
- 2028 FCF per share with 70% repurchase: ~$41 (30% above January guidance)
- Projected FCF yield: ~11% at 2028 levels
- ~$1 billion additional upside identified across 2027–2028

**Cornerstone Acquisition Financing:**
- $4 billion senior unsecured notes raised at blended rate just above 6.25% (5- and 7-year tranches)
- Refinanced $1.2B senior secured notes at 8.625% coupon
- Annual interest savings: >$40 million (~$1/share impact)
- Secured debt mix decreased from ~60% to ~30% of total debt

**Capital Allocation:**
- $100 million share repurchases executed in Q1
- $1.9 billion remaining authorization through 2028
- Plan assumes 70% of available FCF for buybacks
- Net leverage ratio: 3.1x (target below 3.5x by YE 2026 post-Cornerstone close)

**Credit Facilities (effective upon Cornerstone close):**
- Revolving credit facility upsized to $1.35B
- Standalone letter of credit facility upsized to $1.5B
- LCF maturity extended through December 2029

### Christopher E. Morice, SVP Commercial

**Power Pricing & Spark Spreads:**
- PJM saw ~3% higher incremental deliveries (weather-adjusted) in Q1 vs. prior year
- Forward spark spreads appreciated across 2026–2028
- Spark spreads improved ~$5/MWh since March 31 in PPL zone — translates to "several more dollars per share" at current marks
- PPL zone basis widened vs. PJM West Hub; management attributes to transmission work and "recency bias," expects normalization as load evolves

**AWS PPA & Contracting Progress:**
- AWS PPA ramping, already contributing to results
- 35% of gross margin now contracted long-term
- Each incremental 1 GW PPA adds 15% to contracted margin (target: 50%)
- Several 1+ GW long-term PPA opportunities at existing and other Pennsylvania sites
- Additional opportunities across broader company footprint

**Development Pipeline:**
- Up to 3,000 acres supporting 3–4 GW data center capacity
- Zoning ranges from fully zoned to in-process (e.g., Montour site)
- Each site enables 500 MW–1 GW of new generation (CTs, batteries, CCGTs)
- Over 2 GW (2.3+ GW) submitted into PJM Cycle 1 interconnection cluster study
- Ohio: 4+ GW gas fleet; developing multiple options

**Hybrid Model:**
- Existing generation provides immediate power; new generation (batteries/peakers/CCGTs) addresses peak capacity needs
- Not requiring 1:1 new-to-existing capacity matching
- Focuses on solving "50 hours per year" resource adequacy problem at lowest cost

## Q&A Highlights

Key topics raised by analysts:

1. **PJM Co-location Rules:** Management sees no identified thresholds hindering development progress.

2. **Reliability Backstop Product (RBP) Timing:** PJM shifted procurement timeline to fall 2026. Talen supports pay-as-bid construct. Capacity product could underwrite incentives for up to 15 years (as PJM proposed). CTs identified as most cost-effective near-term solution.

3. **New Generation Economics:** CCGT LCOE "significantly above" merchant economics; bilateral PPAs or RBP awards required to justify new builds. Capital discipline — new generation tied to customer contracts, RVP awards, or project financing.

4. **PPL Zone Basis Widening:** Temporary; management expects reversion as transmission work completes and load growth occurs in the region.

5. **Ohio Strategy:** Active engagement with hyperscalers and other large-load customers; similar development approach as Pennsylvania; 4+ GW gas fleet positioned for opportunity.

6. **Regulatory Clarity:** While helpful, management stated it is not "determinative" for customer contracting decisions — customers are signing deals regardless.
