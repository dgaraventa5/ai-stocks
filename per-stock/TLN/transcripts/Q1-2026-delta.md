## 1. Data Center Power Deal Pipeline Progress

**AWS Susquehanna PPA (17-yr, up to $18B):**
- The front-of-meter transition (restructured after FERC rejected behind-the-meter in Nov 2024) is proceeding on schedule. Susquehanna Unit 1 completed its spring 2026 refueling outage and was synced back to the grid on May 4 — this is the outage during which transmission reconfigurations for the FTM arrangement were being executed.
- AWS PPA already contributing to Q1 results under the ramp schedule.
- Full ramp: 840–1,200 MW by 2029, 1,680–1,920 MW by 2032.
- At full volume (~1,920 MW), PPA expected to generate up to $1.4B/yr in revenue.
- 35% of gross margin now contracted long-term; each additional 1 GW PPA adds ~15 percentage points (target: 50%).

**New data center development:**
- Up to 3,000 acres across the portfolio supporting 3–4 GW of data center capacity.
- Several 1+ GW PPA opportunities at existing PA sites and across broader footprint.
- Ohio: 4+ GW gas fleet; actively engaging hyperscalers.
- 2.3+ GW of new generation projects (CTs, batteries, CCGTs) submitted into PJM Cycle 1 interconnection study.
- No new specific customer names disclosed this quarter.

**Hybrid model strategy:**
- Existing generation for speed-to-market; new build for later years.
- Not requiring 1:1 matching — solving for the "50 hours/year" peak adequacy problem.
- New CCGT economics are "significantly above" merchant — requires bilateral PPAs or RBP awards.

## 2. Nuclear Plant Operations and Capacity Factor

- Susquehanna Unit 1 refueling outage completed ahead of schedule; unit synced to grid May 4.
- Fleet-wide capacity factor: 55% (reflects outage drag from refueling).
- Fleet generated ~16 TWh in Q1.
- Safety: recordable incident rate 0.37 (below industry average).
- No unplanned nuclear outages or issues disclosed.

## 3. Power Pricing Trajectory

- PJM demand: ~3% incremental deliveries YoY (weather-adjusted) — structural load growth visible.
- Forward spark spreads appreciated across 2026–2028 curves.
- PPL zone spark spreads improved ~$5/MWh since March 31 — "several more dollars per share" uplift.
- PPL-to-West Hub basis widened (negative for TLN in near-term); management calls it temporary due to transmission work, expects normalization with load growth.
- Intermediate/peaking assets (Montour, Martins Creek) running more hours — supportive of higher realized prices.

## 4. New Customer Announcements

- No new named customers disclosed.
- Management referenced "several 1+ GW PPA opportunities" and active Ohio engagement but declined to name counterparties.
- Regulatory clarity described as helpful but "not determinative" — customers signing regardless.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Beat + Affirm:** Adj. EBITDA and FCF both came in strong, guidance reaffirmed. The 2027-2028 FCF/share outlook was raised 15-30% vs. January — this is the real signal.
2. **AWS transition on track:** The Susquehanna FTM reconfiguration is happening now (spring 2026 outage). This removes the FERC overhang from the old BTM rejection.
3. **Pipeline is deep but unsigned:** 3–4 GW of data center capacity, 2.3+ GW of new gen in PJM queue — but no new named customers. Contracted margin at 35% needs more deals to hit 50%.
4. **Spark spread tailwind continuing:** Forward curves appreciating, PJM demand growing ~3% YoY. Basis risk in PPL zone is a watch item but management argues temporary.
5. **Cornerstone de-risked:** All major regulatory hurdles cleared or unopposed; summer close expected. Transforms TLN from a nuclear-heavy PA IPP into a diversified multi-state fleet.
