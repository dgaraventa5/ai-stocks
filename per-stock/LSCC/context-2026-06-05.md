# LSCC (Lattice Semiconductor) — Refresh-Context Briefing — 2026-06-05

Layer: 06 AI Compute Silicon / Programmable logic. Low-power FPGAs (Nexus, Avant). AI angle: server platform management / control plane, hardware root-of-trust, attach to AI server builds.

---

## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim)

> LSCC is a small-cap, fabless low-power FPGA leader (Nexus, Avant) that got hammered by the 2024-25 inventory correction across comms/computing and industrial/auto end-markets — revenue likely fell 30%+ from the ~$190M/quarter peak. The AI angle is real but indirect: small-die FPGAs attach to AI server builds for platform management/control-plane and hardware root-of-trust, so it rides AI server unit growth rather than GPU dollar content. Key questions: is the revenue recovery actually underway (book-to-bill, sequential growth), is Avant (mid-range, larger SAM) ramping into AI servers, and has the ~62-69% gross margin held through the trough. New CEO (Ford Tamer, ex-Inphi) is a positive signal for execution and AI focus.

**Biggest diffs surfaced:** (1) recovery is firmly underway and accelerating, not just bottoming; (2) the AI play runs through **Nexus/pre-Nexus**, not Avant (Avant is industrial/embedded) — my mental model had this backwards; (3) a transformative **$1.65B debt-funded acquisition of AMI** (firmware/BMC) was announced May 4 2026, which moves LSCC from a point FPGA vendor to a server "secure management & control platform" and flips the balance sheet from net-cash to net-debt.

## 6. Implications for ratings (flag only — not pre-decided)

- **D1 AI Revenue %:** Company-stated AI = 25% of 2026 rev → squarely a "4" band (25–50%). Up from high-teens, trajectory positive.
- **D2 Position / D3 Moat:** Low-power FPGA duopoly-ish leadership (vs Altera/Microchip in small FPGAs); high GM (~68-70%) signals pricing power; AMI adds firmware/manageability stickiness. Moat strengthening but FPGA content is small-die, replaceable in theory — push between 3 and 4, guard against halo (stock up ~50% YTD).
- **D4 Capacity:** Fabless, no fab capex; "capacity" expression is product roadmap (Nexus 2, Avant 30/50) + AMI M&A. Likely a 3 (steady-state) unless we credit M&A-driven expansion.
- **D5 Hyperscaler Exposure:** Asia +71% "due to hyperscaler demand"; design wins at top cloud providers; servers 38% of rev. Meaningful but indirect/through distributors, no disclosed % — likely 3–4.
- **M1 EPS Revisions:** Clear beat-and-raise, PTs $91→$145, multiple upward revisions → 5.
- **M2 Relative Strength:** Stock +~50% YTD, outperforming SOXX → likely 5 (verify 6-mo vs SOXX at rating time).
- **M3 Insider:** Single CEO open-market buy >$1M ($1.89M) → rubric path to **4** (one insider buying meaningfully). Check for additional buyers; if ≥2, it's a 5.
- **R1 Customer Concentration:** 94% distributor revenue masks end-customer concentration; no >10% customer disclosed in this 10-Q (verify against FY2025 10-K). Likely 4.
- **R2 Geo Risk:** 77.6% Asia ship-to, hyperscaler-driven; low-power FPGAs not on the leading-edge export-control list; no Entity List overhang. Likely 3–4 (China demand-routing risk, not export-control restriction).
- **R3 Balance-Sheet Risk:** Pre-deal net cash (~$100M). Post-AMI: ~$950M new term loan + $200M revolver against pro forma EBITDA. Pro forma ND/EBITDA likely ~2–3x → drops R3 from a 5 (net cash) to ~3. **This is the dimension most affected by fresh research.**
- **R4 Regulatory/Litigation:** Clean except HSR antitrust review of AMI deal (routine for this size). Likely 4.

**Thesis-break signals:** None negative. Watch items: (a) AMI integration / debt service (firmware software is a different operating model than fabless silicon); (b) 94% distributor + 77.6% Asia concentration = sell-through visibility risk if hyperscaler ordering normalizes (echo of the 2024-25 inventory correction); (c) 2H 2026 supply-chain cost pressure on GM; (d) valuation rich (fwd P/E ~58x, P/S ~32x) leaves no margin for a stumble.
