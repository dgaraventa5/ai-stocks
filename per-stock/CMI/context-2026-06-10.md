# CMI — Pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-10)

Prepared for AI Thesis re-rating of Layer 1 power names. Session lens: how DIRECTLY does CMI own the AI power bottleneck (disclosed datacenter revenue/backlog vs narrative).

---

## Pre-research mental model (written before searching)

CMI is primarily a truck/commercial engine company (~$34B revenue) where Power Systems is one of five segments (~$6.5B, roughly 18-19% of total), and datacenter gensets are a sub-slice of Power Systems alongside mining, marine, and industrial. Recollection: datacenter-tied revenue ~$2-2.5B growing 20-30%/yr with record Power Systems margins (~20%+ segment EBITDA), while the NA Class 8 truck core is in a freight-recession downturn with EPA 2027 prebuy uncertainty. Most recent data point on file: 2026-05-21 Analyst Day news-log entry (2030 target raised to $45-50B, FY2026 guide +8-11%). Did NOT recall any disclosed datacenter backlog or named hyperscaler customers.

## Findings (sources)

1. **Q1 2026 results (reported 2026-05-05).** Revenue $8.4B (+3% YoY); GAAP EPS $4.71 (includes $199M / $1.44 per share charges on sale of low-pressure fuel cell business); adjusted EPS $6.15 vs $5.96 prior year; GAAP EBITDA 15.4% of sales, adjusted 17.7%. Power Systems: sales $1,956M (+19%), segment EBITDA $577M = **29.5% of sales, a record, vs 23.6% prior year**. Engine segment margin fell to 10.4% from 16.5%; Components 13.3% from 14.3% (truck downturn). Source: Q1 2026 8-K EX-99, SEC, filed 2026-05-05 (sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/26172/000002617226000013/cmi2026q18-kex99.htm); Investing.com Q1 2026 slides summary.

2. **Product-line detail from the 8-K (the D1 raw material).** Power Systems "Power generation" product line: **$1,283M Q1 2026 vs $1,001M Q1 2025 (+28% YoY)**; FY2025 total $4,731M. Distribution segment "Power generation" product line: **$1,275M Q1 2026 vs $1,090M (+17% YoY)**; FY2025 total $4,932M. Caution: these two lines double-count — Power Systems had $863M intersegment sales in Q1 2026 (much of it gensets resold through Distribution). 8-K quote: Power Systems growth "driven primarily by increased power generation demand, particularly for data center markets in North America, China and Asia Pacific." Source: Q1 2026 8-K EX-99, segment and product-line tables, 2026-05-05.

3. **Analyst Day (2026-05-21): first explicit datacenter revenue disclosure.** Power Systems President Jenny Bush: targeting **$9B in data center revenue by 2030 vs ~$5B expected in 2026**, backed by **$450M of new capacity investment to expand high-horsepower genset output by 20 GW**. Company-level 2030 targets raised to $45-50B revenue (from $43-48B) with EBITDA >20%. Of the 6-9% 2026-2030 revenue CAGR, datacenter contributes 2-3 points. Source: TIKR Analyst Day recap (tikr.com/blog/cummins-analyst-day-raises-2030-revenue-target-to-50-billion), corroborated by second search snippet citing the May 21 event. NOT yet verified against the primary IR deck — see FLAGS.

4. **Guidance raised on datacenter demand (Q1 call, 2026-05-05).** FY2026 revenue guide raised to +8-11% (from +3-8%); EBITDA margin 17.75-18.50% (from 17.0-18.0%). **Global power generation revenue now guided +15-25% (raised from +10-20%)**. NA power generation revenue +23% in Q1 "driven primarily by continued strong data center demand." CEO Rumsey: "demand for data center power generation across a range of our products continues to outpace expectations." CFO Smith attributed Power Systems results to "increased volumes, positive pricing, net tariff recovery." Source: Q1 2026 8-K EX-99; Motley Fool Q1 2026 earnings call transcript (fool.com, 2026-05-05).

5. **Capacity/lead-time and competitive picture.** QSK95 (95-liter) capacity was doubled (investment completed 2025); on the Q1 call, analyst Jerry Revich framed 95L lead times as extending "into the back half of '28" even at the higher production level (management did not contradict; analyst framing, not a management quote). Feb 2026: $150M expansion at Fridley, MN for 3-4MW QSK95 gensets (Mordor Intelligence market report). Cummins' own May 2026 market commentary: industry genset lead times 12-36 months (cummins.com/en-na/news/2026/05/06). Market share: CAT ~38% vs Cummins ~32% of global datacenter generators in 2024 (MarketsandMarkets). Competitive watch item: the marquee Jan 2026 prime-power orders went elsewhere — AIP/Monarch Compute 2GW natural-gas gensets to Caterpillar; 72MW Frankfurt hyperscale gas order to Rolls-Royce mtu — CMI's datacenter franchise is predominantly diesel BACKUP, not gas prime power.

## DIFF (mental model vs evidence)

- **Datacenter revenue is ~2x bigger than I thought.** Model said ~$2-2.5B; company now says ~$5B expected 2026, with a disclosed $9B-by-2030 target. This moved from inference to explicit company disclosure at the May 21 Analyst Day — material upgrade in directness.
- **Power Systems margins far above model.** Model said ~20%+ segment EBITDA; actual Q1 2026 was 29.5% (record), FY2025 22.7% (+430bps YoY). Pricing power is showing up in the P&L, not just the narrative.
- **Datacenter revenue spans TWO segments.** Model treated it as a Power Systems sub-slice; in fact Distribution's power generation line ($4.9B FY2025) is the bigger disclosed power-gen line, and the ~$5B datacenter figure must draw from both (gensets + distribution/service). Power Systems alone ($7.5B FY2025 incl. intersegment) couldn't contain $5B of datacenter at the disclosed product mix.
- **Truck cycle: bottoming, not deteriorating.** Rumsey: "North America truck markets began to improve from a cyclical low" — slightly better than the model's freight-recession framing, but Engine margin (10.4%) confirms the core is still weak, making Power Systems the entire earnings story right now.
- **Unchanged:** no named hyperscaler customers, no dollar backlog disclosure — that part of the model was correct.

## Evidence for D1 (% of total revenue datacenter-tied today)

**The math:**
- FY2025 total revenue: $33.7B (FY2025 10-K, via stocktitan/sahmcapital summaries of the filing).
- FY2026E total revenue: $33.7B x (1.08 to 1.11) = **$36.4-37.4B** (guidance, Q1 2026 8-K).
- Datacenter revenue expected 2026: **~$5B** (Jenny Bush, Analyst Day 2026-05-21, via TIKR).
- **D1 ≈ $5B / ~$36.9B ≈ 13-14% of TOTAL company revenue datacenter-tied today.**
- Cross-check vs disclosed product lines: combined power generation product lines FY2025 = $4,731M (Power Systems) + $4,932M (Distribution) = $9.7B gross, but with intersegment double-counting (PS intersegment was $863M in Q1 2026 alone, ~44% of PS sales). Net external power gen plausibly ~$7-8B, of which $5B datacenter ≈ 60-70% — internally consistent with "data centers are the primary power gen driver" commentary.
- Trajectory: $9B by 2030 on $45-50B total = ~19-20% of revenue if hit.

**Directness verdict for D1:** Datacenter exposure is now DISCLOSED and quantified by management ($5B/2026E, $9B/2030 target) — this clears the session's "direct" bar. But it is ~13-14% of a company whose other ~86% is trucks/components/distribution; growth driver, not the core.

## Evidence for D2 (pricing power in datacenter gensets)

- Record Power Systems EBITDA margin **29.5% Q1 2026 vs 23.6% Q1 2025** (+590bps); FY2025 22.7% (+430bps YoY). Source: Q1 2026 8-K; FY2025 results.
- CFO explicitly cited **"positive pricing, net tariff recovery"** as Power Systems drivers (Q1 2026 call, Motley Fool transcript).
- Lead times: 95L lead times characterized as extending into 2H 2028 on the Q1 call (analyst framing); Cummins' own market commentary cites industry lead times of 12-36 months (cummins.com, 2026-05-06). Capacity sold forward = pricing leverage.
- Supply response underway: QSK95 capacity doubled (completed 2025), $150M Fridley MN expansion (Feb 2026), $450M for +20GW high-horsepower output (Analyst Day).
- Competitive limits: duopoly-ish in high-horsepower diesel (CAT 38% / CMI 32% share, 2024, MarketsandMarkets) with Rolls-Royce mtu premium niche — but the gas prime-power orders (CAT/AIP 2GW Monarch; RR mtu Frankfurt 72MW) show the highest-growth adjacent market is being won by competitors. Mordor: competition centers on fuel flexibility, lead-time compression, and TCO "rather than upfront price."

**D2 verdict:** Genuine, evidenced pricing power TODAY (margins + explicit pricing commentary + multi-year lead times), in an oligopoly. Watch the diesel-backup vs gas-prime distinction — CMI's pricing power is in backup diesel; prime power gas is contested.

## Evidence for D5 (named customers / disclosed backlog)

- **No named hyperscaler customers** in the Q1 2026 8-K, Q1 call, or Analyst Day coverage. Only public named datacenter customer found: Naver (Korean cloud) hyperscale datacenter case study on cummins.com — promotional, undated/older, not a US hyperscaler.
- **No disclosed dollar backlog.** Secondary sources (getclimatebrief.com) claim large-genset backlog extends into late 2026/early 2027 at record levels — UNVERIFIED, low-quality source, do not lean on it.
- What IS disclosed: the $5B 2026E / $9B 2030 datacenter revenue figures and the 20GW capacity expansion (Analyst Day) — management-quantified materiality, which partially substitutes for backlog disclosure.

**D5 verdict:** Weakest of the three dimensions. Materiality is disclosed; customer identity and backlog are not.

## FLAGS

1. **$5B/$9B datacenter figures are second-hand.** Both trace to press coverage (TIKR + one search corroboration) of the 2026-05-21 Analyst Day; I could not access the primary IR presentation or webcast transcript. Before locking the rating, verify against the Analyst Day deck on investor.cummins.com or the related 8-K.
2. **No backlog dollar figure exists anywhere in primary sources.** The "sold out into 2027" claim is from a low-quality secondary site. Treat as unverified color.
3. **Intersegment double-counting** prevents an exact bottom-up reconciliation of the $5B datacenter figure against disclosed product lines (PS intersegment $863M in Q1 2026 alone). The cross-check above is approximate by necessity.
4. **95L lead-time "back half of '28"** came from analyst Jerry Revich's question framing, not a management statement. Management referenced the capacity doubling but gave no explicit lead-time figure.
5. **GAAP EPS noise:** Q1 2026 GAAP EPS $4.71 missed consensus (~$5.61) on $199M fuel cell divestiture charges; adjusted $6.15 beat. Use adjusted for trend analysis; don't let screeners flag this as an operating miss.
6. **FY2025 10-K figures** ($33.7B revenue, PS $7.46B/22.7% margin) were taken from reputable summaries of the filing (stocktitan, sahmcapital, Motley Fool), not read directly from the 10-K. Fine for a context briefing; pull the 10-K (sec.gov .../cmi-20251231.htm) before putting these in the scoring spreadsheet.
