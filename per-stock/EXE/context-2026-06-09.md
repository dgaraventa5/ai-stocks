# EXE (Expand Energy) — Pre-rating context briefing, 2026-06-09

Session lens: re-rating Layer 1 power names on how DIRECTLY they own the AI power bottleneck (direct = datacenter power/capacity sales at premium, signed hyperscaler PPAs, disclosed BTM MW/terms; indirect = commodity link via gas demand → Henry Hub → E&P earnings).

## Pre-research mental model

Expand Energy (EXE) was formed in October 2024 from the Chesapeake Energy + Southwestern Energy merger and is the largest US natural gas producer (~7 Bcf/d net), with assets split between the Haynesville (Louisiana) and Appalachia (Marcellus/Utica). The AI/datacenter thesis is second-derivative: gas-fired generation buildout for datacenters plus LNG export growth lifts gas demand and Henry Hub, lifting EXE's realizations — I do not recall any signed hyperscaler PPA or disclosed behind-the-meter deal, only management commentary about being "in conversations" to supply gas to power projects. Company has guided toward ~7.5 Bcf/d of productive capacity in 2026, and the most recent quarter I'm confident about pre-research is Q4 2025. Competitive position: scale leader and low-cost in both basins, but fundamentally a price-taker on Henry Hub/regional basis with a hedge program, unlike utilities/IPPs (VST, TLN, CEG) that sell the actual bottleneck (electrons/capacity).

## Evidence for D1, D2, D5

### D1 — AI Revenue %
Effectively 0% direct, low-single-digit % at most on a generous indirect attribution. Q1 2026 revenue $4.39B = $3,315M commodity sales + ~$1.07B marketing (10-Q filed 2026-04-28); zero dollars are disclosed as flowing from datacenter- or AI-specific contracts (no such contracts exist — Q1 2026 call, 8-K record). The only defensible attribution math is demand-share: 2026 US power-burn growth is ~0.5 Bcf/d vs ~120.7 Bcf/d total demand (Natural Gas Intelligence, 2026), and only a fraction of power burn growth is AI datacenters — so even price-channel attribution puts AI-linked revenue in the ~1-3% range, and that flows through Henry Hub, not through AI customers. Datacenter ≠ AI and price uplift ≠ AI revenue per the session lens; on the strict definition D1 ≈ 0%.

### D2 — Supply chain position
Price-taker, demonstrably. Q1 2026 average sales price $4.92/Mcf vs average NYMEX $5.04 — EXE sells at a discount to the benchmark, and realized only $4.28/Mcf after hedges (10-Q filed 2026-04-28). Hedge floors cover >65% of projected gas volumes (same 10-Q) — the posture of a company protecting against price downside, the opposite of a bottleneck owner extracting premiums from AI buyers. The flagship Delfin LNG SPA is priced at Henry Hub (10-Q). No disclosed contract anywhere at premium-to-hub pricing tied to datacenter demand. Scale (#1 US gas producer, 7.4 Bcfe/d) gives cost/logistics advantages within the commodity game, not pricing power over AI buyers.

### D5 — Hyperscaler exposure
Narrative only. No signed, disclosed deal with any named hyperscaler or datacenter developer as of 2026-06-09. Management language on the Q1 2026 call (2026-04-29, Motley Fool transcript): "actively negotiating with power providers in PJM-connected territory," power deals "secondary right now" to LNG — i.e., even the pipeline of potential deals runs through power-plant intermediaries, not hyperscalers directly, and is unsigned. EDGAR 8-K record March–June 2026 contains no Item 1.01 agreement. Contrast within Layer 1: utilities/IPPs (e.g., DTE's 1.4 GW signed hyperscaler deal, Talen/CEG nuclear PPAs) and even midstream (Energy Transfer's Oracle/CloudBurst gas-supply agreements) have what EXE lacks — named counterparties with disclosed terms.
