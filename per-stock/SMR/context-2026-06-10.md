# SMR (NuScale Power) — Pre-rating context briefing, 2026-06-10

Prepared for AI Thesis re-rating (Layer 1 directness lens). Current ratings (2026-05-24): D1=3, D2=3, D3=3, D4=2, D5=2.

---

## Pre-research mental model

NuScale is the only SMR developer with full NRC design approval (50 MWe certified 2023; uprated 77 MWe US460 approved May 2025), but still has zero binding commercial orders — revenue is a small engineering/licensing stream, mostly RoPower (Romania) FEED work via Fluor. Commercialization runs through ENTRA1 Energy, the exclusive partner supposed to land datacenter/industrial customers; everything announced to date has been MOUs/frameworks, not signed power contracts. Repo news-log (2026-05-18) flagged a securities class action over ENTRA1 qualification misrepresentation tied to a $495M payment driving a large quarterly net loss and ~$315M operating cash burn — suggesting the ENTRA1 structure itself is a thesis risk. Cash was ~$500M+ after ATM raises; needs rechecking post-Q1.

**Mental model errors found during research:** (a) the ~$495M ENTRA1 payment hit Q3 2025 G&A (disclosed 2025-11-06), not Q1 2026 — the repo news-log entry conflated quarters; (b) cash is far higher than I assumed ($1.0B at 3/31/26, CFO says >$1.2B by early May); (c) I did not know the PMA payment mechanics, which turn out to be the single most important fact for the directness rating (see Findings #2).

## Findings (sources)

### 1. Q1 2026 results: revenue effectively zero
- Revenue $565K vs $13.4M in Q1 2025 (10-Q Q1 2026, filed 2026-05-07, income statement). Decline = RoPower technology license + Fluor FEED Phase 2 work completed in 2025 and not repeating (StockTitan summary of Q1 PR, 2026-05-07).
- Net loss $46.7M; operating loss $57.5M; EPS -$0.14 (10-Q Q1 2026 income statement).
- Liquidity: cash $341.1M + short-term investments $549.0M + long-term investments $118.6M ≈ $1.0B, no debt (10-Q Q1 2026, Liquidity section). CFO Hamady on the Q1 call: liquidity "an increase to over $1.2 billion by early May" (Q1 2026 earnings call transcript via Globe and Mail, 2026-05-07) — implies heavy April ATM issuance; $962.1M of the $1.0B 2026 ATM Program remained available at 3/31/26 (10-Q).
- Q1 2026 ATM usage: 3.16M shares for $37.3M net at avg $12.01 (10-Q, Liquidity).
- Operating cash burn $314.7M in Q1, of which $259.9M was the ENTRA1 Milestone Contribution payment — underlying opex burn ~$55M/quarter (10-Q, cash flow discussion; CFO on Q1 call).

### 2. PMA mechanics (Note 9, 10-Q Q1 2026) — NuScale PAYS ENTRA1, not vice versa
- Partnership Milestones Agreement executed 2025-08-27. NuScale is "key supplier" to ENTRA1 energy projects, but **"ENTRA1 retains sole and full discretion to select, contract with, or purchase from NuScale"** (10-Q Note 9, exact quote).
- NuScale owes ENTRA1 a "Milestone Contribution" per NPM placed in an ENTRA1 project, in 3 tranches: MC1 = 15% on a NON-BINDING term sheet/MOU; MC2 = 35% on a binding PPA/offtake; MC3 = 50% on an OEM agreement (10-Q Note 9).
- MC1 was triggered in 2025 by ENTRA1 entering "a non-binding agreement relating to 72 NPMs" — expense of $507.4M, with $247.5M settled in 2025 and $259.9M settled in Q1 2026 (10-Q Note 9). 72 NPMs × 77 MWe = 5,544 MWe, consistent with the TVA "up to 6 GW" program (inference, not named in the 10-Q — see FLAGS).
- **Implied math (my computation, not disclosed):** if $507.4M = 15%, the total Milestone Contribution on the 72-NPM program ≈ $3.38B; MC2 (binding PPA) ≈ $1.18B and MC3 (OEM agreement) ≈ $1.69B would become payable BY NuScale TO ENTRA1. Payment timing/staging for MC2/MC3 is not disclosed. Against ~$1.0-1.2B liquidity, the bull-case "catalyst" (TVA PPA signing) triggers a contingent obligation potentially exceeding the entire balance sheet, presumably to be funded from NPM sale proceeds (PMA includes a "negotiated maximum sale price" per NPM) and/or further ATM dilution.
- PMA term runs to 2045, auto-renews 20 years (10-Q Note 9).

### 3. TVA / ENTRA1: still non-binding, PPA promised "later this year"
- TVA–ENTRA1 agreement (signed ~2025-09-02) is a "collaborative agreement" for up to 6 GW of NuScale SMR capacity across six plants — explicitly nonbinding (POWER Magazine 2025-09; World Nuclear News; ANS 2025-09-03).
- CEO Hopkins, Q1 2026 call: "discussions with TVA are advancing well toward a definitive PPA"; "we're hopeful that TVA can come across the line at some point later this year" (Q1 2026 transcript, 2026-05-07). Seeking Alpha (May 2026) confirms PPA still pending as of that date.
- No SMR 8-K announcing a PPA through 2026-06-09 (SEC EDGAR submissions feed: last 8-Ks are 2026-05-07 Item 2.02 earnings and 2026-06-02 Item 5.07 annual-meeting votes only).
- Note TVA is hedged across vendors: its Clinch River construction permit application (filed Apr/May 2025, NRC review ~17 months) is for GE-Hitachi's BWRX-300, which received a $400M DOE grant in Dec 2025 (ANS 2025-05-21; Federal Register 2025-07-01; DOE Dec 2025 via search summary — grant amount needs primary-source confirmation).

### 4. RoPower (Romania): FID approved, but revenue gap until pre-EPC contract
- Nuclearelectrica shareholders approved the FID for the 6-module, 462 MWe Doicești plant on 2026-02-12, moving the project from analysis to implementation (POWER Magazine, "Romania's Coal-to-NuScale SMR Conversion Secures FID," Feb/Mar 2026).
- But CFO on Q1 call: RoPower pre-EPC work generated only "about $8 million worth of revenue" across 2024-2025; COO Fisher: "we're not going to know exactly what kind of revenue streams" until pre-EPC scope finalized (Q1 2026 transcript). Stage 3 pre-EPC ≈ 15 months; first deployment early 2030s (POWER Magazine).
- This is NOT a datacenter project — government-driven coal-to-nuclear conversion.

### 5. Securities class action over ENTRA1 disclosures
- Filed in U.S. District Court, District of Oregon, case 3:26-cv-00328, Sections 10(b)/20(a); lead-plaintiff deadline was 2026-04-20 (Bleichmar Fonti & Auld case page; GlobeNewswire 2026-04-01).
- Allegations: NuScale misrepresented ENTRA1's experience — ENTRA1 "had never built, financed, or operated any significant project," organized around one principal (Wadie Habboush); the $495M Q3 2025 payment to ENTRA1 ($519M G&A vs $17M prior year, disclosed 2025-11-06) drove a 12.4% stock drop (PR Newswire / Morningstar, Mar-Apr 2026).

### 6. Regulatory / competitive position
- US460 (77 MWe, 6-module 462 MWe plant) received NRC Standard Design Approval 2025-05-29; NuScale remains the only SMR vendor with NRC-approved designs (NRC press release 25-033; Federal Register 2025-06-04; Q1 2026 PR).
- But "approved design" ≠ "first deployed": GE Vernova-Hitachi's BWRX-300 is under construction at OPG Darlington (Canada) targeting ~2029, the closest Western SMR to commercial operation, and TVA's own US construction permit is for BWRX-300 (GE Vernova; exoswan SMR overview 2026; ANS).
- Market data: price $9.67, market cap ~$3.35B per yfinance 2026-06-10 (Class A/B share structure — yfinance market cap may not reflect full economic share count; flag).

## Evidence for D1 (% of revenue AI-tied TODAY)

- Total Q1 2026 revenue: $565K (10-Q income statement). Composition: engineering/licensing services; the 10-Q segment note says "all significant revenue generated to date arises from engineering and licensing fees and services provided to potential customers" (10-Q Note 10).
- Historical revenue was RoPower (Romania coal-replacement, government project) — ~$8M cumulative 2024-2025 (CFO, Q1 call). Zero revenue traces to a datacenter or AI customer.
- **D1 conclusion-relevant fact: AI-tied revenue today = $0 of an already de-minimis revenue base.** Anything AI is aspiration via ENTRA1, who owns the customer relationship.

## Evidence for D2 (position vs other SMR developers)

- Pro: only NRC-approved SMR designs (US600 2020/2023 certification; US460 SDA 2025-05-29, NRC 25-033); NPM components physically in production at Doosan (10-Q LLM note; Q1 PR "NPM components currently in production for commercial use" — count not disclosed); Framatome fuel partnership expanded (Q1 PR).
- Con: GEH BWRX-300 is further along to actual operation (Darlington under construction, ~2029; TVA's Clinch River CP application is BWRX-300, plus $400M DOE grant); TVA — NuScale's flagship prospect — is simultaneously building the competitor's reactor. Oklo/X-energy have direct hyperscaler-adjacent relationships (Switch, Amazon) while NuScale's datacenter channel is exclusively intermediated by ENTRA1, a single-principal entity now subject of a fraud class action.
- Net: regulatory leader, commercialization laggard-to-peer; the ENTRA1 intermediation is a structural disadvantage vs. peers who contract directly with offtakers.

## Evidence for D5 (signed hyperscaler/datacenter agreements vs MOUs)

- Signed binding datacenter-power contracts: **none found.** TVA-ENTRA1 6 GW = explicitly "nonbinding collaborative agreement" (POWER, WNN, Sep 2025). The 72-NPM trigger in the 10-Q is described as "a non-binding agreement" (10-Q Note 9, exact words).
- ENTRA1 "$25B" US-Japan framework (Oct 2025) = government investment-framework designation, not a customer contract (NuScale PR 2025-10; Neutron Bytes 2025-10-30).
- CEO: customers "generally don't want to own a nuclear asset"; PPA hoped "later this year" (Q1 call). CFO links cash-flow-positive-by-YE2026 target to "securing definitive power purchase agreements" (Q1 PR via StockTitan).
- Even when a PPA signs, the counterparty would be ENTRA1↔TVA (a utility), not NuScale↔hyperscaler. NuScale is two contractual hops from any datacenter.

## FLAGS

1. **72 NPMs = TVA is my inference.** The 10-Q does not name the counterparty of the non-binding agreement that triggered MC1. 72 × 77 MWe = 5,544 MWe matches "up to 6 GW" TVA program; no other ENTRA1 deal that size is public. Verify in 10-K/proxy or next call.
2. **MC2/MC3 totals (~$1.18B / ~$1.69B) are computed, not disclosed.** Derived from $507.4M = 15%. Payment timing, staging, and any netting against NPM sale proceeds are NOT disclosed. Could be materially less cash-strained than the headline math implies — or not.
3. **"Over $1.2B liquidity by early May"** is a CFO verbal statement on the call; the implied ~$200M April ATM issuance (and dilution) is not yet in a filing I read. Q2 10-Q will quantify.
4. **$400M DOE grant to TVA for BWRX-300 (Dec 2025)** came from a search summary citing DOE; confirm against DOE/energy.gov primary source before using in scoring.
5. **yfinance market cap (~$3.35B)** may exclude Class B/LLC units (Fluor's stake); economic market cap is likely higher. Use share-class-aware count for any valuation work.
6. **Repo news-log entry (2026-05-18) conflates quarters:** the $532M net loss / $495M ENTRA1 payment belongs to Q3 2025 disclosure (per class-action filings); Q1 2026 net loss was $46.7M. The "~$315M Q1 operating burn" part is correct. News-log correction recommended (not made — append-only convention, Dom's call).
