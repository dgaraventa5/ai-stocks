### 2b. FCF-conversion red-flag check
- TTM statement FCF **−$62.2M** (negative); TTM net income negative (GAAP loss-driven). Conversion ratio is **not meaningful** (both negative). Negative FCF is a **growth/working-capital phenomenon** — note the $60.9M new noncurrent customer contract asset and AP jump $69M→$109M on the Base Electron ramp. **Not the accrual-games signature** (the signature requires receivables+inventory ballooning *with reported profits*; here reported profit is a non-cash warrant loss). Flag: **negative FCF during a large project ramp + a still-live 2026 maturity = real liquidity-watch item**, not an earnings-quality fraud flag.

### 2c. Expectations red-flag (`scripts/expectations_flag.py BW`)
- Result: **`clean: P/S 3.65 = 95.2th pctile of own 3y range; rev YoY 40.5% vs 3y median 1.4%`**
- Reading: the *flag did NOT fire* (it returned "clean") because revenue is **accelerating**, not decelerating — the flag only fires on peak-multiple-on-*decelerating*-growth. But note the **substance**: P/S 3.65 is at the **95th percentile of BW's own 3-year range**. This is a multiple that has fully re-rated on the AI-datacenter narrative. The briefing must own this: at $16/share (~$2.4B mcap) the stock is **no longer cheap on trailing fundamentals** — the bull case requires the $14B pipeline and 1.2 GW Base Electron backlog to convert. This is now a *momentum/narrative* name as much as a deep-value one.

- **D1 (AI rev %):** Q1 AI-datacenter revenue still small ($31M of $214M ≈ 14%), but backlog is ~89% AI-datacenter. Tension between *current* mix and *forward* mix — D1 measures genuine AI-tie; backlog says high, trailing P&L says moderate. Debatable.
- **D2 / D3 (position / moat):** boiler + steam-turbine island is real engineering capability with few US suppliers at GW scale, but it's EPC-style project work, not a bottleneck component. Moderate.
- **D4 (capacity expansion):** strongly positive — funded, NTP-backed, multi-GW buildout underway.
- **D5 (hyperscaler exposure):** customer is Applied Digital (a neocloud/AI-factory developer), not a hyperscaler directly; but the demand is AI-datacenter power. High exposure, single-name.
- **M1/M2/M3 (momentum):** revenue/backlog inflection + share re-rating → strong; but verify EPS-revision and insider direction.
- **R1 (customer concentration):** **severe** — Base Electron/Applied Digital ≈ 89% of backlog. Likely a 1–2.
- **R2 (geo/export):** US-centric (North Dakota, Ohio) — low geographic/policy risk.
- **R3 (balance sheet):** improved dramatically (net debt <1.0x) BUT going concern was alive 12 months ago, a 2026 maturity is live, $191.7M preferred + warrant dilution overhang, negative FCF during ramp. Holistic view is **not** the rosy <1.0x headline.
- **R4 (reg/litigation):** no major disclosed regulatory threat; routine. Likely 4.
