## AI exposure & directness assessment

**This is the entire reason QCOM is even a candidate, and it is mostly a 2026–2027 *forward* story, not current revenue.**

- **Data-center AI inference entry (announced 2025-10-27):** AI200 (commercial 2026) and AI250 (2027) — rack-scale inference accelerators built on Hexagon NPU IP. AI200 card carries 768 GB LPDDR; rack-level ~160 kW; positioned as memory-rich, lower-cost inference vs NVDA/AMD ([Qualcomm PR 2025-10-27](https://www.qualcomm.com/news/releases/2025/10/qualcomm-unveils-ai200-and-ai250-redefining-rack-scale-data-cent); [CNBC 2025-10-27](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/27/qualcomm-ai200-ai250-ai-chips-nvidia-amd.html)). Stock rose ~11% on the announcement.
- **HUMAIN (Saudi):** committed to deploy **200 MW** of AI200/AI250 starting 2026 (Qualcomm PR; CNBC). Anchor customer, but single geography/single customer concentration.
- **Hyperscaler:** CEO Amon said on the Q2 call Qualcomm is "close" to placing AI200 into a "major hyperscaler"; press (TrendForce 2026-04-30) reports custom-chip supply to a hyperscaler in the Dec-2025 quarter. **Unconfirmed/unsigned at briefing date — treat as pipeline, not booked.**
- **Three product lines being built:** data-center CPUs, inference accelerators, custom ASICs (Computer Weekly).
- **Edge/on-device AI:** the genuinely *current* AI angle — Snapdragon NPUs in AI phones/PCs and automotive (Snapdragon Digital Chassis). Automotive +38% is the strongest current diversification proof point.

**Directness verdict (proposed, for collaborative rating):** **Indirect/prospective on data-center AI.** As of Q2 FY2026, essentially **0% of revenue** is AI-datacenter; the AI-supply-chain-relevant revenue today is edge AI embedded in handset/IoT/auto SoCs (hard to isolate, arguably a feature not a separate line). The data-center thesis is real but unproven — first revenue 2026, scale uncertain, and competing head-on with NVDA/AMD in inference where incumbency and CUDA lock-in are formidable. **The bulk of QCOM revenue (~70%+) is still handsets + licensing**, a low-growth/declining base facing the Apple modem cliff.

## Red flags

1. **Rule-15 (EPS YoY one-time-dominated) — MUST BLANK.** Q2 FY2026 GAAP EPS $6.88 (+173% YoY) is driven by a **$5.7B income-tax benefit ($5.33/share)** from releasing a deferred-tax valuation allowance (CAMT/domestic-R&D-deduction guidance, Treasury/IRS Feb 2026). Non-GAAP EPS actually **fell ~7%** ([stocktitan 8-K/10-Q](https://www.stocktitan.net/sec-filings/QCOM/10-q-qualcomm-inc-de-quarterly-earnings-report-3c0b90ab8861.html)). The mirror item: **FY2025 GAAP net income collapsed to $5.5B** on a $7.1B tax provision (the Q4 FY2025 −$2.89 EPS quarter) — that was when the valuation allowance was *established*. Both the GAAP collapse and the GAAP spike are tax-allowance noise. → **Blank EPS YoY; let Growth average revenue metrics.**
2. **Apple modem cliff:** Apple in-housing modems; estimated **$0.5–1.0B FY2026 revenue loss**, accelerating into FY2027 as Apple share falls toward zero ([heygotrade](https://www.heygotrade.com/en/blog/qualcomm-q2-2026-beat-qcom-reset-apple-modem/)). This is the structural overhang the data-center push is meant to offset.
3. **Flat-to-down core growth:** revenue −3% YoY MRQ; 3Y revenue CAGR FY22→FY25 ≈ **0%** (FY2022 $44.2B → FY2025 $44.28B). The 2-yr CAGR (FY23→FY25) of ~11% only looks good because FY2023 was a cyclical trough. Consensus FY2026/FY2027 non-GAAP EPS roughly **flat (~$8 / ~$7.9)** with recent downward revisions ([tickeron](https://tickeron.com/earnings/QCOM/)).
4. **Data-center execution/competitive risk:** AI200 is unproven silicon vs entrenched NVDA/AMD; inference TAM is real but Qualmcomm has no installed base or software ecosystem moat there. Concentration on HUMAIN + one unsigned hyperscaler.
5. **China handset exposure:** large share of QCT handset revenue is China OEMs — export-control / geopolitical tail risk (general Layer-6 risk, not QCOM-specific worsening noted this quarter).

## Proposed subjective view (NOT written to spreadsheet — for collaborative rating session)

- **AI Thesis directness: LOW–MEDIUM.** Today ~0% data-center AI revenue; the AI-supply-chain claim rests on (a) edge/on-device AI baked into handset/auto SoCs (real but inseparable, low-growth host market) and (b) the AI200/AI250 data-center inference push, which is a credible but **unproven, forward** option with single-anchor-customer (HUMAIN) concentration and one unsigned hyperscaler. Until AI200 books material revenue and a named hyperscaler signs, this is a call option, not exposure. Score should reflect prospective/indirect, not direct AI leverage.
- **Key risks:** (1) Apple modem cliff ($0.5–1.0B FY26, worse FY27); (2) flat/declining core (handset + licensing ~70%+ of revenue, ~0% 3Y CAGR); (3) data-center execution vs NVDA/AMD with no software moat; (4) China handset/geopolitical exposure; (5) earnings optics distorted by tax-allowance swings (rule 15).
- **Offsets:** strong cash generation (28% FCF margin, 5.4% FCF yield, ROIC ~25%+), light leverage (~0.4x), cheap headline multiples (Fwd P/E 20.7, EV/EBITDA 17.6) — but cheap-for-a-reason given flat growth (PEG penalty).
