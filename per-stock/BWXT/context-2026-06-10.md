# BWXT — Pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-10)

Prepared for: Layer 1 AI Thesis re-rate. Session lens: how DIRECTLY does BWXT own the AI power bottleneck (D1 = % revenue genuinely AI-tied today, D2 = bottleneck position in nuclear components, D5 = direct hyperscaler linkage).

---

## Pre-research mental model

BWXT is ~75-80% government revenue: sole-source manufacturer of naval nuclear reactors/components for US Navy submarines and carriers (Government Operations), plus DOE site work. Commercial Operations (~20-25%) is Canadian CANDU components/refurbishment, medical isotopes, and the AI-adjacent piece: SMR component work (BWRX-300 reactor pressure vessel for Darlington, TRISO fuel, Project Pele microreactor) — mostly backlog/development rather than large current revenue. Last quarter known in detail ~Q3 2025 (FY2025 run-rate ~$3B; Kinectrics closed mid-2025). News log had one 2026-05-07 entry: $1.4B+ naval awards, FY2026 guidance >$3.75B, PCG acquisition planned.

## Findings (sources)

1. **Q1 2026 results (8-K Ex. 99.1, filed 2026-05-04, SEC EDGAR accession 0001486957-26-000027):** Revenue $860.2M (+26% YoY). Government Operations $577.9M (+4%); Commercial Operations $283.6M (+121%). Commercial op income $24.0M (vs $6.5M); commercial adj. EBITDA $36.5M (+162%). Net income $91.2M; non-GAAP EPS $1.12. FCF $50.1M. Guidance raised: revenue >$3,750M, adj. EBITDA $650-665M, non-GAAP EPS $4.60-$4.75, FCF $315-330M. Guidance excludes pending PCG acquisition.
2. **Segment mix (denominator for D1):** FY2025: Government $2,350.1M (73.5%), Commercial $853.1M (26.7%) of $3,198.4M consolidated (Q4/FY2025 8-K Ex. 99.1, 2026-02-23). Q1 2026: Commercial = 33.0% of revenue. Government growth drivers: special materials + naval reactors, "partially offset by lower microreactor volumes" (Q1 8-K) — microreactor (Pele) sits in Government and is DoD-funded, not AI.
3. **Commercial growth decomposition (Q1 2026 earnings call, 2026-05-04, Investing.com transcript):** Commercial Operations organic growth was **39%**; the rest of the +121% is the Kinectrics acquisition (closed Jan 2025; utility/grid services, CANDU lifecycle — not datacenter revenue). Growth drivers named in 8-K: commercial nuclear components, field services, fuel and fuel handling, medical sales, Kinectrics contribution. Medical and CANDU refurb are not AI-tied.
4. **Backlog:** Total $8.7B at Q1 2026, +77% YoY, +19% sequential (earnings call). Government backlog "nearly $7B" (CFO, call) → commercial backlog ≈ $1.7B implied — flagged as derived, not directly disclosed. FY2025 ended at $7.3B, +50% YoY, "driven by large multi-year naval propulsion, special materials, and commercial nuclear power awards" (Q4 2025 8-K, 2026-02-23). The 2026 sequential jump is dominated by the $1.4B+ May naval awards (BWXT PR 2026-05-07) — government, not AI.
5. **SMR position (D2 core evidence):** BWXT holds the reactor pressure vessel contracts for the first BWRX-300 at OPG Darlington — part of >C$1B (~US$696M) of contracts awarded Jan 2025 spanning Pickering life extension + Darlington new build (BWXT PR 2025-01-27; NucNet 2025-01; World Nuclear News). First member of GE Vernova Nuclear's SMR supplier group (bwxt.com). C$80M Cambridge, Ontario plant expansion completing 2026 (World Nuclear News). Darlington unit 1 targeted on-grid ~end-2029 — component revenue flows now, plant power (and any datacenter offtake) is 2029+.
6. **Precision Components Group acquisition (announced with Q1 2026 results, 2026-05-04):** ~$200M purchase, ~$125M 2025 revenue, low-double-digit EBITDA margins, 2 facilities, 400+ employees (earnings call). CEO framing: "BWXT's first step in establishing U.S.-based commercial nuclear manufacturing capacity" (Q1 8-K). Strategic positioning for US SMR/large-nuclear buildout, incl. the announced US-Japan plan "to invest up to $40 billion to build up to 3 gigawatts of GE Hitachi SMRs in the southeastern United States" (CEO, Q1 call) — pipeline, zero revenue today.
7. **Direct datacenter linkage — the money quote (D5):** On the Q1 2026 call an analyst asked whether BWXT has "any direct exposure to the data center buildout." CEO Rex Geveden: **"Yeah, I don't know the details on that. I suspect that we do."** (Q1 2026 call, Benzinga transcript, 2026-05-04). Management itself could not cite a direct datacenter revenue line. No mention of "AI" or "hyperscaler" anywhere in the call per transcript search.

## DIFF vs prior mental model

- **Commercial is bigger than modeled:** 26.7% of FY2025 revenue and 33% of Q1 2026, vs. my ~20-25% prior — but the delta is mostly Kinectrics M&A (utility services), not AI demand.
- **SMR component revenue is real and current**, not just backlog: Darlington RPV work is in revenue now and management says customers are "increasingly coming to BWXT to supply critical nuclear components for their current and future SMR and large-scale nuclear projects."
- **New: PCG acquisition** creates a US commercial nuclear components footprint that didn't exist — relevant to future D2, not current D1.
- **Confirmed (stronger than expected): no direct hyperscaler/datacenter linkage.** CEO couldn't name one when asked directly.
- **Microreactor volumes are DOWN** in Q1 2026 — the most futuristic piece (Pele-class) is currently shrinking, and is DoD-funded anyway.

## Evidence for D1 (% revenue genuinely AI-tied today)

- Government = 67% of Q1 2026 revenue (naval/DOE/special materials/microreactors) — zero AI linkage by definition of the lens.
- Commercial $283.6M (33%) decomposes into: Kinectrics utility services (M&A, grid/CANDU work), medical isotopes, CANDU components/field services/fuel handling (Pickering/Darlington refurb — Ontario grid programs that predate the AI boom), and BWRX-300 SMR components. Only the SMR component slice has even indirect AI linkage, and OPG's Darlington program was sanctioned (2021-2025) on Ontario electrification grounds, not hyperscaler contracts. No company disclosure quantifies SMR component revenue. **Estimate: direct AI-tied revenue ≈ 0%; indirect-at-best (SMR components) is a single-digit % of total revenue — FLAG: not separately disclosed, cannot be computed precisely.**

## Evidence for D2 (bottleneck position in nuclear components)

- Sole RPV supplier for the first Western on-grid SMR (BWRX-300 Darlington); first member of GE Vernova's SMR supplier group → incumbent supplier position for the BWRX-300 fleet (TVA Clinch River names BWXT a supply-chain partner; US-Japan 3GW initiative would expand the funnel).
- Heavy nuclear-grade component manufacturing (large vessels, steam generators) is a genuine Western chokepoint; BWXT is adding capacity (C$80M Cambridge expansion, PCG for US footprint) — consistent with a supplier that sees demand exceeding capacity.
- Counterweight: competitors exist for non-BWRX designs (Doosan Enerbility for X-energy/NuScale-adjacent work, IHI, Framatome), and other SMR vendors (Oklo, Kairos, TerraPower, X-energy) — the designs hyperscalers actually contracted — largely do NOT use BWXT as anchor supplier. BWXT's bottleneck position is strongest specifically in the GE Vernova BWRX-300 ecosystem and Canadian CANDU.

## Evidence for D5 (direct hyperscaler linkage)

- None found. The 13 announced hyperscaler nuclear deals (~9.8 GW: MSFT/Constellation TMI, AMZN/X-energy, GOOGL/Kairos, META/Oklo-TerraPower-Vistra, per smrintel.com tracker, May 2026) involve reactor developers and IPPs — BWXT appears in none of them.
- CEO on Q1 2026 call, asked directly about datacenter exposure: "I don't know the details on that. I suspect that we do." No "AI" or "hyperscaler" mentions on the call.
- Closest indirect path: if GE Vernova BWRX-300 wins hyperscaler-adjacent orders (TVA, US-Japan program), BWXT components benefit one step removed.

## FLAGS

- Commercial backlog (~$1.7B) is **derived** (total $8.7B minus "nearly $7B" government per CFO call remark), not a disclosed figure — verify against 10-Q when filed.
- SMR component revenue is **not separately disclosed** anywhere found; the D1 single-digit estimate is an inference, not a measurement.
- Commercial +121% YoY is **heavily M&A-flavored** (organic 39%); any AI-narrative screen using segment growth overstates underlying AI-driven demand.
- Could not access the Q1 2026 earnings presentation slides for the segment backlog split (investor site not fetched); transcript-derived numbers used instead.
