## 1. Pre-research mental model

CDNS is half of the EDA/IP duopoly (with Synopsys) — a ~95% recurring-revenue, high-multiple compounder whose tools are mission-critical infrastructure for every advanced chip designed. The AI buildout is a direct tailwind on two fronts: (a) more/more-complex AI silicon designed = more EDA seats and emulation hardware (Palladium/Protium), and (b) Cadence is embedding agentic AI (Cerebrus, JedAI, the new "Stack" agents) into its own flow to monetize productivity. The live risks are the rich valuation (priced for sustained ~15%+ growth) and China export-control exposure, which whipsawed in 2025 (BIS restriction May 2025 → rescinded July 2025) plus a $140M DOJ/BIS settlement for past unauthorized exports.

## 4. Earnings transcript highlights

Q1 2026 call (2026-04-27). Full transcript retrieved via Motley Fool (raw text); no markdown transcript saved to `/transcripts/` in this pass (out of scope — briefing only).

- **AI/agentic monetization:** dual model — subscription remains the anchor for existing customers; new agentic workflows priced **usage/consumption-based**, and they expect agents to drive "increased usage of the underlying base tools through more exploration, verification, optimization and compute." CEO Devgan: "no pushback at all. If we can deliver productivity, the customer is more than willing to engage."
- **Hardware/emulation:** Palladium Z3 had its **best quarter ever** in Q1, driven by AI/HPC + automotive/robotics; claims "at least a 10-year lead" vs FPGA-based competitors; Z3 supports designs up to ~1 trillion transistors (sufficient through 2030 while Z4/Z5 are developed).
- **China:** **13% of Q1 revenue**, expected to stay similar through 2026; YoY comp flattered by weak Q1 2025 in the region. Guidance assumes "export control regulations that exist today remain substantially similar for the remainder of the year" — i.e., a guidance assumption, not a guarantee.
- **Rule of 60** expected for the first time in 2026.
- **Hexagon:** ~$160M revenue, $0.28 dilutive in 2026, accretive 2027 (financing = 70% cash).
- **Capital return:** ~50% of FCF to buybacks.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not pre-decide)

- **D1 (market size/secular demand):** AI-silicon design demand is the explicit driver of the guidance raise + record backlog — flag for review (likely strong).
- **D2 (competitive position / moat):** EDA duopoly intact; "10-year lead" claim on emulation; Google/NVIDIA partnerships. Flag as strong-moat candidate.
- **D3 (revenue exposure directness):** EDA/IP is direct, picks-and-shovels exposure to all AI chip design — flag.
- **D4 (AI product/monetization):** NEW agentic stack with usage-based pricing is a genuine new monetization vector, not just marketing — flag for upgrade consideration vs. any template baseline.
- **D5 (execution/management):** Beat-and-raise, record backlog, Rule-of-60 target — flag positive; offset by the $140.6M export-control settlement (a real compliance/governance black mark — weigh in D5 and/or risk).
- **Momentum (M):** Beat-and-raise + record backlog; refresh objective 50DMA% via `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` before any momentum rating (rule 11). Flag.
- **R-geography (R2):** China = 12.8% of revenue; export-control regime is live (10-Q risk factor), whipsawed in 2025, guidance explicitly assumes status quo. This is the primary THESIS-BREAK watch item — flag for R2.
- **R-balance sheet (R3):** Hexagon financed 70% cash → some financing headwind/leverage uptick; $0.28 EPS dilution in 2026. Flag to check net debt post-deal.
- **R5 (Disruption Risk):** **Defaults to 5 (max).** CDNS is **Layer 04 (EDA/IP), NOT Layer 10** — per rule 16, sub-5 R5 applies only to Layer 10 apps. EDA is mission-critical design infrastructure, not an agentic-AI-disruptable application; agentic AI is a *tailwind to* Cadence's own tools, not a threat. R5 stays 5.
- **Rule-14 (expectations / what's-priced-in):** CDNS is a rich-multiple name guiding ~17% growth — exactly the profile where Total Score can peak on crowded consensus. Run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py CDNS` before scoring and treat the result as a qualitative red-flag input, not a scored metric. Flag.
