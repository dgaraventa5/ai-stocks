## 1. Pre-research mental model

Atkore makes electrical conduit, cable, metal framing and cable-management products (PVC + steel conduit). The story since 2022 is **margin normalization**: the COVID-era PVC price spike inflated 2022–23 EPS to an unsustainable peak, and as PVC/steel prices normalized DOWN, gross margins, EBITDA and EPS have compressed hard off that peak — the *opposite* trajectory of the AI-buildout grower names. Data-center electrical raceway/conduit demand is a genuine but partial offset to that commodity-driven decline. The headline P/E looks cheap, but it's cheap on *normalizing* earnings, and the FY runs to Sept 30 (so "Q2 FY2026" = quarter ended ~March 2026). Buybacks have been a meaningful capital-return lever.

## 6. Implications for ratings

Flags only — no rating numbers pre-decided.

- **D1/D2/D5 (AI Thesis — directness, magnitude, durability):** Data-center electrical conduit/cable-management exposure is more material than the prior template implied (double-digit growth in DC-linked lines, deliberate 80/20 capacity shift toward electrical). However, ATKR is an *indirect/commodity* AI play — conduit is necessary but un-differentiated infrastructure sold into broad non-resi/solar/DC end markets, not AI-specific. **Revisit D1 (directness) and D2 (magnitude)** with the absolute-lens discipline (rule 12): the DC tailwind is real but diluted across a cyclical-commodity revenue base. Don't over-credit a generic raceway maker for the AI buildout.

- **Quality & Growth inputs — CRITICAL commodity-margin-cycle flag (per task):** This is the **opposite** of the buildout grower names. Gross margin and EBITDA margin are still **declining off the 2022–23 PVC peak** (gross ~18.6% vs ~26.4% YoY; adj. EBITDA margin ~11.1% vs ~18.5%). Any Quality (margin/ROIC) and Growth (EPS-growth) inputs pulled from TTM will be measuring a *down-cycle*, and a stale or peak-anchored base will mislead in the WRONG direction (flatters past, understates current decline — or vice versa depending on window). Refresh objective Quality/Margin inputs against the latest quarter; do not let a 2022-peak TTM tail inflate the picture.

- **Rule-15 (EPS YoY blank when one-time-dominated) — LIKELY APPLIES:** Q2 GAAP net loss $(124.1)M / diluted $(3.65) is **dominated by the $136.5M non-operating PVC antitrust settlement** (documented in 8-K 2026-04-29 and 10-Q Note 16). A GAAP EPS-YoY input would score garbage off this one-time charge. Per rule 15, the EPS-YoY input should likely be **blanked** for the affected period (Growth then averages the revenue metrics), citing the settlement item in the Rating Audit. This is a documented, non-operating-dominated swing — exactly the rule-15 case. (Conversely, *adjusted* EPS $1.23 beat — note the GAAP/adjusted divergence explicitly when scoring.)

- **Rule-14 (expectations red-flag) angle:** ATKR is the inverse of the crowded-consensus problem — it is a beaten-down/normalizing-margin name, not a peak-multiple decelerator. Run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py ATKR` at scoring; expectation is it will NOT flag (low multiple, and revenue just re-accelerated). Worth confirming rather than assuming.

- **M (Momentum):** Stock rose ~3% on the Q2 beat; revenue inflected positive for the first time since FY2022; refresh objective **50DMA %** (`scripts/momentum_50dma.py ATKR`) — narrative momentum improved but verify the price-trend metric objectively. EPS-revisions subjective rating: FY adjusted guide *held* (not raised), so revisions are stable-to-modestly-positive, not a clear upgrade.

- **R1–R4 (Risk):** **R4 (regulatory/legal):** the PVC antitrust matter is a live consideration — $136.5M settled but **End User Plaintiffs class still pending** = residual legal tail; also ongoing PVC import/tariff dynamics. **R1 (customer/end-market concentration):** broad end markets (non-resi, solar, DC) — diversified, but cyclically exposed to construction. **R3 (balance sheet):** company states settlement funded from cash, no material leverage impact — verify with 10-Q balance sheet at scoring.

- **R5 (Disruption Risk):** ATKR is **physical electrical infrastructure (Layer 02), not Layer 10** → **R5 defaults to 5** (no credible AI/structural disruption to conduit/cable/framing in ~5yr). No change warranted.

- **THESIS-BREAK scan:** Watch for (a) continued margin/ASP erosion beyond input-cost timing (partially present — margins still falling, but volume-led recovery and reaffirmed guide are offsetting); (b) volume softening (NOT seen — volume +5%, the bright spot); (c) guidance cut (NOT seen — FY adj. EPS reaffirmed; net-sales trim is divestiture mechanics, not demand); (d) demand loss (NOT seen — DC/electrical strong); (e) regulatory/antidumping action (PVC import pressure persists with "limited tariff protection," and End User antitrust class pending — monitor). **Net: no clean thesis break this quarter; the margin-compression risk is contained by a volume-led top-line inflection, but PVC ASP/import structural pressure and the residual End-User antitrust class are the things to keep watching.**
