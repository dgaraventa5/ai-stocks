## The cycle story (essential context)

onsemi is **emerging from a deep cyclical trough** driven by auto/industrial inventory correction:
- 3Y revenue CAGR FY22→FY25 = **−10.4%** (FY22 $8,326M → FY25 $5,995M). This is a down-cycle, not secular decline.
- Auto revenue grew +5% YoY in Q1 2026 — **first annual growth after seven quarters of decline** (call commentary).
- TTM (Q2'25–Q1'26) understates the recovering run-rate: TTM rev $6,063M, TTM GAAP GM 37.5% vs MRQ 38.5% and rising. **Rule-9 TTM-limitation flag applies** — high-momentum recovery name; MRQ > TTM on margin.

## AI / Datacenter exposure & directness — the bull case

onsemi is the **most AI-levered of the analog/power group via datacenter power delivery** (the brief's framing — confirmed):
- **AI data center revenue more than doubled YoY and grew >30% sequentially in Q1 2026** (press release, verbatim). onsemi does **NOT** break out the dollar figure as a reported segment (confirmed — withheld from release). Earnings-call analyst color put the prior-year base at ~$250M with a projection to roughly double again in 2026 (→ ~$500M, ~8% of revenue) — **call-derived, LOW confidence, not in any filing; do not treat as cited.**
- **NVIDIA 800 VDC collaboration** announced 2025-07-29: onsemi supplies power conversion across the full power tree — solid-state transformers, PSUs, 800 VDC distribution, core/Vcore power delivery — for next-gen AI data centers (NVIDIA MGX ecosystem). Product stack: EliteSiC MOSFETs, PowerTrench T10 silicon MOSFETs (<1 mΩ Rds), GaN HEMTs, PoL buck regulators, smart fuses, SiC JFET e-Fuses for 400/800V hot-swap.
- **SiC capacity:** ramped 200mm SiC at Bucheon, South Korea in 2026 (150mm→200mm, ~80% more die/wafer, structural cost advantage). SiC also serves the EV/auto core (900V architectures, Geely/NIO).

**Directness assessment:** This is a **real but still-minority AI angle** layered on a cyclical auto/industrial core. ~8% of revenue (if call color is right) is AI-data-center vs PSG's much larger EV/auto SiC and industrial base. Directness is genuine (power IS the binding AI constraint per the macro thesis; onsemi is named in NVIDIA's 800V ecosystem alongside Infineon/MPWR) but the magnitude is small and the dollar disclosure is opaque. The AI growth rate is spectacular off a tiny base; the company-level numbers are still driven by the auto/industrial cycle turn, not by AI. Treat AI as an **option/accelerant on top of a cyclical recovery**, not the core driver yet.
