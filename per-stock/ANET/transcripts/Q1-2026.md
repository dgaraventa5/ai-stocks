# Arista Networks (ANET) — Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript Summary

## Prepared Remarks

### Jayshree Ullal, CEO

**Financial highlights:**
- Q1 2026 revenue: $2.709B, up 35.1% YoY (beat consensus ~$2.665B by ~$44M)
- Non-GAAP EPS: $0.87, up 31.8% YoY (beat consensus $0.82 by $0.05)
- Record operating cash flow: $1.694B (+163.8% YoY)
- Net Promoter Score improved from 87 to 89; 94% of customers strongly positive

**AI networking strategy — three fabric use cases:**

1. **Scale Out** (primary today): Horizontal leaf-spine architectures for AI training clusters. 800G deployments active with 100+ cumulative customers. 1.6T expected at production scale in 2027.

2. **Scale Across**: Distributes workloads across geographically distributed data centers. Expected to contribute "at least a third" of AI revenue in 2026. Competitive advantage cited.

3. **Scale Up**: Entry expected 2027-2028 with ESUN specifications. 5-7 rack design opportunities identified with customers. Currently limited to NVLink/PCIe domain — Arista targeting the Ethernet interconnect layer.

**FY2026 guidance raised:**
- Revenue growth: 27.7% to $11.5B (raised from prior)
- AI networking target: $3.5B (raised from $3.25B, more than doubling 2025)
- Campus networking target: $1.25B (maintained)
- Operating margin: ~46%
- Gross margin: 62-64% range

**XPO (Extended Pluggable Optics):**
- Supports 12.8 Tbps per module, 204.8 Tbps per rack unit with integrated liquid cooling
- Endorsed by 100+ consortium vendors
- "XPO has a ten-year run, especially at 1.6T and 3.2T"
- Reduces racks by up to 75% and floor space by 44% vs. traditional options
- Production expected 2027

**Customer wins:**
- Neo Cloud AI customer selected Arista for 800G scale-out with AMD MI-series accelerators
- Fourth major customer officially moved from InfiniBand to Ethernet at production scale (transition took ~2 years)
- Potential 1-2 new 10%+ customers contingent on shipment volumes

**Supply chain warning:**
- "Demand is outstripping our supply" across wafers, silicon chips, CPUs, optics, and memory
- Shortages expected to persist 1-2 years
- Purchase commitments increased from $6.8B to $8.9B (multiyear, primarily TSMC)
- "We are gonna do everything, including hurt our gross margins to supply to that demand this year and next year"

## Q&A Session — Key Exchanges

### Scale-Across Revenue (Simon Leopold, Raymond James)
**Q:** What is Scale Across's contribution to the $3.5B AI target?
**A (Ullal):** "Scale Across will definitely contribute at least a third of our AI number" for 2026. Scale Out comprises the remainder. Scale Up negligible until 2027-2028.

### Scale-Up Timeline (George Nader, Wolff Research)
**Q:** Update on 5-7 scale-up rack design opportunities?
**A (Ullal):** 5-7 opportunities remain active, but majority target 1.6T speeds in 2027. "Scale Up will only really happen in 27 and 28."

### Supply Chain Duration (Antoine, New Street Research)
**Q:** Are supply limitations impacting the 28% unit growth guidance?
**A (Ullal):** Supply challenges are "a one or two year phenomena," not temporary. Lead times at "52 week lead times pretty reliably" with reservation needs beyond that.

### New 10%+ Customers (Erin Rakers, Wells Fargo)
**Q:** Additional 10%+ customers beyond Microsoft and Meta?
**A (Ullal):** "We still expect at least one, maybe two" new 10%+ customers displaying all three use cases. Microsoft and Meta remain core: "have been 10% and greater customers for over a decade, and the partnership could never be stronger."

### Gross Margin Drivers (Matt Nicknam, Truist)
**Q:** What's driving the 170bps YoY margin decline?
**A (Breithaupt):** "Primary driver is mix of customer segments" with secondary impacts from memory and silicon costs. Management absorbing costs: "a strong factor of costs going up and us still eating a lot of the costs and giving our customers the benefit."

### Deferred Revenue Recognition (Tal Leiani, Bank of America)
**Q:** When does the $6.2B deferred balance convert to recognized revenue?
**A (Ullal):** Recognition timeline extended from historical "two to four quarters" to "six to even eight quarters" due to new product qualification and customer facility readiness. "Most of what's sitting in deferred is AI use case and product."

### XPO Timeline (Amit Daryanani, Evercore)
**Q:** XPO from OFC demos to production?
**A (Ullal):** "XPO will be particularly prevalent in scale out and scale across" at 1.6T+ speeds. Co-packaged copper remains viable for short distances within scale-up racks.

### Enterprise Inference (Ben Bolen, Cleveland Research)
**Q:** Enterprise inference opportunity — how big and when?
**A (Ullal):** Inference currently "hundreds and thousands" of units vs. training's "hundreds of thousands." Characterized as "the calm before the storm" with multi-year deployment horizon.

### Campus Revenue (James Fish, Piper Sandler)
**Q:** Campus trajectory given flat $1.25B target?
**A:** Management reiterated the $1.25B target alongside AI growth. Big Switch/VeloCloud integration driving MSP channel motion. Insurance, manufacturing, and service provider verticals showing strong momentum.
