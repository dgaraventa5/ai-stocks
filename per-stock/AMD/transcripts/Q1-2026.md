# AMD Q1 2026 Earnings Call Transcript

**Date:** May 5, 2026, 5:00 PM EDT
**Participants:** Lisa Su (Chair, President & CEO), Jean Hu (EVP, CFO & Treasurer)
**Source:** Motley Fool, Investing.com, AlphaSpread, TickerReport (synthesized from multiple transcript sources)

---

## Prepared Remarks — Lisa Su, Chair, President & CEO

### Opening

"These results mark a clear inflection in our growth trajectory and a structural shift in our business."

"Growth was broad-based with every segment increasing year-over-year."

"Data center is now the primary driver of our revenue and earnings growth."

"This is a clear step-up in our growth trajectory with accelerating momentum across the business."

### Data Center Segment ($5.8B, +57% YoY, record)

- Server CPU revenue increased more than 50% YoY — 4th consecutive quarter of record server CPU revenue
- Both Cloud and Enterprise each grew more than 50% YoY
- EPYC-powered cloud instances nearly 50% increase to more than 1,600 globally
- Record enterprise revenue and sell-through
- New wins in financial services, healthcare, industrial, digital infrastructure
- Data Center AI revenue grew by "a significant double-digit percentage year-over-year"
- Data Center operating income: $1.6B (up from $932M), 28% of segment revenue

Turin (5th-gen EPYC) now represents more than 50% of server CPU revenue — crossed over this quarter.

#### Server CPU TAM Expansion

"Inferencing and Agentic AI are increasing need for server CPU compute."

"Additional CPU processing for orchestration, data movement and parallel execution."

Server CPU TAM revised from prior ~$60B estimate at ~18% CAGR to "over $120 billion by 2030" at "greater than 35% annually." Driven by "fundamental increase in compute requirements driven by Agentic AI."

CPU-to-GPU ratio shifting from "1:4 or 1:8 configuration" to "closer to 1:1 configuration" due to agentic AI orchestration needs. "Even more CPUs than GPUs" in scenarios with "lots and lots of agents."

#### Venice (6th-gen EPYC) and Verano

- Venice: "built on Zen 6 and 2-nanometer process technology"
- Verano: "our first EPYC CPU purpose-built for AI infrastructure"
- Delivers "greater than 2x throughput per socket versus leading Arm-based AI solutions"
- Launch planned for later in 2026
- Q2 server CPU revenue guidance: "more than 70% year-over-year" growth

#### GPU / Instinct AI Accelerators

Instinct adoption expanding across "cloud, enterprise, sovereign, and supercomputing customers." Customers moving from pilots to large-scale production. Deeper long-term engagements and multi-generation deployments.

Inference momentum particularly strong due to memory capacity and bandwidth advantages.

**Meta partnership:** "expanded strategic partnership with Meta to deploy up to 6 gigawatts of AMD Instinct GPUs spanning several product generations." Includes "a custom GPU accelerator based on our MI450 architecture." Helios rack-scale platform integrating Instinct GPUs with EPYC Venice CPUs.

**OpenAI:** "Together with our previously announced OpenAI partnership, these engagements position AMD as a core partner to the world's largest AI infrastructure builders."

**MI450 timeline:** Sampling underway to lead customers. "Lead customer forecasts now exceeding our initial plans." "Growing number of new customers engaging on large-scale deployments." "Additional multi-gigawatt opportunities" identified beyond announced deals. Helios: "initial volume in Q3" with "significant ramp in Q4."

**ROCm software:** "Significantly accelerated our ROCm development cadence through increased software investments." Using "agent-based coding workflows" for "faster performance improvements and more rapid deployment." MI355X delivered "strong competitive performance across the full suite with leadership results in multiple categories" in MLPerf. "Expanded day 0 support for leading open models, including latest Google Gemma 4 family, Qwen, Kimi."

#### Confidence in AI Revenue Trajectory

"Strong and increasing confidence" in delivering "tens of billions of dollars in annual data center AI revenue in 2027."

Path to ">80% CAGR" for data center AI business through 2027.

### Client & Gaming Segment ($3.6B, +23% YoY)

- Client revenue: $2.9B (+26% YoY)
- Gaming revenue: $720M (+11% YoY)
- Ryzen PRO PC sell-through: +50% YoY
- PC vendor expansion: Dell, HP, Lenovo
- Second half gaming revenue projected to "decline more than 20%" due to memory/component costs

### Embedded Segment ($873M, +6% YoY)

- Growth in test/measurement, emulation, aerospace/defense, communications
- Embedded x86 adoption increasing
- Design wins: double-digit percentage YoY increase with "billions of dollars" in new wins
- Semi-custom engagements expanded

### Strategic Outlook

"AMD is uniquely positioned to lead in this next phase of AI."

"We see a clear path to exceed our long-term financial targets, including delivering more than $20 in EPS over the strategic timeframe."

## Analyst Q&A — Selected Questions and Answers

### On ARM / Custom ASIC Competition

**Joshua Buchalter (TD Cowen)** asked about AMD's >50% share target with "x86 competitor improving supply" and "more momentum on merchant and custom ARM CPU side."

**Lisa Su:** "CPUs are critical for so many tasks." Market requires "broad portfolio of CPUs" not interchangeable products. Three categories: general purpose, head nodes for accelerators, Agentic AI workloads. "People are going to use x86 and ARM for many large hyperscalers" — even those developing custom solutions still purchase "lots of CPUs in the merchant market."

**Ross Seymore (Deutsche Bank)** asked how AMD differentiates when ARM signing same customers and x86 competitor has more supply.

**Lisa Su:** "Optimizing very closely with customers" on CPU roadmap. "Depth of our roadmap just expands as we go forward." Portfolio approach addresses different workload optimization needs.

**Timothy Arcuri (UBS)** asked whether low-latency compute (20%+ of market per competitor) represents structural shift.

**Lisa Su:** "Natural evolution" as market optimizes around inference, decode vs. prefill. AMD developing "full compute portfolio" including CPUs, GPUs, and "ability to do customization for certain customers." Semi-custom capabilities position AMD to "address large portion of this market, including low latency portion."

### On Meta/OpenAI Deal Progress

**Joshua Buchalter** asked whether "forecasts exceeding expectations" and "pipeline growing" refers to upsizing announced deals vs. new customers.

**Lisa Su:** "Very excited about MI450 and Helios" with "significant customer interest." "Large partnerships with OpenAI and Meta going really well." "Deep co-engineering" occurring on both deals. Customer forecasts now "above our initial plans...had planned for 2027." "Breadth of customers now very interested in deploying at significant scale MI450 series." Both training and inference — "largest deployments are for inference." "Path to really get to exceed our original targets of >80% CAGR." Multiple customer interest suggests expansion beyond two named partnerships.

### On Margin Trajectory

**Ross Seymore** asked about MI450 ramp impact (below-average margin) offsetting EPYC strength.

**Jean Hu:** Q2 guidance 56% gross margin. Tailwinds from server CPU +70% growth, client mix shift upmarket, gaming revenue decline, embedded strength. "MI450 will start to ramp in Q3 and then ramp significantly in Q4...below corporate average." But "all the positive trends...feel really good about the setup of gross margin for 2026." Long-term target: "55% to 58%."

**On OpEx growth:** "Tremendous market opportunities" justify aggressive R&D investment. "R&D growth will outpace SG&A."

### On Agentic AI and CPU TAM

**Vivek Arya (Bank of America)** asked if Agentic CPU growth is "incremental" or "coming at expense of GPUs" and what percentage of AI TAM was server CPU before vs. now.

**Lisa Su:** "Largely additive to the TAM." "Need all of the accelerators to run foundational models." "As these agents do work, they spawn more CPU tasks." CPU-to-GPU ratio shifting from "1:4 or 1:8" to "closer to 1:1." "Even more CPUs than GPUs" in scenarios with "lots and lots of agents." "Everyone is now planning and thinking about CPUs at the same time that they're thinking about accelerator deployments."

**Joshua Buchalter** asked how TAM doubled from $60B to $120B in a few months despite Agentic AI being known.

**Lisa Su:** "First signs of AI demand really pulling CPU demand last year." Initial update to "18% CAGR or approximately $60 billion." "All of the things that we believed...Agentic AI and inferencing...happening at much faster pace." "Talking to customers...how AI adoption really unfolding." "Significant more CPU demand from really every major cloud provider...enterprise customers." Validated through "bottoms up...talking to customers...longer-term forecasts" plus "workload analysis."

### On Supply Chain

**Lisa Su:** "The supply chain is tight. I would definitely say that. But I also think this is an area where we excel." "Very deep relationships across the supply chain." "Working closely with our supply chain partners to meaningfully increase wafer and back-end capacities." "Confident in our ability to supply to the levels of growth that we're talking about."

Some "cost increases on the memory side." "Larger impact...watching is impact on consumer markets." Expecting "demand impact as result of memory price increases on things like PC business in second half...Gaming business."

"Now talking about '27 CPU demand...talking about '28 CPU demand" — visibility extending further out.

"Deep partnerships with memory providers" and "good visibility to data center power" coming online in 2027.
