## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim)

> What I currently believe about TTMI:
> - TTM Technologies manufactures printed circuit boards (PCBs) -- the boards that all electronic components mount on
> - AI thesis: AI servers and networking switches require high-layer-count, advanced PCBs with tight tolerances. As GPU density and power increase, PCB complexity rises
> - Revenue likely $2-3B range, low-to-mid teens margins typical for PCB manufacturers
> - Competes with AT&S, Unimicron, Compeq, Ibiden (Asian competitors dominate)
> - Market cap ~$4B -- small cap, under-followed
> - Segments likely include aerospace/defense (high-reliability boards), data center/networking, automotive, industrial
> - Defense PCB exposure provides some revenue stability
> - Key question: how much of their revenue is directly tied to AI/data center builds vs. legacy applications

## 4. Implications for scoring dimensions

### Value (Weight: 15%)
- **Likely scores poorly.** At 83-103x trailing P/E, this is deeply expensive relative to historical PCB company valuations. FCF yield is negative. Even on FY2026 projected earnings (~$3.20 non-GAAP EPS annualized from Q2 midpoint), forward P/E is still ~55-60x. PCB manufacturers historically trade at 10-20x earnings.
- Key data needed: Forward P/E on consensus 2026/2027 EPS, EV/EBITDA, PEG ratio.

### Quality (Weight: 20%)
- **Mixed.** Gross margins improving (21.4%) and operating margins expanding (12.8% non-GAAP) are positive. But FCF is negative due to capex, and the company carries $912M in debt. ROIC likely moderate given the capital intensity. Segment margins (15-16%) are decent for a PCB manufacturer but not exceptional.
- Key data needed: ROIC, debt/EBITDA, interest coverage, consistency of margin improvement.

### Growth (Weight: 15%)
- **Likely scores well.** 30% YoY revenue growth in Q1, 38% implied for full year. Data center segment growing 61%. Guidance raised substantially. This is an inflection story.
- Key data needed: 3-year revenue CAGR, earnings growth rate, sustainability of AI-driven demand.

### AI Thesis (Weight: 30%)
- **Strong but needs nuance.** Data Center & Networking at 36% of revenue and growing 61% positions TTM as a clear AI supply chain beneficiary. They manufacture the high-layer-count PCBs that go into AI servers and networking switches. However, PCBs are a commodity-adjacent product -- the moat is thinner than silicon or optical components. The question is whether TTM has pricing power or just volume uplift.
- Key data needed: Customer concentration (hyperscaler exposure), competitive differentiation vs. Asian PCB makers, pricing trends, technology barriers (ultra-HDI, high-layer-count).

### Momentum (Weight: 10%)
- **Likely scores very well.** Stock up ~600%, beat-and-raise quarter, analyst upgrades, Investor Day catalyst on May 27. The momentum is strong.
- Key data needed: Price vs. 200-day MA, recent analyst revisions, short interest.

### Risk (Weight: 10%)
- **Several flags:**
  - Extreme valuation (83-103x P/E for a PCB manufacturer)
  - Negative free cash flow in a capex-heavy investment cycle
  - Heavy insider selling ($10.4M net in Q1, zero buys)
  - Customer concentration risk (hyperscaler dependency)
  - China expansion capex ($200-300M) introduces geopolitical risk
  - Cyclicality of PCB demand -- if AI capex slows, this is exposed
  - Competition from Asian manufacturers with lower cost structures
- Key data needed: Customer concentration %, geographic revenue split, China facility specifics, balance sheet stress test.
