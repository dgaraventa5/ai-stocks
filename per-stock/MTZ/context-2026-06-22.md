## 1. Pre-research mental model

MasTec is a diversified North American infrastructure EPC contractor across four segments — Communications, Clean Energy & Infrastructure, Power Delivery (utility T&D), and Pipeline Infrastructure — that levered up materially via the 2021–22 IEA and Henkels & McCoy acquisitions and spent 2023–24 working that debt down while clean-energy/pipeline margins normalized. AI relevance is indirect but real: AI/data-center load is the demand driver behind a multi-year utility grid (transmission/distribution) buildout that flows into Power Delivery, plus direct data-center site/civil/power/fiber work and long-haul/metro fiber connectivity. My prior assumption was a recovering, mid-single-digit-margin cyclical with a large backlog, deleveraging toward ~2x, trading on a normalizing-EBITDA multiple — solid grid/data-center tailwind but execution- and cycle-sensitive, not a high-conviction compounder.

## 4. Earnings transcript highlights

Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-01 (Motley Fool / Seeking Alpha / Investing.com). CEO Jose Mas, CFO Paul DiMarco.

- **Data center:** "Connecting data centers, both long haul and metro, is becoming a major driver of spend, and we think that creates a multiyear opportunity measured in the tens of billions of dollars." Mas: data-center construction "is a cycle that is just starting" (first award announced 2024); a "recent turnkey data center award is progressing very well." Cross-segment capability (civil, power, telecom, maintenance) lets them "exponentially grow this part of our business."
- **Power Delivery:** revenue +16%, EBITDA +40%; book-to-bill **1.6x**, backlog record **$6.2B** (+$600M QoQ); FY rev guide raised to ~$4.8B (+14%). Driven by transmission, system-hardening, reliability spend.
- **Clean Energy & Infra:** revenue +45%, EBITDA +56%; backlog record **$7.3B**, book-to-bill 1.6x (1.3x organic); renewables revenue +60%, +70 bps margin; FY rev guide ~$6.7B.
- **Capital allocation / M&A:** net leverage **1.8x**, liquidity $1.8B, ROIC >10% (+~100 bps). Mas: "You are going to see us be a lot more active in M&A than we have been in the last couple years... that is where you will see deployment of capital." Capex raised to ~$220M (historically low intensity).
- **Cautions/risks:** Pipeline backlog "not fully representative... only includes signed contracts" (lumpy). DSO rose to 72 days (from 65) — drag on Q1 cash; expected back to mid-60s. DIRECTV fulfillment exit = ~100 bps Communications headwind, not expected to recur. **Renewables tax-equity uncertainty** — pause by major banks acknowledged; "we feel good about our portfolio" but sector needs policy fixes.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — no rating numbers pre-decided)

- **D1 / D2 (AI demand directness & magnitude):** Revisit upward-leaning. Evidence strengthened materially — explicit turnkey data-center award in progress, "tens of billions" connectivity TAM, Power Delivery record backlog tied to AI grid load. Still *indirect* (EPC contractor, not a chip/power-equipment OEM) and AI-attributable revenue is not separately disclosed — flag the disclosure gap; do not over-credit on narrative.
- **D5 (moat/durability within AI thesis):** Revisit. Cross-segment turnkey capability (civil + power + telecom + maintenance) is the differentiation management is leaning on; weigh against the commoditized-EPC reality and customer concentration in utilities.
- **M (Momentum):** Revisit. Big beat-and-raise, record backlog, EPS revisions almost certainly upward — EPS-revisions and relative-strength sub-ratings likely improved. Refresh objective 50DMA % via `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` before any rating.
- **R1–R4 (Risk):** Balance sheet leg improved (net leverage ~1.8x, IG parameters, liquidity $1.8B) — R3 leg looks better than the IEA/Henkels-era prior. Watch: (a) **renewables tax-equity / policy uncertainty** (CE&I = ~38% of revenue) — a real regulatory/demand risk; (b) Pipeline lumpiness; (c) DSO uptick (working-capital, transient). Customer concentration in utilities/hyperscaler-adjacent — check R1.
- **R5 (Disruption Risk):** Defaults to **5** — physical infrastructure construction, no credible AI/structural disruption to the core revenue model (rule 16). No change expected.
- **Rule-14 expectations angle:** Multiple is likely elevated after a beat-and-raise into a consensus grid/AI theme. Before scoring, run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py MTZ` to check P/S percentile vs revenue-growth trend — but note growth is *accelerating* here (+34.5% rev), which is the opposite of the peak-multiple/decelerating-growth pattern the flag targets, so a clean flag is less likely. Value bands are absolute; let them fall where they fall (no nudging).
- **Earnings-refresh note (rule 9):** MTZ reported Q1 2026 on 2026-04-30 with revenue beat >10% and Adj EBITDA +73% — objective inputs (margins, growth, FCF) in the Watchlist should be refreshed against these figures in a scoring pass. TTM will *understate* the current run-rate given the sharp QoQ inflection (rule-9 TTM caveat applies).

**THESIS-BREAK signals: NONE.** No backlog deceleration (record +28%), no cancellation, no guidance cut (raised), no margin compression (expanding), no leverage/covenant issue (deleveraged, IG). Watch-items only: renewables tax-equity policy risk, Pipeline lumpiness, DSO normalization.
