## 1. Pre-research mental model

TEL is a Layer-07 connector & sensor maker whose center of gravity is **auto/transportation** (cyclical), with a fast-growing but **minority** AI-datacom business inside Industrial Solutions (Digital Data Networks / DDN — high-speed copper + optical connectors and cabling for AI servers/datacenters). The thesis is that DDN AI growth is real and accelerating off hyperscaler capex, but it does not yet dominate the revenue base, so directness to the AI buildout is moderate, and the larger auto book brings ordinary semi/auto cyclicality. Going in I expected solid-but-cyclical fundamentals with AI as an upside kicker rather than the core driver. Key unknowns: how big DDN AI is as a share of total, whether auto is softening, and whether datacom orders are still accelerating.

## 4. Earnings transcript highlights

Full transcripts available (Motley Fool, Benzinga, Insider Monkey, AOL/Yahoo — all 2026-04-22); not saved as a separate markdown file in this pass (briefing-only). Synthesized points:

- **DDN / AI is the headline:** DDN AI revenue approaching ~$2.4B for FY26; orders up >60%; backlog extends into 2027; hyperscalers giving better forward visibility and building buffer stock — i.e., demand is being pulled forward, not just sustained. AI raise concentrated in H2.
- **Optical strategy:** RampPhotonics acquisition + 6%-of-sales capex aimed at moving TEL from primarily high-speed copper toward copper **and** optical interconnect — the durability question for the AI thesis (copper-to-optical transition could be a risk or an opportunity depending on TEL's optical position).
- **Auto is the drag, not the story:** Automotive −4% organic; management leaning on content-per-vehicle and next-gen-vehicle architecture rather than unit volume. Auto cyclicality is the live risk on the larger half of the business.
- **Orders >> sales:** $5.3B orders vs $4.74B sales (book-to-bill ~1.1) signals accelerating second-half sales — a positive momentum signal but also a high-expectations setup.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not pre-decide)

Ratings stay in the human loop (rule 12). Flags for the collaborative session:

- **D1 (Directness of AI exposure):** Keep honest. **AI datacom (DDN AI ~$2.4B FY26) is only ~12–13% of revenue; auto/transportation (~51%) is the largest exposure.** TEL is a *partial/indirect* AI play — a real beneficiary but not a pure-play. D1 should reflect minority direct exposure, not be inflated by the loud DDN growth narrative.
- **D2 (Growth attributable to AI):** Strongly positive — DDN +48% YoY, orders +60%, two consecutive AI guidance raises (~$200M cumulative), backlog into 2027. The incremental growth IS largely AI-driven even though the base is auto.
- **D3/D4 (size/durability of demand):** Hyperscaler visibility improving and buffer-stock build is a positive; but watch copper→optical transition risk (RampPhotonics buy is the hedge). Demand pull-forward + buffer stocking can reverse.
- **D5 (moat / competitive position):** Connector duopoly dynamics vs. Amphenol (APH); RampPhotonics adds optical capability. No change flagged, but verify against APH datacom commentary.
- **Momentum (M):** Positive — record orders +25%, book-to-bill ~1.1, beat-and-raise, margin expansion. Refresh objective **50DMA %** via `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` at scoring time (rule 11).
- **Risk R1 (customer concentration):** AI/DDN growth concentrates exposure to a handful of hyperscalers; auto side is diversified. Mixed.
- **Risk — auto cyclicality:** Auto (37% of revenue) at −4% organic is the principal cyclical risk; a deeper auto/EV downturn would hit the larger half of the book regardless of AI. Flag for R-dimension discussion.
- **Risk R5 (Disruption):** **Defaults to 5** — TEL is non-Layer-10 physical interconnect hardware; no agentic-AI disruption to the core revenue model (rule 16). The relevant tech risk is copper→optical substitution, which is a product-mix/competitive risk, not an R5 business-model-disruption risk.
- **Rule-14 expectations flag:** Stock dipped on a clean beat-and-raise → high expectations likely priced in. Run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py TEL` at scoring time and treat any P/S-percentile + decelerating-growth hit as a qualitative caution, NOT a scored metric.
- **Rule-15 (EPS YoY blank):** Q2 FY25 GAAP EPS was $0.04 (depressed base, large prior-year deferred-tax item), making Q2 GAAP EPS YoY (+$2.86) a garbage input dominated by base distortion / non-operating items. **Recommend blanking GAAP EPS YoY** for TEL this pass and letting Growth average the revenue metrics; cite the prior-year tax/base distortion in the Rating Audit if applied. Adjusted EPS (+24%) is the clean operational figure.
- **Inorganic-growth caveat:** Reported +15% overshoots organic +7%; Richards Manufacturing adds inorganic Energy/total revenue. Use **organic** growth where the rubric intends underlying demand.

**THESIS-BREAK watch (none triggered this pass):** auto-demand softening — *present but mild* (−4% organic, not a collapse); datacom order decel — *opposite* (orders +60%, backlog into 2027); guidance cut — *opposite* (raised); margin compression — *opposite* (expanding). No thesis break. The one real watch item is **demand pull-forward / hyperscaler buffer-stocking reversing** and the **copper→optical transition** eroding TEL's high-speed-copper position faster than its optical capability (RampPhotonics) ramps.
