# XEL — Pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-10)

Prepared for AI Thesis re-rating of Layer 1 power names. Session lens: how DIRECTLY does XEL own the AI power bottleneck (disclosed datacenter load share, rate structures capturing growth, signed ESAs) vs. a generic "load growth" narrative — and how much upside actually flows through given allowed-ROE caps.

## Pre-research mental model

XEL is a fully regulated multi-state utility (NSP-Minnesota, PSCo Colorado, SPS Texas/New Mexico, NSP-Wisconsin) with a wind-heavy generation fleet and a datacenter story concentrated in Minnesota and Colorado plus oil-and-gas-driven load in the SPS Permian territory. My working picture, last refreshed at the 2026-05-18 scoring pass (Watchlist row: AI Rev % = 3, Hyperscaler = 4, Capacity = 4), was a "data-center geographic exposure" thesis: large interconnection queue and a Google Minnesota agreement announced early 2026, but no precise contracted-GW framework in my head and no quantified revenue contribution. Most recent quarter I had internalized was Q4 2025; I had NOT processed the Q1 2026 call (Apr 30) with its 6 GW-by-2027 contracting target and the GE Vernova / NextEra alliances. The local thesis.md is an unfilled template — this is effectively first real research on the name.

## Findings (sources)

1. **Q1 2026 results (reported 2026-04-30):** GAAP EPS $0.89 vs $0.84; ongoing EPS $0.91 vs $0.84 (+8.3%); net income $556M; total operating revenues $4,021M vs $3,906M (+2.9%); electric revenues $2,976M vs $2,835M. Driven by recovery of electric infrastructure investment + sales growth, despite warm weather. 2026 ongoing EPS guidance reaffirmed $4.04–$4.16. (Q1 2026 earnings release 8-K, SEC EDGAR accession 0000072903-26-000070, filed Apr 2026; Motley Fool Q1 2026 transcript, 2026-04-30.)

2. **Google ESA — the signed, named hyperscaler deal:** In Q1 2026 NSP-Minnesota entered an electric service agreement to power a new Google data center at Pine Island, MN (announced 2026-02-24). Google pays ALL costs of its new service for the duration of the agreement. Filed with MPUC April 2026 including a proposed "Clean Energy Accelerator Charge" for 1,900 MW of clean resources (1,400 MW wind / 200 MW solar / 300 MW 100-hour iron-air storage). Expected ~$1.1B of benefits to NSP-MN customers per the 8-K; management said "$1B–$1.5B over the term" on the call; 15-year term per call commentary. **Campus MW not disclosed in primary sources** — Utility Dive says "nearly 1 GW," secondary summaries say 750 MW; flagged below. (8-K Q1 2026 "Large Load Agreement" section; Xcel newsroom 2026-02-24; Motley Fool transcript 2026-04-30; Utility Dive 2026-05.)

3. **Contracted GW and pipeline:** >2 GW of data center capacity contracted/under construction today; ~4 GW high-probability pipeline; 20+ GW of additional prospects. Targets: 3 GW contracted by end-2026, 6 GW by end-2027 (doubled from the prior ~3 GW two-year goal set Oct 2025). Frenzel (Q1 call): "we expect to execute on enough ESAs this year to get to a 3 gigawatt target, and another 3 gigawatts next year." One data center operating today; three more energizations planned in 2026. (POWER Magazine 2026-02; TD World; Motley Fool Q1 2026 transcript. Note: one transcript extraction read "~1 GW contracted" — company IR materials consistently say >2 GW contracted/under construction; see FLAGS.)

4. **Generation buildout economics:** Each incremental GW of data center load drives $6–8B of incremental capex (Van Abel, Q1 call); the 3 GW of 2027-target data centers "will drive another 6 to 10 gigawatts of generation." Five-year capital plan raised 33% to $60B (2026 plan $14B, largest in history), funded ~$30.2B cash from ops, $22.8B debt, $7B equity (over half of equity need already addressed at Q1-end). Long-term EPS growth 6–8%+, with ~9% average EPS growth expected through 2030. (Motley Fool Q1 2026 transcript 2026-04-30; Investing.com Q1 slides summary; POWER Magazine.)

5. **Supply-side derisking + tariff template:** Feb 3, 2026 GE Vernova strategic alliance — 5 incremental F-class gas turbines (24 total CTs on order across vendors), multi-GW wind turbine reservations, synchronous condensers; ~20 GW of renewable/storage equipment safe-harbored. Feb 4, 2026 NextEra JDA/MOU — co-developing generation for 2 GW of data center capacity initially, expandable, mix of company-owned + PPA. Large-load tariffs: Colorado filed April 2026; Texas, New Mexico, Wisconsin filings planned; template = long-term contracts, minimum bills, termination fees, credit requirements, incremental cost tests. (POWER Magazine 2026-02; Utility Dive 2026-05 "Google deal sets template"; Q1 call.)

6. **Current sales mix signal (D1-relevant):** Q1 2026 weather-normalized retail electric sales +2.8% YoY; C&I +4.3%. But the C&I growth is driven by SPS +10.8% — attributed in the 8-K to "increased activity in the energy sector in SPS" (Permian oil & gas electrification), NOT data centers. NSP-MN C&I +2.2%, PSCo C&I +0.3%. (Q1 2026 earnings release, Sales section.)

## DIFF (vs. 2026-05-18 watchlist row / prior mental model)

- **NEW since my model:** The 6-GW-by-2027 contracting framework, the $6–8B capex per DC gigawatt rule of thumb, the 6–10 GW generation trigger, the GE Vernova and NextEra alliances, and the $60B (+33%) capital plan were all absent from my mental model. The story is materially more structured and quantified than "geographic exposure."
- **CONFIRMED:** Google is the only named hyperscaler; everything else is anonymized "hyperscalers/developers." My prior Hyperscaler rating of 4 was based on thinner evidence but directionally lands fine.
- **CORRECTED:** I had vaguely attributed XEL's load growth to data centers; Q1 actuals show current C&I growth is mostly SPS oil-and-gas, with data center load still ~prospective (one DC operating). The AI-tied share of TODAY's revenue is smaller than the narrative implies.
- **Watchlist staleness:** Row last updated 2026-05-18, after Q1 results (4/30), so objective inputs may already reflect Q1 — but FCF Yield -15.36% / FCF Mgn -50.54% reflect the capex supercycle and will worsen with the $60B plan; not stale, just structurally negative.

## Evidence for D1 (% of revenue datacenter-tied TODAY)

- **No disclosed figure.** Neither the Q1 2026 8-K nor the call quantifies data center revenue or sales share. (Checked: 8-K Sales and Electric Revenues sections; transcript.)
- Only ONE data center is operating in territory today; three more planned to energize in 2026 (POWER Magazine 2026-02, citing company materials). Google Pine Island is pre-MPUC-approval, not yet serving load.
- Q1 2026 weather-normalized C&I +4.3% is driven by SPS energy-sector (oil & gas) demand, not data centers (Q1 8-K Sales section). 
- **Inference, flagged as estimate:** datacenter-tied revenue today is very low single digits at most — the contracted 2+ GW is mostly under construction with sales ramp expected 2029–2030 into the 2030s (POWER Magazine). The watchlist AI Rev % of 3 looks defensible-to-generous for TODAY; the exposure is overwhelmingly forward-dated.

## Evidence for D2 (regulated return capture vs pricing power)

- 100% regulated. Upside flows through RATE BASE GROWTH at allowed ROE, not power prices: $60B 5-yr plan → 6–8%+ long-term EPS growth, ~9% average through 2030 (Q1 call, Van Abel). No merchant capture of scarcity pricing — contrast with VST/CEG/TLN.
- The Google structure deliberately CAPS upside: Google pays all incremental costs, and the deal is marketed on $1.1B of benefits flowing to OTHER customers (8-K Large Load Agreement section), not to shareholders. Shareholder benefit = the regulated return on the 1,900 MW CEAC resources + transmission Xcel gets to build.
- Tariff template (CO filed April 2026; TX/NM/WI coming): minimum bills, termination fees, credit requirements, incremental cost tests — these are downside-protection devices for existing ratepayers, not upside-capture devices for shareholders (Utility Dive 2026-05).
- Financing drag: ~40% of incremental capex equity-funded (Q1 call); $7B equity over 5 years dilutes the per-share capture of rate base growth. Pending rate cases seeking $933M of increases across four states (Utility Dive) are the actual earnings transmission mechanism — subject to regulatory lag and allowed-ROE outcomes.
- Net: structurally INDIRECT economics. Datacenter load is a volume/rate-base accelerant inside a capped-return wrapper.

## Evidence for D5 (named hyperscaler commitments, contracted GW, signed agreements)

- **Named + signed:** Google ESA, NSP-Minnesota, Pine Island MN, 15-year term, signed Q1 2026, filed with MPUC April 2026, pending approval. Google funds all incremental service costs; 1,900 MW of CEAC resources attached. (Q1 2026 8-K; Xcel newsroom 2026-02-24.)
- **Contracted but unnamed:** >2 GW total contracted/under construction (so ≥1 GW beyond Google is with unnamed counterparties). (POWER Magazine 2026-02; Q1 materials.)
- **Targets, not contracts:** 3 GW by end-2026, 6 GW by end-2027, ~4 GW high-probability pipeline, 20+ GW prospects — management forecast, not signed. Guidance "assumes successful contracting of 6 GW by year-end 2027" — i.e., the capital plan embeds an uncontracted assumption.
- **Partner commitments (supply side, not demand side):** GE Vernova alliance (5 incremental gas turbines, multi-GW wind reservations, 2026-02-03); NextEra JDA for 2 GW of DC generation co-development (2026-02-04, formal agreement pending). These derisk execution but are not customer commitments.

## FLAGS

Do not cite a campus MW without the MPUC docket (ESA filing, April 2026) — worth pulling if D5 scoring hinges on it.
2. **Contracted-GW figure inconsistency:** one transcript extraction suggested "~1 GW contracted (Google + one other)" while company IR/trade press consistently say ">2 GW contracted and under construction." Treat >2 GW (company slide figure) as primary; the ~1 GW reading may distinguish "contracted" vs "under construction/commission approval."
3. **D1 is an inference, not a disclosure.** No company quantification of datacenter % of revenue or sales exists. Sales ramp is 2029-2030+.
4. **Customer-savings figure varies:** 8-K says ~$1.1B of customer benefits; call says $1.0–1.5B over 15 years. Cite the 8-K figure as primary.
5. **$933M rate-case figure** is from Utility Dive's summary, not verified against the individual dockets.
