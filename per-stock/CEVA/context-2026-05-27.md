## 1. Business Overview

CEVA Inc is a pure-play semiconductor IP licensor headquartered in Rockville, Maryland. The company designs and licenses processor cores, platform IP, and software -- it does not manufacture chips. Customers (semiconductor companies and OEMs) integrate CEVA's IP blocks into their own system-on-chip (SoC) designs and pay licensing fees upfront plus per-unit royalties on shipped devices.

**Core IP portfolio:**

- **DSP Cores:** Historical foundation of the business. CEVA claimed ~90% DSP IP market share as of 2011 ([chipestimate.com](https://www.chipestimate.com/CEVA-Continues-to-Dominate-DSP-IP-Market-with-90-Market-Share/Tensilica/news/17161)). Still a significant part of the platform.
- **AI/ML Processors:**
  - **NeuPro NPU family:** Scalable neural processing units from tens of GOPS to hundreds of TOPS. NeuPro-Nano (ultra-low-power edge devices), NeuPro-S (mid-range), NeuPro-M (high-performance). NeuPro-Nano won the Artificial Intelligence category at embedded world 2026 ([PR Newswire, 2026-03-12](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/cevas-neupro-nano-npu-wins-artificial-intelligence-award-at-embedded-world-2026-302711611.html)).
  - **SensPro AI DSP:** Optimized for sensor processing, AI inference, and control algorithms in automotive (camera, LiDAR, radar). Deployed in the Renesas R-Car V4H platform (2026 Toyota RAV4 production).
- **Wireless Connectivity:**
  - **Bluetooth:** ~35% worldwide Bluetooth IoT market share (2023), ~45% TWS earbuds share ex-Apple. Bluetooth HDT (High Data Throughput) full-stack solution launched.
  - **Wi-Fi:** Wi-Fi 6 and Wi-Fi 7 IP. 40+ licensees for Wi-Fi 6; targeting 25-30% Wi-Fi IoT market share. Record 91M Wi-Fi unit shipments in Q1 2026.
  - **UWB:** Next-generation IEEE 802.15.4ab-compliant UWB IP launched March 2026 with 30x extended ranging and 4x faster data rates ([PR Newswire, 2026-03](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ceva-launches-next-generation-uwb-ip-with-extended-range-and-higher-throughput-302708842.html)).
  - **5G:** PentaG modem platform. PentaG-NTN launched March 2026 for satellite (LEO/MEO) user terminals, supporting 3GPP Release-18, FR1/FR2, Ka/Ku bands ([PR Newswire, 2026-03-03](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ceva-launches-pentag-ntn-5g-advanced-modem-ip-enabling-satellite-native-innovators-to-rapidly-deploy-differentiated-leo-user-terminals-302701324.html)).
- **Multi-protocol platforms:** Ceva-Waves Links integrates Wi-Fi + Bluetooth + 802.15.4 + UWB in turnkey platforms for SoC designers.

**Revenue model:** Two streams:
1. **Licensing & related revenue** -- upfront fees for IP licenses, NRE, and support/maintenance.
2. **Royalty revenue** -- per-unit royalties on shipped devices containing CEVA IP.

**Scale:** 20+ billion cumulative device shipments. 400+ customers worldwide. 458M devices shipped in Q1 2026 alone.

## 5. AI Revenue and Growth Trajectory

**AI is the key growth vector for CEVA and the primary reason to consider it for the AI supply chain watchlist.**

- **2025 was the "breakthrough year" for AI licensing:** 10 NeuPro NPU agreements signed across consumer IoT, industrial, automotive, infrastructure, and PC applications ([PR Newswire, 2026-01](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ceva-highlights-breakthrough-year-for-ai-licensing-and-physical-ai-adoption-in-2025-302688806.html)).
- **AI > 20% of licensing revenue** in Q1 2026 and for FY 2025. In Q2-Q3 2025, AI reached ~33% of licensing revenue.
- **Key AI wins:**
  - **Renesas R-Car V4H:** SensPro AI DSP deployed; entering production in 2026 Toyota RAV4 (first mass-volume automotive AI deployment for CEVA). Management called this "the beginning of a meaningful long-term royalty stream."
  - **NXP:** AI DSP collaboration for software-defined vehicle processors.
  - **Microchip:** Portfolio license for full NeuPro NPU family (one of world's leading MCU providers).
  - **Leading PC OEM (undisclosed):** Strategic NPU license for personal computing (Q4 2025).
  - **BOS Semiconductors:** Licensed SensPro AI DSP for Eagle-A standalone ADAS SoC.
- **Production timeline:** Multiple AI customers expected to have silicon by end of 2026, increasing royalty visibility for 2027+.
- **NPU range:** Scalable from tens of GOPS (NeuPro-Nano for wearables/sensors) to hundreds of TOPS (NeuPro for automotive/infrastructure).

**Important caveat:** AI licensing revenue is currently contributing to the licensing line (upfront fees). The royalty payoff from AI deals has a 2-4 year lag as customers design chips, tape out, and ramp production. The 2026 Toyota RAV4 deployment is the first real AI royalty stream.

## 8. Competitive Positioning

### Market Context
The semiconductor IP market was valued at $47.9B in 2024, projected to reach $75.2B by 2032 (CAGR 6.4%). Major players: Arm Holdings, Synopsys, Cadence Design Systems, CEVA, Imagination Technologies ([GlobeNewsWire, 2026-03](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/03/02/3247502/28124/en/Semiconductor-Intellectual-Property-Research-Report-2026-2035-A-13-54-Billion-Market-by-2030-with-Arm-Synopsys-Cadence-Design-Systems-CEVA-Imagination-Technologies-Leading.html)).

### CEVA vs. Key Competitors

| Dimension | CEVA | Arm | Synopsys/Cadence IP |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Revenue** | ~$110M/yr | ~$3.5B/yr | IP divisions ~$1-2B each |
| **Focus** | Wireless connectivity + edge AI + DSP | General-purpose CPU/GPU cores | Broad IP portfolio (interface, memory, analog, processor) |
| **AI positioning** | Edge AI NPUs (GOPS to TOPS), sensor fusion | Ethos NPU family, broader compute ecosystem | DesignWare AI processors, ARC NPU |
| **Wireless moat** | Deep: Bluetooth (35% IoT share), Wi-Fi, 5G, UWB all in-house | Limited wireless IP | Some interface IP, not wireless-focused |
| **Business model** | License + royalty (pure IP) | License + royalty (pure IP) | Part of broader EDA/IP businesses |
| **Advantage** | Full-stack wireless + AI integration for edge devices; reduces customer development risk | Ecosystem dominance, massive installed base | Bundled with EDA tools, design flow integration |
| **Vulnerability** | Tiny relative to competitors; limited pricing power | Increasingly competing in edge AI | Could bundle wireless IP into EDA deals |

**CEVA's differentiation:** The intersection of wireless connectivity and edge AI. A customer designing a smart sensor SoC that needs Bluetooth + Wi-Fi + an NPU can license the entire stack from CEVA instead of assembling IP from 3-4 vendors. This "value per design" strategy is the core thesis. Management emphasized the strategic shift from customers doing internal development toward licensing "proven platforms" to reduce development risk.

**CEVA's vulnerability:** Arm is the 800-pound gorilla. If Arm aggressively bundles edge AI NPU IP with its dominant CPU cores, CEVA could lose deals. Synopsys and Cadence can bundle IP with their EDA tools. CEVA has no EDA or CPU ecosystem to leverage.

## 10. Key Risks

### 1. Royalty Model Lag and Unpredictability
CEVA's licensing revenue comes first; royalty revenue follows 2-4 years later when customers ship products. The AI licensing surge (2024-2026) will not produce meaningful AI royalties until 2027-2029. If customer chip programs fail, get delayed, or pivot away from CEVA IP, the licensing revenue will never convert to royalties. Royalty revenue was essentially flat YoY in Q1 2026 despite strong licensing growth.

### 2. Customer Concentration
Top 3 customers = 48% of Q1 2026 revenue. Loss of any major licensee (especially if Customer A at 24% is a single large wireless chipmaker) would be material.

### 3. Competition from Arm
Arm is the dominant CPU IP licensor and is expanding into edge AI (Ethos NPU). If Arm bundles NPU + CPU + interconnect at attractive pricing, CEVA's standalone NPU wins could dry up. Arm's ecosystem (toolchains, software libraries, developer community) is orders of magnitude larger.

### 4. SBC-Driven Profitability Illusion
CEVA is GAAP-unprofitable. The $21M gap between GAAP operating loss and non-GAAP operating income is almost entirely SBC. Non-GAAP EPS of $0.42 (FY 2025) turns into GAAP EPS of $(0.44). Share count dilution is the real cost shareholders bear.

### 5. Small Scale in a Consolidating Market
At ~$110M revenue, CEVA is tiny vs. Arm ($3.5B+), Synopsys ($6B+), or Cadence ($4B+). The semiconductor IP market is consolidating (Synopsys acquired Ansys; CEVA acquired Intrinsix). CEVA could be squeezed on pricing and R&D investment.

### 6. Mobile Royalty Secular Decline
Smartphone volumes are mature/declining. CEVA's historical royalty base is heavily mobile (baseband, Bluetooth, Wi-Fi in phones). The company is pivoting to IoT/automotive/industrial, but mobile weakness has pressured royalty growth.

### 7. China Exposure
Several CEVA licensees (UNISOC, ASR Microelectronics, Goodix, etc.) are Chinese semiconductor companies. Trade restrictions, entity list additions, or geopolitical tensions could impair licensing and royalty revenue from China.

### 8. Minimal Free Cash Flow
FCF is near zero. The company is not self-funding growth -- it relies on its $215M cash/securities balance (largely from historical operations and potentially equity issuance). If SBC continues at 20% of revenue and revenue growth stays in single digits, the real economics are marginal.

## 11. Bull Case / Bear Case Summary

### Bull Case
- AI edge NPU IP is an emerging category with rapid adoption (10 deals in 2025, 20%+ of licensing). As customers tape out and ramp production (2027+), royalty revenue could inflect sharply.
- Wireless connectivity moat (Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, UWB) provides a durable base and a differentiated "full-stack" offering when combined with AI.
- Massive liquid asset base (~$216M vs. $1.07B market cap) provides downside protection and optionality for M&A.
- 2026 guidance implies non-GAAP EPS of $0.53, up ~26% YoY -- operating leverage is starting to show.
- Automotive AI deployment (Toyota RAV4) validates the technology for a multi-year royalty stream.

### Bear Case
- GAAP unprofitable with heavy SBC. Non-GAAP profitability masks real dilution.
- Revenue has barely grown (2% in FY 2025, guided 8-12% in FY 2026). For a ~$1B market cap stock, this is modest.
- Royalty model has a long payback period and low visibility. Licensing deals may not convert.
- Tiny player in a market dominated by Arm, Synopsys, and Cadence. No CPU ecosystem, no EDA bundling advantage.
- Customer concentration risk: one customer leaving would be a 10-25% revenue hit.
- China exposure introduces geopolitical risk.
