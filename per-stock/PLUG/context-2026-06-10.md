## Pre-research mental model

PLUG is a hydrogen fuel cell company whose core business has historically been material handling (GenDrive forklift fuel cells for Amazon/Walmart warehouses), plus electrolyzers (GenEco) and a green hydrogen production buildout (Georgia, Louisiana). Long history of heavy cash burn, repeated dilution, and a going-concern warning first disclosed in late 2023; took a ~$1.66B DOE loan guarantee for hydrogen plants. Datacenter relevance was assumed to be mostly narrative — stationary fuel cell backup power (GenSure HP) for datacenters has been marketed but no material named datacenter customer revenue known. Most recent quarter known with confidence: ~Q3 2025, revenue ~$150-200M/quarter, deeply negative gross margins improving slowly.

## DIFF (what changed vs. mental model)

- **Better than modeled:** going-concern disclosure is GONE from the Q1 2026 10-Q; gross margin (13%) vs my assumed deeply negative; EBITDAS-positive target Q4 2026 with cash burn tracking better than plan; electrolyzer revenue 4.4x YoY ($9.2M → $40.9M).
- **New facts:** CEO change (Crespo, Mar 2026); DOE loan program activities suspended (Nov 2025); $275M asset monetization program with Stream deal as step 1.
- **Crucially, the datacenter angle inverted:** PLUG's main 2026 "datacenter" event is selling its own hydrogen-plant site and grid/substation assets TO a datacenter developer for liquidity. PLUG is on the *supply-land-and-interconnect* side of the AI power bottleneck as a seller of assets, not a seller of power solutions. The fuel-cell-to-datacenter business remains a non-binding LOI plus a 2020-era product line and old demos.
- **Unchanged:** zero datacenter revenue; material handling + electrolyzers + fuel are the business; heavy dilution overhang (431.6M potentially dilutive shares); net loss still ~$246M/quarter GAAP.

- Supports D2 = 1-2.

### D5 — named datacenter/hyperscaler customers
- Stream Data Centers: named counterparty, disclosed terms ($132.5-142M, PSA 2026-02-24) — but as an asset BUYER, not a power-solutions customer. The power-solutions piece with the (same? unnamed) developer is a non-binding LOI to "explore."

- Supports D5 = 2 at most (named counterparty exists but the relationship is asset divestiture + exploratory LOI, not power supply).
