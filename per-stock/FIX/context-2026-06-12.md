## 5. DIFF since training (mental model vs current data)

1. **Tech concentration far higher than modeled:** I said "roughly 33-40% of revenue." Actual: **56.4% of Q1 2026 revenue** (10-Q customer table), up from 37.0% a year ago. The watchlist's "DC pure-play" framing is now literally majority-of-revenue true — both the upside lever and the concentration risk are bigger than the mental model.
2. **Backlog ~40% above modeled level:** I said "records around $8-9B." Actual $12.45B (+80.8% YoY), with the single largest YoY bookings driver being the Texas modular operation (+$1.50B) (10-Q backlog section).
3. **Margin regime shift, not drift:** gross margin 26.3% (22.0% prior year), adjusted EBITDA margin 18.3% (13.3%). Even ex-closeouts (~25.2% core), this is structurally above anything in my training-era model of a ~19-20% gross margin contractor. Driven by capacity scarcity + modular mix + pricing leverage (multi-year commitments as a condition of capacity).
4. **Modular expansion is concrete and large:** ~3M → 4M sq ft by end-2026 (+30%), $103.0M Q1 building purchase, FY capex ~5% of revenue vs historical ~1.5-2% (FY2025 capex $154.9M on ~$10.1B revenue ≈ 1.5%, yfinance). My model had modular as a capability, not an active capacity race.
5. **Growth now meaningfully M&A-supplemented:** Feyen Zylstra + Meisner (electrical, 10/1/25) and Right Way (5/1/25) added 5.0pts of Q1 growth and pushed Electrical to 28.1% of revenue. Training model underweighted the electrical segment's scale (+87.5% YoY).
6. **Shareholder returns ahead of model:** dividend doubled YoY ($0.40 → $0.80 quarterly across four raises incl. +14.3% in April); buyback reportedly completed its long-running authorization ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/why-comfort-systems-usa-fix-044013248.html) — not verified in filings this pass).
7. **What did NOT change:** seasonality (Q1 is the seasonally weakest quarter — making the Q1 records more striking), decentralized operating model (50 operating units / 197 locations), net-cash balance sheet, 27-year positive-FCF streak (10-Q MD&A).

## 6. Implications for ratings (dimensions + direction only — no scores assigned)

- **AI Thesis:** direction UP-pressure on directness/exposure dimensions. The "no AI-specific moat" critique in the watchlist needs re-examination: 56% tech revenue, modular capacity that customers must book with multi-year commitments, and the largest backlog driver being the modular DC business is closer to a scarce-capacity supplier than a commodity sub-contractor. Counterpoint for the rating session: it is still construction services — backlog is cancellable ("unexpected adjustments or cancellations in our backlog" is a named risk factor, Ex. 99.1), and the moat is capacity/labor scarcity, which is cyclical, not structural.
- **Growth:** UP-pressure. Q1 +56.5%, FY guide mid-to-high-20s same-store, RPO 65-75% converting within 12 months. Check whether Watchlist growth inputs predate Q1 (they reflect the headline, but EPS growth inputs may be stale vs +121% Q1 EPS).
- **Quality:** UP-pressure. GM 26.3%, EBITDA margin 18.3%, FCF conversion 113% TTM, net cash. TTM averages still understate the current run-rate (rule 9 TTM limitation) — Q1 margins are the high-water mark, TTM blends in lower 2025 quarters.
- **Risk:** DOWN-pressure (i.e., risk has increased). Customer concentration jumped from 37% → 56% tech in four quarters; new-construction mix 74.5%; growth tied to a handful of mega-projects in TX/NC/IN/VA. This is now the single most important rating debate for FIX: the same fact (tech concentration) pushes AI Thesis up and Risk down. Also: ~$43M of Q1 gross profit was closeout benefit — margin durability assumption should use ~25.2% core, not 26.3%.
- **Momentum:** likely UP-pressure (record results, guidance above prior consensus per [Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/transcripts/earnings-call-transcript-comfort-systems-usa-q1-2026-earnings-outshine-expectations-93CH-4636354)); refresh 50DMA % objectively via `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` per rule 11 — not run in this pass.
- **Value:** direction unclear — earnings exploded but the stock has re-rated (reported ~43x 2026E EPS, unverified). Needs fresh yfinance multiple pull at scoring time; do not reuse pre-Q1 inputs.
