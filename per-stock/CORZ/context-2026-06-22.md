## 2. Latest filing highlights

**HEADLINE — acquisition TERMINATED.** The CoreWeave/Core Scientific all-stock merger (~$9B, signed July 7 2025) was **rejected by CORZ shareholders and terminated effective Oct 30, 2025**. CORZ is now a standalone public company. Confirmed by all subsequent filing behavior:

- **10-Q (filed 2026-05-06, period 2026-03-31):** describes CORZ as an ongoing standalone operator of "ten data centers across seven U.S. states, representing approximately **1.9 gigawatts of gross utility power capacity, or approximately 1.3 GW of total leasable customer power capacity**." No merger / no termination-overhang language in the business description.
  - Total revenue **$115.244M** Q1 2026 vs **$79.525M** prior-year period (+45% YoY). Segments: Colocation, Self-mining, Hosted mining. Colocation revenue $30.105M (vs $67.179M — note self-mining/colocation mix shifting). Digital asset hosted-mining revenue $7.600M.
  - **Net loss $347.188M** for the quarter (large; driven substantially by non-operating fair-value / warrant items typical of this cohort — verify the EPS-YoY garbage-input question per rule 15 before any Growth use). Reported EPS ≈ **−$1.06** (per Q1 2026 results coverage).
  - **Cash and cash equivalents $1,005.148M** at 2026-03-31 (up from $311.378M at YE2025); digital assets $37.312M.
  - CoreWeave contract expanded to **590 MW** of leased customer power capacity via option exercises (12-year lease, straight-line revenue recognition).
  - **New acquisition:** agreement to buy data-center operations + electric service agreements for up to **440 MW gross utility power**, aggregate purchase price **~$421M cash** (Hunt County / Muskogee-area expansion, subject to conditions/regulatory approval).
- **8-K (2026-05-06):** $3.3B 7.750% Senior Secured Notes due 2031 issued at 99.250% (net ~$3.24B); proceeds fund a debt-service reserve + distribution to CORZ, used to repay the Bridge Facility delayed-draw term loans in full. Standalone capital raise — a company being acquired does not refinance $3.3B of its own debt.
- **8-K (2026-05-15):** Item 5.07 — 2026 Annual Meeting held May 12 2026; CORZ elected its own 5-person board (Sullivan, Booth, Crain, Rozov, Weiss), ratified KPMG. Standalone governance.
- **8-K (2026-05-26):** Item 5.02 — appointed Steve M. Smith (ex-Equinix CEO, current Zayo CEO) to the board — datacenter-industry talent, consistent with standalone HPC buildout.

Ticker still trades as CORZ on Nasdaq Global Select; warrants CORZW ($6.81) and CORZZ ($0.01) still listed (per all three 8-K cover pages).

EV side moved materially (+$3.3B notes, +$1.0B cash) — EV/MW should be recomputed off the post-raise balance sheet at any scoring pass. Per rule 13, EV/MW measures price-per-capacity only.
- **AI Thesis (D1–D5) — where conversion belongs (rule 13 division of labor):** CORZ has *real* AI/HPC conversion underway — 243 MW already billing to CoreWeave at HPC, raised margin guide (80–85%), 590 MW contracted, ~$10B 12-yr revenue. This is genuinely further along than cohort peers with zero AI contracts (CLSK). D1 (directness) and D2 (revenue visibility) likely have positive evidence vs prior template. Bitcoin-vs-AI conversion stays in AI Thesis, NOT Value.
- **R1 Customer concentration:** Single dominant counterparty (CoreWeave) for essentially all colocation revenue; ~400 MW new-client diversification is *targeted* for 2026 but **not yet signed**. R1 should reflect persistent high concentration on one HPC customer. Note CoreWeave's own credit/financing profile is itself a counterparty-risk input.

- **R5 Disruption Risk:** Defaults to **5** (non-Layer-10 physical infra/power/compute capacity). No change expected.
