# PATH (UiPath) -- Context Briefing 2026-05-25

## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim)

What I currently believe about PATH:
- UiPath is the leading robotic process automation (RPA) platform
- AI thesis: LLMs and agentic AI are transforming RPA from rigid rule-based bots to intelligent automation. UiPath is well-positioned to be the "execution layer" for AI agents
- Revenue ~$1.5-2B, growth has decelerated from hypergrowth to mid-teens
- SaaS transition underway -- moving from on-prem licenses to cloud subscriptions
- Market cap ~$12B
- Competes with Microsoft Power Automate, Automation Anywhere, SS&C Blue Prism
- CEO transition: founder Daniel Dines stepped back, then returned -- some governance concerns
- Key products: UiPath Platform, Autopilot (AI assistant), Document Understanding, Process Mining
- Risk: Microsoft Power Automate is bundled with Office 365, creating competitive pressure
- Has been integrating generative AI capabilities into the platform

### Finding 4: Competitive position vs. Microsoft -- price gap is massive, but UiPath holds complexity moat

Microsoft Power Automate costs **60-90% less per user** ($15-$40/user/month vs. UiPath at $140-$420/robot/month) and covers 70-80% of enterprise automation use cases. Power Automate's native integration with M365, Teams, Azure, and Copilot makes it the default for Microsoft-centric shops.

UiPath's competitive moat is in **complex, high-volume desktop RPA** involving legacy systems, Citrix environments, mainframe automation, and mixed IT environments (SAP, Oracle, custom apps). The 2026 consensus is that enterprises are increasingly running **both platforms** -- Power Automate for simple cloud workflows and UiPath for complex legacy automation.

This is a structural risk: as Microsoft adds AI/agentic capabilities to Power Automate, the "complexity moat" may narrow over time.

## 4. Implications for ratings

### Value (Weight: 15%)
- **Affected: strongly.** At $5.7B market cap and $4B EV, the stock trades at ~2.5x EV/Revenue and ~11x EV/FCF. For an 85% gross margin, GAAP-profitable software company, this is cheap. The $500M buyback provides a floor. Value score should be **well above average**.

### Quality (Weight: 20%)
- **Affected: mixed.** 85% gross margins are elite. First GAAP profitability is a milestone. $1.69B cash, no debt is a fortress balance sheet. However, **NRR of 107% and declining** is a quality concern -- it signals existing customers are not expanding. The WorkFusion acquisition is small and strategic but adds integration risk.

### Growth (Weight: 15%)
- **Affected: negatively.** Revenue growth decelerating from 13% to ~9%. **Organic net new ARR guided negative** is the critical data point. ARR growth of 11% masks inorganic contribution from WorkFusion. This is a below-average growth profile for a software company at this scale.

### AI Thesis (Weight: 30%)
- **Affected: this is the core debate.** UiPath is positioning itself as the orchestration/execution layer for AI agents -- a potentially massive role in the AI supply chain. The agentic platform, BYOM, vertical agents (healthcare, financial crime), on-prem deployment for regulated industries, and Google Cloud/Deloitte partnerships are all strategic positives. But the revenue is not here yet -- management says FY2027. The question is whether UiPath becomes the "picks and shovels" of agentic AI, or whether Microsoft/hyperscalers build this layer natively and commoditize it. Positioning is strong; proof is pending.

### Momentum (Weight: 10%)
- **Affected: strongly negative.** Stock is down 36% YTD, near 52-week lows ($9.20). Trading 25% below analyst targets. CEO insider sales at $15-16 look poorly timed but were 10b5-1. Earnings on May 28 could be a catalyst or a further leg down. Momentum is terrible.

### Risk (Weight: 10%)
- **Affected: several dimensions.**
  - **Microsoft competitive risk** is real and structural -- the price gap is enormous.
  - **Concentration risk** -- unclear from public data how concentrated the customer base is.
  - **Execution risk** on the agentic pivot -- if AI agent revenue doesn't materialize in FY2027 as guided, the narrative collapses.
  - **Governance** -- dual-class structure with Dines controlling significant voting power. Insider sales are 10b5-1 but optically negative at these levels.
  - **Mitigant** -- $1.69B cash, no debt, strong FCF generation, active buyback. This company is not going bankrupt.

### Key question for scoring session:
Is PATH a **Layer 10 (Applications)** stock in the AI supply chain, or does it sit somewhere between Layer 9 (Cloud) and Layer 10 as an orchestration/middleware play? Its role as the "execution layer for AI agents" could make it more of an infrastructure play than a typical application company. The layer classification affects how we benchmark it.
