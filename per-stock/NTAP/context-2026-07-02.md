# NTAP — Pre-Rating Research Briefing (2026-07-02)

**Company:** NetApp, Inc. · **Layer:** 08 Servers, Systems & Liquid Cooling → Storage infrastructure
**Fiscal year:** ends ~late April (FY2026 ended **April 24, 2026**). Reports on a fiscal calendar that is ~2 months ahead of the ticker's "quarter number."
**Purpose:** Fresh-research pass to de-stale the mental model before a 13-rating session (rubric Step 0). Objective inputs already pulled (yfinance 2026-07-02) are used as anchors, not re-pulled.

---

## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim, written BEFORE pulling data)

> NTAP is NetApp — a legacy enterprise storage vendor (ONTAP data-management OS, all-flash FAS/AFF arrays, plus first-party cloud storage services on Azure/AWS/GCP). My stale picture: a mature, ex-growth, high-FCF, capital-return story (big buyback + dividend) trading at a low-teens P/E, growing low-single-digits, gross margins ~70%, net-cash balance sheet. AI angle is real but modest and mostly *narrative* — they sell storage into AI/HPC data pipelines (the "data foundation for AI" pitch, NVIDIA reference architectures) but AI is a small, unquantified slice of a ~$6.5B revenue base that is still dominated by traditional enterprise on-prem storage refresh cycles. Competitive position: #2-ish in enterprise storage behind Dell, share-war with Pure Storage (who has the momentum/mindshare in all-flash), plus commodity NAND/component cost cyclicality on gross margin. My guess at ratings: middling AI thesis (D1 ~2), decent moat via ONTAP switching costs (D3 ~3-4), low-risk balance sheet, sleepy momentum.

**The single biggest thing this mental model got wrong: momentum.** NTAP is *not* sleepy right now — the stock is up ~33% recently to an all-time high on a Q4 beat-and-raise (see §5 DIFF).

### Customer concentration (10-K FY26, Item 1 + Note 14) — **most important R1 input**
- "**Sales to two of our major customers accounted for 43% of our net revenues in fiscal 2026.**" (10-K Item 1, Customers.)
- Note 14 detail: **Customer A = 22%** of net revenue (FY26; 21% FY25, 22% FY24); **Customer B = 21%** (FY26; 24% FY25, 22% FY24). **Both are distributors** (10-K states the >10% names "each of which is a distributor"; historically Arrow Electronics and TD SYNNEX). AR concentration: Customer B = 18% of AR, Customer A <10%.
- **Interpretation:** this is *two-tier distribution* concentration, not two end-customers. Underlying demand behind each distributor is thousands of resellers/enterprises. Same channel structure as STX/WDC. The disclosed 22% top-"customer" number sits in R1 band-4 territory *mechanically*, but the economic concentration risk is far lower than a 22% end-customer (a distributor swap is disruptive but not demand-destroying). Flag for the rater: R1 is a judgment call between 4 (literal reading of the 22% disclosure) and 5 (economic reality of diffuse distributor-fronted demand). Prior-year stability (21-24% for years) argues this is structural, not a rising-risk signal.

### Geographic revenue (10-K Note 14 / MD&A) — R2 input
- **Americas 51%** (Americas Commercial 41% + **U.S. Public Sector 10%**); **EMEA 34%**; **APAC 15%**. Stable at 51/34/15 for FY24-FY26.
- No disclosed China revenue concentration; no fabs/plants in a conflict geography (uses contract manufacturers). Worst plausible single-geo shock = **U.S. Public Sector (10% of rev)** under federal budget/shutdown/RIF pressure — the 10-K explicitly flags "uncertainty related to the U.S. government budget and debt levels … reductions in force." That is the named R2 shock, and it is ~10% of revenue and diffuse.

### §2b FCF-conversion red-flag check (refresh-context Step 2b)
FY26 FCF $1,869M ÷ GAAP net income $1,276M = **146% conversion** (>100%). No accrual-games signature; conversion is >100% (D&A + working-capital/deferred-revenue inflows exceed capex), and deferred revenue is *growing* (a cash inflow, healthy). Working capital not ballooning vs revenue. → **CLEAN.**

### §2c Expectations red-flag check (refresh-context Step 2c, rule 14) — **FIRES ⚠**
`scripts/expectations_flag.py NTAP` → **P/S 4.66 = 97.9th percentile of NTAP's own 3-year range, while revenue YoY (3.3% on the trailing XBRL basis) is BELOW its 3-year median (4.2%).** This is the textbook "peak multiple on decelerating growth" pattern the flag exists to surface — and it is *especially* live here because the multiple just re-rated ~33% higher in five weeks to an all-time high on the AI narrative, while the actual top line is guided to +8% and GM is guided *down*. **This is a qualitative counterweight, NOT a scored metric (rule 14).** It does not lower any rating, but it is the single most important thing for the human rater to hold in mind: the scoring system's Value (20%) + Momentum (10%) construction will *reward* NTAP precisely because it has run — the briefing's job is to make sure "great post-earnings tape" is not silently read as "great forward return." The bull argument that would justify the multiple is the AI-storage/AFX inflection; that argument is real but **unproven in the revenue line** (see D1).

## 6. Implications for ratings (flagging only — not pre-deciding)

- **D1 AI Revenue %** — LOW and honest. No disclosed AI revenue; management refuses to break it out. AI wins are a *count* (1,100 FY26), landing on-prem inside the ~$4.2B all-flash line. Any AI-revenue % is an estimate well under 10% of total → band 2 (some/growing but not material & not disclosed). Cite the transcript refusal-to-break-out as the sourcing.
- **D2 Supply-chain position** — mid. Storage is a mid-chain, multi-competitor market (Dell, Pure, Hitachi, Pure/PSTG, HPE); NetApp differentiates on ONTAP + native-cloud embedding but is not an upstream bottleneck. ~3.
- **D3 Moat** — ONTAP switching costs + $4.8B deferred-revenue annuity + native first-party cloud integration are genuine, but Pure is taking share and pricing is competitive. Solid narrow moat → ~3-4 (bias-check: don't inflate on the stock pop).
- **D4 Capacity expansion** — for a storage OEM this reads as product-cycle/R&D investment, not fab capex. Funded, active AFX/AIDE/EF cycle + native-cloud buildout; capex is low (asset-light) by design. Judgment call ~3 (steady, self-funded; not a "major multi-year buildout" in the fab sense).
- **D5 Hyperscaler exposure (SUPPLIER framework)** — nuanced. The disclosed >10% customers are **distributors, not hyperscalers**. NetApp's hyperscaler tie is as an ISV *inside* AWS/Azure/GCP marketplaces (first-party services), not as a hardware supplier to hyperscaler GPU builds — and those first-party services are only ~$0.5-0.7B of revenue. No disclosed hyperscaler *customer* concentration. → low-ish, ~2-3.
- **M1 EPS revisions 90d** — unambiguous **5** (beat-and-raise + a wall of upward PT/estimate revisions).
- **M2 Relative strength** — **5**. Storage/data-infra benchmark is software (IGV), which is **down ~17% YTD/1Y**; NTAP is **up ~33%** to an all-time high → outperforming its sector benchmark by >40 pts. (Even vs the broad tape it's a clear leader; only the red-hot SMH, the wrong benchmark for a storage name, beats it.)
- **M3 Insider activity** — **3** (post-rally diversification default). EDGAR Form 4 check: **zero code-P open-market buys**; recent activity is RSU/PSU vesting (M), tax withholding (F), and **10b5-1 plan sales (S)** — e.g., President C. Cernuda sold ~49.5K shares (~51% of ending holdings) on 2026-06-25 but **explicitly under a 10b5-1 plan adopted 2026-03-24**, which the rubric's decision procedure Step 2 says to treat as *routine* → do NOT push to band 1/2. Only "buy" was 253 ESPP shares (payroll plan, not discretionary). Absence-of-buying is the signal; no discretionary open-market selling → 3.
- **R1 Customer concentration** — judgment call **4 vs 5** (see §2). Literal disclosure: top "customer" 22%, two >10% = 43% combined → band 4. Economic reality: both are *distributors* fronting diffuse demand → closer to 5. Lean 4 on the literal disclosure but flag the distributor nuance for discussion.
- **R2 Geo/export** — **5** (with the mandated named-shock rationale). Worst plausible single-geo shock = U.S. Public Sector budget/shutdown, ~10% of revenue, diffuse; no China concentration, no conflict-zone assets, 51/34/15 diversified. Under the revised R2 bands, "no leg impairs >10%" = 5. (Could argue 4 if you treat the U.S.-federal overhang as a demand leg, but it's ~10% and not a foreign-geography strait/policy risk.)
- **R3 Balance sheet** — **5** (net cash +$1.1B, strong FCF, repaid current debt, up-to-100%-FCF return, no maturity wall).
- **R4 Regulatory/litigation** — **4** (routine NPE patent suits only; no material investigations/antitrust). Clean-ish but hardware/software NPE litigation is perennial, so 4 not 5.
- **R5 Disruption** — **5** by default (non-Layer-10; rule 16). No specific reason found to go sub-5 — enterprise storage/data-infrastructure is a physical-infra durability profile; if anything AI is a net tailwind (more data to store/manage). Note the *one* contra-argument: agentic/AI-native could commoditize the software-management layer over a long horizon, and hyperscaler-native storage could disintermediate on-prem — but neither is active erosion, and the rule-16 default is 5 for non-Layer-10, so 5 stands.

### Data gaps / anomalies to know before finalizing
1. **No AI revenue dollars anywhere** (10-K or transcript) — D1 must be an explicit estimate with the "company does not break it out" citation, per rule 3. Do not let the strong AI-win *count* inflate D1 into implying a disclosed revenue mix.
2. **Expectations flag FIRES** (P/S 97.9th pctile, decel growth) — the name is priced at a 3-year-high multiple after a 33% pop; the scoring construction will structurally favor it. Human rater should weigh this against the Value inputs, which will look "fine" (Fwd P/E 16) precisely because EPS grew, not because the stock is cheap on its own history.
3. **R1 distributor-vs-customer nuance** — the 22%/21%/43% figures are the two big *distributors*, not end customers; the mechanical band-4 read overstates true customer-demand concentration. Explicit discussion item.
4. **FY27 gross-margin guide is DOWN ~200bps** on NAND/component inflation ("July quarter is the trough") — a real near-term fundamental headwind that none of the momentum/Value inputs capture. Watch item, not a rating change.
5. **yfinance "EPS YoY +18.8%"** appears to reflect the Q4 GAAP print (+23%) or a TTM blend; full-year GAAP EPS grew +12%. Growth is operational (the Spot divestiture gain hit FY2025, not FY2026), so **no rule-15 EPS-YoY blank is warranted** — the YoY change is not one-time-dominated.
