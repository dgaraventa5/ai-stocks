## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim, written BEFORE searching)

> Rambus is a memory interface chip + IP licensing company. My stale understanding: historically a patent-licensing house (DRAM patents, security IP), but pivoted hard into selling actual silicon — DDR5 RCD (registering clock drivers) for RDIMMs, plus companion chips (SPD hub, temp sensor, PMIC partnership). DDR5 server transition drives chip revenue growth, and Rambus is one of three RCD suppliers alongside Montage and Renesas/IDT. Product revenue was ramping into the $50-60M/quarter range with licensing providing high-margin recurring base. AI server demand (more DRAM content per server, MRDIMM for bandwidth) is the bull thesis. As of my training, HBM was more of an IP/future-companion-chip story than meaningful revenue. Net cash balance sheet, no real debt.
