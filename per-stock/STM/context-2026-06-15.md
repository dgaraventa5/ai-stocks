Q1'26 marks a clear inflection: **+23% YoY** is the first strong YoY print after the trough, and Q2 guide (+24.9% YoY) confirms acceleration. But the trough still dominates trailing metrics — TTM EPS $0.16, TTM operating income ~$392M (4-qtr sum), gross margin compressed to ~32% TTM vs. ~50% at the 2022-23 peak. **Trajectory: early-cycle recovery off a multi-quarter bottom**; the rally has front-run the fundamentals (see Momentum/expectations flag).

## AI / datacenter exposure & directness

STM's AI angle is **real and accelerating but small as a % of total**:
- **NVIDIA 800VDC collaboration (Mar 2026):** STM expanded its 800VDC AI-datacenter power-conversion portfolio (800V→12V and 800V→6V stages, per NVIDIA's 800VDC reference design; complements an earlier 800V→50V stage). Combines ST silicon + **SiC + GaN** power, analog/mixed-signal, and MCUs. Oct 2025 prototype: GaN LLC converter direct from 800V at 1MHz, >98% efficiency. (ST newsroom / GlobeNewswire 2026-03-17)
- **Datacenter revenue:** guided "nicely above $500M for 2026" (~4% of ~$13B run-rate) and "well above $1B for 2027" — roughly doubling. This is the genuine AI-supply-chain content: power delivery + optical/RF for AI factories.
- **Directness assessment:** **Moderate-low.** STM is a diversified broad-line analog/power/MCU vendor for which AI datacenter is one emerging vertical (~mid-single-digit % of revenue in 2026, scaling to high-single-digit in 2027). The 800VDC win is a credible, named, accelerating design-in into the AI power stack — but the company's fortunes are still dominated by the auto/industrial cycle. Far less direct than MPWR/NVTS (board-level AI power) or pure datacenter names. Genuine AI-supply-chain share: ~**4-8% of revenue**, rising.

## Red flags & caveats

1. **Foreign-filer data caveat (per task brief):** STM files 20-F/6-K, not 10-K/10-Q. SEC EDGAR companyfacts XBRL is sparse/inconsistently tagged for FPIs — **`expectations_flag.py` (rule 14) and XBRL-dependent scripts may not map STM's revenue tags and will likely self-skip.** All financials here sourced from yfinance + ST press release (6-K), not EDGAR XBRL. Verify against the 6-K filing before any rating finalization.
2. **Trough-distorted multiples:** Forward P/E 32x, EV/EBITDA 27x, and PEG 0.56 are all distorted by depressed trailing earnings. On normalized mid-cycle margins these compress sharply — but that's a forward judgment, not a current fact. Standard Layer 6 bands will score Value harshly on the headline multiples; flag that the denominators are cyclically depressed.
3. **Momentum / expectations risk (rule 14 spirit):** Stock ~$79 vs 52-wk low ~$21 — **nearly 4x off the bottom**, 98% of last 120 days above 50-DMA. P/S 5.67 is elevated vs STM's own downturn range while fundamentals are only beginning to recover. Classic "peak multiple on recovering-but-still-low numbers" — high momentum score will mechanically reward the most crowded part of the trade. The EDGAR-based expectations flag can't run (FPI), so this judgment is manual.
4. **AI is a small slice:** Don't let the NVIDIA 800VDC headline overstate it. Datacenter is ~4% of 2026 revenue. The base business is auto/industrial cyclical, where Power & Discrete was still −1.8% YoY in Q1'26.
5. **Capex-heavy / fab owner:** Structural high capex (TTM capex $2.17B ≈ 17.5% of revenue) keeps FCF thin even in recovery; FCF Yield will screen poorly.
6. **TTM understatement (rule 9):** TTM averages span the trough and understate the current run-rate. MRQ GM 33.8% vs TTM ~31.7%; the recovery is real but trailing metrics lag. Score bias is DOWN — flag for any quality-metric read.
