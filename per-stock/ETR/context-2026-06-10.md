# ETR — Pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-10)

Prepared for AI Thesis re-rating (Layer 1 power, "direct vs indirect AI bottleneck ownership" lens).

## Pre-research mental model

ETR is a regulated Gulf Coast utility holdco (E-LA, E-TX, E-MS, E-AR, E-NO, ~3M customers, MISO). Flagship AI item: Meta's ~$10B Hyperion datacenter in Richland Parish, LA (announced Dec 2024), originally ~2GW with three new CCGTs approved by the LPSC in late 2025, since expanded toward 5GW. Guided ~8-9% adjusted EPS CAGR on a large capex plan (~$37-43B over four years, likely raised) and a multi-GW datacenter pipeline. As a regulated utility, datacenter load grows rate base at allowed ROE (~9.5-10%); no merchant premium. Expected D1=5 (">50% revenue AI-tied") to be wildly wrong — datacenter revenue today likely low-single-digit % at most. Last quarter I was confident about: Q1 2026 (late April), believed to show capex raise and signed ESA pipeline.

Consolidated as-reported ROE (LTM) 11.0%; adjusted ROE 11.0%.

EPS outlooks raised: 2027 $4.90-5.20 (+$0.35), 2028 $5.55-5.85 (+$0.50), 2029 $6.25-6.55 (+$0.50); >8% CAGR.

## DIFF vs mental model

- **Bigger and faster than modeled:** capex plan is $57B (not ~$43B); Meta is now TWO datacenter projects/ESAs (Hyperion 15-yr + Evest 20-yr), 10 gas plants >7 GW, ~$27B Meta spend. EPS outlooks raised through 2029.
- **New since my model:** Evest LLC second ESA (March 2026); AWS Mississippi scale-up to $25B (April 2026); Google AR approval of Cypress Solar (Q1 2026); LPSC "Lightning Amendment" fast-track regime.
- **Confirmed:** regulated ROE capture (no merchant premium); "Fair Share Plus" framework explicitly returns surplus to ratepayers, not shareholders.
- **D1=5 is wrong by an order of magnitude** — see below.

## Evidence for D1 (% revenue datacenter-tied today)

- Entergy does NOT disclose datacenter revenue or load as % of total. **FLAG: no disclosed number.**
- Upper-bound math: Q1 2026 industrial volume rose 2,062 GWh YoY (15,895 vs 13,833; 8-K App C), attributed to data center + primary metals + transportation combined. Even attributing 100% to datacenters at ~$50-60/MWh industrial realized rates (assumption, not disclosed), that is ~$103-124M ≈ **3-4% of Q1 2026 revenue of $3.19B** (yfinance). That's the YoY increment; total datacenter-tied revenue today is plausibly **~2-5% of revenue — nowhere near 50%**.
- Forward check: Meta Hyperion at full ~2 GW ESA-1 load ≈ ~15-17 TWh/yr vs ~120 TWh retail sales ≈ ~12-14% of MWh (and less of revenue, since hyperscale tariffs are below-average $/MWh). Even the full 7-12 GW pipeline by ~2030 keeps datacenter revenue well under 50%.
- **Conclusion: D1=5 is unsupportable. Evidence supports roughly "low-single-digit % today, double-digit % by 2029-2030 if signed + pipeline ramps."**

## Evidence for D2 (return capture / who keeps the upside)

- 100% regulated. ELL adjusted ROE (excl. affiliate preferred) was 9.7% TTM ending 3/31/26, "within its authorized range" (Q1 2026 slides per Investing.com — **flag: secondary source; verify against ELL FRP filing**). Consolidated LTM as-reported ROE 11.0% (8-K App).
- Structure: Entergy rate-bases the new plants ($57B capex plan), funded with $6.6B equity + debt; Meta/Google/AWS sign long-term ESAs paying "full cost of service" under "Fair Share Plus" — surplus revenue is explicitly pledged to EXISTING CUSTOMERS as savings ($2.65B Meta LA, >$2B AWS MS, >$1.1B Google AR; $5B total claimed, entergy.com), NOT to shareholders.
- Shareholder upside = rate base growth at allowed ROE → >8% EPS CAGR guided through 2029 (raised twice in 2026). No price upside per MWh; no scarcity premium capture. This is the textbook "volume not price" regulated model — the opposite of merchant (CEG/VST/TLN) capture.
- Offsetting risks: $6.6B equity dilution; execution on ~10 GW of construction; contract-life (15-20 yr) vs asset-life (30-40 yr) mismatch lands on ratepayers and, politically, on the utility (The Lens 2026-04-14).
