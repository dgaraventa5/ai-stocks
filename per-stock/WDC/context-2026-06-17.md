## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim)

> Western Digital is one of two remaining nearline HDD oligopolists (with Seagate). As of my training it was a combined HDD + flash/NAND company, mid-cycle, with the flash business (Sandisk brand) dragging margins through the 2023-24 memory downturn. The plan was to spin off the flash/NAND business as a standalone "Sandisk." HDD gross margins were recovering off cyclical lows; HAMR/ePMR and UltraSMR were the density roadmap. Revenue combined ~$13-15B; market cap historically ~$15-25B. I expected a stock in the ~$40-90 range.

**This model is materially stale.** The spin completed in early 2026 (WDC is now pure HDD), the AI-storage supercycle has driven a ~10-11x stock run, gross margins crossed 50% for the first time ever, and the company is sold out of HDD capacity through CY2026 with LTAs into CY2029. See §6.
