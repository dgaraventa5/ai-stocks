# EQT — Pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-09)

Layer: 01 Power Generation / Natural gas E&P
Session lens: how DIRECTLY does EQT own the AI power bottleneck (direct premium power/capacity sales vs second-derivative commodity exposure)?

## Pre-research mental model

EQT is the largest US natural gas producer (~6+ Bcfe/d, Appalachian Marcellus/Utica), vertically integrated after the 2024 Equitrans Midstream acquisition. The AI/datacenter thesis runs through in-basin demand: announced gas supply deals for the Homer City 4.4 GW data-center campus and (I believed) the Shippingport/Bruce Mansfield coal-to-gas conversion, plus the 2025 Olympus Energy acquisition which added datacenter-adjacent acreage and midstream in SW Pennsylvania. Most recent quarter I had internalized was roughly Q3–Q4 2025: improving balance sheet post-Equitrans, results still overwhelmingly driven by Henry Hub and local basis, datacenter deals announced but not yet generating revenue. Competitive position: low-cost integrated producer, fundamentally a price-taker on commodity gas.

## DIFF vs mental model

1. Mental model was directionally right and now confirmed with specifics: the datacenter deals (Homer City, Shippingport) remain pre-revenue, and Q1 2026's blowout quarter was driven by commodity price ($5.08 vs $3.77 realized) + unhedged exposure + Olympus volumes — not by any AI contract.
2. New precision on pricing: I did not previously know the deals are "index-plus" (TETCO M-2 + midstream cost). That materially weakens any "premium pricing from AI buyers" claim — it's local index recovery, closer to a basis hedge than a premium contract.
3. New: Homer City first power slipped 2027 → 2028 (April 2026, secondhand), and the deal apparently remains an agreement in principle ~11 months after announcement. AI-linked revenue start is further out than my model assumed.
4. New: scale of ambition is larger than modeled (management claims 2–3 Bcf/d underwritten, 8–10 Bcf/d potential), but none of it is current revenue and most lacks disclosed definitive terms.
5. Correction to my recall: Olympus Energy acquisition CLOSED 2025-07-01 (10-Q Q1 2026, Olympus Energy Acquisition note); I had loosely remembered it as an April 2025 event (that was the announcement window).

## Evidence for D1 (AI Revenue %)

Current AI-tied revenue is approximately 0%. Q1 2026 total operating revenues of $3,378.7M decompose into commodity sales of natural gas/NGLs/oil ($3,439.9M), derivative losses (-$238.3M), and pipeline/other ($177.1M) (10-Q 2026-04-22) — there is no datacenter-contract revenue line, and neither announced datacenter deal is flowing gas: Homer City first power is now 2028 (April 2026 report) and Shippingport's plant/transport capacity targets fall 2026 startup (NGI/East Daley, July 2025). Even at full ramp, the two contracts total ~1.46 Bcf/d (665,000 MMBtu/d + 800 MMcf/d) against current sales of ~6.9 Bcfe/d (618 Bcfe in Q1), or roughly 21% of volumes — but at index-plus pricing this is ordinary gas sales volume with assured offtake, and only a fraction of the campuses' power serves AI specifically (datacenter ≠ AI caveat applies; tenant mix undisclosed). Any 2026 revenue uplift attributable to "AI" today comes only via stronger in-basin basis, which is commodity price, not AI revenue.

## Evidence for D2 (Supply chain position / pricing power)

EQT is a price-taker with a developing volume-assurance story, not a premium-pricing story. Both disclosed datacenter-linked contracts price at "index-plus" tied to Texas Eastern M-2, with the plus covering midstream transport cost (S&P Global, 2025-07-21) — that is local-index realization plus cost recovery, not pricing power extracted from AI buyers. Confirmatory behavior: EQT embedded 10–15 Bcfe of strategic curtailments in Q2 2026 guidance "in response to natural gas price volatility" (10-Q) — a company with pricing power doesn't curtail into weak prices. Management's own framing of the AI opportunity is to pull demand in-basin to "strengthen local basis" (Q1 2026 call) — i.e., the prize is narrowing a historical NEGATIVE basis differential toward zero/positive, a second-derivative commodity effect. The Q1 basis improvement to +$0.38/Mcf (vs -$0.01 YoY, 10-Q) is encouraging but winter-storm-distorted. What EQT does have: scale (largest US producer), integrated midstream post-Equitrans/Olympus enabling firm in-basin delivery others can't match, and first-mover firm supply deals (~1.4 Bcf/d, S&P Global) — strong position to win volume, weak evidence of winning price.

## Evidence for D5 (Hyperscaler exposure)

No signed, disclosed deal with any named hyperscaler exists. The two disclosed counterparties are Homer City Redevelopment (a power/campus developer; 665,000 MMBtu/d, "agreement in principle" since 2025-07-15, no definitive agreement found, first power 2028) and Frontier Group of Companies (Shippingport/Bruce Mansfield, ~800 MMcf/d, co-located datacenter, 2026 startup). Neither campus has publicly named a hyperscaler tenant as of 2026-06-09. Management's "2–3 Bcf/d underwritten, 8–10 Bcf/d potential" (Q1 2026 call) is pipeline-of-opportunity narrative without disclosed counterparties, terms, or durations. EQT's hyperscaler exposure is therefore third-hand: hyperscaler → (unsigned/unnamed tenant) → power developer → EQT gas at index-plus. Caution: press coverage frequently conflates EQT Corporation with EQT AB / EQT Infrastructure (the Swedish PE firm behind EdgeConneX and the AES take-private) — those are unrelated entities and must not be credited to the E&P.
