# APP — AppLovin Corporation

**Layer:** 10 — Models, Software & Applications / AI advertising
**Last reviewed:** 2026-06-17
**Current conviction:** ✓✓ (Total 78.9)
**Current position size:** ~7.0% of portfolio (76-bar model)
**Thesis-break trigger:** The SEC/short-seller overhang resolves into a documented finding that AXON's ad-performance metrics were materially misstated, OR the e-commerce expansion stalls (self-serve GA slips / ROAS disappoints) — either breaks the "durable AI flywheel with a 5–10x adjacent TAM" core.

---

## 1. One-line thesis

> AppLovin is a near-pure-play AI-adtech platform: after divesting its games business in mid-2025, ~100% of revenue is the advertising platform driven by AXON, its machine-learning ad-recommendation and auction engine — the AI engine *is* the product, not a feature — running at ~88% gross margin and ~78% operating margin, with revenue +58.9% YoY (Q1 2026) and a credible 5–10x adjacent TAM as AXON expands from gaming into e-commerce/web advertising.

---

## 2. Position in the AI supply chain

Application/monetization layer (Layer 10). AppLovin is one of the most *direct* AI-monetization stories in the watchlist outside of semis: AXON 2.0 processes >2M ad auctions/sec learning across >1B devices, and AXON 3.0 (2026 roadmap) integrates generative AI to *create* ads in real time, not just place them. It is demand-side (a consumer of cloud compute, not AI infrastructure) — high AI directness (D1=5) but supply-chain-tangential (D5=2).

## 3. Customers

Advertisers (historically mobile-game studios, now expanding to e-commerce/web merchants). No single customer >15% disclosed; the structural dependency is on the Apple/Google mobile platforms (IDFA/attribution policy) more than on any one advertiser. Self-serve Axon Ads (referral-only today) targeted for general availability in H1 2026, converting ~57% of qualified leads to active campaigns.

## 4. Pricing & demand

Performance-priced (advertisers pay for measurable ROAS), so demand scales with AXON's measured outperformance. The growth catalyst is the **non-gaming TAM, estimated by management at 5–10x gaming**, with early e-commerce ROAS reportedly comparable to Meta's Audience Network. Q1 2026 revenue +58.9% YoY ($1,842.4M vs $1,159.0M restated) — *accelerating* on the clean advertising-only base.

## 6. Moat

A data/algorithm flywheel: more ad spend → more auction/outcome data → better AXON predictions → better ROAS → more spend. Scale across >1B devices and a multi-year ML head start. **But the moat is contested** — Meta and Google are larger with deeper data, and the durability of AXON's edge (and the integrity of its reported performance) is the central debate (see §9).

**Moat rating:** Narrow (real flywheel, but larger competitors and an unresolved credibility overhang cap it).

## 7. Valuation

Premium but not absurd on growth: forward P/E ~21.9, **PEG ~0.31** (fwd P/E ÷ ~69.5% EPS growth), EV/FCF ~39.4 (Layer-10 EV/FCF basis). P/S ~26x is high in absolute terms but growth is accelerating, so the rule-14 "peak multiple + decelerating growth" trap does *not* fire. The stock is down ~31% over 6 months — the litigation/SEC overhang is at least partly priced in. The genuine tension: elite fundamentals (59% growth, 78% op margin, 0.31 PEG) versus an active legal/credibility cloud.

## 8. Catalysts (next 4 quarters)

- Self-serve Axon Ads **general availability (H1 2026)** — opens the long-tail/SMB and e-commerce funnel.
- AXON 3.0 generative-ad-creation rollout (interactive page generator live; video-ad generator upcoming).
- E-commerce ROAS data points validating (or not) the 5–10x TAM claim.
- Resolution/clarification of the SEC inquiry and short-seller allegations.

## 9. Risks

- **Credibility/legal overhang (the dominant risk):** active 10b-5 securities class action + derivative suits (CEO/CFO named); SEC reportedly examining data practices; short-seller fraud allegations targeting AXON's reported ad-performance. This is why Risk R4 = 2.
- **Platform dependence:** Apple/Google attribution and privacy policy changes can impair targeting.
- **Competitive:** Meta/Google have larger data and could compress AXON's edge in the e-commerce push.
- **Momentum:** −31% over 6 months, lagging the software group (M2 = 2).

## 10. The "inverse the thesis" test

The thesis is wrong if the short-sellers are right — i.e., AXON's measured ROAS outperformance is partly an attribution/measurement artifact rather than genuine incremental ad value, which an SEC finding or advertiser pullback would expose. In that world the 78% margins and 59% growth are built on a metric that doesn't survive scrutiny, and the multiple — even at PEG 0.31 — is a trap. The bull case requires the e-commerce ROAS to validate independently of AppLovin's own attribution. Watch third-party advertiser ROI confirmation and the SEC/litigation track.
