## 4. AI thesis, business model & directness — the key findings

**AppLovin is a near-pure-play AI-AdTech platform.** After the 2025-06-30 Tripledot divestiture, ~100% of continuing revenue is the advertising platform powered by **AXON**, AppLovin's ML/AI ad-recommendation and auction engine. This is one of the most *direct* AI-monetization stories in the watchlist: the AI engine **is** the product, not a feature bolted onto a legacy business.

- **AXON 2.0** processes >2M ad auctions/sec learning across >1B devices; the 2024 AXON 2.0 rebuild drove the multi-quarter re-rate. **AXON 3.0** (2026 roadmap) integrates generative AI to *create* ads in real time, not just place them. New AI creative tools: interactive page generator, upcoming video-ad generator.
  Source: Wedbush/PredictStreet deep-dive; Bitget academy; Zacks/TalkMarkets coverage 2026.
- **E-commerce / non-gaming expansion** is the central growth catalyst. AXON, originally gaming-ad-focused, is extending into web/e-commerce advertising. Management estimates the **non-gaming TAM at 5-10x gaming**, with early e-commerce ROAS reportedly comparable to Meta's Audience Network. Self-serve Axon Ads (currently referral-only) targeted for **general availability in H1 2026** — onboarding funnel converting ~57% of qualified leads to active campaigns.
  Source: Zacks via TradingView/Yahoo "E-Commerce Expansion Could Unlock a New Growth Engine" (2026); MLQ.ai earnings highlights.
- **Divestiture mechanics:** Apps/games (incl. Lion Studios) sold to **Tripledot Studios**, completed **2025-06-30**. Deal restructured from an initial ~$900M (Feb 2025) to **$400M cash + ~20% retained Tripledot equity stake** (~$800M total stated value). Purpose: remove low-margin first-party game revenue and become a software-led, high-margin advertising pure-play.
  Source: Wikipedia/AppLovin (corroborated by ainvest); 10-Q TripledotMember XBRL references.
- **Margins** reflect the pure-software model: GM ~88%, operating margin **78.2% (Q1'26)**, FCF margin ~72%, capex negligible.
- **S&P 500 inclusion:** 2025-09-22.

**Assessment of AI directness:** very high. AXON is a genuine AI/ML monetization engine driving the entire P&L, with a credible large adjacent-TAM expansion (e-commerce). This is closer to a "5" on AI-revenue-share than almost any non-semis name. The debate is not *whether* it's AI-driven — it's whether the moat is durable and whether the reported ad-performance numbers are clean (the short-seller overhang, Section 6).

## 7. Flags

1. **RULE 15 (EPS YoY one-time check):** The **total-reported** EPS YoY of +113% ($3.56 vs $1.67) is distorted by the prior-year $147.1M *discontinued-operations (games) loss*, NOT by a current one-time gain. The **continuing-operations** EPS YoY is **+69.5%** ($3.56 vs $2.10) and is fully operational (Q1'26 other income only $42.6M; no divestiture gain in Q1'26 — the Tripledot gain was a 2025 event). **Recommendation: use +69.5% (continuing-ops). Do NOT blank, and do NOT use the +113% figure.** This is the cleaner analog of the rule-15 principle: strip the non-operating distortion (here a prior-year discontinued-ops loss inflating the YoY%), keep the operational number.
2. **RULE 9 (earnings-triggered refresh):** Q1 2026 reported 2026-05-06 (>1 week ago). Revenue +58.9% YoY qualifies as a >15% beat-of-trajectory; objective inputs in this briefing are fresh as of 2026-06-17. If added to the watchlist, the row is current.
3. **Rev 3Y CAGR is mixed-basis** (FY22 games-inclusive vs FY25 advertising-only). The +24.8% understates the true advertising trajectory; the clean advertising-only 2yr CAGR is ~72.5%. **Growth category should lean on MRQ YoY (+58.9%) and the clean advertising CAGR, not the +24.8% mixed figure.**
4. **ROIC is approximate and distorted** by a tiny equity base (aggressive buybacks → equity $2.36B). ROE 266% and naive ROIC >100% are not usable. ~52-64% is indicative of genuinely excellent returns on a near-capital-free model; cite as Medium-Low confidence and pull balance-sheet detail before finalizing.
5. **EV/FCF used (rule 10), not EV/EBITDA.** EV/FCF = 39.4 (band ≤40 → 75). Reference EV/EBITDA 35.7. FCF computed from XBRL OCF − XBRL capex because yfinance lacks 4 quarters of capex rows and `statement_fcf()` returns None.
6. **PEG split:** my computed PEG = 0.31 (fwd P/E ÷ continuing-ops EPS growth); yfinance trailingPegRatio = 1.42 uses the distorted trailing base. Spreadsheet PEG formula = Fwd P/E ÷ EPS growth — feed it the clean +69.5%.
7. **Expectations flag script not run** (mid-series revenue-tag restatement) — manual assessment substituted (Section 2c).

- **D1. AI Revenue % of total = 5.** AXON AI ad engine drives ~100% of continuing (advertising) revenue; loss of AI ad-targeting edge = existential. As direct an AI-revenue story as exists outside semis. Source: 10-Q (advertising = sole continuing segment post-divestiture). **Confidence: High.**
- **D2. Substitutes exist (Meta, Google, Unity, TTD) but AXON's measured ROAS edge confers real switching cost for performance advertisers. Not an upstream bottleneck → not 5. Source: margin structure 10-Q; competitive context. **Confidence: Medium.**
- **D3. Moat strength = 3-4 (lean 3).** THE halo-prone rating — be ruthless. The moat is a data/algorithm flywheel (>1B devices, 2M auctions/sec → better predictions → more spend → more data). Real, but (a) competitors have larger data scale (Meta, Google), (b) the moat is partly *reputational/contested* given the ad-fraud/attribution allegations, and (c) ad-engine edges have historically proven competible. Bias check — would I rate this 4 if the stock were down 40%? It IS down ~31% and litigation-clouded; I'd hesitate. → **3**, raise to 4 only if e-commerce ROAS parity with Meta is independently confirmed and the attribution allegations resolve cleanly. Source: AXON scale claims (IR/coverage); short-seller reports (contra). **Confidence: Medium.**
- **D4. Capacity expansion = 4.** "Capacity" for a software platform = product/market expansion: AXON 3.0 genAI rollout + self-serve GA in H1 2026 + e-commerce TAM 5-10x gaming. Funded from massive internal FCF, actively underway. Not a 5 (no physical multi-year capex program; it's product rollout, lower-certainty than a funded fab). Source: 2026 e-commerce/self-serve coverage. **Confidence: Medium.**
- **D5. Hyperscaler exposure / AI-infra commitment = 2.** Layer-10 application — it neither sells to hyperscalers as a supplier nor is itself a hyperscaler buildout. It's a *consumer* of cloud compute, not a driver of the AI-infrastructure capex cycle the watchlist tracks. Per the supplier framework, minimal hyperscaler revenue → low. Source: business model. **Confidence: High.** (This correctly pulls down the AI-Thesis composite for a name whose AI story is real but orthogonal to the *infrastructure* supply chain the project centers on — worth flagging to Dom: APP is AI-direct but supply-chain-tangential.)
