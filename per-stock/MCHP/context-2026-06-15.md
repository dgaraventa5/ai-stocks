## Snapshot — where MCHP actually is now

MCHP is a broad-line embedded-control franchise (MCUs, analog, FPGA, connectivity), heavily **auto + industrial** end-market exposed, that just came through one of the deepest inventory-correction downcycles in its history and is now in a clear, sharp recovery.

**Q4 FY2026 (quarter ended Mar 31, 2026), reported May 7, 2026** (8-K EX-99.1, accession 0000827054-26-000012):
- Net sales **$1.311B, +35.1% YoY, +10.6% QoQ** — above the high end of guidance (mid $1.260B).
- GAAP gross margin **61.0%**; non-GAAP gross margin **61.6%**.
- GAAP operating income **+$217.4M (16.6% of sales)** — vs prior-year Q4 GAAP operating **loss of $(100.3)M**. Big swing to profitability.
- GAAP EPS **$0.21** vs prior-year Q4 GAAP **$(0.29)** loss. Non-GAAP EPS **$0.57** vs $0.11 prior-year Q4.
- Inventory $1.035B; days of inventory cut to **185 (from 201 in Dec, 201→185)**; distributor inventory 26 days (near low end of historical range). Inventory down $320.9M from the Dec-2024 peak.

**Full FY2026:** Net sales **$4.713B, +7.1% YoY**. GAAP EPS **$0.22** vs **$(0.01)** FY2025 (GAAP net loss $2.7M prior year). Non-GAAP EPS $1.64 vs $1.31 (+25.2%). FY2026 OCF **$962.1M**, capex **$91.1M**, FCF **$871.0M** (18.5% of sales). Returned $984.0M via dividends. (All 8-K EX-99.1 Q4 FY2026.)

**Q1 FY2027 guidance (June 2026 qtr):** revenue **$1.442B–$1.469B (mid $1.456B, +35.3% YoY, +11% QoQ)**; non-GAAP gross margin **62.25%–63.25%**; non-GAAP op margin 33.0%–34.5%; non-GAAP EPS **$0.67–$0.71**. FY2027 capex guided to **~$100M** (8-K EX-99.1). Recovery is accelerating, not stalling.

CEO Steve Sanghi (returned to lead the "nine-point recovery plan"): "meaningful progress from the challenging conditions we were navigating just a few quarters ago."

## AI / data-center exposure & directness

MCHP is **mostly NOT an AI-supply-chain name** — it is auto/industrial/aerospace/defense embedded — but it has a small, genuine, fast-growing data-center business that is the AI-relevant slice:

- **Data Center Solutions business unit: ~$302.7M in CY2025 (~7% of revenue), guided ~+65% to ~$500M in CY2026** (~8–9% of revenue per UBS) — disclosed in a dedicated IR release (ir.microchip.com detail/1395; Investing.com 2026, "data center unit revenue seen rising 65%"). DC unit grew **62.9% YoY in the March-2026 quarter**.
- Content: high-speed connectivity & compute — **PCIe switches, NVMe/storage controllers, and newly Gen6 PCIe retimers** (entered the retimer market June-2026 quarter as a companion to Gen6 switches; disclosed an OEM design win displacing a competitor). COO Simoncic: "strong customer engagement and expanding design activity in data center and AI applications."
- **% genuinely AI-supply-chain: ~7–9% of revenue (and rising).** This is real disclosed AI/DC content (PCIe retimers/switches are direct AI-server-rack content), but the other ~91% is conventional embedded (autos, industrial, aero/defense) on a generic cyclical recovery, not AI-driven.

**Proposed AI-Thesis directness:** LOW. Direct AI content exists and is the growth story management is selling, but it is <10% of the company; the bulk of the 35% YoY rebound is the embedded cycle turning, not AI demand. Don't credit MCHP with high directness — its AI exposure is a minority kicker, comparable in spirit to a "competes with the cohort but value object is mostly elsewhere" name. (Contrast pure retimer/connectivity names like CRDO/ALAB which are ~100% AI-DC.)

## Cyclical & restructuring red flags (rule 15 + rule 9 relevance)

1. **MRQ EPS YoY is uncomputable / sign-flip — recommend BLANK.** Q4 FY2026 GAAP EPS $0.21 vs prior-year Q4 $(0.29) loss; FY2026 GAAP EPS $0.22 vs $(0.01). A negative-to-positive swing has no meaningful % and would score as garbage. Additionally, FY2026 GAAP net income was **"adversely impacted by purchase accounting adjustments... restructuring charges and the Series A Preferred Stock dividend"** (8-K) — i.e. heavily one-time/non-operating contaminated. Per **rule 15, blank the EPS YoY input** (Growth then averages the revenue metrics). Revenue YoY (+35.1% MRQ, +7.1% FY) is clean and operational — keep it.
2. **Restructuring / fab sale ongoing.** "Ongoing evaluation of restructuring activities including the **sale of our Fab 2 wafer fabrication facility**" (8-K guidance footnote). Special charges & other, net: $6.4M Q4 / $39.7M FY2026 (down from $71.6M / $79.2M FY2025). Capex slashed to ~$100M FY2027 guide (asset-light pivot, outsourcing more wafers). Restructuring is winding down but not finished — GAAP guidance explicitly excludes future restructuring gains/charges.
3. **Series A Preferred Stock overhang.** $111.2M of preferred dividends in FY2026 (vs $2.2M prior year) — issued to delever during the downturn; drags GAAP common EPS. Cap-structure repair still in progress.
4. **Trough-distorted valuation metrics.** EV/EBITDA 49x and ROIC ~3.7% are NOT structural — they reflect depressed trough EBITDA/operating income. On Q1 FY2027 run-rate (op margin 33–34%) both normalize dramatically. Flag: standard EV/EBITDA scores MCHP as expensive when it is mid-recovery; the metric understates earning power here (mirror image of the rule-9 TTM-staleness problem for high-momentum names).
5. **Balance sheet still levered.** Net debt ~$5.29B; net debt/EBITDA ~4.3x on trough EBITDA (improves fast as EBITDA recovers). Cash only $240.3M. Dividend ($984M FY2026) exceeds FCF ($871M) — payout >100% of FCF, funded partly by balance sheet; watch sustainability if recovery stalls.
6. **3Y revenue CAGR is deeply negative (-17.6%)** — pure cyclical artifact: FY2023 peak $8.44B → FY2026 $4.71B trough-and-recovering. This is NOT secular decline; it's the embedded down-cycle. The 3Y CAGR metric will heavily penalize Growth on a name that is actually re-accelerating (+35% YoY now). Flag the disconnect; do not over-read the negative CAGR as a thesis break.

## Proposed subjective view (NOT written to xlsx — for collaborative session)
- **AI Thesis directness: LOW.** Genuine but minority (~7–9% of revenue) AI/DC content via PCIe switches/retimers/storage controllers, growing ~65%/yr; the other ~90%+ is embedded auto/industrial on a generic cyclical recovery. Real exposure, not a pure-play.
