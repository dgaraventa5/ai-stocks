## 1. Pre-research mental model

SNPS is the EDA duopoly co-leader (with Cadence), selling the chip-design software and IP that every advanced-node and AI-accelerator design depends on — a toll-booth on rising design complexity, which is the most defensible spot in Layer 04. The AI tailwind is twofold: (a) more/harder chips to design (AI accelerators, chiplets, custom silicon at hyperscalers) drives core EDA + IP demand, and (b) Synopsys.ai/agentic tools let Synopsys charge more per seat. Two overhangs dominate the current setup: the ~$35B Ansys acquisition closed in 2025 (large debt + share issuance + purchase-accounting amortization that will crush GAAP EPS/margins — a rule-15 candidate), and China export-control exposure (China was ~16% of revenue). Going in, I expect the underlying EDA business healthy and AI-aided, GAAP optics ugly from Ansys, and China the main thesis-break risk.

Other income flipped lower too ($32.2M vs $114.1M). → **Rule 15 applies: GAAP EPS YoY is non-operating/acquisition-accounting-dominated; the input should be blanked, not scored, if/when rated.**

**10-Q filed 2026-05-27 (balance sheet, as of 2026-04-30 vs prior balance date):**
- **Long-term debt $10,013,845** (down from $13,462,398) — meaningful deleveraging in progress (rule-3 note: this is the carrying LT-debt line; total gross debt figures in press/Yahoo coverage cited ~$10.0B current vs ~$13.5B at FY-end Q4). Total liabilities $16.412B (from $19.897B).

- China-related: a floating-rate facility "in China to support our facilities expansion" at 5-yr Loan Prime Rate + 0.74% (10-Q) — minor; the material China item is export-control revenue exposure (see §3).

**Full-year FY2026 guidance raised (8-K / Reuters, 2026-05-27):** revenue **$9.625–9.705B** (up from $9.56–9.66B); non-GAAP EPS **$14.72–14.80**; FCF **~$2.0B** expected for the year. Q3 FY2026 guide: revenue **$2.41–2.46B**, non-GAAP EPS **$3.63–3.69**, GAAP EPS **$0.84–0.98** (wide GAAP range reflects ongoing amortization noise).

**8-K filed 2026-05-27 (Item 1.01, separate filing) — Elliott activist / governance:** On 2026-05-26 Synopsys entered a **Cooperation Agreement with Elliott Investment Management**, appointing **Jesse Cohn to the Board effective 2026-06-01** (Corporate Governance & Nominating Committee; in the 2027 slate). ## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — not pre-deciding; collaborative session required, rules 2/12)

- **Rule 15 (GAAP EPS YoY) — STRONG TRIGGER:** Q2 GAAP continuing-ops EPS $0.09 vs $2.23 and 6-mo $0.43 vs $4.13 are dominated by Ansys purchase-accounting amortization (~$11.9B intangibles) and acquisition-debt interest ($133M/qtr vs $94M) — documented non-operating/one-time-driven. **EPS YoY input should be blanked; Growth then averages the revenue metrics** (cite this 10-Q in any Rating Audit entry). Do NOT score the −96% as a Growth miss.
- **Rule 14 (expectations red flag):** Stock priced as an AI-design compounder into raised guidance — run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py SNPS` before scoring to check P/S percentile vs decelerating-growth combo. (Note FY-end Oct + Ansys consolidation will distort the P/S vs revenue-growth history the script reads — interpret with caution; it may need the foreign/odd-tag honesty skip.)
- **D1–D5 (AI Thesis):** D1 directness HIGH — EDA/IP is a direct, picks-and-shovels beneficiary of AI chip design; agentic monetization adds a second AI revenue vector. D2 (customer/contract momentum) supported by new large-customer AI agreements. Moat dim strong (EDA duopoly). Net: AI-Thesis flags positive; verify against an absolute-lens, not relative ranking (rule 12).
- **Momentum (M / 50DMA %):** refresh `python3 scripts/momentum_50dma.py SNPS` — post-beat + raised guide likely supportive, but the 2025 China-ban gap and any Elliott-news volatility could muddy the 120-day trend; let the objective metric speak.
- **R3 (balance sheet) — flag DOWN:** ~$10.0B gross debt + $26.9B goodwill + $11.9B intangibles from Ansys; interest expense materially up. Deleveraging is real (target <2x in 2yr) but R3 should reflect elevated leverage + integration/impairment risk until net leverage normalizes. This is the key Risk-category change vs a pre-Ansys SNPS.
- **R2 (geography) — China:** export-control exposure (~16% rev) is back online but FY guide explicitly assumes no further restriction changes — keep R2 reflecting live re-imposition risk; it is the cleanest thesis-break vector.
- **R5 (disruption risk) — DEFAULT 5:** SNPS is Layer 04 (EDA/IP), mission-critical design infrastructure, not an agentic-AI-disruptable application-SaaS workflow. Agentic AI is a *tailwind* (Synopsys sells the agent engineers), not a threat to the core model. Per rule 16, non-Layer-10 default = 5; no sub-5 warranted.
- **Governance (no direct rubric dim):** Elliott/Jesse Cohn board seat — note in thesis/news-log; can pressure margins/capital allocation (potentially positive for shareholder returns, or signals integration concern). Watch for portfolio/cost actions.
- **THESIS-BREAK watch:** (1) China export controls re-imposed (guide assumes status quo); (2) Ansys integration trouble / goodwill or intangible impairment against the $38.7B intangible+goodwill base; (3) backlog/IP deceleration turning structural (IP −6% YoY); (4) guidance cut; (5) leverage stress / deleveraging plan slips. None tripped this pass — Q2 was a beat-and-raise; flags are forward watch-items.
