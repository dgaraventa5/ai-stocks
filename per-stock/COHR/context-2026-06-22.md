## 1. Pre-research mental model

COHR is a direct AI-datacenter optical-interconnect beneficiary — datacom transceivers (800G/1.6T for AI clusters), lasers, compound semis and materials, vertically integrated down to indium phosphide. The post-merger (II-VI + legacy Coherent) story has been about deleveraging a heavy debt load while a new CEO prunes the portfolio and redirects capital toward datacom. Entering this pass I expect strong datacom-driven revenue/margin momentum but carry an open R3 (leverage) watch and want to confirm the deleveraging trajectory and whether transceiver demand is still accelerating vs. decelerating.

## 4. Earnings transcript highlights

Source: Coherent Q3 FY2026 earnings call (held 2026-05-06; transcript via Motley Fool / Investing.com / Globe and Mail — fool.com returned HTTP 403, content captured via WebFetch summary of the same call). Numbers below are management statements; treat as company-sourced, not independently filed.

- **Bookings / backlog:** CEO Jim Anderson cited "record bookings" with orders extending into calendar 2028 and customer long-term agreements (LTAs) "to the end of the decade." Record backlog. Sequential growth this quarter "a good portion driven by the 1.6T ramp." (Q3 FY2026 call, 2026-05-06)
- **Datacenter & comms:** 75% of revenue, segment up over 40% Y/Y; demand "exceptional" and "broad-based" across multiple customers and product categories. (call, 2026-05-06)
- **Customer concentration / Nvidia:** Nvidia partnership prominent ($2B equity investment); management says they are engaged with "multiple other customers across a broad range of CPO and MPO opportunities" and expect LTAs from both hyperscalers and systems customers. (call, 2026-05-06) — FLAG: Nvidia is a named anchor customer and now a ~$2B equity holder; concentration/related-party dynamics worth tracking.
- **Leverage:** Leverage ratio dropped to **0.5x** in Q3 (from 1.7x in Q2 and 2.1x a year ago), with $162M of debt payments in the quarter; cash rose to ~$3B largely from the Nvidia investment. CFO Luther emphasized balance-sheet strengthening. (call, 2026-05-06) — NOTE the sharp 1.7x→0.5x drop is cash-driven (Nvidia $2B), not pure EBITDA growth; the deleveraging is real but the headline ratio flatters the operating story.
- **Restructuring / portfolio pruning:** Sold aerospace & defense (Q1 FY2026) and the Munich, Germany product division (Q3 FY2026); Munich was ~$25M avg quarterly revenue at gross margin "well below" corporate. New CEO redirecting capital to Datacenter & Communications. (call, 2026-05-06)
- **Gross margin trajectory:** Non-GAAP GM 39.6%, +57 bps sequentially / +105 bps Y/Y, driven by 6-inch InP yield gains, input cost reductions, pricing optimization. ~530 bps cumulative improvement over 8 quarters; reiterated Investor Day target of >42%. 6-inch capacity targeted to double next quarter and quadruple by end of 2027. (call, 2026-05-06)

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do NOT pre-decide)

- **D1 (AI thesis directness):** Datacenter & Comms = 75% of revenue, up >40% Y/Y, with 800G/1.6T transceivers sold directly into AI clusters and a named Nvidia partnership. This is among the most direct AI-datacenter optical-interconnect exposures in the watchlist. Worth examining whether the current directness rating reflects this.
- **D2/D5 (growth durability / demand visibility):** Record bookings into CY2028 and LTAs "to end of decade" argue for unusually long demand visibility; 1.6T ramp and 6-inch InP capacity 4x by end-2027 are concrete drivers. Flag for re-examination.
- **M (Momentum):** Revenue +21% Y/Y, sequential acceleration, margin expansion, GAAP swing to profit ($0.97 EPS vs prior loss), Q4 guide up — strongly positive momentum inputs. (Note: 50DMA % is a price metric refreshed separately via momentum_50dma.py; not assessed here.)
- **R3 (balance sheet / leverage — KEY RISK, prior HIGH LEVERAGE watch):** Net leverage reportedly **0.5x** in Q3 vs 1.7x Q2 vs >4.5x post-merger. The deleveraging thesis has materially played out. CAVEAT: the 1.7x→0.5x step is driven by the ~$2B Nvidia equity infusion (cash), not solely EBITDA — the operating-deleveraging story is strong but the headline ratio overstates it. Still likely a meaningful R3 improvement vs. when the row was last rated. Do NOT pre-decide; verify the leverage definition and post-Nvidia balance sheet before any rating change.
- **R5 (disruption risk):** COHR is physical optical/networking hardware + compound semis — NOT Layer 10. Per CLAUDE.md rule 16, R5 defaults to **5** (no credible agentic-AI disruption to the core revenue model). No change indicated.
- **Rule-14 expectations red flag:** COHR is a consensus AI-optics favorite trading on strong momentum — exactly the "what's priced in" risk rule 14 targets. Before any scoring pass, run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py COHR` to check whether P/S sits at the top of its 3-yr range against decelerating growth. NOT assessed in this briefing (qualitative flag only).
- **Rule-9 objective refresh caveat (TTM distortion):** Q3 capex of $547.2M and OCF of only $10.1M will distort any TTM FCF-yield / FCF-margin input downward (the 6-inch capacity build is investment, not deterioration). If/when objective inputs are refreshed, note the MRQ-vs-TTM divergence per rule 9; FCF conversion (rule 11 companion) will misfire here due to the capex spike — treat as a heavy-capex-investment case, not a red flag.
