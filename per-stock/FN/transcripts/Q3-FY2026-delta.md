# FN — Q3 FY2026 Delta Summary

**Quarter ended:** March 27, 2026
**Report date:** May 4, 2026
**Prior quarter context:** Q2 FY2026 revenue ~$1,135M; Q3 FY2025 revenue $873M

---

## 1. Optical Transceiver Demand for AI Data Centers

**What changed:** DCI revenue surged to $196.9M (+90% YoY, +38% QoQ), driven by 800G ZR product ramps for hyperscale data center interconnect. Two new 800G scale-out transceiver programs qualified and began shipping to a hyperscale customer in Q3, with ramp through FY2027. Datacom at $260.4M was supply-constrained, not demand-constrained — management explicitly stated "demand far exceeded what we were able to ship."

**Delta vs. prior quarter:** DCI acceleration is the standout. The sub-segment nearly tripled from $71.5M in Q3 FY2025 to $196.9M, signaling a structural shift in the revenue mix toward high-bandwidth AI interconnect products. Datacom declined 6% QoQ but this was entirely supply-driven (lasers, memory, ASICs) — not a demand signal.

**Implication:** FN's optical business is increasingly tilted toward AI infrastructure spend. The constraint is supply, not demand — a bullish setup for revenue recovery once component availability improves.

---

## 2. 800G to 1.6T Transition Timing

**What changed:** 800G programs are in full production and ramping with both a hyperscaler customer and multiple merchant programs (launching H2 CY2026). 1.6T transceiver production entered the production phase in H2 FY2026, with a steep ramp expected in FY2027. FN reportedly held ~100% share in 1.6T transceivers for NVIDIA's Blackwell platform as of late CY2025.

**Delta vs. prior quarter:** The 800G-to-1.6T transition is progressing on schedule. FN is not yet at the inflection point — 800G still dominates the revenue contribution — but the 1.6T pipeline is being qualified and will layer on top of (not replace) 800G volumes as data center buildout expands.

**Implication:** FN is positioned at both ends of the current upgrade cycle. The 1.6T ramp in FY2027 represents a second wave of growth that should extend the multi-year revenue trajectory beyond the current 800G cycle.

---

## 3. Customer Mix (Hyperscaler vs. Telecom)

**What changed:** Telecom hit a record $628.3M (+55% YoY), now 52% of total revenue. This reflects broad-based strength, not a single customer. DCI ($196.9M) is the fastest-growing sub-segment and is largely hyperscaler-driven. HPC revenue hit $106.7M (+25% QoQ), directly tied to a hyperscaler's product generation transition.

**Delta vs. prior quarter:** The customer mix continues to diversify. Two new hyperscaler datacom programs are in early ramp (Q4 initial volumes, full ramp FY2027). Merchant transceiver programs add further customer breadth. The HPC $150M/quarter milestone pushed out one quarter to ~Q2 FY2027, but management says the total opportunity is expanding beyond that level.

**Implication:** FN's revenue base is broadening across telecom, DCI, datacom, and HPC. The hyperscaler pipeline is deepening (two new direct programs + merchant programs), reducing single-customer concentration risk while increasing total AI-linked revenue exposure.

---

## 4. Manufacturing Capacity Utilization

**What changed:** FN is running against capacity limits — datacom supply constraints are partly internal (component availability) and partly capacity-related. Building 10 (2M sq ft, ~$3B capacity) is being fast-tracked: first floor June 2026, full completion January 2027. New Navanakorn facility acquired for $11M, adding ~$250M initial capacity. Total capacity roadmap: current ~$4.8B, expanding to ~$8.5B with all announced projects.

**Delta vs. prior quarter:** The capacity expansion cadence accelerated. Building 10 is ahead of the timeline many expected, and the Navanakorn acquisition adds optionality. The negative FCF ($-11M in Q3) reflects aggressive CapEx spending ($64M) on capacity build-out, not operational weakness.

**Implication:** FN is investing aggressively to remove the capacity bottleneck. The ROI math is compelling — management says 6 months of operating profit at full capacity covers a 2M sq ft building. The risk is execution on bringing Building 10 online on schedule while ramping multiple new programs simultaneously.

---

## 5. Additional Signals

**CPO (Co-Packaged Optics):** Minority investment in Raytech Semiconductor ($32M for ~14%). Shipping CPO products to 3 customers across scale-up and scale-out. Revenue still "largely in front of us" — this is a FY2027+ optionality play, not a current revenue driver.

**Margin trajectory:** Gross margin 12.1% (down 30 bps QoQ from FX + ramp costs). Near-term guidance is "similar to Q3" but management expects improvement as new programs mature. Operating leverage improving (OpEx 1.4% of revenue).

**Valuation context:** Stock near $713, P/E ~69x trailing. 229% gain over 12 months. The market is pricing in continued 30%+ growth — the Q3 results and Q4 guidance support that trajectory, but any demand or execution stumble at these multiples would be punished severely.
