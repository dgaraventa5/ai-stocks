## 1. Pre-research mental model

KLA is the dominant process-control (inspection & metrology) franchise in WFE — a wide-moat near-monopoly (~50%+ share of process control) with high gross margins (~60%+), a large and sticky high-margin services annuity, and rising process-control intensity as nodes shrink and advanced packaging (HBM/AI) proliferates. Key swing factors: WFE cycle direction, China revenue mix (historically high-20s to low-30s %, exposed to export controls), and advanced-packaging ramp tied to AI/HBM demand. Going in, I expected a strong, AI-levered up-cycle with China as the main offsetting risk — the question was whether guidance/margins were holding or showing the first cracks.

## 4. Earnings transcript highlights

Q3 FY2026 call (via Investing.com transcript article, 2026-04-29; speakers: CEO Rick Wallace, CFO Bren Higgins):
- **WFE 2026 raised to ">$140B"** (Higgins); KLA expects to outgrow the market with high-teens revenue growth; 2027 > 2026.
- **Advanced packaging ~$1B in 2026** (from ~$635M in 2025) — upper-50% growth, revised up on stronger-than-expected demand "over the past 90 days."
- **China / export controls**: Huawei restriction impact "fairly immaterial," already in guidance; Chinese WFE flat, greenfield-focused, trailing industry.
- **Process control >20% growth** in 2026, +360 bps share since 2021.
- **AI / customer urgency**: Wallace — "massive shortage of semiconductor capacity" through 2030; unprecedented urgency to secure slots.
- **Services +16% YoY** in Q3; long-term services growth guide 13–15%.
- **Gross margin**: ~62% ±50 bps for calendar 2026; **~100 bps headwind from elevated DRAM (memory) component costs** persisting through 2026; additional 50–100 bps tariff pressure expected to ease through the year.
- **Caution flags**: supply-chain constraints, DRAM pricing pressure through 2026, tariff cost headwinds, fab-construction timelines limiting near-term equipment absorption despite strong demand.
