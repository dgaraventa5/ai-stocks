## Evidence for D1, D2, D5

### D1 — % of revenue genuinely AI-tied TODAY (strict)
- **0% directly AI-tied.** 9M FY2026 revenue = $20.2M, 100% from one spot-market sale of purchased inventory to one undisclosed customer (Customer B) (10-Q Note 12). Q3 revenue = $0.
- No revenue from any datacenter, hyperscaler, or even disclosed-utility AI-adjacent contract. The AI connection is entirely macro: AI → electricity demand → nuclear → uranium price. Two derivative steps; UEC's own 10-Q frames AI/datacenters only as a market-fundamentals tailwind.
- Strict read: AI affects the PRICE UEC may someday realize on production, not any current revenue stream.

### D2 — Pricing power (term contracts vs unhedged spot)
- **Pure price taker by deliberate design: 100% unhedged, no term contracts, no contracted floors/escalators** (Q3 FY2026 PR, 2026-06-09). Maximum commodity beta in both directions.
- Execution evidence cuts both ways: realized $101/lb on the Q2 sale vs $80.76 quarterly average spot (good opportunistic timing, Q2 FY2026 PR), but sold nothing in Q3 as spot consolidated at $85-86 — revenue is discretionary and lumpy, not recurring.
- Cost position vs. spot: Q3 total cost $54.61/lb vs ~$86 spot implies healthy unit margin IF production scales, but volume is currently de minimis (32,195 lbs/quarter).

### D5 — Direct hyperscaler/datacenter linkage
- **None.** No supply agreements, MOUs, or partnerships with hyperscalers or datacenter operators found in the 10-Q, Q3 PR, or web searches (June 2026). Zero "hyperscale" mentions in the 10-Q.
- Closest adjacency is aspirational: UR&C conversion facility positioning UEC in the domestic fuel-cycle buildout that the AI/nuclear narrative depends on — still pre-license-application, pre-site-selection, zero revenue.
- Customers (when sales occur) are undisclosed single counterparties per period — pattern consistent with traders/utilities, not end-user power buyers.
