## 1. Pre-research mental model

Eaton is a diversified power-management company — Electrical (Americas + Global) plus Aerospace, after spinning out/separating the legacy Mobility/vehicle business. It is one of the cleanest large-cap AI-datacenter electrification plays: it sells the switchgear, busways, UPS, transformers, and power-distribution gear that goes inside data centers, and is riding the electrification + reshoring capex super-cycle with a large, growing multi-year backlog. I expect strong double-digit data-center order growth, a high-teens revenue print, raised 2026 guidance, and a premium valuation (high-20s forward P/E) — meaning much of the good news is already priced. Main risks I'd watch: order/backlog deceleration, margin compression from factory ramp costs, and a rich multiple vulnerable to any datacenter-capex wobble.

## 6. Implications for ratings (flags only — no rating numbers pre-decided)

- **D1 / D2 (AI demand directness & magnitude):** Strongly reinforced. +~240% DC orders, ~50% DC revenue growth, $13.2B Electrical Americas backlog, Boyd Thermal cooling expansion, CEO's 32 GW / 228 GW framing. Revisit upward-bias check — exposure is direct and accelerating, not decelerating.
- **D5 (catalysts / contract momentum):** Active — Boyd Thermal close, ~$11B M&A, Mobility/Dana RMT separation, new-factory ramp. Multiple near-term catalysts; revisit.
- **M (Momentum):** Mixed signal. Fundamentals/orders strongly positive, but the stock fell ~3% on the print and a multiple-compression-on-margin-miss dynamic is in play. Refresh the objective 50DMA % (rule 11, `scripts/momentum_50dma.py`) before trusting Momentum — price action diverged from the beat.
- **R1–R4 (concentration/geo/balance-sheet/regulatory):** Watch (a) **margin compression** from factory ramp + input-cost inflation (transitory per mgmt, but unproven — the one thesis-soft-spot to monitor); (b) **data-center customer/end-market concentration** risk if hyperscaler capex ever wobbles; (c) **integration risk** from ~$11B of M&A in a single quarter. No regulatory red flags surfaced.
- **R5 (Disruption Risk):** Defaults to **5** — ETN is Layer 02 physical electrical/power infrastructure, not Layer 10; no AI/agentic disruption to the core revenue model (rule 16).
- **Rule-14 expectations red flag (elevated-multiple angle):** ETN trades at a premium and a textbook beat-and-raise still drew a ~3% selloff on a margin miss — consensus is crowded and the bar is high. Run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py ETN` at scoring time (P/S vs. own 3-yr range × revenue-growth-vs-median) and read as a qualitative red flag, NOT a scored metric.
- **No EPS-YoY one-time distortion flagged** (rule 15) from this pass — Q1 growth reads operational, not divestiture-driven. The Mobility/Dana RMT may create non-operating GAAP noise in a future quarter; flag then, not now.

### THESIS-BREAK scan
**None.** No order deceleration (orders accelerating), no DC demand softening (record orders/backlog/pipeline), no guidance cut (raised), no regulatory action. The only genuine watch item is **near-term margin compression** (segment margin −120 bps YoY, Electrical Americas 25.6% below plan) — currently framed by management as transitory factory-ramp/input-cost investment with a 2H >30% exit rate. Monitor Q2 2026 to confirm the sequential margin recovery actually shows up; a failure to recover would be the first real crack.
