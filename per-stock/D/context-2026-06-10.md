# D — Pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-10)

Prepared for AI Thesis re-evaluation of Layer 1 power names. Session lens: how DIRECTLY does D own the AI power bottleneck — disclosed datacenter load share + approved rate structures vs. generic "load growth" narrative. Regulated-utility caveat applies: returns capped at allowed ROE.

---

## Pre-research mental model

D is the regulated utility serving Northern Virginia "Data Center Alley," the world's largest datacenter cluster, with a ~51 GW contracted datacenter pipeline (per context-2026-05-26.md). The dominant recent event is the 2026-05-18 NextEra all-stock acquisition (~$67B, 0.8138 exchange ratio, D holders get 25.5% of combined entity, 12-18 month close), which anchors D's price to NEE and adds a regulatory overhang. As a rate-regulated utility, D's datacenter growth flows through rate base at the Virginia SCC's allowed ROE, not merchant pricing — the open D2 question is how much scarcity value D actually captures. Q1 2026 earnings (reported ~early May) had NOT been processed into the news log before this session.

## Findings (sources)

### 1. Q1 2026 earnings (reported 2026-05-01) — beat, datacenter-driven
- Operating EPS $0.95 vs consensus $0.86 (+10.5%); revenue $5.02B vs $4.47B forecast (+12.3%). 2026 guidance maintained at $3.45–$3.69 operating EPS (midpoint $3.57); long-term operating EPS growth target 5–7% through 2030 maintained. (Investing.com Q1 2026 slides recap, 2026-05-01; Benzinga 2026-05.)
- Commercial sales +8.4%, driven primarily by datacenters; weather-normalized regulated electric sales +4.3% LTM. (Investing.com, Q1 2026 slides.)
- $65B five-year capital plan (2026–2030) maintained. (Investing.com Q1 2026 slides; Utility Dive 2026-02 on capital plan detail.)

### 2. Datacenter contracted pipeline: 51 GW as of March 2026, with stage breakdown
Per Q1 2026 earnings slides (Investing.com recap, 2026-05-01):
- **Total: ~51 GW** contracted datacenter capacity (as of March 2026), +2.5 GW vs December 2025, ~3x the 16.5 GW of July 2023.
- **Substation engineering letters of authorization: 29.5 GW** (earliest stage, ~58% of total)
- **Construction letters of authorization: 11.1 GW** (~22%)
- **Electric service agreements (ESAs): 10.4 GW** (~20%) — ESA = meter set, electricity flowing or imminent (stage definitions per DataCenterDynamics reporting on Dominion's stage framework).
- No customer names disclosed for any stage.

### 3. Datacenter share of Virginia Power electricity sales: 28% (FY2025)
- "Data centers represent 28% and 26% of Virginia Power's electricity sales for the years ended December 31, 2025 and 2024, respectively." (D 10-K FY2025, filed 2026-02-23, EDGAR accession 0001193125-26-063120.) Up from 24% in 2023 (prior 10-K via DCD).
- Virginia Power FY2025 operating revenue: $11,812M, vs $10,235M FY2024 (+15%) (D 10-K FY2025, Virginia Power MD&A). D consolidated FY2025 revenue: $16,523M (D 10-K FY2025, XBRL RevenueFromContractWithCustomerExcludingAssessedTax, CY2025).

### 4. Virginia SCC biennial review order (2025-11-25): ROE 9.8%, new GS-5 datacenter rate class
- Allowed ROE raised 9.7% → **9.8%** (company requested 10.4%). (Virginia SCC news release, 2025-11-25.)
- Base rate increases approved: $565.7M in 2026 + $209.9M in 2027 (vs $822M/$345M requested). (Virginia SCC news release, 2025-11-25.)
- **GS-5 rate class** for customers with demand ≥25 MW (datacenters), effective 2027-01-01: **14-year contract term** required; minimum payment of **85% of contracted distribution and transmission demand and 60% of generation demand** even if load doesn't materialize. (Virginia SCC news release 2025-11-25; Virginia Mercury 2025-11-25; AAF analysis; DCD.)
- Read: this is ratepayer-protection / stranded-cost insurance, not premium pricing. It de-risks D's capex but does not let D capture scarcity value above the 9.8% allowed ROE.

### 5. NEE merger — what's new since the 2026-05-26 briefing
- Companies propose a **$2.25B customer bill credit** spread over the first two years post-close, ~79% to Virginia customers. (Virginia Mercury 2026-05-29; WMRA/VCIJ 2026-06-09.)
- Virginia SCC statutory clock: must rule within 60 days of application filing, extendable by up to 120 days (max 180 days). Could not confirm an SCC application has been filed as of 2026-06-10 — FLAG. (Williams Mullen analysis; WMRA/VCIJ 2026-06-09.)
- Watchdogs (Clean Virginia, VCIJ piece 2026-06-09): combined-entity rate base of ~$138B projected to grow ~11%/yr through 2032; advocates note FPL profits = 27% of customer bills in 2025 vs ~14% national average and want ROE concessions as a merger condition. Total state+federal review could run up to 2 years.

### 6. Named-hyperscaler commitments
- **Amazon–Dominion MOU (announced 2024-10, still exploratory):** jointly explore SMR development at North Anna, ≥300 MW. It is an MOU to explore development/financing structures — not a contracted power sale with disclosed pricing. (Dominion IR press release 2024-10; Utility Dive 2024-10.)
- The 51 GW pipeline is disclosed only in aggregate; D names no datacenter customers and discloses no contract pricing (regulated tariff applies).

## Evidence for D1 (% of revenue datacenter-tied today)

- Primary disclosure: datacenters = **28% of Virginia Power electricity sales, FY2025** (D 10-K FY2025, filed 2026-02-23).
- Virginia Power operating revenue FY2025 = $11,812M = **71.5% of D consolidated revenue ($16,523M)** (both from D 10-K FY2025).
- Naive math: 28% × $11,812M ≈ $3.3B ≈ **~20% of consolidated revenue** as the UPPER BOUND.
- **FLAG (material):** "electricity sales" in the 10-K is almost certainly volume (MWh) share, not revenue share. Datacenter/large-industrial tariff revenue per MWh is below the residential/commercial average, so the revenue share is likely meaningfully below 28% of DEV revenue. Dominion does not disclose datacenter revenue in dollars. Defensible band: **mid-teens to ~20% of consolidated revenue datacenter-tied**, with the volume/revenue ambiguity flagged.
- Direction: rising fast — 24% (2023) → 26% (2024) → 28% (2025) of DEV electricity sales; commercial sales +8.4% in Q1 2026 driven by datacenters.

## Evidence for D2 (return capture vs pricing power)

- D keeps **allowed ROE of 9.8%** on Virginia rate base (SCC biennial review order, 2025-11-25; requested 10.4%, got 9.8%). The SCC also cut the requested base-rate increases by ~31% (2026) and ~39% (2027). This is the cleanest possible evidence that the regulator, not D, sets the return — scarcity value flows to ratepayers (and to merchant generators selling into PJM), not to D shareholders.
- What D DOES capture: rate-base GROWTH. $65B 5-yr capex plan (2026–2030), datacenter load as the driver, 5–7% EPS CAGR through 2030 (Q1 2026 slides). Combined NEE-D entity rate base projected ~11%/yr growth through 2032 (VCIJ/watchdog figure, 2026-06-09).
- GS-5 (eff. 2027-01): 14-year contracts + 85%/60% minimum demand payments shift stranded-asset risk onto datacenter customers — improves the RISK profile of the growth, doesn't raise the return ON it.
- Merger context cuts the other way: watchdogs are explicitly campaigning for ROE concessions as a condition of NEE deal approval (WMRA/VCIJ 2026-06-09) — i.e., the political response to datacenter-driven bills is pressure to LOWER D's return capture, the opposite of pricing power.
- Net for D2: textbook regulated capture — volume/rate-base upside, zero price upside, with visible political pressure on the allowed return.

## Evidence for D5 (named commitments, contracted GW, ESA terms)

- Contracted pipeline 51 GW (March 2026, Q1 2026 slides) by stage: 29.5 GW substation engineering LOA / 11.1 GW construction LOA / **10.4 GW ESA** (meter set). +2.5 GW QoQ; 3x since July 2023.
- **No hyperscalers are named** in any stage of the pipeline; no contract pricing disclosed (service is at regulated tariff — GS-5 from 2027).
- ESA terms that ARE disclosed (via the GS-5 order): 14-year term, 85% minimum of contracted distribution/transmission demand, 60% minimum of generation demand, ≥25 MW threshold, effective 2027-01-01 (Virginia SCC order 2025-11-25).
- Only named-hyperscaler arrangement: Amazon MOU for SMR exploration at North Anna (≥300 MW, announced 2024-10) — exploratory, no contracted power sale, no disclosed economics.
- D5 conclusion: the contracted GW is real and enormous but anonymous and tariff-priced. Versus the session lens, D scores "direct" on disclosed datacenter load share and approved rate structure, but "indirect" on premium/named contracts — there are none.

## Flags

1. **Volume vs revenue ambiguity:** 28% is share of electricity sales (likely MWh); dollar revenue share is lower and not disclosed. Don't cite "28% of revenue."
2. **Pipeline quality:** only 10.4 of 51 GW (20%) is ESA-stage; 58% is earliest-stage LOA. Headline 51 GW ≠ contracted load in the strict sense. GS-5 minimum-demand protections don't take effect until 2027-01.
3. **SCC merger application filing status unconfirmed** as of 2026-06-10 — statutory 60+120-day clock only starts at filing.
4. **Objective-input refresh check:** D reported Q1 on 2026-05-01; CLAUDE.md rule 9's 1-week refresh window has passed. Verify the Watchlist row was refreshed (the 2026-05-26 context file was merger-focused, not an earnings refresh).
5. **Merger anchoring:** D price action now tracks NEE via the 0.8138 fixed exchange ratio; standalone D fundamentals matter mainly for deal-break scenario and for the 25.5% pro-forma stake's quality.
