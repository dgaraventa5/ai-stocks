## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim)

What I currently believe about ADSK before research:
- Autodesk is the dominant design-software franchise — AutoCAD, Revit, Fusion, Maya — serving architecture/engineering/construction (AEC), manufacturing, and media. Subscription model, ~$6B revenue, high-80s/low-90s% gross margin, strong FCF, ~35-40% non-GAAP op margin.
- "Design + Make" platform strategy; AECO is the biggest and fastest-growing vertical; AutoCAD is the legacy cash cow.
- AI angle: Project Bernini (generative 3D), "Autodesk AI" features sprinkled across products. Generative design is the long-running pitch but monetization has been vague.
- Starboard Value ran an activist campaign (2024-2025) pushing for margin expansion and accountability after a non-GAAP/FCF disclosure issue and a delayed 10-K.
- Was transitioning the billings model (annual billing of multi-year contracts) which distorted billings/FCF optics for a couple of years.
- Stock has been a chronic underperformer / "value-trap"-ish quality name; high quality, modest growth, perennial "margins should be higher" debate.

**The diff that matters most:** how much of the margin-expansion story has actually landed post-Starboard, whether AI (neural CAD/agentic) is monetizable or just R&D, and where the billings transition + SEC investigation overhang now stand.

## 3. Latest filing / earnings highlights — Q1 FY2027 (ended Apr 30, 2026)

Sources: Q1 FY2027 8-K press release (SEC EDGAR accession 0000769397-26-000041, filed 2026-05-28); 10-Q (accession 0000769397-26-000044, filed 2026-05-29); Q1 FY27 earnings call transcript (Motley Fool, 2026-05-28).

- **Total revenue $1.934B, +18% YoY (+16% cc).** Beat consensus (~$1.89-1.93B). Billings $1.688B, +18% YoY.
- **GAAP operating margin 28% (+14pts YoY); non-GAAP 39% (+2pts YoY).** GAAP EPS $2.32 (vs $0.70); non-GAAP EPS $2.99 (vs $2.29). Net income $491M vs $152M. SBC $155M (guided to fall below 10% of revenue).
- **Free cash flow $876M, +58% YoY; operating cash flow $893M.** (FY27 FCF guide raised to $2.725–2.80B.)
- **RPO $7.808B (+9%); current RPO $5.383B (+18%); deferred revenue $4.457B (+13%); unbilled deferred revenue $3.351B (+4%).** RPO growth slowed by *deliberately shorter contract durations* (less multi-year discounting → better price realization) — a chosen trade-off, not demand weakness (transcript, Moorjani).
- **Product-family revenue (Q1):** AECO **$970M, +20%**; AutoCAD/AutoCAD LT $474M, +15%; Manufacturing $367M, +19%; Media & Entertainment $86M, +13%. By end-market: **Design $1.612B (+18%); Make $224M (+25%)**. By geography: Americas $844M (US $682M = 35% of total), EMEA $761M (39%), APAC $329M (17%) — **international is a majority** of revenue.
- **FY2027 guidance raised:** revenue **$8.155–8.215B**; billings **$8.505–8.58B**; non-GAAP op margin **~39%**; non-GAAP EPS **$12.40–12.65**; GAAP EPS $8.07–8.63; FCF **$2.725–2.80B**. (All *excludes* MaintainX.) Billing-model transition tailwind: +1.5% billings / +3.5% rev in Q1, fading to ~1.5% full-year.
- **MaintainX acquisition (subsequent event, signed 5/28/26):** **$3.6B all-cash** — Autodesk's **largest acquisition ever** — for a modern maintenance/asset-operations SaaS (>$135M ARR in 2026, growing >50%). Strategic logic: extends platform from "design + make" to **"design, make, *operate*"**, ~$40B TAM expansion, asset-condition data for predictive maintenance/digital twins. Expected close by FY27 year-end (Jan 2027). (Yahoo Finance; 10-Q Note 19; transcript.) **Balance-sheet impact:** ADSK is ~net-cash today; a $3.6B cash deal will draw cash + likely add debt, flipping net cash to modest net debt — but ~$2.7B annual FCF makes it digestible within ~2 years.

**Legacy overhang — Internal Investigation + securities class action (10-Q, Legal Proceedings / Note 14):** In **March 2024** the Audit Committee commenced an internal investigation into Autodesk's **free-cash-flow and non-GAAP-operating-margin practices** (channel/contract-timing optimization). A **federal securities class action** (Barkasi, N.D. Cal., filed 4/24/2024) names the company, CEO Anagnost, and former CFO Deborah Clifford. This was the catalyst for both the delayed 10-K and the Starboard campaign. Still disclosed as pending litigation in the Q1 FY27 10-Q (loss "either immaterial or not estimable"). The cleanup of the very practices under scrutiny is part of why current FCF/margin disclosure is being run conservatively.

**Key tension (the crux for ADSK):** This is a high-quality, wide-moat design-software near-monopoly (92% GM, ~39% non-GAAP op margin, ~36% TTM FCF margin, net-cash pre-MaintainX) that the activist-driven margin thesis is *validating in real time*, trading at the bottom of its own 3-year valuation range with the CEO and CFO personally buying on the dip. The bull case is "de-rated quality compounder + margin self-help + optionality on AI/operate." The risks the cheapness is pricing: (1) a still-pending SEC-adjacent investigation + securities class action, (2) AI as a *cost/feature* with no proven incremental monetization (and a long-tail threat to seat-based licensing), (3) a $3.6B leveraging acquisition into a lower-margin adjacency, and (4) the perennial question of whether design-software TAM growth can sustain mid-teens. Where ADSK is **weak for this project specifically:** it is NOT an AI-infrastructure-buildout name — it is a *consumer* of AI compute and an AI-applications play. Its direct exposure to the hyperscaler capex supercycle (the project's thesis) is essentially zero. Score the AI Thesis category on application-AI directness, not buildout exposure.

## 6. Implications for ratings (flags only — not pre-deciding)

- **D1 AI Revenue %:** AI is woven through products (Neural CAD, Assistant, agentic) but **no AI revenue disclosed** and it is clearly <5% of revenue today. Marketing-forward, financials don't yet show it. Lean **1–2**.
- **D2 Supply Chain Position:** Layer-10 application vendor; near-monopoly in CAD/BIM with deep workflow lock-in and pricing power (deliberately shortening contracts to *raise* realization). But it's a software app, not an upstream bottleneck in the AI-buildout chain. Lean **3**.
- **D3 Moat — strong here:** AutoCAD/Revit file-format + workflow lock-in, education-pipeline network effect, 40+ year install base, switching costs across whole AEC/mfg workflows. Among the most durable software moats. Halo-check, but evidence supports **4**. AI is a *threat vector* to seat-based licensing (watch), not yet a moat-breaker.
- **D4 Capacity Expansion:** Read as product/AI investment for software — heavy AI R&D, platform build, $3.6B MaintainX TAM expansion. Lean **4**.
- **D5 Hyperscaler/AI-infra commitment:** Autodesk is a *consumer* of cloud compute, not an AI-infrastructure investor; no hyperscaler revenue (supplier framework). Lean **1**.
- **M1 EPS revisions:** Beat + raised FY27 guide across the board; estimates moving up. Lean **4** (verify Yahoo/Zacks revision tally at rating time).
- **M2 Relative strength vs IGV:** −34% over 6mo vs roughly flat-to-up software sector → severe underperformance. Lean **1–2**.
- **M3 Insider:** **CEO + CFO + Director all bought open-market on the dip → 5, full stop** (M3 step 1). High confidence.
- **R1 Customer concentration:** No end-customer >10%; TD Synnex (distributor) = 9% of revenue but that's channel, not demand concentration. Diversified. Lean **5**.
- **R2 Geographic/export:** International is a majority (EMEA 39%, APAC 17%), US 35%. No single geographic shock plausibly impairs >10% via a strait/conflict; some China/emerging-market and tariff sensitivity noted in 10-Q but not concentrated. Worst plausible shock: broad EMEA recession (~39% of rev, but diversified across countries). Lean **4**.
- **R3 Balance sheet:** Net cash today (ND/EBITDA −0.09x), ~$2.7B annual FCF, but **$3.6B all-cash MaintainX pending** will add leverage. Maturities spread ($500M 2028, $500M 2030, $1.5B thereafter). Still strong but no longer pristine-net-cash. Lean **4** (would be 5 pre-deal).
- **R4 Regulatory/litigation:** **Pending Audit-Committee internal investigation into FCF/non-GAAP practices + securities class action (Barkasi)** naming CEO and former CFO. Material disclosed litigation/regulatory overhang, not yet resolved. Lean **2–3** (lean 2 given the active securities class action + investigation are precisely about financial-reporting integrity).
