## 1. Pre-research mental model

Equinix is the world's largest interconnection-led colocation REIT — IBX data centers plus Equinix Fabric (virtual interconnection) plus xScale hyperscale JVs — with the deepest, hardest-to-replicate network/cloud ecosystem moat in the industry and a 90+ consecutive-quarter revenue-growth streak. The AI angle is real but second-order vs. a pure GPU-cloud play: AI shows up as (a) inference/private-AI enterprise deployments landing in IBX footprints at higher power density, and (b) xScale hyperscale training capacity via the JV structure that keeps the heaviest capex off Equinix's own balance sheet. The 2025 "Build Bolder" analyst day raised capex and growth ambitions materially, which introduces a near-term AFFO-per-share dilution-vs-growth tension and a rising cost of debt. Valuation has historically been rich (premium REIT multiple), so the question for ratings is whether bookings/MRR-per-cabinet momentum and the AI density tailwind justify the multiple, not whether the franchise is durable (it is).

## 6. Implications for ratings

Flags only — do NOT pre-decide rating numbers; this feeds a collaborative rating session.

**AI Thesis (D1–D5):**
- **D1 (directness of AI exposure):** Revisit upward bias check — AI now ~60% of largest deals, +33% density. But still a landlord/enabler, not compute — keep the "indirect/enabling" calibration honest; do not over-credit.
- **D2 (demand durability / growth runway):** Strengthened — AI-inference + private-AI enterprise demand broadening beyond hyperscalers; xScale capacity expanding. Revisit.
- **D5 (pricing power / competitive position):** Strengthened — MRR/cabinet +7%, 500K+ interconnections, low churn = pricing power intact. Revisit.
- D3/D4: no new signal; leave unless rating session digs deeper.

**Momentum (M):**
- Subjective M dimensions (EPS revisions → read as AFFO revisions): guidance RAISED, AFFO/share +12% → positive revision signal. Revisit EPS-Revisions and Relative-Strength subjective inputs.
- Objective 50DMA% is script-driven (`momentum_50dma.py`) — not part of this briefing; refresh at scoring time.

**Risk (R1–R5):**
- **R3 (balance sheet):** Watch — Build Bolder adds ~$8B debt and pushes cost of debt 3.4%→~4.9%; leverage rising to fund capex. This is the dimension most likely to move (modestly weaker) on the funding plan. Revisit.
- **R5 (disruption risk): defaults to 5** — physical infrastructure / interconnection / power; not exposed to agentic-AI software disruption. No sub-5 R5 (Layer 03, not Layer 10). Confirm 5.
- R1/R2/R4: diversified customer base (interconnection model is inherently fragmented), global footprint — no new adverse signal.

**CRITICAL data-quality caveat (REIT economics):**
- **EPS-based Quality/Growth inputs and EV/EBITDA Value input are UNRELIABLE for EQIX.** GAAP EPS and net income are depreciation-distorted; the xScale JV structure further muddies consolidated metrics. **AFFO / AFFO-per-share is the correct lens** for both growth (AFFO/share +12% YoY, guided +10–12%) and value (price-to-AFFO ≈ 25–27x at ~$1,100 share vs. $42–43 AFFO/share — premium but in normal historical range). Any scoring pass that uses EPS YoY or EV/EBITDA off-the-shelf for EQIX will misprice it — flag and substitute AFFO-based reasoning, analogous to the rule-10 (Layer-10 EV/FCF) and rule-13 (Layer-9 EV/MW) garbage-input corrections. **There is no codified REIT carve-out in the rubric** — this is a manual data-quality override for the rating session, not a methodology change.
- **Rule-14 / "what's priced in":** formal `expectations_flag` is CLEAN (growth at median, not below), but P/S is at the 100th percentile of its 3-yr range — the multiple is maxed. Couple with capex-ramp/AFFO-dilution tension when judging whether Total Score is rewarding a crowded, fully-priced name. Qualitative flag for the briefing, not a scored input.

**Capex-ramp / AFFO-dilution tension (central judgment for this name):** Build Bolder is ~$4.1B FY26 capex funded substantially with new debt at rising cost (4.9%). The bull case is that AFFO/share is STILL guided +10–12% and was raised — dilution is not yet showing through, and xScale accretion (now 4–5%) offsets. The bear case is that the debt cost step-up and capex intensity compress future AFFO/share growth and that the stock already prices the success. This is the live debate for the rating session, not a settled flag.

**THESIS-BREAK scan:** NONE triggered. Bookings +9% (decel in rate but lumpy, not a break); churn LOW (1.7%); MRR/cabinet UP (+7%); AFFO/share guidance RAISED (not cut); funding is debt (rising cost, watch — but not dilutive equity); no oversupply/pricing-pressure or regulatory signal found. Monitor bookings rate and cost-of-debt execution next quarter.
