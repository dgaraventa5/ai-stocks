# AAON — Refresh-Context Briefing (2026-06-05)

Layer: 03 Data Center Construction, REITs & Cooling. Business: semi-custom HVAC (AAON Oklahoma rooftop units, AAON Coil Products) + data-center liquid/air-side cooling via BASX (AAON Data Center Solutions).

---

## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim)

> AAON is a mid-cap semi-custom HVAC manufacturer (rooftop units, air handlers) historically serving commercial/industrial buildings. My stale view: ~$1.2B revenue, strong margins for an HVAC name, recently pivoted into data-center cooling via its BASX subsidiary (Oregon-based, modular/cleanroom heritage) rebranded as AAON Data Center Solutions. I believe data-center backlog was ramping sharply in 2024-2025 with hyperscaler liquid-cooling orders, but base commercial HVAC was softening (destocking, refrigerant transition to A2L). I'm uncertain on the exact data-center segment size, current backlog level, latest quarter's margin trend, and how concentrated the hyperscaler exposure is.

## 2. Latest filing highlights (10-Q Q1 2026, filed 2026-05-07)

Source: 10-Q for period ended 2026-03-31, SEC EDGAR accession 0000824142-26-000036.

- **Net sales $496.9M**, up 54.3% YoY (vs $322.1M Q1 2025). Segment mix (10-Q segment table):
  - AAON Oklahoma: $244.0M (rooftop units) — up from $161.8M
  - AAON Coil Products: $117.6M
  - BASX: $135.4M — up from $66.2M (≈2x)
- **By brand:** AAON-branded $268.4M (+41.6%); **BASX-branded $228.6M (+72.4%)**. BASX-branded products are produced across both BASX (Redmond OR) and AAON Coil Products (Longview TX) facilities.
- **Net income $39.8M; diluted EPS $0.48** (vs $0.35; +37.1%). Pretax income $52.1M. Effective tax rate 23.6% (vs 9.8% prior-year, which had larger excess-tax-benefit offset).
- **Backlog (record):** Total **$2,129.5M, +107.4% YoY** ($1,026.9M Q1 2025), +16.5% sequential vs $1,828.5M at 2025-12-31.
  - AAON-branded backlog: $509.8M
  - **BASX-branded backlog: $1,619.6M, +160.0% YoY** (+$996.6M); +24.4% sequential. "Most of these orders were associated with the BASX-branded data center" products. (10-Q, Backlog section)
- **Gross margin compressed:** consolidated **GM 25.1% vs 26.8%** prior year. Drivers: Memphis plant overhead (~$9.8M, all Memphis sales are intercompany at cost, booked to BASX segment), and BASX outsourcing costs offsetting volume leverage. AAON Oklahoma GM up to 26.3% on price + Tulsa volume. (10-Q MD&A)
- **Balance sheet shift — now levered:** Revolver borrowings outstanding **$425.2M** (up from $398.3M at YE2025) against a $600M revolver; only $173.5M availability left. Cash $13K (essentially zero). Interest expense $5.1M (up from $2.8M). Capex $52.9M in Q1 for capacity. Term loan also outstanding (entered Dec 2024). This is a notable change for a name that was historically debt-free.
- **AI framing (management's own words, MD&A):** "Our BASX brand is heavily dependent on the data center market. The growing maturity and adoption of Artificial Intelligence and high-performance compute is driving profound innovation..." BASX described as serving "the rapidly growing hyperscale data center market."
- **Customer concentration:** No single-customer >10% disclosure in the 10-Q (AAON discloses concentration only in the annual 10-K; historically no customer >10%). BASX serves "a more concentrated customer base." Flag: actual hyperscaler concentration % not disclosed in this filing.

## 5. DIFF — what changed since my training

- **Revenue scale is much bigger than my "~$1.2B" model.** Q1 alone is $497M; FY2026 guided +40-45% implies a ~$2.4-2.5B run-rate. AAON re-rated to an **$11.6B large-cap** (I had it as mid-cap).
- **Data-center is now the dominant growth engine, not a side project.** BASX-branded = 46% of Q1 revenue and **76% of total backlog** ($1.62B of $2.13B). Backlog +107% YoY is far stronger than my "ramping" mental model.
- **Base commercial HVAC recovered, not soft.** AAON Oklahoma +51% ($162M→$244M); the A2L refrigerant transition that hurt Q1 2025 is lapped.
- **Margin DOWN, not "strong for HVAC."** GM compressed to 25.1% (from 26.8%) on Memphis overhead + BASX outsourcing — a growing-pains signal. FY guide 27-28% implies recovery H2.
- **Balance sheet flipped from net-cash to levered.** ~$425M revolver drawn, ~$0 cash, rising interest expense. New for this historically conservative name; funds the capacity buildout.
- **CEO changed** (Tobolski replaced Fields May 2025) — I had Fields as CEO.

- **D5 Hyperscaler exposure** — UP but UNVERIFIED on concentration %. Backlog is overwhelmingly data-center/hyperscale, but exact named-customer concentration not disclosed in 10-Q.
- **M1 EPS revisions** — strongly positive (beat-and-raise, 3 up/0 down). High.
- **M2 Relative strength** — at all-time highs, large outperformance; strong.
- **M3 Insider** — heavy director selling (Fields + others), no buys; post-CEO-transition diversification context. Likely the "routine/diversification" band, watch for 10b5-1 vs discretionary.
- **R1 Customer concentration** — RISK UP. Backlog concentrated in a few large data-center orders; management acknowledges "increased near-term customer concentration." Unquantified.
