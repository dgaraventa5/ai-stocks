## 1. Pre-research mental model

AMZN is a hyperscaler where AWS is the AI growth engine bolted onto a much larger, lower-margin retail + advertising business. The AI thesis lives almost entirely in AWS (cloud re-acceleration, Bedrock, custom Trainium/Inferentia silicon, and the multi-billion Anthropic stake), so consolidated metrics blend a 30%+ AI-cloud business with slow-growth retail and will understate the AI signal. Key watch is whether the massive AI-datacenter capex ramp converts to durable AWS growth and margin rather than dragging consolidated FCF; thesis-break candidates are AWS growth deceleration, AWS margin compression, capex-without-return, or a guidance cut. `thesis.md` is the untouched template — this name has never been per-name research-backed (rule 12 gate concern).

## 2. Latest filing highlights

Primary source: 10-Q for quarter ended March 31, 2026, filed 2026-04-30 (accession 0001018724-26-000014); earnings 8-K filed 2026-04-29. All figures below are from the 10-Q financial statements unless noted. Calendar fiscal year (Q1 = quarter ended Mar 31, 2026).

**AWS (the AI driver) — lead metrics:**
- AWS net sales **$37,587M** in Q1 2026 vs **$29,267M** Q1 2025 = **+28.4% YoY** (10-Q, segment footnote + product/service breakout). Fastest AWS growth rate in ~15 quarters (corroborated by press, §3).
- AWS operating income **$14,161M** vs $11,547M PY = +22.6% YoY (10-Q segment table).
- AWS segment operating margin = 14,161 / 37,587 = **37.7%** (computed from 10-Q; vs 11,547/29,267 = 39.5% PY → ~180bps YoY margin compression, watch item — heavy AI capacity/depreciation ramp the likely cause).

**Consolidated:**
- Consolidated net sales **$181,519M** vs $155,667M PY = **+16.6% YoY** (10-Q).
- Consolidated operating income **$23,852M** vs $18,405M PY = +29.6% YoY; consolidated operating margin = 23,852/181,519 = **13.1%** (record, computed from 10-Q).
- Product/service mix (10-Q): Online stores $64,254M (+11.9%), Third-party seller services $41,578M (+13.9%), Advertising services **$17,243M (+23.9%** YoY, from $13,921M), Subscription $13,427M (+14.6%), AWS $37,587M (+28.4%).

**EPS quality flag (rule 14 / rule 15 relevant):**
- Total non-operating income was **$15,982M** in Q1 2026 vs $3,274M PY (10-Q). The bulk is pre-tax gains on the Anthropic investment (~$16.8B per §3 press). This non-operating swing dominates the EPS beat (diluted EPS ~$2.78 vs ~$1.63 consensus, §3) — **GAAP EPS YoY is non-operating-dominated this quarter** and would score garbage on a Growth EPS-YoY input (rule 15 blanking convention applies if/when scored). Operating income (+29.6%) is the cleaner growth read.

**Capex / FCF tension (lead watch):**
- Purchases of property and equipment (cash flow statement): **$44,203M** in Q1 2026 vs $25,019M Q1 2025 (10-Q) — +77% YoY. TTM purchases of property and equipment **$151,003M** vs $93,093M prior TTM (10-Q).
- D&A of PP&E/content/leases $18,945M Q1 (vs $14,262M PY); SBC $4,032M Q1 (10-Q).
- Implication: capex (~$44B/qtr) is now running far above quarterly D&A (~$19B) and consumes most/all of operating cash flow — statement-based FCF is thin/negative pre-working-capital. This is the central hyperscaler tension: AI-datacenter spend vs cash generation. FCF-yield and any FCF-based Value input will read poorly and must be interpreted as "investment phase," not deterioration (rule 11 FCF-conversion qualitative flag context).
