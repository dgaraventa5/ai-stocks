## 4. AI thesis, business model & the auto-vs-AI split — the key findings

**The central fact: Tesla today is ~73% an automaker by revenue and ~100% an automaker by realized profit; the AI thesis is entirely optionality.** The Street explicitly prices it as such.

### How much of the valuation is AI optionality?
- Market cap ~$1.49T at a P/E >300 trailing / 158x forward. Street target range is extraordinarily polarized: **bull ~$600+ (Wedbush/Ives: "AI and autonomous opportunity worth at least $1 trillion alone"), bear ~$119** (i.e., roughly the auto-only value).
- One framing: ~**$750B of the ~$1.5T cap is an explicit AI/autonomy premium** (FSD data moat + Cybercab + robotaxi). **ARK Invest projects robotaxi could be ~90% of Tesla's enterprise value by 2029.** A separate analyst models robotaxi revenue at $250B by 2035 (assumes 30% AV penetration, 50% Tesla share, $1/mi).
- Translation for scoring: **the AI thesis is the bull case for the *multiple*, not a current revenue stream.** D1 (AI revenue % of total) must reflect realized revenue ≈ 0%, even though the *narrative* is the whole stock. This is the honest-rubric tension the framing prompt warns about.
- Source: intellectia.ai, ibtimes.com.au, tradingkey.com, kalkine.com, fool.com (2026 coverage); Wedbush/Ives; ARK Invest.

### FSD / Robotaxi — commercial status (the crux, verified to 2026)
- **Robotaxi is now commercially live and partially unsupervised**, a real step beyond my mental model: full commercial service in **Austin** (launched 2025-11-18), **Dallas & Houston** (launched 2026-04-18, "fully unsupervised, uncrewed rides"). SF Bay Area = employee testing. ~**135 robotaxis** total as of Dec 2025; **unsupervised** integration began Jan 2026.
- **BUT scale is tiny and regulation is the binding constraint:** active *unsupervised* fleet ≈ **20 cars** (mid-2026). Las Vegas permit request covers 5,000 vehicles + both airports — aspiration vs ~20-car reality.
- **Safety/regulatory overhang is material and escalating:** **NHTSA Engineering Analysis EA26002 opened 2026-03-18, covering ~3.2M vehicles** — "the step NHTSA takes immediately before issuing a recall demand" — over FSD camera-degradation (sun glare/fog/particulates) detection. Tesla reported **14 crashes across ~800,000 Austin robotaxi miles through Feb 2026** (~1 per 57,000 mi). A Reuters investigation (2026-05-28) found Tesla's own AI trainers distrust FSD safety stats; lawmakers urged NHTSA action; Tesla has resisted turning over FSD traffic-violation data (two extensions). Camera-only (no lidar/radar) approach is the contested bet.
- Source: Wikipedia/Tesla Robotaxi; techtimes.com (2026-06-16); electrek.co (2026-02-23, 2026-05-28); nai500.com (2026-03); seekingalpha.com; cbsnews.com.

### AI silicon — Dojo is DEAD; pivot to AI5/AI6 in-house inference chips
- **Project Dojo (training supercomputer) was discontinued in August 2025.** The framing prompt's "Dojo training compute" premise is **obsolete** — Tesla killed it to concentrate resources on AI5/AI6 and is evaluating a "terafactory."
- **AI5** in-house inference chip **taped out April 2026** (~2 years behind schedule); Musk called the tape-out "almost existential." Claimed ~40x perf over predecessor / "5x AI4 compute"; targets "lowest cost silicon and best perf/watt" for inference on <250B-param models. **Manufactured by TSMC (Arizona) + Samsung (Texas)** — Tesla signed a **$16.5B multiyear Samsung** chip contract. Samples 2026, volume 2027.
- Implication: Tesla's AI-silicon story is now an **in-house edge-inference** play (for FSD + Optimus), NOT a training-compute/Dojo play, and NOT merchant silicon it sells. It is a *consumer* of TSMC/Samsung foundry capacity, not an upstream supplier. (Relevant to D2/D5 — TSLA is demand-side, not a supply-chain bottleneck.)
- Source: electrek.co (2026-04-15); tomshardware.com; tweaktown.com; techpowerup.com; wccftech.com; carboncredits.com.

### Optimus (humanoid robotics) — the biggest, latest-stage optionality
- April 2026: Musk plans first-gen production lines in **Fremont for 1M robots/yr**, with a Gigafactory Texas 2nd-gen line targeting **10M/yr from 2027**. Gen 3 not yet revealed ("unveiled closer to production start"); **Gen 3 requires the AI5 chip** for practical utility. Musk: Optimus "potentially the biggest product ever."
- **Reality check:** no disclosed units built/deployed, **no revenue**, no firm Gen-3 spec. These are forward targets from a CEO with a documented history of missed autonomy timelines (Wikipedia maintains a "List of predictions for autonomous Tesla vehicles by Elon Musk"). Treat as pure optionality.
- Source: Wikipedia/Optimus (robot); Musk 2026-04-22 statements.

### Energy storage — real, growing, NOT AI
- 8.8 GWh deployed Q1'26; FY2024 31.4 GWh (+113% YoY), $10.1B FY2024 revenue. A genuine high-growth business but it is grid/storage, not AI. Counts toward auto-side fundamentals, not the AI thesis.

**Assessment of AI directness:** **Real but unrealized.** Tesla has the largest real-world driving dataset and a vertically integrated autonomy + edge-silicon + robotics stack — a credible, large AI optionality. But (a) AI revenue today ≈ 0% of P&L, (b) the autonomy rollout is ~20 unsupervised cars against an active pre-recall NHTSA analysis, (c) Dojo (the training-compute leg) is cancelled, (d) Optimus has zero revenue and a poor timeline track record. **A defensible MID score, not a forced-high one** — exactly as the framing instructed. Do not inflate D-dimensions to justify inclusion.
