## 1. Pre-research mental model

SNOW is the consumption-priced cloud data platform — the value object is data gravity + governance, and the metrics that matter are product revenue growth, net revenue retention (NRR), and RPO (forward visibility), not seat counts. Entering this pass my prior was that growth had decelerated into the high-20s%, NRR was drifting down toward ~125%, and the AI question was open: does agentic/Cortex AI add consumption (more queries, more compute) or cannibalize it (more efficient queries)? My working hypothesis was that consumption-priced data infra is a net beneficiary of AI workloads (R5 4, durable), with the genuine risk being Databricks taking share rather than AI eating the model. The thesis.md is the untouched template, so this name has never had a research-backed write-up.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not finalize)

- **Value — CRITICAL Rule 10:** the Watchlist Value/"EV/EBITDA" column for SNOW holds **EV/FCF on SaaS bands**, NOT EV/EBITDA. GAAP op loss ($326.2M) and GAAP net loss ($295.6M) confirm EBITDA is garbage here. Use TTM/forward adjusted FCF (Q1 $265.5M/19.1%; ~23% FY guide) for EV/FCF. Re-pull EV and TTM FCF at scoring time. The post-print dip suggests valuation is rich → likely scores low on absolute Value bands.
- **Rule 14 (expectations flag):** beat-and-raise but stock fell → consensus is crowded. Run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py SNOW` at scoring time (P/S ≥90th pct of own range + growth below 3-yr median). Note growth *accelerated*, which may keep the growth leg from tripping — but the P/S leg is the one to watch. Qualitative flag for the briefing only.
- **D1 (AI revenue directness / attach):** strong and rising. >9,100 accounts on AI weekly; Cortex Code >50% adoption, >2,500 Snowflake Intelligence accounts; mgmt attributes the guide-raise primarily to AI. AI is now a directly attributable consumption driver, not an aspiration. Argues for a high AI-Thesis directness reading.
- **D2/D5 (catalysts/moat):** AWS $6B/5-yr GTM commitment; "agentic control plane" positioning; governance + data gravity moat. Catalyst-positive.
- **Growth:** +34% product revenue, accelerating; NRR 126%. Use revenue metrics; EPS YoY is non-GAAP-only positive but GAAP remains a loss — growth leg should lean on product-revenue growth (cite the +34%).
- **Momentum (M):** growth re-accel + raise are positive, but the stock dipped post-print — refresh `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` for the objective 50DMA % leg before rating; don't infer momentum from fundamentals alone.
- **SaaS renewal-risk watch (MEMORY: project_saas_renewal_risk):** NRR 126% and *rising*, AI attach broad and growing — for a consumption (not seat-based) model, the AI-renewal-cliff thesis looks like a *tailwind* here, not a risk. Track NRR trend quarterly.
- **R5 Disruption Risk (CRITICAL Rule 16 — proposed, NOT finalized): propose R5 = 4 (durable; AI net-tailwind to a consumption model).** Reasoning: SNOW is consumption-priced, so the standard agentic-AI threat to *seat-based* SaaS does not apply — more AI agents/queries = more compute consumed = more SNOW revenue, which the Q1 numbers and management commentary directly corroborate (Cortex Code as the #1 guide-raise driver; NRR up). It is not a 5 (Rule 16 reserves 5 for non-Layer-10 physical/infra durability, and a residual risk exists: AI-driven query-efficiency could compress per-query consumption, and Databricks is a genuine structural competitor for the same AI-data workloads). It is clearly not a 2/3 (no evidence the core revenue model is a direct agentic target or eroding — the opposite). The live downside to monitor for a future R5 downgrade: evidence that AI makes queries *cheaper per unit of value* faster than volume grows (consumption deflation), or Databricks taking the AI-workload share that would otherwise drive SNOW consumption. Apply the Rule-16 whole-Layer-10 absolute-lens stress test before finalizing.

**THESIS-BREAK watch (none triggered this pass):** NRR/consumption deceleration (currently *accelerating* — clear), AI-driven query-efficiency cannibalization (no evidence; AI is additive so far), guidance cut (opposite — raised), competitive share loss to Databricks (DBX growing faster and poised to pass SNOW in scale — the one item to watch, but SNOW growth re-accelerated and is far more profitable). No thesis-break.
