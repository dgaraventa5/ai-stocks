## 1. Pre-research mental model

*Written before pulling any data, per refresh-context protocol.*

Snowflake is the dominant cloud data warehouse platform, sitting at the intersection of data infrastructure and AI. My prior mental model:

- Revenue model is consumption-based (not seat-license), making it more volatile but also more tied to actual compute usage
- Sridhar Ramaswamy replaced Frank Slootman as CEO in early 2024; thesis was that Ramaswamy (ex-Google Ads, ex-Neeva) would accelerate the AI product roadmap
- Most recent reported quarter per training: somewhere in FY2026 — likely showing ~26-29% product revenue growth, decelerating from 30%+ peaks
- Key AI product: Cortex (LLM/AI feature set within the Snowflake platform); Snowpark for Python ML workloads
- NRR had been declining from 170%+ peaks toward ~124%; a major concern was whether Snowflake would stabilize or continue decelerating
- Databricks was the primary competitive threat — winning AI/ML workloads, growing fast on Mosaic AI stack
- Valuation had compressed significantly from IPO highs; SNOW had traded at steep premium to peers
- Moat: deep enterprise data gravity (customers store PBs of data in Snowflake), switching costs are high
- My uncertainty: (1) whether AI products would meaningfully accelerate revenue growth, (2) whether NRR would stabilize

## 3. WebSearch findings

### Finding 1: Revenue re-acceleration is real and broad-based
- Growth rate: 26% (Q1 FY2026) → 30% (Q4 FY2026) → 34% (Q1 FY2027)
- This is three consecutive quarters of acceleration — not a single beat
- Sources: Zacks/TradingView (2026-05-28), Yahoo Finance (2026-05-28)

### Finding 2: CoCo (Cortex Code) is the primary AI monetization driver
- Active in >7,100 accounts
- CFO Robins explicitly stated it was the "largest single driver" to the $180M guidance raise
- This is the first time a specific AI product has been called out as a primary financial driver, not just a platform feature
- Source: TradingView/MarketBeat earnings call highlights (2026-05-28)

### Finding 3: AWS $6B deal is a structural cost and partnership milestone
- New 5-year, ~$6B AWS commitment — more than doubled prior agreement
- Reduces Snowflake's data egress costs for customers; accelerates joint AI go-to-market
- Stock +37% on earnings day (May 28, 2026)
- Source: 24/7 Wall St. (2026-05-28): https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/05/28/snowflake-explodes-37-on-6-billion-amazon-deal-as-ceo-calls-q1-an-ai-inflection-point/

### Finding 4: Databricks competitive pressure remains real
- Databricks ARR: ~$5.4B, +65% YoY
- Analysts note Databricks winning ~70% of incremental AI/ML budget
- Snowflake's response: governance + enterprise data security; "agentic enterprise" framing
- The two platforms increasingly compete on AI workloads; co-existence could shift to displacement
- Source: Prior session research synthesis (2026-06-05)

### Finding 5: OpenAI $200M partnership signals platform positioning
- Snowflake is positioning as the governed enterprise data layer for OpenAI deployments
- This differentiates from Databricks (which has its own model stack/DBRX)
- Source: Search synthesis (2026-06-05)
