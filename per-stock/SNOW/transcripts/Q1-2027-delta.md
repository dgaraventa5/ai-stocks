## 1. Management tone on AI demand

**Shift: Meaningfully more confident — framed as "inflection point"**

Q1 FY2027 is the first quarter where AI (Cortex, specifically CoCo) became the stated primary driver of a guidance raise, not just a qualitative story. Prior scoring assumed AI was an emerging contributor; it is now moving into a structurally significant revenue line. Ramaswamy's "agentic enterprise" framing is a deliberate pivot from Snowflake being a data warehouse to being the infrastructure layer for AI agents.

CFO Robins labeled CoCo the "largest single driver" to the FY2027 $180M guidance raise. That is a quantitative claim that AI consumption is accelerating, not a narrative overlay.

## 3. AI-revenue mix change

**AI revenue is now explicitly material and accelerating**

- Cortex AI active in >9,100 accounts
- ~$100M annualized AI revenue run-rate (Cortex-related) — not disclosed as a % of total but implies ~1.8–1.9% of $5.34B annualized product revenue
- CoCo (Cortex Code) active in >7,100 accounts — the highest-velocity AI product
- Snowflake Intelligence adoption doubled QoQ

The ~$100M run-rate is a baseline; trajectory matters more. If CoCo drives the guidance raise cadence, the AI revenue mix will compound quickly. The current scoring AI Rev = 3 may need revisiting — I'd flag this as potentially underrating current trajectory. **See Section 7 for proposed scoring change.**

## 4. Capex / capacity / lead-time changes

**Not directly applicable (Snowflake is software/consumption, not hardware)**

## 9. Overall assessment

The Q1 FY2027 results **support and strengthen the thesis** that Snowflake is inflecting on AI revenue adoption. The primary concern at last scoring was whether AI was a real revenue driver or just a product narrative. The $180M guidance raise attributed explicitly to CoCo, the NRR re-acceleration, and the AWS deal collectively move SNOW from "potential AI beneficiary" to "demonstrating AI monetization."

The main risk that hasn't changed: Databricks competition. Snowflake is winning on governance and enterprise data security; Databricks is winning on raw ML/AI workloads. This is a co-existence scenario right now, but the next 2 quarters will indicate whether SNOW can maintain 30%+ growth as Databricks scales.

Net: thesis intact, conviction trending higher.
