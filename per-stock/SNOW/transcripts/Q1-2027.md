## CFO Opening: Financial Results (Brian Robins)

**Product revenue:** $1.334 billion, +34% YoY — accelerating from 30% in Q4 FY2026 and 26% in Q1 FY2026. Strongest sequential dollar growth in company history.

**Total revenue:** ~$1.39 billion, +33% YoY (includes professional services).

**Non-GAAP product gross margin:** 75.1% (guided 75% for FY2027).
**GAAP product gross margin:** 71%.
**GAAP total gross margin:** ~67%.

**Non-GAAP operating income:** Margin of 11.9%, expanded 300+ bps YoY (from ~9% in Q1 FY2026).
**GAAP operating loss:** Continued; SNOW remains GAAP operating loss.

**Non-GAAP EPS:** $0.39, beat consensus of $0.32 by 22% — fourth consecutive EPS beat.
**Prior year (Q1 FY2026) non-GAAP EPS:** $0.24 → **+62.5% YoY**.

**Free cash flow:** FCF margin ~34% TTM (yfinance, 2026-06-05).

**Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO):** $9.21 billion, +38% YoY.

## CEO Opening: AI Strategy (Sridhar Ramaswamy)

Ramaswamy framed the quarter around the "agentic enterprise" thesis: employees and AI agents working together across data, applications, and workflows. Snowflake's platform combines:
1. Governed enterprise data
2. Access to AI models
3. Connectivity to business applications
4. An "agentic control plane" via Snowflake Intelligence and Cortex Code

**Key quote (paraphrase):** "AI strengthens every element of our business."

**Key quote (paraphrase):** [On Q1 as an inflection point] CEO called Q1 an AI "inflection point."

### AI Products Update

**Cortex AI (overall):**
- Active in >9,100 accounts
- ~$100M AI revenue run-rate (Cortex-related)
- Driving platform expansion across existing customer base

**Cortex Code (CoCo):**
- Active in >7,100 accounts
- CFO Robins: CoCo delivered a "step function change" in AI revenue potential
- Designated the **largest single driver** to the FY2027 guidance raise

**Snowflake Intelligence:**
- Adoption more than doubled QoQ

**Natoma acquisition:**
- Extends Snowflake's agentic control plane capabilities (MCP platform)

## Strategic Partnerships

**AWS ($6B, 5-year deal):**
- New multi-year agreement worth ~$6 billion over 5 years
- More than doubled the size of the prior AWS agreement
- Purpose: accelerate AI adoption, lower data egress/bandwidth costs for customers
- Stock jumped ~37% on the day (May 28, 2026) partly on this announcement

**OpenAI ($200M partnership):**
- Partnership focused on Cortex enterprise AI agents
- Positions Snowflake as the data platform for enterprise OpenAI deployments

## Q&A Highlights (synthesized from secondary sources)

### On CoCo being the largest guidance driver
CFO Robins stated CoCo had the largest driver to the increase in forecast, characterizing it as a "step function change." [Source: TradingView/MarketBeat summary]

### On AI revenue trajectory
Management indicated AI revenue is accelerating within the consumption model. The ~$100M run-rate for Cortex was described as still early. [Source: search synthesis]

### On competitive dynamics vs. Databricks
No verbatim quotes available from this call. Background context from research: Databricks at $5.4B ARR, growing ~65% YoY, winning ~70% of incremental AI/ML budget per analyst surveys. Snowflake's response is platform consolidation — data + AI in one governed layer.

### On NRR inflection
NRR returned to 126% from 124% — first positive re-acceleration since the deceleration that began in FY2025. Management attributed this to Cortex AI upsell into existing accounts.
