4. **Meta** — April partnership: multi-generation MTIA XPUs, **3 GW through end-2028**; initial
   1 GW order received (XPUs + networking), delivery starts 2H 2027.
5-6. **Two unnamed customers** — shipments begin late 2026, accelerate 2027; **$6B in POs to date**.

10 GW total planned shipment in 2027 (unchanged vs prior quarter, back-half loaded).

## NEW: third-party-financed XPU platform (biggest structural item)

Broadcom is creating an **"AI XPU platform" with Apollo, Blackstone and other investors to deploy
>20 GW of compute through 2028; first tranche $35B currently being launched by Apollo.** Mechanism
per Tan: outside capital funds the chips/compute capacity for frontier labs (Anthropic, OpenAI)
that "otherwise might have difficulty getting access." Tan explicitly denied the Anthropic deal is
"backstopped" by Broadcom chips — Broadcom supplies, the vehicle funds.

- Bull read: removes customer balance-sheet constraint as the binding limit on XPU demand; Broadcom
  stays a chips business ("No racks. It is all a chip business only").
- Bear read: this is the AVGO flavor of circular AI financing — demand underwritten by financial
  engineering rather than end-customer cash flow. If token-demand growth disappoints, the vehicle's
  capacity becomes the overhang. Also note 8-K language: Anthropic expansion consumption "is
  dependent on Anthropic's continued commercial success."

## Margins / capacity / supply

- Consolidated GM 77.1% (−230bps YoY), **Q3 guide ~74%** — XPU mix dilution, explicitly framed as
  mix not structure; semi op margin 62% (+460bps YoY); software GM 93%. CFO: model segments
  separately from here.
- Inventory days **68 → 86** ($3.0B → $4.3B) — deliberate build for 2H ramp. Receivables $8.5B →
  $10.8B. Supply for 2026-2027 secured ("working on 2028 and 2029 right now"); customers coming
  incrementally and being accommodated.
- Content per GW: stable in $/GW near-term, rising generation-over-generation (SRAM, embedded CPU
  cores, multi-die, more HBM). Next-gen **200T switch tapes out this quarter**; Tomahawk 6 shipping
  >1 year with ~1-generation lead (NVDA Spectrum-X equivalent expected 2H 2026 per trade press).

## Software segment

Q2 $7.2B **+9% YoY — the soft spot** (headlines called it a miss; vs guide it was "in line"). But
**Q3 software guide $8.9B +31% YoY** on VCF 9.1 (AI inferencing support, heterogeneous GPU/CPU);
ARR +17%. Tan: no observed AI cannibalization of renewals. Watch whether the +31% lands — that's a
big sequential claim for this segment.

## New risks raised

1. **Financing-vehicle circularity** (above) — new risk class for AVGO, mirrors NVDA vendor-financing
   concerns; capacity now partly decoupled from customer balance sheets.
2. **Google second-sourcing acknowledged** by management for the first time on a call.
3. **GM mix compression** now guided, not hypothetical (74% Q3).
4. Working-capital build (receivables +66%, inventory +95% over 4 quarters vs revenue +48%) — FCF
   conversion still 93%, clean; watch if conversion degrades while WC keeps outpacing revenue.

## Thesis verdict: SUPPORTS — and the thesis number is now too small

The thesis one-liner says "growing fast toward $100B+"; $100B+ is now the *guided floor for FY27*
with FY28 growth on top, 2028 visibility, and a 20 GW third-party-funded platform behind it. The
thesis needs a rewrite upward in scale and a new risk section for the financing structure — flagged
[STALE - revisit] on sections 1, 3, 4, 9.
