# PSTG (Everpure, fka Pure Storage) — Refresh Context Briefing
**Date:** 2026-05-25

---

## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim from Step 1)

- Pure Storage makes enterprise all-flash storage arrays (FlashArray, FlashBlade)
- AI thesis: AI training workloads require massive, fast data pipelines — FlashBlade is positioned as the storage layer for GPU clusters
- Subscription/recurring revenue model (Evergreen) gives some SaaS-like economics
- Competes with NetApp, Dell EMC, HPE Nimble, and to some extent hyperscaler-internal storage
- Market cap ~$20B, growing but not at hypergrowth rates
- Customers include enterprises and some cloud/hyperscaler deployments
- Key differentiator: DirectFlash technology (custom flash modules, no SSD controllers)
- Revenue likely $3-4B range with improving profitability

## 4. Implications for scoring dimensions

### Value (15% weight)
- **P/E will look expensive** on GAAP earnings ($115M op income on ~$22-26B market cap). Non-GAAP is more favorable ($635M op income).
- **EV/Sales** at ~6-7x on FY2026, ~5-6x on FY2027 guidance. Premium to NetApp but arguably justified by growth + margin expansion.
- **FCF yield** — $616M FCF / ~$24B market cap = ~2.6%. Not cheap, not egregious for a 19%+ grower.
- Likely scores in the **middle of the Value range** — not a value trap, not a bargain.

### Quality (20% weight)
- **Gross margin 72% non-GAAP** is outstanding for a hardware-adjacent company.
- **Recurring revenue mix** ($1.9B ARR, ~46% of revenue from subscriptions) provides visibility.
- **RPO up 40% YoY** to $3.7B — strong forward visibility.
- **FCF conversion** is strong: $616M FCF on $635M non-GAAP operating income.
- **SBC gap** (GAAP vs non-GAAP operating margin spread of ~14pp) needs to be examined — this is a quality concern.
- Should score **well on Quality** — high gross margins, recurring revenue, strong cash conversion.

### Growth (15% weight)
- Revenue growth accelerating: 16% FY2026 -> ~19% FY2027 guided -> Q1 FY27 guided at ~28%.
- RPO growth 40% is well ahead of revenue growth, suggesting pipeline is building.
- ARR growth 16% is the floor; product revenue growth at 25% in Q4 is the ceiling.
- Should score **strong on Growth** — accelerating, not decelerating, with structural drivers.

### AI Thesis (30% weight) — THE KEY DIMENSION
- **Direct AI exposure:** FlashBlade//EXA is purpose-built for GPU cluster storage in AI factories. NVIDIA HPS-certified.
- **Hyperscaler AI deal:** Meta DirectFlash royalty model is direct AI infrastructure revenue.
- **AI revenue mix is unclear** — the company does not break out "AI revenue" as a discrete line. This is a data gap.
- **Competitive moat in AI:** DirectFlash IP, NVIDIA reference architecture status, and >90% GPU utilization claims give a differentiated position.
- **BUT:** Pure is not the GPU, the chip, or the networking — it's the storage layer. Even in an AI factory, storage is a smaller fraction of the BOM than compute/networking.
- Should score **well but not at the top** of AI Thesis — real product with real revenue, but storage is a supporting role in the AI stack, not the starring role.

### Momentum (10% weight)
- Stock down ~25% from highs. May 2026 range $67-78.
- Earnings beats and guidance raises provide positive fundamental momentum.
- Negative price momentum vs positive fundamental momentum = mixed signal.
- Likely scores **average on Momentum**.

### Risk (10% weight)
- **Hyperscaler in-housing:** If AWS/Google build proprietary flash controllers, the royalty model's TAM is at risk.
- **Customer concentration:** Meta appears to be the dominant hyperscaler customer. Single-customer dependency risk.
- **GAAP vs non-GAAP gap:** ~14pp operating margin spread implies heavy SBC, diluting existing shareholders.
- **Competitive risk from NetApp:** NetApp has stronger EBIT margins and established enterprise relationships.
- **Rebrand execution risk:** Everpure rebrand + platform pivot could confuse existing customers/channel.
- Risk score should reflect **moderate risk** — not thesis-killer level but meaningful concentration and in-housing risks.

### Key data gaps to resolve before scoring
1. **AI revenue as % of total** — not disclosed; will need to estimate from hyperscaler deal sizing + FlashBlade segment
2. **Share count trend / SBC as % of revenue** — GAAP/non-GAAP gap suggests significant dilution
3. **Customer concentration** — is Meta >10% of revenue? Need 10-K disclosure
4. **Net debt / balance sheet** — not surfaced in web searches, need filing data
5. **Insider activity** — no data found; need to check Form 4 filings
