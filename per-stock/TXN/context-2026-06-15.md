## AI / data-center exposure assessment

**The signal:** Data center is TI's fastest-growing end market. Per management, data-center sales grew **~90% YoY in Q1 2026** and **>25% sequential** (Q1'26 call, 2026-04-22); FY2025 data-center revenue reached roughly **$1.0–1.2B, up ~70% YoY** (Digitimes 2026-09-11 [forward est.]; Futurum Q4'25 review; management commentary). TI sells analog content into AI servers/racks: power management (multiphase controllers, DC/DC, power-stage / hot-swap), signal-chain (sensing, isolation, data converters), and power density solutions that scale with rack wattage.

**The magnitude check:** Even at ~$1.2B, data center is only **~6–7% of TTM revenue (~$18.4B)**. The other ~93% is industrial (largest), automotive, personal electronics, communications equipment, and enterprise systems. Industrial (+>30% YoY in Q1'26) and the broad analog upcycle — not AI — are the dominant drivers of the current beat.

**Directness verdict (proposed, see below):** TXN is a **diversified analog franchise with an AI-levered option**, not an AI-supply-chain pure-play. The AI exposure is real, accelerating, and high-incremental-margin, but it is a single-digit-% revenue sleeve riding on top of a cyclical-industrial recovery. Treat AI-Thesis directness as **moderate** — clearly above a no-AI industrial, well below a CoWoS/optical/accelerator name whose revenue *is* AI.

## Red flags / watch items

1. **FCF Yield is thin (~1.3%).** TI is mid-megafab-buildout (Sherman/Lehi); capex ran $4.55B in 2025 (~26% of revenue) — among the heaviest in analog. TTM FCF margin recovered to ~20% as OCF rebounded, but FCF yield on a $285B cap is low. The FCF-conversion check (rule 11 / refresh-context Step 2b): TTM FCF/NI ≈ 0.69 — depressed by the elevated capex cycle, not an accrual-quality flag. This is a known heavy-capex investment phase, not a red flag per se, but it caps the Value score.
2. **Valuation is rich.** Fwd P/E ~33x, P/S ~15.5x, EV/EBITDA ~33x. Stock up ~58% YTD (tikr, 2026). Rule-14 expectations check: high multiple on a name where consensus AI/data-center optimism is increasingly priced in — momentum is strong but the Value leg will score low.
3. **3-yr revenue CAGR is weak / near-flat** because the window straddles the 2022 peak → 2023-24 industrial downcycle → 2025-26 recovery. 2022→2025 CAGR is **−4.1%**; 2023→TTM is **+1.7%**. The *current* trajectory (MRQ +18.6% YoY) is far stronger than the 3-yr average — classic cyclical-trough-to-recovery distortion. Flag for scoring: the 3Y CAGR understates current momentum.
4. **Debt rising for Silicon Labs** — net debt/EBITDA currently ~1.06x (manageable); will step up materially when the ~$7.5B deal closes H1 2027.
