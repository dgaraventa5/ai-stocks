# OKLO — pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-10)

Session lens: re-rating Layer 1 power names on how DIRECTLY they own the AI power bottleneck. Direct = signed datacenter-power contracts with disclosed terms. Key distinction for OKLO: signed binding agreements vs LOIs/frameworks; Aurora licensing/INL timeline status.

---

## Pre-research mental model

(Written before searching.) Oklo is a pre-revenue advanced fission developer building the Aurora liquid-metal fast reactor (~15-75 MWe "powerhouse," build-own-operate, sells power not reactors). First NRC COLA denied Jan 2022; as of my prior knowledge it was working through a phased COLA resubmission for Aurora-INL with first power targeted ~late 2027-2028, fueled by DOE/INL-recovered HALEU from EBR-II. Customer pipeline heavy on LOIs/non-binding frameworks: Switch ~12 GW master agreement (Dec 2024), Equinix 500 MW pre-agreement with $25M prepayment, Diamondback, DOE reactor pilot program selection (2025), and a recently logged Meta 1.2 GW agreement. Revenue $0; repeated equity raises; very large market cap relative to zero revenue. Most recent quarter known: ~Q3/Q4 2025. Local thesis.md is an unfilled template; news-log has one entry (2026-05-06 PDC topical report approval, ~14 GW pipeline, Meta 1.2 GW).

## Findings (sources)

1. **Q1 2026 results (reported ~2026-05-12): still zero revenue, big cash pile.** Net loss $33.065M, loss from operations $51.249M, net cash used in operations $17.867M for Q1 2026; cash + marketable debt securities $2,536.898M ("$2,536,898" thousand) at 3/31/2026 (10-Q Q1 2026, filed 2026-05-12, Liquidity and Capital Resources, sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1849056/000162828026034095/oklo-20260331.htm). Income statement has no revenue line. Stock fell ~10.8% after the release to $69.66 (Investing.com earnings-call coverage, May 2026).
2. **Massive Q1 dilution via ATM.** 2025 ATM Program (entered Dec 2025, up to $1.5B gross) completed during Q1 2026: 12,376,352 shares at avg net $96.95/share, gross $1,199.868M, net $1,181.897M (10-Q Q1 2026, Stockholders' Equity note). Shares outstanding 173,867,839 at 3/31/2026 vs 160,514,103 at 12/31/2025 (~+8.3% in one quarter) (10-Q balance sheet).
3. **NRC COLA has NOT been resubmitted as of the 10-Q (2026-05-12).** Direct quote: "We became the first advanced fission company to submit a custom combined license application with the NRC in March 2020, which was denied without prejudice in 2022. We are currently working toward submitting an updated custom combined license application for NRC review. In July 2025, we completed a Phase I pre-application readiness assess[ment]" (10-Q Q1 2026, regulatory section). Separately, NRC approved the Aurora Principal Design Criteria (PDC) topical report in early May 2026 on an accelerated timeline (news-log 2026-05-06; oklo.com newsroom; carboncredits.com Q1 coverage) — a topical report, not a license.
4. **Aurora-INL is proceeding under DOE authorization (Reactor Pilot Program), not an NRC license.** Groundbreaking at INL Sept 2025 (ANS Nuclear Newswire, 2025-09-29). DOE Idaho Operations Office approved the Nuclear Safety Design Agreement (NSDA) for the Aurora powerhouse on 2026-03-17 after an Other Transaction Agreement was signed (oklo.com newsroom, 2026; World Nuclear News "Licensing of Oklo pilot reactor facilities advances"). Oklo "plans to subsequently pursue NRC licensing to support commercial operations" (WNN). The DOE pilot program's headline goal is criticality of at least 3 test reactors by 2026-07-04; no Aurora-INL-specific criticality date is given (WNN; ANS 2025-11-14). The 10-Q states: "We have an ambitious target of deploying our first powerhouse in 2028" (10-Q Q1 2026, MD&A).
5. **Customer agreements remain LOI/framework-stage; Meta is the newest and most substantive.** 10-Q language: "we have signed non-binding letters of intent with Equinix, Inc., Diamondback E&P LLC, and Prometheus Hyperscale... In December 2024, we signed a 12 gigawatt (GW) Master Power Agreement with Switch, Ltd." (10-Q Q1 2026, MD&A). Trade press at signing: the Switch MPA is "nonbinding... establishes a framework for collaboration, with the expectation that individual binding agreements will be finalized as project milestones are reached," initial deployments possibly 2029 (Utility Dive, Dec 2024; Power Magazine, Dec 2024). Meta: Prepayment Agreement signed 2026-01-05 supporting a 1.2 GW campus in Pike County, Ohio; "provides a mechanism for Meta to prepay for power and provide funding"; Oklo will use Meta's funding to secure nuclear fuel for Phase 1 (10-Q Q1 2026; businesswire 2026-01-09). Press release timeline: phase 1 online "as early as 2030," scaling to 1.2 GW by 2034; site is 206 acres of Oklo-owned former DOE land (businesswire 2026-01-09).
6. **Equinix $25M is a right-of-first-refusal payment, still LOI-stage.** Balance sheet carries "Right of first refusal liability $25,000 [thousand]" unchanged at 3/31/2026 and 12/31/2025; the March 2024 payment secured ROFR rights (36 months, ≥100 MWe up to cumulative 500 MWe per DCD) with power supplied at a discount to MFN pricing in a future PPA, "location to be determined" (10-Q Q1 2026, ROFR note; datacenterdynamics.com, 2024). No PPA has been executed.
7. **Fuel/vertical integration progress.** Oklo-Centrus planned JV announced 2026-03-11 for HALEU deconversion services at Piketon, OH (Piketon Today / Industrial Info, 2026-03). Aurora Fuel Fabrication Facility (A3F) NSDA approved by DOE Nov 2025; selected under DOE Fuel Line Pilot Program (ANS 2025-11-13; 10-Q Q1 2026). NVIDIA + Los Alamos partnership for AI-accelerated fuel/reactor R&D announced around Q1 2026 results (carboncredits.com, May 2026).
8. **First-revenue path may be radioisotopes, not power.** 10-Q: Atomic Alchemy-related project "Abundantia... is expected to produce revenue as early as 2026 from the sale of purified radium and other desired radioisotopes"; Meitner is later-stage, post-construction (10-Q Q1 2026, intangibles/projects note). Groves (TX) isotope test reactor targets criticality by 2026-07-04 and has an NRC materials license for isotope handling (WNN, 2026).
9. **Pipeline size:** ~14 GW of customer interest cited at Q1 2026 (company communications via carboncredits.com May 2026; news-log 2026-05-06). Also tentatively selected for Eielson AFB, Alaska microreactor (10-Q Q1 2026).

## DIFF (mental model vs findings)

- **Wrong/stale:** I assumed the COLA Phase 1 had likely been resubmitted by now. It has NOT — as of 2026-05-12 Oklo is still "working toward submitting" it. NRC progress is real (PDC topical approved May 2026) but there is still no license application under active NRC review for the powerhouse.
- **Materially new:** Aurora-INL's near-term path runs through DOE authorization (Reactor Pilot Program OTA + NSDA approved 2026-03-17), not NRC — NRC licensing comes "subsequently" for commercial operations. This both de-risks first criticality and muddies what "first power" legally enables commercially.
- **New since my model:** Meta Prepayment Agreement (2026-01-05, 1.2 GW Pike County OH, phase 1 "as early as 2030") — this is the closest thing OKLO has to a hyperscaler commitment with money attached, though disclosed dollar amount of Meta's prepayment was not found (flag).
- **New:** $1.2B ATM completed in Q1 2026 (~8.3% share-count growth in one quarter); cash now ~$2.54B. Funding risk down, dilution very real.
- **Confirmed:** zero revenue; Switch 12 GW is a non-binding framework; Equinix $25M is a ROFR sitting unchanged as a liability, no PPA signed.
- **Timeline drift:** 10-Q says first powerhouse deployment target 2028 ("ambitious"); my prior "late 2027/early 2028" was the optimistic end. Meta-served power starts "as early as 2030."

## Evidence for D1 (revenue reality)

- Revenue is $0. Q1 2026 10-Q income statement has no revenue line; net loss $33.065M, loss from ops $51.249M (10-Q Q1 2026, filed 2026-05-12).
- Credible first-revenue dates: (a) radioisotope sales (Abundantia) "as early as 2026" per 10-Q — likely small; (b) first powerhouse power sales: deployment target 2028 per 10-Q, characterized by management as "ambitious," and Aurora-INL runs under DOE authorization with NRC licensing for commercial operations still ahead; (c) Meta campus phase 1 "as early as 2030."
- Cash runway: $2.537B cash+securities vs ~$18M/quarter operating cash burn (Q1 2026 10-Q) — multi-year runway, but construction capex will scale this up substantially.

## Evidence for D2 (competitive position / fuel)

- Differentiators: build-own-operate PPA model (recurring revenue vs reactor sales — 10-Q MD&A); DOE Reactor Pilot Program selection with NSDA approved 2026-03-17 (fastest path to a critical reactor among AI-adjacent SMR names); fuel vertical integration — A3F fuel fab at INL (NSDA Nov 2025, Fuel Line Pilot Program), recovered EBR-II material for first core, Centrus HALEU deconversion JV (2026-03-11).
- Vs peers: NuScale remains the only US SMR with an NRC-certified design; Oklo has no NRC license and a once-denied COLA (2022) not yet resubmitted (10-Q Q1 2026). Oklo's edge is speed via the DOE pathway plus fuel supply positioning; its risk is that DOE authorization covers the INL pilot, not the commercial fleet customers are signing up for.
- HALEU remains an industry-wide bottleneck; Centrus is the only licensed domestic HALEU supplier (trade press, 2026). The JV is positioning, not yet secured commercial-scale fuel supply.

## Evidence for D5 (datacenter contract directness)

- **Switch 12 GW (Dec 2024): non-binding framework.** Trade press at signing: "nonbinding... establishes a framework for collaboration, with the expectation that individual binding agreements will be finalized as project milestones are reached" (Utility Dive, Dec 2024). 10-Q calls it a "Master Power Agreement... one of the largest corporate power purchase agreements in history" without claiming binding status. No disclosed pricing. Initial deployments possibly 2029.
- **Equinix 500 MW: non-binding LOI + $25M ROFR payment.** 10-Q explicitly lists Equinix among "non-binding letters of intent." The $25M (received March 2024) sits as a right-of-first-refusal liability, unchanged 12/31/2025→3/31/2026; future PPA location "to be determined" (10-Q Q1 2026, ROFR note).
- **Meta 1.2 GW (2026-01-05): strongest of the three — a signed Prepayment Agreement with funding mechanism** tied to a specific site (Pike County, OH) and use of proceeds (nuclear fuel for Phase 1), but timeline is 2030-2034 and the prepayment dollar amount is not disclosed in the 10-Q sections reviewed (flag). Not a PPA with disclosed power terms.
- Net: OKLO has zero binding datacenter PPAs with disclosed terms. Directness on the AI power bottleneck is prospective — a 14 GW pipeline of frameworks/LOIs plus one funded prepayment agreement, vs incumbents (VST/CEG/TLN-type names) with executed, disclosed-term contracts and operating plants.

## FLAGS

1. **Meta prepayment amount not found.** The 10-Q describes the mechanism but I could not locate a disclosed dollar amount received from Meta, and no customer-deposit/deferred-revenue line attributable to Meta was found on the balance sheet greps. Either nothing has been drawn yet or it's disclosed elsewhere (8-K exhibit, Q4 2025 10-K). Verify before rating D5.
2. **"Criticality by July 4, 2026" is the DOE pilot program's aggregate goal (any 3 test reactors), not a confirmed Aurora-INL date.** The Oklo-specific near-term criticality target found was the Groves isotope test reactor (TX), not the Aurora powerhouse. Do not credit Aurora-INL with a mid-2026 criticality date.
3. **COLA status could have changed after 2026-05-12.** The 10-Q says "working toward submitting"; a submission between mid-May and 2026-06-10 can't be ruled out from sources reviewed. NRC docket check recommended at rating time.
4. **Revenue from Aurora-INL is legally murky:** the plant is being built under DOE authorization; NRC licensing for "commercial operations" comes later (WNN). Whether/how Oklo books revenue from a DOE-authorized pilot unit is unclear — flag for the earnings call transcript (not yet in /transcripts, which is empty).
