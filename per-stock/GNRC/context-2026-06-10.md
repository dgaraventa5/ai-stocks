# GNRC — Pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-10)

Prepared for AI Thesis re-evaluation of Layer 1 power names. Session lens: how DIRECTLY does GNRC own the AI power bottleneck (disclosed datacenter revenue/backlog vs. narrative).

---

## Pre-research mental model

GNRC is Generac, the dominant US residential standby generator maker (~55-60% of revenue residential, rest C&I and energy tech), with a new datacenter push: in 2024-2025 it announced entry into the large diesel genset market (~2-3 MW class) targeting hyperscale backup power, with first meaningful revenue guided for 2026 — a greenfield product line against entrenched incumbents Caterpillar, Cummins, and Rolls-Royce/MTU. The repo's only datapoint was a 2026-05-22 news-log entry: Q1 2026 revenue $1.06B (+12.4%), adj EBITDA $193.6M beat, and a Jefferies upgrade citing "imminent" hyperscaler backup contracts (OpenAI Abilene reportedly among them) — i.e., the datacenter story was still anticipation, not disclosed orders/backlog, in my model. thesis.md was an unfilled template; this is effectively first-pass evidence gathering.

## DIFF (mental model vs. findings)

1. **Datacenter push has converted to real, disclosed orders — bigger and faster than my model.** I had "anticipation, not orders." Reality: $700M+ disclosed product backlog (ex-hyperscale NTP), a ~$600M non-binding NTP for 2027 deliveries, and a SIGNED global hyperscale supply agreement (2026-06-02). Backlog went $300M → $700M+ in ~6 months.
2. **The hyperscale agreement came AFTER the Jefferies "imminent" call — and after an 11% Investor Day selloff for NOT announcing it.** The market was trading this binary; it resolved positive on June 2.
3. **Mix shift is real but resi is still the majority.** Q1 2026: Residential $549M (52% of sales) vs C&I $510M (48%) — C&I nearly caught resi for the first time. My "55-60% resi" model was right for FY2025 ($2.27B/$4.21B = 54%) but is eroding fast.
4. **Vertical integration moves I didn't have:** Enercon (enclosures/switchgear, closed 2026-04-01) and Allmand (closed 2026-01-05); capacity to >$1B large-MW by Q4 2026.
5. **Engine supply chain nuance I didn't have:** the large-MW line rides on Baudouin/Weichai (Chinese-owned) engines — a tariff/geopolitical exposure none of CAT/CMI carry.

## Evidence for D1 (% of revenue datacenter-tied TODAY)

**Company does NOT disclose a data center revenue dollar figure — FLAG.** Best available math:

- Q1 2026 C&I external sales +$111M YoY (+28%); ~10pp of segment growth was acquisitions/FX, so core growth ≈ 18% ≈ ~$72M incremental, "primarily" data center (8-K Ex-99.1, 2026-04-29). If ~$50-70M of the YoY increment is datacenter and the base had modest DC revenue (Q4 2025 C&I growth was also DC-led), Q1 2026 datacenter revenue is plausibly **$75-150M of $1.06B total, i.e., roughly 7-14% of quarterly sales — ESTIMATE, not disclosed.**
- Forward: $700M backlog "majority weighted toward 2026 shipments" (TIKR on Investor Day) + FY2026 C&I guide +30% (≈ +$440M, primarily DC + Allmand) against ~$4.85-4.95B implied FY2026 sales → datacenter plausibly **~8-12% of FY2026 revenue**, rising toward ~$1B (≈15-20%) by 2028 per management's stated path.
- Confidence: Medium on direction, Low on point estimates. The 2028 $1B target is management aspiration, not backlog-covered.

## Evidence for D2 (pricing power vs CAT/Cummins/Rolls-Royce)

- **Winning on AVAILABILITY, not price:** Generac large-MW lead times ~30 weeks vs up to 107 weeks for CAT/Cummins/RR (Hunterbrook, 2026-02-23). This is a scarcity-window win — the moat is incumbents' full order books, not Generac's product superiority.
- ASP $1.5-2M per data center genset (Q3 2025 call via Yahoo Finance) — in line with market pricing for 2-3MW Tier IV units; **no evidence found of either price premium or discounting. FLAG: no direct pricing-power evidence either way.**
- Structural caveats: (a) new entrant in a segment where CAT ~18% / Cummins ~16% share and reputational/serviceability incumbency matters; (b) engine platform is Baudouin M55 (Weichai/China-owned) — tariff and procurement-policy risk; (c) qualification moat is real but newly earned (hyperscaler audits passed per 2026-06-02 release); (d) at Abilene, permits show Generac-style Baudouin units SHARING the site with 34 CAT units — second-source role, not displacement.
- Pricing power verdict from evidence: **None demonstrated yet; selling into a supply-constrained market on lead time.** If CAT/CMI capacity catches up (Cummins announced $150M Minnesota expansion for 3-4MW QSK95 gensets, per Mordor Intelligence), the availability edge compresses.

## Evidence for D5 (named customers / disclosed backlog)

- **No customer has been NAMED.** Signed global supply agreement (2026-06-02) is with an anonymous "leading hyperscale data center operator"; the ~$600M NTP is with "a certain hyperscale customer" (Q1 call, 2026-04-29).
- **Disclosed backlog: >$700M data center product backlog (2026-04-29), explicitly excluding the ~$600M NTP.** This is real, quantified, management-stated backlog — strongest D5 evidence in Layer 1 distributed-power names short of named customers.
- OpenAI/Stargate Abilene link is INFERRED (Texas air permits listing 28 Baudouin 2.8MW units; Hunterbrook 2026-02-23) and repeated by Jefferies (2026-05-22 upgrade, PT $239→$302) — **not company-confirmed, and Baudouin units could come from other packagers. FLAG.**
- "Final stages of vendor approval with TWO hyperscale customers" (Q1 call) — one presumably converted on June 2; the second is pipeline.

## FLAGS / gaps

1. No company-disclosed data center revenue dollar figure for any period — D1 is estimated from C&I growth attribution. Do not treat the 7-14% Q1 figure as disclosed.
2. The $600M NTP is explicitly NON-BINDING and for 2027 deliveries; the June 2 supply agreement disclosed no dollar value. A "global supply agreement" sets terms/pricing — it is not necessarily a volume commitment.
3. $400M (YE25) and $700M (March 26) backlog figures come from Investor Day coverage (Barchart/TIKR), not from an SEC filing I could verify directly; the >$700M figure IS verified via the Q1 call transcript.
4. Baudouin/Weichai (China) engine dependency — tariff and US-procurement-policy risk specific to GNRC's large-MW line; not found addressed by management in sources reviewed.
5. Q1 2026 10-Q (accession 000143774926014882) not parsed for segment/backlog footnotes — worth a pass before locking the rating.
6. Hunterbrook is a short-seller-adjacent outlet (here effectively long-thesis on GNRC); permit analysis looks solid but is third-party inference.
