Simultaneously, the standalone business is in a **two-year cyclical trough** (revenue −17.6% in FY2025, guided ~flat in FY2026), yet the stock is **+109% YTD 2026** and at a 52-week high (~$160) versus a median sell-side PT of **$95**. ## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim, written before data pull)

> Axcelis makes ion implantation equipment (the Purion family) — a niche but essential wafer-fabrication process step. They are the #2/#3 player behind Applied Materials, roughly co-leader with AMAT in certain implant segments.
> Their demand has been heavily skewed toward **power devices (silicon carbide / SiC)** for EVs and **mature/trailing-edge** logic, plus some image sensors and memory — NOT leading-edge logic. This is the key tension for an "AI supply chain" thesis: implant is more of a mature-node / power story than an AI/leading-edge story.
> My last clear memory is roughly FY2023/early-FY2024: record revenue around ~$1.1B, very strong on SiC/power, China a large share of revenue (I recall China being 40-50%+ of sales, a concentration/export-control concern).
> I'd expect them to be entering a **cyclical downturn** as the SiC/EV cycle cooled and China digested capacity — likely a 2025 trough. Balance sheet historically clean (net cash, no real debt).

**Verdict on the mental model:** Directionally correct on the *business* (implant niche; power/SiC + mature exposure; not leading-edge; cyclical trough now; ~$1.1B FY2023 peak; clean balance sheet; large China). **Materially wrong / incomplete on two things that dominate the current setup:** (a) I had **no knowledge of the Veeco merger**, which redefines the entire investment object; (b) I underestimated how far the **stock has detached from fundamentals** (+109% YTD on flat-to-down earnings). Both are corrected below.

### Customer concentration (10-K FY2025)

### Geography — TWO different cuts, do not conflate
- **By location of the operation recording the sale (10-K Note 17):** FY2025 US **$482.6M (57.5%)**, Asia Pacific **$306.5M (36.5%)**, Europe **$50.0M (6.0%)**. This is a *booking-location* view (Axcelis manufactures in the US), NOT end-customer destination.
- **By end-customer destination (mgmt commentary):** **China = 42% of FY2025 sales** (quarterly: 46% Q3 → 32% Q4 2025; Q1 2026 back up to **40%**, with Korea 28%, Europe 16%, US 12%, Taiwan 1%, Japan 1%). Source: Q1 2026 earnings call. ~88.6% of sales USD-denominated (10-K).
- **The China-destination number (~40%+) is the one that matters for export-control / geographic risk**, not the 6% "Europe / 36% Asia" booking-location table. Flagged so future-Dom doesn't read the wrong table.

### Export controls (10-K FY2025 risk factors — directly relevant)
- China is "a significant portion of our sales." Major Chinese customer **SMIC is on the U.S. Entity List** but covered by a **2020 licensing policy** for mature-process fabs; Axcelis "to date" has obtained those licenses. Other Chinese chipmakers are on the Entity List **without** such a policy.
- The Oct-2022 BIS framework (modified 2023) limits equipment to Chinese customers producing advanced logic/DRAM/NAND. **December 2024: BIS created a new ECCN specifically for certain 300mm ion implanters** and added Chinese customers to the Entity List — i.e., ACLS's own product category was *specifically* targeted by the most recent tightening. This is a live, name-specific policy leg, not generic.

### End markets & AI linkage (the crux for AI-thesis scoring)
- Business is **98.2% ion implantation** (1.8% legacy aftermarket). Mgmt's three end-market buckets: **Memory, Mature process technology, Leading-edge foundry & logic**. Source: 10-K FY2025.
- **Power / SiC** is explicitly the segment where Axcelis is "technology leader and supplier of choice in the implant-intensive power device segment" (10-K letter). Currently in digestion (EV/industrial softness).
- **AI linkage is INDIRECT and partial, via two channels:**
  1. **Memory:** Q1 2026 memory system shipments at "highest level since 2023," driven by **DRAM and HBM for AI**; FY2026 memory growth "pretty strong," concentrated in DRAM. This is the genuine AI-demand channel. (Caveat: implant is a *small* share of memory capex vs. litho/etch/depo; ACLS rides the HBM/DRAM capacity wave but is not a primary HBM-process tool.)
  2. **SiC for data-center power:** mgmt flags emerging SiC (and GaN) demand for **AI data-center power conversion / 800V architectures** — early, qualitative, not yet a revenue line.
- **Leading-edge logic:** essentially zero — **no leading-edge system revenue in Q1 2026**; one tool shipped early Q2 for "material modification at 2nm." So the AI-leading-edge-logic angle is nascent at best.
- **Net:** AI is a real but *minority and indirect* tailwind (memory/DRAM/HBM capacity + nascent SiC power). The company does **not** disclose an "AI revenue" line; any AI-% estimate is inferential.

## 6. Implications for ratings (flagged, not pre-decided)

- **Layer fit:** ✔ Confirmed. ACLS is squarely **Layer 04 / Semiconductor Equipment** — a front-end wafer-fab process-tool (ion implant) maker. (Post-merger it broadens within the same layer; no layer change needed.)
- **AI Thesis (D1–D5):** The hard question. AI exposure is **indirect and minority** (memory/DRAM/HBM capacity + emerging SiC power), with leading-edge logic ~nil — argues for **modest** D1 (AI rev %) and D5 (hyperscaler exposure: ACLS sells to chipmakers, not hyperscalers, and only a slice of that is AI-memory). D2/D3 (supply-chain position / moat) are genuinely strong *within implant* — Axcelis is one of two serious implant franchises (vs Applied Materials), with a real technical/installed-base moat, but implant is a **smaller, less-bottlenecked** step than litho/etch/depo, so not a "5." D4 (capacity expansion) is muddied: the company itself is asset-light (capex ~$11M) and in a demand trough, but is *expanding product scope via the merger* and FY2026 is flat — argues mid.
- **Momentum (M1–M3):** **M2 Relative Strength is unambiguously high** (+109% YTD vs SOXX +89%, >20pp outperformance → 5 by the rubric) — but flag explicitly that this is **merger/sentiment-driven on flat fundamentals**, the exact pattern rule 14 warns about. **M1 EPS revisions are mixed** (Q2 guide above consensus + a B. Riley upgrade, but a standing BofA underperform and price ~70% above median PT) → lean neutral-to-slightly-positive, not a clean 5. **M3 Insider = routine small sells, no buys → 3** (the post-rally diversification default; per rubric decision-procedure, no open-market buys = ceiling of 3 here).
- **Risk (R1–R5):**
  - R1 Customer Concentration: one customer 11%, top-10 55% → **4**.
  - R2 Geographic/Export: **the key risk.** ~42% China destination revenue (policy leg) **+** a BIS ECCN created specifically for 300mm ion implanters (Dec 2024) **+** Asia shipment concentration (demand leg). Two clear legs, with policy currently *constraining but not zeroing* (SMIC mature-fab licenses still obtained). Maps to **R2 ≈ 2–3** (worse than the AMAT/LRCX "3" because the most recent tightening named ACLS's product category; named worst-case shock = China advanced-node/implant-ECCN escalation cutting off a large slice of ~42% China revenue). **Plus a merger-specific overhang: China SAMR is the gating approval for the Veeco deal** — a geopolitical single-point-of-failure for the whole transaction (note in rationale).
  - R3 Balance Sheet: net cash, no debt, positive FCF → **5**.
  - R4 Regulatory/Litigation: no major litigation surfaced; the live items are export-control (captured in R2) and **merger/antitrust review (SAMR)** — arguably a **3** given the pending cross-border deal and the $5M customer settlement, vs a clean 4–5.
  - **R5 Disruption Risk: 5 (default).** Non-Layer-10 physical process-equipment maker; ion implant is a durable, irreplaceable doping step with no agentic-AI disruption to the revenue model. Per rule 16, defaults to 5.
- **Cross-cutting caution for the rating session:** Value (cheap-looking on a falling business), Momentum (rallying on a corporate action), and AI sentiment will all push Total Score **up**; the durable earnings power is in a trough and the valuation is at a 3-year extreme far above sell-side targets. The merger is the dominant variable and is **not** captured anywhere in the rubric — call it out at sign-off.
