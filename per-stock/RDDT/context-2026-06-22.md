## 1. Mental model (pre-research)

RDDT is a consumer-internet ad platform whose "AI adopter" angle has two distinct legs that should not be conflated:
1. **AI as a tool inside the ad/ops stack** — ML-driven ad optimization (bidding, targeting, conversion modeling) and AI machine translation that opens international markets. This is the genuine "operating-impact" story Bucket 3 is meant to capture.
2. **AI as an existential threat to the distribution funnel** — a large slice of Reddit's traffic (esp. logged-out users) arrives via Google Search. AI Overviews / zero-click search structurally compresses that referral channel. Separately, Reddit is *also* a beneficiary: its human-conversation corpus is the most-cited social source in AI answers and is being monetized via data-licensing (Google, OpenAI deals).

The tension — AI helps the P&L (legs above) while threatening the top of the funnel (Google referral) — is the whole RDDT story and lands squarely on D3 (defensibility of the edge) and R5 (disruption risk). The corpus is the asset; the referral channel is the vulnerability.

The asymmetry: AI both feeds (licensing, citations) and starves (referral funnel) the same business — that two-sidedness is exactly a 2, not the cohort-default 5. *Source: referral-dependency reporting; AI-Overviews backdrop; Reddit mgmt guidance on "bumpy" search.
