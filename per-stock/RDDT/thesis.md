## 1. One-line thesis

> Reddit is a human-conversation corpus business that happens to run an ad platform: AI makes the corpus more valuable (it is cited in 46.4% of all AI-generated social-media answers, and Google/OpenAI data-licensing deals are live), while AI-optimized bidding and machine translation have driven 7 consecutive quarters of >60% revenue growth — but the same AI wave is eroding the Google-referral funnel that delivers ~40-50% of Reddit's traffic, creating a two-sided bet where the AI tailwind (corpus, licensing) and the AI headwind (referral compression) run through the same asset.

---

## 2. Position in the AI supply chain

- **What they do:** Ad-supported social media platform (~$625M ad revenue in Q1 2026, +74% YoY) with a secondary and growing data-licensing business (contracts with Google and OpenAI; revenue not separately disclosed but confirmed material). The platform hosts 126.8M daily active unique users (DAUq) as of Q1 2026.
- **Where they sit:** Layer 10 (AI Adopters / AI-monetized consumer internet). Not an AI infrastructure provider; instead, the human-generated conversation corpus is raw material for AI training, and AI is being applied to optimize Reddit's own ad stack and international expansion.
- **Unique vs. commoditized:** The corpus is genuinely scarce — Reddit hosts 18+ years of unfiltered human debate, Q&A, and domain-specific communities that LLMs disproportionately cite. Reddit represents 46.4% of AI-generated social citations (YouTube 31.8%, all others much smaller). No synthetic replacement exists at this authenticity/breadth. The ad business itself is more commoditized — any ad platform can implement bidding ML — but the corpus creates a moat under the data-licensing angle.
- **AI revenue mix:** Licensing revenue directly from AI (Google/OpenAI contracts): disclosed as material but % of revenue not broken out (Q1 2026 10-Q). AI-assisted ad revenue (conversions, targeting) is indistinguishable in reported numbers. Direct AI-licensing is small today relative to ad revenue, but high-margin.

## 6. Moat

- **IP / process know-how:** AI machine translation (30 languages) is a proprietary implementation driving ROW DAU growth (+26% vs U.S. +7%); not patented but operationally embedded.
- **Scale advantage:** Network effect in communities — subreddit-level engagement is self-reinforcing and hard to replicate from cold start. The data corpus built over 18 years is not replicable.
- **Switching costs:** High for dedicated-community users (communities have social capital that doesn't transfer). Low for casual users (easily substituted by other social media).
- **Network effects:** Strong within-platform (community density × quality). The data-licensing moat is a form of "data network effect" — more human conversation → better LLM training data → more licensing demand.
- **Regulatory / geographic:** No current regulatory advantage. Potential liability under evolving AI copyright/data-licensing norms — fair-use doctrine governs many current LLM training practices; a court ruling expanding fair-use defenses would reduce the leverage of Reddit's licensing position.

**Moat rating:** Narrow. The corpus is real and scarce; the distribution funnel (Google referral ~40-50% of traffic) is a structural fragility.

## 9. Risks

- **Thesis-killer #1:** Google referral collapses (AI Overviews → zero-click dominates) AND Google does not renew the data-licensing contract. Both legs of the AI-double-sided bet go against Reddit simultaneously.
- **Thesis-killer #2:** U.S. DAUq growth turns negative for 2+ quarters, indicating the logged-out Google-referral user base has structurally shrunk (not just grown slower). This breaks the scale-flywheel that drives ad pricing improvements.
- **Google / AI Overviews:** ~40-50% of Reddit traffic is Google-referred, concentrated in logged-out users. AI Overviews / zero-click search actively compresses this. Already visible: U.S. DAU +7% vs. ROW +26% in Q1 2026; prior −15% single-day drop on a DAU miss in Q4 2024.
- **Data-licensing IP risk:** Fair-use norms are unsettled. A ruling that expands LLM training fair-use defenses reduces Reddit's licensing negotiating power. Conversely, a ruling that data-licensing requires individual consent would face Reddit with a complex retroactive consent problem.
- **Cyclical risk:** Ad markets are macro-correlated. A recession or ad-spend pullback (e.g., if hyperscalers cut marketing in an AI investment pause) would compress revenue growth — but Reddit's ROW/international growth diversifies somewhat.
- **Technology substitution:** If AI-generated synthetic content becomes indistinguishable from authentic human debate, the corpus scarcity premium erodes over time. Not imminent but a 5-year horizon risk.
- **Regulatory:** Content moderation liability, data-sharing regulations (GDPR in EU, CCPA evolution), and potential antitrust scrutiny of Google's dominant position (which also affects Reddit's referral dependency).
- **Execution / management:** Steve Huffman (CEO) has been at Reddit since 2015. Team has navigated IPO and first-year public company challenges. No near-term succession concerns. Risk is whether management can navigate the AI-Overviews transition without losing the logged-out user base.

**R5 Disruption Risk = 2 (flagged; not the default 5):** Core ad-revenue funnel is partially dependent on Google search referral (~40-50% of traffic, logged-out users), which AI Overviews directly compresses. This is an active, visible compression vector — NOT a hypothetical 5-year risk. Offset by the corpus licensing angle (Reddit is an AI *beneficiary* on that leg). The two-sidedness justifies 2 rather than 1. (Source: context-2026-06-22.md, confirmed by Q1 2026 mgmt commentary)

## 10. The "inverse the thesis" test

> What would convince me I'm wrong? If I cannot answer this in two specific, falsifiable statements, I do not understand the position well enough to own it.

1. **U.S. DAUq growth hits 0% or negative for two consecutive quarters** — this would indicate that AI Overviews has materially eroded the Google-referral logged-out user base faster than ROW growth and logged-in user growth can offset. At that point the ad-scale flywheel breaks. The 7% U.S. growth in Q1 2026 is the early warning signal; a further deceleration to 0% is the falsifiable threshold.
2. **Data-licensing contracts (Google, OpenAI) are not renewed OR combined data-licensing revenue is disclosed at <$50M/year** — this would indicate that the corpus-as-scarce-asset thesis is not translating to durable licensing economics. Reddit's AI bull case depends on corpus monetization growing; if the counterparties are already finding synthetic alternatives, the scarcity premium evaporates.
