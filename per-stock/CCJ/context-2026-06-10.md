# CCJ — Pre-rating context briefing — 2026-06-10

Prepared for AI Thesis re-rate of Layer 1 power names. Session lens: directness of AI power-bottleneck ownership. Direct = selling power/capacity to datacenters with disclosed terms; uranium is two steps removed (AI → nuclear power demand → uranium demand → U price). Westinghouse (49%) assessed separately.

---

## Pre-research mental model

Cameco is the largest Western uranium producer (McArthur River/Key Lake, Cigar Lake in Saskatchewan, plus a stake in JV Inkai in Kazakhstan), selling primarily through a long-term utility contract book rather than at spot, with a smaller fuel services segment (refining/conversion). It owns 49% of Westinghouse (Brookfield 51%, acquired Nov 2023), the AP1000 new-build and nuclear fuel/services franchise; in late Oct 2025 Westinghouse signed a US-government partnership targeting ~$80B of new AP1000 builds, which spiked the stock. Last quarter I was confident about pre-research was Q3 2025; I believed uranium spot was ~$75–80/lb and term ~$80/lb. The AI link I assumed was second-order: hyperscaler datacenter demand → utility nuclear PPAs/restarts → future uranium contracting — no hyperscaler buys uranium from Cameco directly.

All C$ unless noted. Primary sources are Cameco 6-K exhibits on SEC EDGAR.

Fuel services: revenue $134M (−1%), realized price $48.53/kgU (−14%). Annual guidance unchanged: 19.5–21.5M lb uranium production (Cameco share). 2. **Westinghouse Q1 2026 (Cameco's 49% share):** net LOSS $46M (vs loss $62M Q1 2025); adjusted EBITDA $122M (+33% vs $92M); adjusted FCF $72M. Cameco received a US$49M cash distribution from Westinghouse in Q1 2026 and US$124M (net) from JV Inkai post-quarter. (same 6-K)
3. **FY2025 results (reported Feb 13, 2026).** Consolidated revenue $3,482M (uranium $2,874M = 82.5%, fuel services $562M = 16.1%); adjusted EBITDA ~$1.9B; net earnings $590M. Westinghouse FY2025 (our share): revenue $3,458M (equity-accounted, NOT in Cameco revenue), net earnings $58M, adjusted EBITDA $780M (+61%) — ~41% of consolidated adjusted EBITDA (which includes the Westinghouse share). Contract book: ~230M lb uranium committed under long-term contracts. FY2025 avg realized price US$62.11/lb. (6-K Ex-99.1, Feb 13, 2026, https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001009001/000119312526049832/d34107dex991.htm)
4. **US government / Westinghouse term sheet (Oct 28, 2025).** US Dept of Commerce to arrange financing/permits and ORDER ≥US$80B of new Westinghouse reactors (10 AP1000s, construction start target by 2030). In exchange, USG gets a participation interest = 20% of WEC Holdco distributions ABOVE US$17.5B; unvested until USG makes a final investment decision and signs binding orders; auto-terminates Jan 2029 if no vesting event. If vested and an IPO values Westinghouse ≥US$30B, USG can force an IPO by Jan 2029 (interest converts to a 5-yr warrant on 20% of value above $17.5B). The term sheet explicitly frames the goal as making nuclear "a cornerstone of the next generation investment into artificial intelligence and the buildout of AGI in the United States." Definitive agreement was to be signed within 90 days (≈ late Jan 2026); the term sheet governs in the interim. (6-K Ex-99.2, Oct 2025, https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001009001/000119312525260490/d23402dex992.htm)
5. **Uranium prices (UxC/TradeTech month-end via Cameco, as of May 31, 2026):** spot US$84.18/lb, long-term US$94.00/lb — term price up from US$80 a year earlier and at its highest since 2008 (per ETF Trends/Sprott commentary); spot peaked ~US$101 on Jan 29, 2026 then sold off on geopolitics. Term > spot = utilities contracting long. (https://www.cameco.com/invest/markets/uranium-price; https://www.etftrends.com/nuclear-energy-content-hub/nuclears-long-term-signal-uranium-contracting-picking/)
6. **Google–Westinghouse (announced Nov 20, 2025) is a TECHNOLOGY partnership, direction reversed:** Westinghouse uses a custom Google Cloud AI platform to optimize AP1000 construction scheduling (claimed 5–6 yr build timelines vs 15 yrs for Vogtle). Google buys NO power or capacity; no financial terms disclosed. (https://www.datacenterknowledge.com/data-center-construction/google-cloud-westinghouse-team-up-on-ai-nuclear-boost)
7. **AI language audit of primary disclosures:** the FY2025 results release and Q1 2026 results release contain ZERO mentions of "AI," "artificial intelligence," "data center," or "hyperscaler" (verified by regex over both 6-K exhibits). The AI framing for Cameco comes from the USG term sheet and sell-side/media commentary, not from Cameco's own results narrative.

## DIFF (mental model vs evidence)

- **Term price much higher than I thought:** US$94/lb long-term (May 2026) vs my assumed ~$80; spot touched ~$101 in Jan 2026. Term premium over spot confirms the contracting cycle is the signal, not spot.
- **Westinghouse still loses money at the bottom line:** Q1 2026 net loss $46M (CCJ share) despite adjusted EBITDA $122M — purchase-accounting amortization. I had mentally filed Westinghouse as "profitable"; it's EBITDA-positive, net-negative quarterly (FY2025 net was barely positive at $58M share).
- **The $80B deal is government-intermediated, not hyperscaler:** counterparty is the US Dept of Commerce; the participation interest is a COST (gives USG 20% of upside above $17.5B distributions), not revenue. The deal is unvested with a Jan 2029 sunset — option value, zero current revenue.
- **Cameco's own disclosure language doesn't claim AI exposure:** zero AI/datacenter mentions in two consecutive results releases. Management frames demand as "energy security, decarbonization, geopolitics."
- **Realized price lags both spot and term badly:** US$66.21/lb Q1 2026 vs $84 spot / $94 term — legacy contract book means the upside flows through over years, not quarters.

## Evidence for D1 (% revenue genuinely AI-tied TODAY — strict)

- **Strictly 0% contractually AI-tied.** FY2025 revenue $3,482M = uranium $2,874M (82.5%) + fuel services $562M (16.1%), all sold to nuclear utilities/fuel-cycle customers; no datacenter or hyperscaler counterparty anywhere in disclosures (FY2025 6-K, Feb 13, 2026; Q1 2026 6-K, May 5, 2026).
- Westinghouse is equity-accounted — contributes ~41% of FY2025 consolidated adjusted EBITDA ($780M of ~$1.9B) but $0 of revenue. Its AI-motivated $80B USG pipeline has produced no binding orders, no revenue; the USG interest is explicitly "unvested." (Term sheet, Oct 28, 2025)
- The AI link is third-order for the uranium book: AI → utility nuclear demand → term contracting → realized price. The Q1 realized-price gain (+6% YoY to US$66.21/lb) comes from "market-related pricing mechanisms" in existing contracts (Q1 2026 6-K) — driven by the overall term price, of which AI demand is one of several drivers (alongside geopolitics, energy security, decarbonization, supply discipline). No disclosed attribution is possible; Cameco itself doesn't attempt one.
- Counter-consideration: AI is plausibly the marginal demand driver setting the term price (Meta/Microsoft nuclear capacity deals, AI cited in every 2026 uranium outlook — Sprott, Crux, Mining.com), but that is attribution-by-narrative, not disclosure.

## Evidence for D2 (pricing power; Westinghouse new-build position)

- **Term-price maker, not spot-taker:** ~230M lb committed under long-term contracts (FY2025 6-K); average annual deliveries >28M lb/yr over the next five years, front-loaded 2026–2028 (Q1 2026 6-K). Management explicitly withholds uncommitted supply rather than chasing volume ("nor do we... bring forward uncommitted supply that risks recreating the overhangs," FY2025 6-K). Largest Western producer; supply discipline supports the term market.
- **But realized price lags the market by ~30%:** US$66.21/lb realized Q1 2026 vs US$94 term / US$84 spot (UxC May 31, 2026 via cameco.com). Legacy fixed-price layers cap near-term torque; new contracts use market-related mechanisms to "capture greater future upside" (Q1 2026 6-K). Pricing power is real but realized with a multi-year lag.
- **Westinghouse new-build position is strong:** AP1000 is the most-deployed Gen III+ design; USG committed to order ≥$80B (10 reactors); Rev 20 of the AP1000 Design Control Document filed with NRC Apr 2026 standardizing on Vogtle 4 as reference plant (Westinghouse PR); Dukovany (Czech) participation already driving distributions (US$171.5M to CCJ Oct 2025, FY2025 6-K). Share of adjusted EBITDA +61% FY2025, +33% Q1 2026.
- **Pricing-power caveat on Westinghouse:** the USG deal trades upside (20% above $17.5B of distributions) for order flow — a price concession to the customer-financier.

## Evidence for D5 (direct hyperscaler linkage)

- **None found at the revenue level.** No hyperscaler buys uranium, fuel services, or reactors from Cameco/Westinghouse under any disclosed deal.
- Google–Westinghouse (Nov 20, 2025): technology partnership, direction REVERSED — Westinghouse is Google Cloud's AI customer for construction optimization; no power purchase, no reactor order, no disclosed dollars (Data Center Knowledge).
- The $80B reactor program's counterparty is the US government, with AI/AGI as the stated motivation in the term sheet — the closest thing to an AI linkage, but government-intermediated and unvested.
- Brookfield (51% owner) has been reported positioning Westinghouse reactors to power Brookfield's own cloud/datacenter ventures (ZeroHedge, 2026) — not a Cameco disclosure, no signed deal found; treat as unverified.
- Hyperscaler nuclear deals that do exist (Meta and Microsoft capacity agreements per Sprott/ETF Trends 2026 commentary) are with merchant nuclear operators, not with Cameco — they support the demand narrative one layer downstream from CCJ's customers.

## FLAGS

1. **Definitive-agreement status unknown.** Term sheet required definitive docs within ~90 days (≈ Jan 26, 2026); no announcement of execution found in this pass. Per its own terms the term sheet governs in the interim, and Cameco's FY2025 risk language still lists "inability... to enter into definitive agreements" as a risk. Needs follow-up — check next 6-K/MD&A.
2. **USG participation interest is a cost, not revenue** — direction frequently misread in media coverage. Vesting requires USG FID + binding $80B orders; sunsets Jan 2029.
3. **Westinghouse net-negative quarterly** (Q1 2026 share net loss $46M) despite adjusted EBITDA $122M — purchase-accounting amortization; relevant if Quality scoring uses earnings rather than EBITDA.
4. **Currency trap:** Cameco reports in CAD; Investing.com cited "revenue $607.22M" (USD-converted) vs actual C$845M. Use the 6-K CAD figures.
5. **Westinghouse seasonality:** ~41% of consolidated adjusted EBITDA FY2025 but only 24% in Q1 2026 (Q4-weighted refueling-outage business) — don't extrapolate single quarters.
6. **Not verified in this pass:** Cameco's exact Inkai stake %, industry-wide 2026 term-contracting volumes (UxC data is paywalled), and Q1 2026 earnings-call AI commentary (transcript not pulled; press release had zero AI mentions).
