## 1. Pre-research mental model

Intel is a broken IDM mid-turnaround under CEO Lip-Bu Tan (since 2025): the whole story rides on whether 18A (and then 14A) ramps with competitive yields and lands committed *external* foundry customers, against a core CPU franchise still bleeding share to AMD/Arm and an AI-accelerator effort (Gaudi) that has effectively lost to NVIDIA. Earnings are deeply depressed/negative, capex is heavy, FCF is negative, the dividend was cut, and the 2025 capital structure now includes a ~10% US government equity stake (CHIPS-grant conversion) plus a ~$2B SoftBank stake — both dilutive and politically entangling. As a scoring object this is the classic value-trap trap: Value metrics flatter a falling price while Quality/Growth/EPS inputs are garbage on negative GAAP numbers. Thesis-break watch is binary and well-defined: 18A yield/ramp failure, foundry customer losses, further guidance cuts, or liquidity/funding stress.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not pre-decide)

**CRITICAL garbage-input warning (rules 9, 15):**
- **Growth (EPS YoY):** GAAP EPS YoY went $(0.19)→$(0.73), and the Q1 loss is **dominated by a documented one-time $4,070M restructuring charge** (10-Q operating-expense line; non-GAAP EPS +$0.29 vs GAAP $(0.73)). Per **rule 15**, the EPS YoY input should be **blanked** (non-operating/one-time-dominated) — Growth would then average the revenue metrics. Cite the restructuring line in the Rating Audit if applied. Do NOT score EPS YoY mechanically.
- **Quality (margins/ROIC/FCF margin):** GAAP operating margin is negative; ROIC negative; FCF margin negative. TTM quality inputs will score floor on a turnaround. Note MRQ GAAP GM 39.4% is materially better than TTM — flag the TTM-understatement bias (rule 9), but do not invent an MRQ override. Quality genuinely low; the floor is informative, not a bug.
- **Value:** classic value-trap risk (CLAUDE.md rule 14 / expectations lens reversed) — Forward P/E and P/S flatter as price falls and consensus is depressed; FCF Yield is negative (blank or floor). Run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py INTC` per rule 14 before trusting Value (foreign-filer self-skip won't apply; INTC is a domestic filer with standard XBRL revenue tags).
- **Momentum (rule 11):** refresh 50DMA % via `python3 scripts/momentum_50dma.py INTC`; price has been beaten down then bounced on stakes/earnings — verify whether the uptrend is consistent or one-gap.

**Subjective AI-Thesis dimensions (D1–D5) — flag for collaborative review, thesis.md is untouched template (rule 12 gate: this briefing satisfies the research-backed gate for this pass):**
- **D1/D2 (AI demand directness / revenue exposure):** Real but indirect and contested — DCAI +22% is partly CPU-for-inference, not AI-accelerator share; accelerator effort effectively lost to NVIDIA. Foundry AI exposure (MSFT Maia 2, AWS AI Fabric on 18A) is the genuine AI-thesis hook but is *prospective* (ramping), and 14A has zero committed customers.
- **D5 (moat/durability within AI thesis):** Foundry is the only Western leading-edge alternative to TSMC — strategic, but execution-gated and government-subsidized rather than market-proven.

**Risk (R1–R5):**
- **R3 (balance sheet):** Heavy — total debt $45.0B, negative FCF $(2.5B)/qtr, $14.2B Fab 34 buyback drew liquidity; offset by ~$32.8B cash+STI and govt/SoftBank capital. Flag as elevated, not acute, but the burn rate matters.
- **R1/R2 (concentration/geography):** govt 10% stake + warrant creates unusual political/strategic-shareholder risk and constrains the foundry-separation optionality (warrant triggers below 51% foundry ownership).
- **Execution risk** is the dominant qualitative risk: 18A yields not yet at profit-grade (industry-standard 2027), 14A customers uncommitted, accelerator gap.
- **R5 (disruption):** defaults to **5** (non-Layer-10; physical semis/fab infrastructure is not the agentic-AI-disruption cohort). No change indicated.
- **CAO resignation** mid-restructuring — minor governance flag, not a thesis item.

**Thesis-break watch (per task):** NONE triggered this pass. 18A ramp is *ahead* of internal plan (not failing); foundry is *adding* names (MSFT/AWS), not losing them; guidance was *raised*, not cut; liquidity adequate. The live risks (14A zero committed customers; sub-profit 18A yields until 2027; negative FCF burn) are deterioration-watch items, not breaks. Re-check at Q2 2026 earnings.
