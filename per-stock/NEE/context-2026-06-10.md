## Findings (sources)

### 1. Q1 2026 results (reported 2026-04-23)
- Adjusted EPS $1.09 vs $0.99 Q1 2025 (+10%); GAAP $1.04 ($2.182B). (8-K Ex. 99, 2026-04-23, SEC EDGAR accession 0000753308-26-000028)
- Energy Resources adjusted earnings $1.038B / $0.50 per share (vs $908M / $0.44 prior yr) — ~46% of NEE's $2.275B total adjusted earnings. FPL net income $1.462B / $0.70. (same 8-K)
- Record origination quarter: **4 GW added to backlog** (incl. 1.3 GW storage); **backlog now ~33 GW** after 0.3 GW placed in service since the January call. (same 8-K)
- Revenue $6,701M, up 7% YoY but below $7,111M consensus (TIKR summary of release, 2026-04-23 — secondary source).
- Guidance unchanged: 2026 adj EPS $3.92–$4.02 targeting high end; 8%+ adj EPS CAGR through 2032. (8-K Ex. 99)

### 2. Hyperscaler share of origination — first hard mix number
- CFO Brian Bolster, Q1 2026 call (2026-04-23, Motley Fool transcript): "Roughly **30% of our backlog additions are driven by hyperscalers**, while the remaining 70% comes from power utility customers, including cooperatives and municipalities."
- Note: this is the mix of NEW Q1 additions (~1.2 GW of the 4 GW), not the mix of the full 33 GW backlog. Hyperscaler share of the total backlog is NOT disclosed.

### 3. Recontracting pricing — direct pricing-power datapoint
- Q1 2026 call (Motley Fool transcript): "The pricing on the new contracts is roughly a **$20 per megawatt-hour on average increase** relative to the prior realized pricing" (recontracting of existing renewables). Attributed to Michael H. Dunne in the transcript extract — attribution/context should be verified against the full transcript before quoting in the audit log.

### 4. Data center hub strategy + gas build (Energy Resources, competitive side)
- Hubs: "over 30 hubs with a year-end goal to secure roughly 40"; base case **15 GW of new generation serving large load by 2035**, upside 30 GW. (8-K Ex. 99 2026-04-23; Ketchum on Q1 call)
- Four origination channels (Q1 call): (1) direct hyperscaler partnerships (Google/Duane Arnold), (2) IOU partnerships (Xcel), (3) co-ops/munis (Basin Electric 1.5 GW), (4) federal — **US Dept. of Commerce selected NEER in March 2026 to build 9.5 GW of new gas-fired generation in Texas and Pennsylvania** under Japan's $550B US investment commitment; US/Japan would own, NEER develops/builds/operates. Definitive agreements targeted "next two- to three-month period" as of late April. (8-K Ex. 99; Q1 call)
- **South Mon** = the Pennsylvania leg: **4.3 GW, ~$17B**, SW Pennsylvania (Marcellus gas, PJM), designed to serve **up to 3.5 GW of large-load demand incl. co-located data centers**; specific site undisclosed as of April 2026. (Marcellus Drilling News 2026-03 and 2026-04; Pennsylvania Business Report; Utility Dive)
- Separately, NEE plans up to ~8 GW of gas online by 2032 and secured 4 GW of CCGT turbine slots. (Latitude Media, 2025-12-08)
- ExxonMobil JV: 1.2 GW gas + 90% carbon capture dedicated to a datacenter for an **unnamed** hyperscaler. (CNBC 2025-12-08)

### 5. Named hyperscaler commitments (competitive side — Energy Resources)
- **Google: ~3.5 GW contracted** incl. Duane Arnold 615 MW under a **25-year PPA** (terms/$ not disclosed) + at least 3 gigawatt-scale datacenter campuses in development (locations undisclosed) + Google Cloud marketplace energy product mid-2026. (Latitude Media 2025-12-08; NextEra newsroom 2025-10-27)
- **Meta: ~2.5 GW** via 11 PPAs + 2 storage agreements (solar+storage, ERCOT/SPP/MISO/NM), CODs 2026–2028, on top of ~500 MW previously contracted. (Latitude Media 2025-12-08)
- Combined Google+Meta: **~6 GW in operation or contracted** — but the operating portion is not broken out; most CODs are 2026–2028, Duane Arnold ≤Q1 2029.
- **Duane Arnold status:** NRC approved license transfer April 2026 (NEE to 100% ownership via CIPCO/Corn Belt 30% buy-in); on track for service **no later than Q1 2029**, possibly Q4 2028; NRC staff indicated license approval ~January 2028. (Q1 call; Utility Dive; ANS/World Nuclear News)

### 6. Dominion merger — new development since 2026-05-26 news log

Close still guided 12–18 months. (Form 425 filed 2026-06-09, via StockTitan/SEC)
- S-4 / joint proxy not yet mailed as of early June 2026. D's ~51 GW contracted DC pipeline and the 130+ GW combined large-load pipeline remain **pre-close** numbers — not NEE today.

## Evidence for D1 (% revenue datacenter/AI-tied TODAY)

- **Not disclosed by the company.** No datacenter revenue % exists in the Q1 8-K or call.
- Structure: FPL (regulated FL; $1.462B Q1 net income) has no disclosed direct datacenter franchise — Florida is not a major DC market; FPL's growth is population + solar rate base. Energy Resources = ~46% of Q1 adjusted earnings; this is where ALL direct datacenter exposure sits.
- Signed-and-DELIVERING today: of the ~6 GW Google+Meta operating-or-contracted (Latitude 2025-12-08), the operating portion is not broken out; Meta CODs are 2026–2028, Duane Arnold 2029, Google campuses undated. Best estimate: well under 2 GW delivering today against a multi-tens-of-GW operating fleet → **datacenter-tied revenue today is likely low single digits of consolidated revenue — ESTIMATE, flag, no company source**.
- Signed-but-backlog: ~6 GW named-hyperscaler contracts + 30% of Q1 origination (~1.2 GW) hyperscaler-driven. Backlog-wide hyperscaler share not disclosed.
- Conclusion for rating: NEE's direct exposure TODAY is small but contractually locked and growing fast; the "directness" is concentrated in Energy Resources' signed PPAs (real, disclosed counterparties and MW, undisclosed prices) — not in current revenue.

## Evidence for D2 (pricing power on renewables+storage PPAs)

- **For:** recontracting at ~+$20/MWh above prior realized pricing (Q1 2026 call — verify attribution). Duane Arnold 25-yr single-customer nuclear PPA (bilateral, premium implied by restart economics; $ undisclosed). Hub strategy is bilateral/origination-driven ("built on the power of scale," 8-K 4/23), not auction-clearing. 4 GW CCGT turbine slots + EPC labor scarcity (Ketchum: labor is "the biggest constraint... to getting gas built faster") = scarcity accrues to incumbents with secured supply chain.
- **Against:** 70% of Q1 origination is utility/co-op/muni — competitive RFP channel, thinner pricing. US-Japan 9.5 GW structure: US/Japan own the assets, NEER is developer/operator — fee economics, not full PPA margin capture. No hyperscaler PPA prices disclosed anywhere.
