# AR (Antero Resources) — Pre-rating context briefing, 2026-06-09

Prepared for the Layer 1 AI Thesis re-rating (D1 AI Revenue %, D2 supply chain position, D5 hyperscaler exposure). Session lens: direct ownership of the AI power bottleneck vs. second-derivative commodity exposure.

## Pre-research mental model

(Written before pulling any data, verbatim per process.)

Antero Resources is an Appalachian (Marcellus/Utica) natural gas and NGL producer whose distinguishing feature is firm transportation capacity delivering ~75% of its gas to the Gulf Coast LNG corridor, so realizations are tied to LNG-feedgas-premium hubs (TGP 500L, NYMEX-plus) rather than weak in-basin Appalachian pricing; it is also one of the largest US NGL/LPG exporters. I am not aware of any signed datacenter or hyperscaler supply deal — the AI link is the second-derivative story that PJM/Appalachian datacenter buildout tightens regional gas demand and that management has talked up in-basin datacenter demand as a future option. Last quarter I have solid awareness of is roughly Q4 2025/early 2026, with strong FCF from the 2025-26 gas price recovery and ongoing buybacks. Competitive position: low-cost producer with no hedges (high beta to gas prices), price-taker on commodity benchmarks.

Secondary sources report net income $535M, adjusted FCF $657M, record net production 3.9 Bcfe/d, EPS $1.72 vs $1.17 consensus (StockTitan summary of the 4/29/2026 8-K press release, https://www.stocktitan.net/news/AR/antero-resources-announces-first-quarter-2026-financial-and-55s8d4sdonkt.html; Investing.com Q1 slides note, 2026-04-29).

2. **HG Energy acquisition closed 2026-02-03.** Added 385,000 net acres, ~400 drilling locations, ~700 MMcfe/d expected 2026 net production; net debt up ~$1.5B vs YE2025; pro forma 2026 production guided to ~4.1-4.2 Bcfe/d (~20% YoY growth) (10-Q Q1 2026 business-combination note; StockTitan 8-K summary, 2026-04-29). Company framing: acquired dry-gas assets provide "optionality for local demand from data centers and natural gas fired power plants" — optionality, not contracts (Antero press release via anteroresources.com, Dec 2025, https://www.anteroresources.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/252/antero-resources-announces-strategic-transactions-with).

3. **No signed datacenter/power-plant/hyperscaler supply agreement exists as of 2026-06-09.** The Q1 2026 10-Q contains ZERO occurrences of "data center," "AI," "hyperscaler," or "behind-the-meter" (full-text scan of local copy, filed 2026-04-29). The four 2026 8-Ks since March are: Raymond James conference Reg FD (3/3), Q1 earnings (4/29), updated investor presentation Reg FD (5/12), annual-meeting vote results (6/4) — no material-agreement 8-K. Q1 earnings call (2026-04-29): CFO Brendan Krueger — "we have participated in requests to provide proposals for gas supply that total over 5 Bcf per day"; SVP Gas Marketing Justin Fowler — regional power projects "amount to over 8 Bcf per day of demand" (transcript via AOL/Motley Fool, https://www.aol.com/articles/antero-ar-q1-2026-earnings-164634000.html). RFP participation is the entirety of the direct-AI-demand evidence.

4. **AR's realizations are LNG/export-driven, not AI-driven.** CEO Michael Kennedy (Q1 2026 call): "selling 2.3 Bcf per day of production to sales points along the LNG fairway" — claimed highest LNG exposure among Appalachian producers (same transcript). ~75% of gas moves to the Gulf Coast on firm transport; ~90% of gas sells at "Tier 1" price points (company investor materials as summarized by Morningstar/Yahoo Finance coverage, 2026, https://finance.yahoo.com/news/antero-poised-benefit-second-wave-120000406.html). NGL side: Q1 C3+ realized at $0.94/bbl premium to Mont Belvieu; FY2026 ethane premium guidance raised to $2.00-3.00/bbl (Q1 call, SVP Liquids Marketing David Cannelongo; StockTitan 8-K summary 4/29/2026) — these premiums are export-dock/LPG-market driven, zero AI linkage.

5. **The in-basin AI demand story is real at the basin level but uncontracted at the AR level.** Antero's own materials graph up to ~5 Bcf/d incremental Appalachian gas demand by 2030 from power plants and AI datacenters (Hart Energy, "Antero: Graphing Appalachia's 5-Bcf/d AI-Fueled Demand Growth," https://www.hartenergy.com/exclusives/antero-graphing-appalachias-5-bcfd-ai-fueled-demand-growth-213704). BofA and Morgan Stanley raised AR price targets citing potential to supply a WV AI datacenter hub (Insider Monkey, 2026, https://www.insidermonkey.com/blog/antero-resources-ar-under-the-radar-energy-stock-pick-from-david-einhorn-1750460/). Contrast: peer EQT has an actual signed 10-year, 1.2 Bcf/d supply agreement with Duke Energy and Southern Company (Natural Gas Intelligence, https://naturalgasintel.com/news/appalachia-eps-take-advantage-of-natural-gas-demand-growth-for-in-basin-data-centers/). ## Evidence for D1 (AI Revenue %)

Genuinely AI-tied revenue is ~0% of current revenue. The math: Q1 2026 revenue $1,945.1M = gas $1,311.5M (67.4%) + NGL $503.6M (25.9%) + oil $46.7M (2.4%) + derivatives/marketing/other (10-Q Q1 2026, filed 2026-04-29). The NGL ~26% clears to international LPG/petchem export markets (Mont Belvieu + export premiums) — zero AI linkage. The gas 67% sells at LNG-corridor and Henry Hub-linked index points; the 44% YoY revenue growth came from gas-price recovery plus HG-acquired volumes, not from any AI-priced contract. There is no revenue line, contract, or disclosed volume sold to a datacenter, power plant serving datacenters, or hyperscaler — the 10-Q literally never mentions data centers or AI. Any AI contribution today is a second-derivative basis/demand effect inside commodity pricing, unquantified and unquantifiable from disclosures. Per the session lens (datacenter ≠ AI; commodity uplift ≠ AI revenue), D1 evidence supports a floor-level score.

## Evidence for D2 (Supply chain position)

AR is a price-taker with a superior logistics portfolio, not a price-maker to AI buyers. Its premium realizations (~90% of gas at Tier 1 points, 2.3 Bcf/d to the LNG fairway, C3+ at +$0.94/bbl to Mont Belvieu in Q1 — Q1 2026 call and investor materials) come from firm transportation it bought years ago, and those premiums are set by LNG-feedgas and LPG-export benchmarks, not negotiated with AI/datacenter counterparties. The only direct engagement with the AI power demand stack is participation in RFPs totaling >5 Bcf/d (CFO Krueger, Q1 2026 call) — proposals, with no disclosed wins, pricing structure, or term. If in-basin demand materializes (AR's own 5 Bcf/d-by-2030 Appalachian forecast, Hart Energy), AR's HG dry-gas position gives it an option on tighter local basis — but an option on better commodity pricing is still commodity pricing. No evidence of pricing power FROM AI buyers.

## Evidence for D5 (Hyperscaler exposure)

Zero signed, disclosed deals with named hyperscalers or datacenter developers. SEC filings March-June 2026 contain no material-definitive-agreement 8-K and no datacenter language in the 10-Q. The closest items are: (1) >5 Bcf/d of RFP participation, counterparties unnamed (Q1 2026 call); (2) geographic proximity narrative — Google's Putnam County WV datacenter and the WV 50-by-50 plan sit on top of AR's acreage, and sell-side (BofA, Morgan Stanley) has raised targets on that narrative; (3) management marketing the HG acquisition as datacenter "optionality." Meanwhile peer EQT has a signed 10-yr 1.2 Bcf/d agreement with Duke/Southern — the benchmark for what "direct" looks like in this layer — and AR has nothing comparable. D5 is narrative-stage, not contract-stage.
