## 1. Pre-research mental model

ARM is the dominant CPU/GPU instruction-set IP licensor (royalty + upfront license model) whose AI exposure runs through (a) Armv9 royalty-rate uplift, (b) Compute Subsystems (CSS) raising per-chip value, and (c) data-center traction where the major hyperscaler custom CPUs (AWS Graviton, Google Axion, NVIDIA Grace) are all Arm-based. Expected to be a structurally advantaged, capital-light, very high-margin franchise carrying a **very rich valuation multiple**, with the live new angle being a possible pivot into selling its own silicon. Governance/float distorted by SoftBank majority ownership; key watch-items are royalty deceleration, Arm China, guidance, and the Qualcomm/Nuvia litigation.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not pre-decide; collaborative session required per rules 2/12)

*No ratings changed here. `thesis.md` is the untouched template, so per rule 12 ARM has no research-backed rating basis until this briefing (or a populated thesis) is used in a collaborative session. Flags below are inputs for that session.*

- **D1 (AI revenue directness/mix):** Strong positive signal — data-center royalty "more than doubled" YoY; ~50% hyperscaler CPU share; AGI CPU purpose-built for agentic AI. Revisit upward bias, but note AI is still a minority of the $4.92B base (royalty $2.61B is all-end-market; mobile/IoT still large). Cite the mix, don't assume "pure AI."
- **D2 (customer/contract momentum):** AGI CPU >$2B committed (Meta lead; OpenAI/Cloudflare/SAP/SK Telecom); ACV +22%. Positive.
- **D5 (moat/defensibility):** Architecture lock-in + ~100% networking share + CSS time-to-market advantage. But own-chip pivot could **strain customer relationships** (Arm now competes with its own licensees) — two-sided.
- **Momentum (M):** Record print but **−7% on the day** and a referenced ~25% subsequent drawdown — relative-strength signal is mixed; needs the objective 50DMA% pull (rule 11) before any momentum view. Flag, don't assume strong.
- **R5 Disruption Risk:** **Defaults to 5** (rule 16) — ARM is Layer 04 foundational compute IP/infrastructure, not an agentic-AI-disruptable application/SaaS model. AI is a tailwind, not a threat to the revenue model. No sub-5 R5.

R2 geography — **Arm China** is an uncontrolled related party and the sole PRC conduit; China/export-control tail risk. R4 regulatory/legal — **Qualcomm/Nuvia litigation ongoing**. Governance: **SoftBank 86.4%** control + ~13% float (squeeze/overhang/related-party risk) — relevant to Risk framing.
