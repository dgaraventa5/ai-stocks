# RRC — Pre-rating context briefing, 2026-06-09

Prepared for AI Thesis re-rating session (Layer 1 directness lens: direct power-to-datacenter sales vs. second-derivative commodity exposure).

## Pre-research mental model

RRC is a pure-play Appalachian (Marcellus) natural gas E&P — one of the lowest-cost dry gas + NGL producers, ~2.2 Bcfe/d, with a multi-decade core inventory. The AI link in the existing thesis is second-derivative: AI datacenter buildout drives gas-fired power demand (in-basin Appalachia and nationally), tightening the gas market and lifting Henry Hub and in-basin differentials, which flows through to RRC's realizations. I recall no signed hyperscaler PPA or named datacenter contract; the most recent quarter I knew of at scoring time (2026-05-19 sensitivity file) was Q1 2026, summarized only as "Marcellus dry gas; AI gas demand thesis," with subjective ratings D1=4, D2=4, D5=4 locked at a 75.96 ✓✓ total. My prior is that those 4s reflect thesis enthusiasm rather than contracted AI revenue, and that RRC is a price-taker on NYMEX/in-basin basis.

## DIFF vs mental model

- **Confirmed:** RRC's AI link is second-derivative (commodity demand → price), not direct power sales. No hyperscaler PPA, no behind-the-meter MW. The mental model was directionally right.
- **New/sharper:** (a) The single signed "premium" deal (75 MMcf/d Midwest power plant) is smaller and later than the narrative implies — 5% of gas volumes, revenue starts late 2027, counterparty unnamed, not confirmed as datacenter-serving. (b) Q1's +$0.18 premium to NYMEX is a seasonal/marketing-portfolio outcome; management itself still guides FY26 to NYMEX minus $0.35–0.45 — the structural basis discount persists. (c) Fort Cherry is 14 months post-announcement with still no disclosed MW/offtake/economics — it is an option, not a contract. (d) Pipeline of "a dozen+" in-basin power/datacenter dialogues is broader than I had registered, and NextEra's 4.3 GW South Mon hub is a genuinely large in-basin demand catalyst near Range's acreage — the OPTION value is real and growing, but none of it is signed revenue.
- **Implication for prior ratings:** D1=4, D2=4, D5=4 look generous under the directness lens. Current AI-tied revenue is ~0%; pricing power from AI buyers is one unstarted contract; hyperscaler exposure is zero signed.

## Evidence for D1 (AI Revenue %)

Genuinely AI-tied share of CURRENT revenue is approximately **0%**. Math: Q1 2026 revenue ~$1.03B (Q1 2026 press release / Investing.com, 2026-04-21) derives from selling 2.21 Bcfe/d of gas/NGLs/condensate at market-linked prices (FY26 guidance NYMEX minus $0.35–0.45 for gas). The only contract with any power/datacenter linkage — 75 MMcf/d, 10-yr, premium to Midwest regional prices (Q4 2025 release, 2026-02-24) — generates no revenue until the plant completes in late 2027, and even then would cover ~5% of current gas volumes with an undisclosed premium and no confirmation the plant serves datacenters. Any 2025–26 uplift in realizations from datacenter-driven gas demand is commodity price beta, which under the session lens does not count as AI revenue. No company disclosure quantifies any AI-linked revenue stream.

## Evidence for D2 (Supply chain position)

RRC is a **price-taker with a marketing-portfolio edge, not a holder of AI pricing power**. Structural evidence: FY2026 gas differential guidance of NYMEX minus $0.35–0.45 (Q1 2026 release, 2026-04-21) — Range sells at a discount to the benchmark in a takeaway-constrained basin. The Q1 +$0.18 premium was a seasonal high-water mark management celebrated as "best in over a decade," which itself tells you premiums are the exception. Counter-evidence (limited): the 75 MMcf/d deal was struck "at a premium to Midwest regional prices" — proof Range CAN extract premium terms from firm-power buyers — and management frames its low-cost, multi-decade inventory as the reason power developers negotiate with it ("long-term surety of supply," Q1 call, 2026-04-22). But that is one unstarted contract on ~5% of volumes; the other ~dozen opportunities (Fort Cherry, NextEra South Mon) have zero disclosed terms. Net: pricing power from AI buyers is demonstrated in principle, immaterial in practice.

## Evidence for D5 (Hyperscaler exposure)

**No signed, disclosed deal with any named hyperscaler or datacenter developer.** Inventory of everything public: (1) 75 MMcf/d Midwest power plant contract — counterparty unnamed, plant type/end-customer undisclosed (Q4 2025 release, 2026-02-24); (2) Fort Cherry strategic alliance with Liberty Energy (a frac-services company) and Imperial Land Corporation (a real-estate developer) — an MOU-grade alliance from April 2025 with no MW, no offtake, no datacenter tenant named, still in "dialogue" as of the Q1 2026 call; (3) NextEra South Mon — Range mentioned only "potential participation" (Q1 call, 2026-04-22). No MSFT/GOOGL/AMZN/META/ORCL or major colo developer (QTS, Vantage, CloudHQ, etc.) appears in any Range disclosure. Hyperscaler exposure is narrative-stage, fully prospective.
