## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim, written before pulling data)

> "NVDA is the central AI compute supplier: Blackwell (GB200/GB300) is in full ramp with Rubin slated for 2026H2/2027, demand still supply-constrained, and networking (NVLink + Spectrum-X Ethernet) is becoming a second growth engine that takes share from Arista/Broadcom Ethernet incumbents. China data-center revenue is effectively zero due to export controls (H20 ban), so the model is now an ex-China story with possible upside if any licensed SKU returns. The main structural threat is hyperscaler custom ASICs (Broadcom XPUs for Google/Meta/OpenAI) eroding NVDA's share of accelerator capex over a multi-year horizon, but in the near term CUDA lock-in, rack-scale systems and supply agreements keep NVDA dominant. I expect cash generation to remain enormous (FCF margin historically ~45-50%), so a reported TTM FCF margin collapse to 18% is more likely a working-capital/prepayment artifact (inventory build for Blackwell/Rubin ramp, supply prepayments) than a profitability break — but that is precisely the thing to verify, not assume."

## 5. DIFF since training / prior mental model

1. **FCF "collapse" is fake** — statement FCF margin is 47.0% TTM / 59.5% in Q1; the 18.3% is a Yahoo levered-FCF artifact. Mental model was right to be suspicious, but wrong about mechanism (suspected inventory/prepayments; actual cause is a data-source field, and Q1 working capital was net positive).
2. **China is no longer strictly "zero with no path"** — H200 licenses exist since Feb 2026 (~10 approved buyers), POs received, manufacturing restarting; but $0 revenue recognized and Chinese-side import approval still unknown. Optionality is now real but unpriced in guidance.
3. **Customer concentration is rising at the billing level** (top-3 direct = 54% of revenue, 64% of AR) even as platform mix diversifies (50/50 Hyperscale/ACIE) — the diversification narrative needs this asterisk.
4. **NVDA is now a major investment house**: $43.4B non-marketable securities, $27B further commitments, with disclosed circularity ("AI model makers that may indirectly purchase or use our products"). $15.9B of Q1 net income is non-cash investment gains — GAAP EPS overstates operating earnings by ~27%.
5. **Vera standalone CPU is a new TAM** ($200B claimed, $20B FY27 revenue visibility) not in the prior model at all; ditto the Groq non-exclusive license (~$4B accrued consideration).
6. **$1T Blackwell+Rubin 2025–2027 revenue visibility** is a new, falsifiable management anchor.
7. **OpEx growth "upper 40s%" YoY** and R&D compute costs +112% — operating-leverage assumption needs trimming.
8. **Supply commitments $145B with $95B payable within FY27** — future quarters' FCF will absorb this; the *real* cash-flow story is forward-loaded, not in Q1.
