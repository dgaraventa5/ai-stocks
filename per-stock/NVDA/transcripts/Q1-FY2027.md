## Prepared Remarks — Colette Kress, CFO

**Total revenue:** $81.6B (reported as ~$82B rounded), up 85% YoY and 20% sequentially. Thirteenth [note: multiple sources say fourteenth] straight quarter of sequential growth; $13.5B sequential increase — a new company record.

**Segment reporting change:** NVIDIA reorganized reporting into two market platforms: **Data Center** (sub-segmented into Hyperscale and ACIE) and **Edge Computing**. This replaces the prior Compute/Networking split within Data Center.

### Data Center: $75.2B (+92% YoY, +21% QoQ)

**Hyperscale:** $37.9B (~50% of data center revenue). Increased 12% quarter-over-quarter.

> "Hyperscale at $38 billion was approximately 50% of data center revenue and increased 12% quarter over quarter."

**ACIE (AI Cloud, Industrial, Enterprise):** $37.4B (~50% of data center revenue). Grew 31% quarter-over-quarter.

> "ACIE revenue was $37 billion and grew 31% quarter over quarter including AI cloud revenue that more than tripled year over year."

**Sovereign AI:** [paraphrase] Sovereign revenue increased more than 80% year over year, with deployments across nearly 40 countries. Jensen Huang has characterized sovereign AI as a national infrastructure imperative.

> "Sovereign revenue increased more than 80% year over year...deployed across nearly 40 countries."

**Networking:** $14.8B (+199% YoY). Management broke this down:

> "InfiniBand has also had a very strong quarter growing more than 4x year over year."
> "Spectrum-X, our end-to-end Ethernet platform purpose built for AI is now larger than all peers combined."

**Supply commitments:** Total supply inclusive of inventory, purchase commitments, and prepaids increased to $145B (up from $119B end of Q4 FY2026; inventory alone $25.8B).

> "We increased total supply inclusive of inventory purchase commitments on prepaids to $145 billion."

**Blackwell:** [paraphrase] Blackwell described as fastest product ramp in company history. Grace Blackwell delivers 2.7x throughput boost and 60% reduction in cost-per-token versus six months prior. Partner data centers larger than 10MW nearly doubled in one year, now exceeding 80 sites.

### Edge Computing: $6.4B

[paraphrase — primarily gaming, automotive, and professional visualization. Not a focus of call commentary.]

### Gross Margin
- GAAP: 74.9%
- Non-GAAP: 75.0%
- Q2 guidance GM: 75.0% ±50bps non-GAAP

### EPS
- Non-GAAP diluted EPS: $1.87

### Free Cash Flow
- FCF: $48.6B for the quarter

### Capital Return
- Dividend raised from $0.01 to $0.25/share
- $80B additional share repurchase authorized

### Q2 FY2027 Guidance
- Revenue: $91.0B ±2% (~$89.2B–$92.8B)
- Non-GAAP GM: 75.0% ±50bps
- Non-GAAP OpEx: ~$5.3B

### Operating Expense Outlook
> "For the full year, we now expect OpEx to grow somewhere in the upper forties on a year over year basis."

[paraphrase: driven by R&D and internal AI tool usage. The company is using AI heavily in its own chip design, which is ironically increasing compute costs internally.]

### China
> "We are not including any China data center compute revenue in our outlook."
> "We are uncertain whether any imports will be allowed into the country. As a result, consistent with last quarter, we are not including any China data center compute revenue in our outlook."

[paraphrase: Zero H200 shipments occurred in Q1 and none are anticipated. No timeline for re-engagement. This is now the second consecutive quarter with zero China data center compute revenue.]

## Prepared Remarks — Jensen Huang, CEO

> "Demand has gone parabolic. The reason is simple. Agentic AI has arrived. AI can now do productive and valuable work. Tokens are now profitable."

> "Agentic AI and reinforcement learning represents new growth opportunities for CPUs."

[paraphrase] Jensen described AI as having crossed a threshold where inference is economically self-sustaining. The key structural shift: AI agents can now generate revenue, making token generation profitable, which creates a self-reinforcing demand loop for compute.

**On hyperscaler capex cycle durability:**
[paraphrase] Jensen cited hyperscale CapEx forecast exceeding $1 trillion in 2027 and reaching $3–4 trillion annually by decade's end. He framed this as the "Industrial Revolution of AI" where every country and company needs AI infrastructure.

**On ACIE growth and customer base expansion:**
> "The second category is growing incredibly fast...hundreds, thousands of companies, and in the future, hundreds of thousands of companies."
[paraphrase] The 50/50 split between hyperscalers and ACIE was explicitly noted as a diversification milestone. Jensen emphasized that the addressable market is expanding from ~5–6 hyperscalers to effectively unlimited enterprise customers.

**On competitive positioning:**
> "NVIDIA Compute supports the richest diversity of demand...NVIDIA is quite unique in being the only company that builds all technology components."
[paraphrase] Jensen positioned NVIDIA as the only full-stack AI platform — from silicon to networking to software (CUDA). The "only platform that runs every frontier AI model" framing was used to rebuff custom ASIC substitution narratives.

[Note: AMD and specific custom ASICs (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Microsoft Maia) were NOT directly named or addressed in prepared remarks or Q&A per transcript sources. This is notable — either analysts didn't press hard on it or the question was fielded at a high level.]

**On Vera Rubin product timeline:**
> "Production shipments of VeraRubin set to begin in Q3, with ramp continuing into Q4."

[paraphrase from Colette Kress]: "We will be launching VeraRubin in the second half. We will start in Q3...Q1 of next year certainly is going to be very big."

**On Vera CPU and new TAM:**
[paraphrase] Vera CPU opens a "$200 billion TAM" — standalone CPU market for agentic AI workloads. Management cited $20B in standalone CPU revenue visibility for 2026. This is a new incremental market NVIDIA had not previously targeted with its own silicon.

**On NVLink Fusion (Marvell partnership):**
[paraphrase from context gathered in weekly scan: NVLink Fusion was announced at GTC. It allows third-party chipmakers to connect their custom ASICs to NVIDIA's NVLink fabric. Marvell was the named partner. This is strategically significant — rather than fighting custom ASICs, NVIDIA is positioning NVLink as the fabric standard that even custom silicon routes through. Not extensively discussed on this earnings call per transcript sources.]

**On Blackwell 300:**
[paraphrase] Blackwell 300 products are driving the current ramp. The Grace Blackwell NVL72 system is the flagship. Management noted they are "shipping product at scale" with all major hyperscalers receiving Blackwell.

**On sovereign AI and geopolitics:**
[paraphrase] Sovereign AI described as a structural secular trend — countries treating AI compute as national infrastructure. ~40 countries deploying. Jensen has previously called this "a new form of national defense."

## Q&A Session

[Note: Specific analyst attribution and verbatim Q&A exchanges were not recoverable from available free sources at time of fetch. Key themes from Q&A as summarized by multiple sources:]

### Demand durability / when does this cycle end?
[paraphrase] Management pushed back on cyclicality framing. Jensen's position: inference demand is structurally different from prior GPU cycles because tokens are now monetized — every token serves a paying customer or generates measurable ROI. The build-out is analogous to internet infrastructure in the late 1990s–2000s, except the payback loop is faster.

### Customer concentration
[paraphrase] Management highlighted the 50/50 hyperscale/ACIE split as evidence demand is broadening. Hyperscale grew 12% QoQ while ACIE grew 31% QoQ — ACIE is the faster-growing half. Enterprise and sovereign are the incremental demand drivers.

### China
[paraphrase] No path to re-engagement was outlined. Management noted the licensing uncertainty and declined to speculate on potential H20 or successor product approvals. China is explicitly excluded from guidance.

### Supply chain
[paraphrase] Supply commitments at $145B signal confidence in demand outlook. Management noted the scale and complexity of the supply chain are unprecedented, but partners are tracking. No specific lead-time numbers disclosed.

### Operating leverage
[paraphrase] OpEx growing ~upper 40s% YoY — analysts flagged this as fast. Management indicated R&D spend acceleration is intentional (Rubin, next-gen silicon) and AI tool usage internally is a driver of compute costs within G&A/R&D.

### Competition (LPX / inference-optimized ASICs)
[paraphrase] Management acknowledged inference-optimized LPX-style products (high SRAM, low throughput) but characterized them as niche — suited for low-latency, high token-rate but low throughput use cases. NVIDIA's position: training + inference + diverse model types = NVIDIA platform advantage.
