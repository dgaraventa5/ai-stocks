## Management Tone on AI Demand

**Verdict: More confident than Q3, structurally bullish.**

The May 25 context briefing captured a slight concern about Q4 gross margin compression (guided 64–66%) and wondered whether it reflected pricing concessions to hyperscalers. That concern is resolved. The actual Q4 gross margin of 68.3% (non-GAAP) is essentially unchanged from Q3's 68.6% — not just a modest beat but a complete reversal of the "pricing pressure" narrative.

Brennan's language on AI demand: described the macro as "fundamentally multi-year" with no capex slowdown signals at hyperscaler customers. The FY27 >80% growth guide is the most direct expression of management confidence — they are guiding nearly doubling from a $1.3B base. This is not cautious guidance language.

New tone element: The CEO explicitly framed FY27 as an "inflection point" for the optical business — a phrase that signals this is the year where the optical thesis stops being forward guidance and starts being revenue. The PSU award structure (revenue hurdles to $7.5B) makes this structural commitment, not just conference call optimism.

**Change vs. prior model:** More confident. No signals of demand deceleration.

## Customer Concentration Changes

**Verdict: Concentrated further at the top; fifth hyperscaler not yet material.**

| Period | Structure |
|--------|-----------|
| Q1 FY2026 | Three customers each >10% of quarterly revenue |
| FY2026 Annual | Customer B 33%, Customer D 32%; top 2 = 65% of annual revenue |

This is a mixed signal. On one hand, the total number of hyperscaler relationships grew (five vs. the three we tracked in Q1). On the other, the FY26 annual figures show that two customers drove ~65% of the year's revenue — a higher absolute concentration than the Q1 snapshot suggested.

Interpretation: The Q3/Q4 ramp was driven disproportionately by Customer B and Customer D (likely two of the largest hyperscalers — Amazon and Microsoft are the most speculative candidates, though not confirmed). The third at-10%-in-Q1 customer either reduced or did not ramp at the same rate. The fourth and fifth hyperscalers are relationships but not yet material.

**Implication for R1 (Customer Concentration):** The current R1 score of 2 (poor) is likely still appropriate — the concentration worsened to 65% from top 2 vs. the "three at 10-15%" picture in Q1. If Customer B or D reduced spend, CRDO's revenue would drop materially. The PSU revenue hurdle structure ($2.5B→$7.5B) implicitly assumes the concentration diversifies over time as more hyperscalers ramp.

**Change vs. prior model:** Concentration increased at the very top. Fifth hyperscaler onboarded but not yet meaningful.

## Gross Margin — The Key Thesis Surprise

The May 25 context briefing flagged Q4's 64–66% gross margin guide as "a yellow flag for pricing power." The actual result of 68.3% invalidates this concern entirely.

**What drove the upside:**
- AEC product mix remained high-margin — no evidence of pricing concessions to hyperscalers
- Operating leverage at $437M quarterly revenue
- DustPhotonics closed May 28 (two days before fiscal year-end) — effectively no DustPhotonics revenue or cost dilution in Q4 FY26 results

**Implication:** The quality of CRDO's revenue is better than the Q3 guide suggested. Gross margins at 68% with $437M quarterly revenue on a fabless model implies exceptional pricing power and/or favorable mix. The Q3 guide was likely conservative or reflected anticipated product mix changes that didn't materialize.

## New Risks Raised

1. **Optical transition execution risk.** The shift from pure-AEC to optical is a major strategic bet ($750M acquisition + >$600M FY27 optical revenue target). Each of the three optical legs (DSPs, SiPho PICs, ZeroFlap) needs to >$100M in a single fiscal year. DustPhotonics was founded in 2018 and has not yet delivered at commercial scale. The FY27 optical inflection is real but execution risk is elevated.

2. **Valuation compression post-earnings.** Stock fell 7.61% on June 16 despite strong results. Analysts cited 18× fwd EV/Revenue as the constraint. At these multiples, any shortfall vs. the >80% growth guide will be punished severely. "The risk-to-reward ratio had become unfavorable for near-term positions" per analyst commentary.

3. **AEC-to-optical revenue gap risk.** FY27 growth is sequenced as AEC-led in H1 and optical-inflecting in H2. If the optical ramp slips to FY28, H2 FY27 could disappoint significantly. The market is pricing in the H2 optical ramp.

4. **Customer B + D concentration.** Two customers = 65% of FY26 revenue. If either reduces AI capex or in-sources connectivity (hyperscaler build-vs-buy risk), the revenue impact would be severe.

5. **Insider selling.** CTO and other insiders continued net selling through Q4 (noted in May 25 briefing: CTO ~$10M sold over 12 months). The PSU structure is encouraging (aligned), but the cash selling is an ongoing signal worth monitoring.
