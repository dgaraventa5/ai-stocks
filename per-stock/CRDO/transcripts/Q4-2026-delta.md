# CRDO Q4 FY2026 — Delta Summary
**Prepared:** 2026-06-05
**vs.:** Prior context briefing (2026-05-25) and Q3 FY2026 results

---

## 1. Management tone on AI demand

**Shift: Confident and expansionary — no deceleration signals**

Q4 FY2026 results show no slowdown in hyperscaler demand for Credo's AEC products. Revenue came in at $437M (+157% YoY), ahead of guidance midpoint of $430M. The Q4 guide had initially concerned the market (sequential growth decelerating from Q3's +52% QoQ to mid-single-digits), but actual performance validated that the business remains healthy at scale.

Management's FY2027 guidance message is notable: mid-single-digit sequential growth in H1, accelerating sharply in H2 driven by optical product ramp. This is a confidence signal that the next wave of growth has a specific, near-term driver (optical) rather than being open-ended speculation.

---

## 2. Biggest surprise vs. prior context: Gross margins did NOT compress

**Prior concern:** Context-2026-05-25.md flagged Q4 FY2026 gross margin guidance of 64–66% as a yellow flag ("gross margin compression while revenue grows is a yellow flag for pricing power").

**Actual result:** Q4 non-GAAP gross margin came in at 68.3% — essentially equal to Q3's 68.6% and well above the 64–66% guidance.

This is a significant thesis-support data point. The Q4 guidance was likely conservative accounting for potential product mix shifts (AEC vs. upcoming optical), and actual execution exceeded expectations. The quality score should be reassessed given this.

---

## 3. DustPhotonics acquisition — unchanged, expected close Q1 FY2027

The DustPhotonics deal is on track to close in Q2 calendar 2026 (= Q1 FY2027, May–August 2026). No new adverse terms disclosed. Management reiterated:
- Combined optical portfolio: >$600M revenue in FY2027
- ZeroFlap + Optical DSPs + SiPho PICs each growing >80% in FY2027

This is a large bet on CPO/NPO architectures becoming important at 1.6T and 3.2T. The risk is integration and whether the >$600M optical revenue materializes.

## 4. Customer concentration / mix changes

**Unchanged structure, continued improvement:**
- Three hyperscalers each >10% of Q4 FY2026 revenue
- Fourth hyperscaler near 10% threshold; fifth secured
- No adverse changes; hyperscaler diversification continues to improve

High customer concentration remains the primary risk. Any pause in spending from the top 3 hyperscalers would have a severe revenue impact. However, the secular AI buildout thesis mitigates this somewhat.

## 5. EPS trajectory

**Q4 FY2026: $1.16 non-GAAP EPS (vs. $0.35 PY = +231% YoY, vs. $0.98 consensus = +18.4%)**

This is the fourth consistent quarterly beat. The pattern of non-GAAP EPS exceeding consensus by 15–22% over multiple quarters is a signal that analyst models are systematically under-modeling CRDO's profitability. The operating leverage in the fabless model is significant at scale.

Full FY2026 non-GAAP EPS: $3.46. At current stock prices (~$183 as of last check), trailing P/E is ~53x — expensive, but has compressed from 90%+ levels earlier in the year.

---

## 6. The "inline guidance" sell-off: Risk signal or buying opportunity?

**Key tension:** Q1 FY2027 midpoint guide of $470M = exactly at consensus. No upside beat. Market sold off.

**Assessment:** This is a valuation / expectations reset, not a thesis break. CRDO has been priced for perfection. The underlying business continues to grow at 150%+ YoY, gross margins beat guidance, and the optical ramp is a multi-year growth driver. The sell-off reflects:
1. Elevated buy-side expectations
2. Law of large numbers — sequential growth moderates at $437M/quarter base
3. FY2027 H1 will be an optical ramp investment period; H2 has the actual revenue

The key watch item: whether Q1 FY2027 actual revenue comes in above the $465-475M guide (as has been the pattern). If H2 optical ramp materializes at $600M+ as guided, forward growth re-accelerates.
