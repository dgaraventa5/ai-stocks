# CRDO (Credo Technology) -- Context Briefing 2026-05-25

## 1. Pre-research mental model

> What I currently believe about CRDO:
> - Credo Technology designs high-speed connectivity solutions -- Active Electrical Cables (AECs), line cards, and SerDes IP for data center interconnect
> - Key customers are hyperscalers (likely Microsoft, Meta, Google) who need GPU-to-GPU interconnect within AI training clusters
> - Revenue has been growing rapidly as 800G/1.6T deployment ramps
> - Competes with Broadcom's connectivity solutions, Amphenol (passive cables), and to some extent optical transceiver companies
> - Products include the Dove AEC for short-reach connections, HiWire switch, and various SerDes IP blocks
> - Recent quarters likely showed strong revenue acceleration tied to AI infrastructure buildout

### Finding 3: DustPhotonics acquisition ($750M cash + stock) extends into silicon photonics

On April 13, 2026, Credo announced the acquisition of DustPhotonics, an Israeli developer of Silicon Photonics Photonic Integrated Circuit (SiPho PIC) technology for optical transceivers, for $750M in cash + ~0.92M shares upfront, plus up to ~3.21M shares in contingent consideration (total deal value up to ~$1.3B including earnouts).

Strategic rationale: Bring SiPho PIC technology in-house, expand addressable market across 800G, 1.6T, and 3.2T for both NPO (Near-Package Optics) and CPO (Co-Packaged Optics). Management expects combined optical portfolio (ZeroFlap Optical Transceivers + Optical DSPs + Silicon Photonics) to generate >$500M in optical revenue in FY2027.

Expected to close Q2 calendar 2026 (Q1 FY2027).

## 3. DIFF: What changed vs. training data / mental model

1. **Revenue ramp far exceeded expectations.** I expected rapid growth, but Q3 FY2026 at $407M was roughly a tripling of Q3 FY2025 revenue. The FY2026 full-year run rate of ~$1.3B was not in my mental model -- this is a company that did ~$200M in FY2024.

2. **DustPhotonics acquisition is new and significant.** A $750M+ deal to acquire silicon photonics PIC technology was not in my model. This is a strategic pivot from pure copper/AEC into optical, positioning Credo for the 1.6T/3.2T era where CPO becomes important. This materially expands TAM but also introduces integration risk and a large cash outlay.

3. **Five hyperscaler customers, not the 2-3 I assumed.** Customer diversification is better than expected. Three at >10% revenue, fourth approaching that level, fifth secured. This is a meaningful de-risking from where they were 12-18 months ago.

4. **All major patent disputes settled.** I had Amphenol/Molex/TE as competitors and potential legal threats. The cross-license settlements suggest Credo's AEC IP is now validated, and these former litigants are now licensees. This is a positive moat signal.

5. **Gross margin guided DOWN for Q4 FY2026 (64-66% vs 68.6% in Q3).** This was a surprise. Could reflect product mix shift, pricing concessions to hyperscalers, or absorption of DustPhotonics costs. Needs investigation -- margin compression while revenue grows is a yellow flag for pricing power.

6. **Active LED Cables (ALCs) are a new product category.** ALCs extend AEC-like reliability to 30-meter reaches, sampling in FY2027 with revenue in FY2028. This bridges the gap between AECs (7m max) and full optical, potentially expanding TAM into row-scale and rack-to-rack connections.

7. **The stock is ~$183, not the $40-60 range from my older mental model.** The stock has roughly tripled in the past year. Forward P/E of ~37x is rich but not extreme for this growth rate.

## 4. Implications for ratings

### Value (Weight: 15%)
- Forward P/E ~37x on earnings that are still inflecting. Trailing P/E 66-96x. EV/Sales likely ~10-12x on FY2026 revenue. This is a premium valuation by any measure, but the growth rate is extraordinary. The Value score will likely be low (below-average to poor) unless we apply a heavy PEG-style adjustment.
- The $750M cash outlay for DustPhotonics will affect balance sheet metrics.

### Quality (Weight: 20%)
- Non-GAAP gross margins of 64-69% are excellent for a semi/connectivity company. Operating margins expanded rapidly. FCF generation likely strong given the asset-light fabless model.
- Moat indicators are positive: proprietary SerDes IP, validated AEC patents (cross-licenses with Amphenol/Molex/TE), switching costs in hyperscaler design wins.
- Management execution has been exceptional -- consistently beating estimates by wide margins.

### Growth (Weight: 15%)
- Revenue CAGR over last 2 years is extraordinary (>100% annualized). FY2026 >200% YoY. Even if FY2027 "only" grows 50-60%, the growth score should be near maximum.
- Sequential growth decelerating from +52% (Q3) to mid-single-digits (Q4) -- law of large numbers. But the base is now >$400M/quarter.

### AI Thesis (Weight: 30%)
- This is a pure-play AI infrastructure connectivity company. Revenue is ~100% driven by AI data center buildout. AECs are the connective tissue of GPU clusters.
- DustPhotonics acquisition positions Credo for the next wave (1.6T/3.2T, CPO). This is forward-looking AI thesis execution.
- Five hyperscaler customers = broad AI infrastructure exposure, not single-customer risk.
- ALCs (30m reach) address scale-up network needs, which grow with cluster size.
- AI Thesis score should be very high.

### Momentum (Weight: 10%)
- Stock has roughly tripled in the past year. Near all-time highs. All 18 analysts have Buy/Strong Buy ratings.
- Consistent earnings beats (Q1: +17% revenue beat, +44% EPS beat; Q3: significant beat).
- Momentum score should be high.

### Risk (Weight: 10%)
- **Customer concentration:** Three customers >10% of revenue. Hyperscaler capex pause would be devastating.
- **Valuation risk:** At ~37x forward P/E, any growth deceleration will be punished severely.
- **DustPhotonics integration risk:** Large acquisition during hyper-growth phase. $750M cash + potential dilution.
- **Q4 gross margin compression:** Guided 64-66% vs 68.6% in Q3. Need to understand whether this is structural or transitory.
- **Competition:** Broadcom in DSP space, potential for hyperscalers to in-house connectivity solutions.
- **Cayman Islands incorporation:** Credo is incorporated in Cayman Islands (not US), which may introduce governance considerations.
- Risk score likely moderate (some meaningful risks, but patent overhang cleared and customer diversification improving).

### Key upcoming catalyst
- **Q4 FY2026 earnings: June 1, 2026** -- This will provide full fiscal year results, FY2027 guidance, and DustPhotonics integration update. This is the single most important near-term data point.
