## 1. Pre-Research Mental Model (articulated before research, per Rule 12)

> CRDO is a SerDes + AEC connectivity play for AI clusters. HiWire AEC cables and ClearLink DSP chips solve signal integrity at high port densities in GPU pods. Revenue was approximately $450-500M TTM as of training, growing ~3× YoY. Customer A (likely Amazon/Microsoft) = ~29% Q1 FY26 revenue. AEC moat: lower power than optical at ≤10m reach, hyperscaler-validated IP (patent cross-licenses with Amphenol/Molex/TE). DustPhotonics ($750M, announced April 2026) adds Silicon Photonics PICs — thesis pivot from pure-copper to full optical. Key risk: customer concentration. Score as of June 12 scan: 78.9.

## 2b. FCF Conversion Red-Flag Check

**FCF Conversion = TTM FCF ÷ TTM net income:**
- TTM FCF: ~$450M (est.; Q4 FCF $177.5M represents the ramp; Q1 FCF was likely much lower)
- TTM GAAP NI: ~$482M (= $2.51 GAAP EPS × 192M diluted shares)
- FCF conversion: ~$450M / $482M = ~93% → **Clean.** High conversion is expected for a fabless model with minimal capex ($4.8M/quarter).

No receivables/inventory red flags for a fabless company. Revenue recognized when shipped; inventory risk carried by TSMC/OSAT partners. **No FCF conversion concern.**

Note: OCF includes large non-cash SBC add-back (~$150M/year), which flatters the conversion ratio. This is the known mechanic for SBC-heavy fabless names. The underlying cash generation is real — $1.4B ending cash after a $750M acquisition confirms it.

## 2c. Expectations Red-Flag Check

**Script blocked** (yfinance 403). Manual check:

- P/S (TTM): ~$46.5B market cap / $1.335B revenue = **34.8×**
- 3-year P/S history context: CRDO traded at 3–6× P/S through FY24 as a small-cap pre-ramp. FY26's 34.8× is near the upper bound of the range since the AI ramp began in FY25. **Flag: P/S is elevated relative to own history** (though the growth rate is also far above the 3-year median, which partially justifies the multiple).
- Revenue growth trajectory: FY26 +206% (peak growth), FY27 guided +80% (material deceleration). This is the classic pattern where the P/S is near peak coinciding with the first year of growth deceleration below the prior peak.

**Expectations red flag: YELLOW.** The P/S likely sits near the 90th percentile of CRDO's own range, AND growth is decelerating from 206% to ~80%. This does not mean the stock is wrong — an optical inflection ($600M from ~$0 is genuinely new revenue) could justify the multiple if it materializes. But the briefing must note that at 18× forward EV/Revenue (analyst consensus post-earnings), expectations are high and execution on the optical H2 FY27 ramp is priced in.

*The June 16 stock decline of 7.6% on premium valuation concerns is consistent with this flag.*

## 6. Implications for Ratings

### D1 — AI Revenue % (current: 5)
Unchanged. Revenue remains ~100% AI data center. Optical adds incremental AI cluster TAM (NPO/CPO for 1.6T/3.2T GPU clusters). D1 = 5 fully supported.

### D2 — Position in Supply Chain (current: 4)
Argument for upgrade to 5: CRDO is no longer a niche AEC/SerDes play — it is now a vertically integrated connectivity platform (SerDes + AEC + DSP + SiPho PICs + ZeroFlap transceiver). This gives it a seat at the table for the full connectivity BOM in next-gen AI clusters. Whether this moves D2 from 4→5 is a rating session question.

### D3 — Moat (current: 4)
Argument for upgrade: DustPhotonics adds a fourth IP layer (SiPho PIC design). Combined with AEC patent cross-licenses (Amphenol/Molex/TE), this is now a multi-IP-stack moat. Rating session question: is the DustPhotonics IP truly defensible, or is silicon photonics commoditizing?

### D4 — Capacity (current: 4)
Supply secured for FY27. Unchanged.

### D5 — Hyperscaler (current: 5)
Five hyperscalers engaged. Top 2 at 65% concentration. The breadth is excellent; the concentration is a risk. Rating unchanged at 5 (all major hyperscalers now customers).

### M1 — EPS Revisions (current: 5)
Non-GAAP EPS $1.16 beat $0.98 consensus by 18%. Post-earnings analyst upgrades (Roth $300, Mizuho $290, etc.). M1 = 5 fully supported.

### M2 — Relative Strength (current: 5)
Stock at $249, up from $183 at May 25 briefing (+36%). Despite the June 16 dip, near all-time highs. M2 = 5 supported.

### M3 — Insider Activity (current: 3)
No new developments. CTO continued net selling (held ~6M shares). CEO PSU structure is long-term bullish signal. M3 = 3 unchanged — asymmetric interpretation (selling ambiguous, PSU structure encouraging).

### R1 — Customer Concentration (current: 2)
FY26 annual: top 2 = 65% of revenue. This is worse than the Q1 picture suggested (three at ~10-15% each). R1 = 2 is correct and if anything may need to be confirmed at 2 (not upgraded to 3). Rating session: should R1 stay at 2 given the concentration worsened?

### R3 — Balance Sheet Risk (current: 5)
Cash $1.4B, debt minimal. Strong. After $750M DustPhotonics, the cash position is lower but still excellent for a fabless company at this profitability level. R3 = 5 maintained.

### R5 — Disruption Risk (current: 5)
CRDO is Layer 7 (Interconnect) — non-Layer-10, defaults to 5. AI makes the connectivity problem harder (more bandwidth, more ports, more AI clusters = more CRDO). No disruption risk.

**Summary for rating session:**
- D2 and D3 are candidates for upgrade (DustPhotonics elevates both)
- R1 may need explicit confirmation at 2 (concentration worsened)
- All other dimensions unchanged from prior session
