### Satish Dhanasekaran (CEO)

Thank you, Jason, and thank you all for joining us today.

Keysight delivered the best quarter in company history with Q2 orders growing 56% year over year, surpassing $2 billion, revenue growing 31%, earnings per share growing 69%, and generating a record $472 million in free cash flow. These results reflect accelerating demand momentum driven by AI infrastructure, next-generation connectivity, and defense modernization.

**AI Business Highlights:**
Our AI-related business has already surpassed the levels achieved in all of 2025 in the first half of fiscal 2026 alone. We are sizing the AI opportunity in the $500 to $600 million range for the first half. Both R&D and manufacturing components of our wireline business doubled in the first half.

We see four key areas of customer activity in AI:
1. **Scaling challenges** — customers are adopting Keysight solutions for end-to-end interoperability and system validation as GPU/CPU/DPU complexity increases.
2. **Speed transitions** — continued robust 800-gig deployments, with accelerating activity in 1.6-terabit and early-stage 3.2-terabit infrastructure.
3. **Optical and photonics expansion** — co-packaged optics and silicon photonics are driving a new wave of test requirements.
4. **System emulation** — strong adoption of our AI workload emulation solutions among hyperscalers. Customers are using these tools to validate AI fabrics under realistic traffic conditions before deployment.

Within our wireline business, we maintain approximately a 70-30 R&D to manufacturing revenue split, with quarterly variability. We are seeing a stronger sense of urgency from our customers to convert pipeline to orders, contributing to higher velocity and conversion rates. The sales pipeline remains very, very strong with increasing funnel velocity.

**Segment Performance:**

*Communication Solutions Group (CSG)* delivered $1.231 billion in revenue, up 35% reported and 27% on a core basis. Operating margin reached 33.4%. Commercial communications revenue of $858 million reflected robust growth of 40% — driven by both wireless and wireline. Aerospace, defense, and government achieved $373 million in revenue, up 24%, reflecting continued defense modernization programs and growing space/satellite activity.

*Electronic Industrial Solutions Group (EISG)* delivered $486 million in revenue, up 24%, with a record quarter — all-time highs for both orders and revenue. All three end markets showed strength: general electronics, semiconductors, and automotive/energy. Our semiconductor business grew double digits, supported by investments in advanced packaging, gate-all-around transistors, and memory validation. Operating margin was 33.1%.

**Product Leadership:**
We continue to extend our technical lead. Notable recent introductions include:
- AresONE 1600GE: purpose-built 1.6T Ethernet AI workload emulation platform for validating AI fabrics over 224G SerDes
- PCIe 7.0 receiver stress calibration tools (announced May 2026)
- CXL 3 Protocol Exerciser and Analyzer for memory pooling validation
- 448Gbps optical research demonstrations pointing toward 3.2T networks
- Scale-up validation solutions for AI data center interconnects

Non-terrestrial networks, including space and satellite, remain sub-1% of total revenue today but represent a significant long-term growth opportunity as LEO constellations expand.

**Tariff Impact:**
Following the U.S. Supreme Court decision invalidating IEEPA tariff authority, we recognized a $40 million reduction in Q2 revenue and a $97 million reduction in costs. Excluding these items, Q2 revenue was $1.758 billion (up 35%) and adjusted EPS was $2.58 (up 52%). On a go-forward basis, we expect tariffs to remain manageable through our global manufacturing footprint, supply chain diversification, and targeted pricing actions. We had previously sized annual tariff exposure at approximately $75 million, and we expect full mitigation through H1 FY2026.

## Q&A Session (Summary of Key Exchanges)

**[Analyst question — AI demand sustainability / pull-forward risk]**
Management: "We are not seeing evidence of pull-forwards. The urgency we're observing reflects the pace of AI infrastructure deployment. Our customers are building data centers at unprecedented scale and need test infrastructure validated ahead of each deployment phase. The pipeline remains very, very strong with increasing funnel velocity and conversion rates."

**[Analyst question — book-to-bill and backlog policy]**
Management: "The majority of our business books and recognizes within six months. We have not changed our backlog policy. The order growth reflects genuine incremental demand, not changes in customer ordering behavior or pull-forwards."

**[Analyst question — organic vs. acquisition-driven growth]**
Management: Core revenue growth was 24% in Q2 and 48% on orders. The reported numbers include acquisition contributions; approximately $375M in FY2026 revenue is expected from acquisitions, with the balance representing organic momentum.

**[Analyst question — tariff refund accounting]**
Neil Dougherty: "The $40 million revenue reduction reflects our obligation to refund IEEPA surcharges we previously collected from customers following the Supreme Court ruling. The offsetting $97 million cost reduction reflects the recovery of tariff duties we ourselves paid. The net effect is a benefit to operating income of approximately $57 million. These are non-recurring items."

**[Analyst question — semiconductor test specifics]**
Management: Semiconductor grew double digits in EISG. Customers are investing in advanced packaging, gate-all-around transistors, and next-generation memory validation. The company sees the semiconductor test cycle as multi-year given the roadmap for 2nm and below nodes.

**[Analyst question — China / geographic exposure]**
Management did not provide a specific China revenue percentage breakout on this call. Noted the company has a global manufacturing footprint designed to manage geopolitical risk and export control compliance.

**[Analyst question — gross margin durability]**
Neil Dougherty: "We feel good about the mid-67% adjusted gross margin durability post-acquisition. The tariff items distort the reported line, but the underlying trajectory is positive. Operating leverage at nearly 50% incrementals this quarter reflects the high rate of growth combined with disciplined expense management."

**[Analyst question — CapEx increase rationale]**
Neil Dougherty: "We are accelerating certain product introductions and adding manufacturing capacity for our highest-demand solutions. The increase from $160M to $200M reflects conviction that the current demand environment warrants investment."

**[Analyst question — space/satellite opportunity]**
Satish Dhanasekaran: "Space and satellite is sub-1% of revenue today, but we are very well-positioned as LEO constellations scale. We expect this to become a more meaningful contributor in the medium term."
