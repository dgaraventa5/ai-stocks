## Key Prepared Remarks Themes

### AI/LLM Observability Product Adoption
- **6,500 customers** using AI integrations (20% of total base but generating ~80% of ARR)
- LLM Observability span volume **nearly tripled QoQ**
- MCP server calls **quadrupled sequentially**
- SRE agent investigations **more than doubled** from December to March
- Landed **seven and eight-figure deals** with two global AI research labs for GPU monitoring of training workloads
- 26 total products: 5 generate >$100M ARR, 3 between $50-100M ARR, 18 in early lifecycle

### Cloud Consumption Trends
- Non-AI customer revenue growth **accelerated to mid-20% YoY**, up from 23% prior quarter
- Stack consolidation continues as customers unify fragmented observability tools onto Datadog platform
- Cloud migration tailwind persists beyond AI

### Net Dollar Retention
- Trailing 12-month net revenue retention: **low 120% range** (up from ~120% prior quarter)
- Gross revenue retention: **mid- to high 90%** range (stable)

### Customer Growth
- Total customers: ~33,200
- $100K+ ARR customers: ~4,550 (up 21% from ~3,770 YoY)
- $100K+ customers generate ~90% of total ARR
- Record new logo bookings

### Multi-Product Adoption
- 56% of customers use 4+ products (up from 51% a year ago)
- 35% of customers use 6+ products (up from 28%)
- 20% of customers use 8+ products (up from 13%)

### Competitive Dynamics
- CEO Olivier Pomel: Datadog is "outperforming all of our competitors at scale" and "taking share"
- Platform consolidation advantage vs. point solutions
- Hyperscalers — historically building monitoring in-house — adopting Datadog for AI training workloads (notable competitive shift)

## Guidance

### Q2 2026
- Revenue: $1.07B-$1.08B (29-31% YoY)
- Non-GAAP operating income: $225M-$235M (21-22% margin)
- Non-GAAP EPS: $0.57-$0.59

### Full Year FY2026
- Revenue: $4.30B-$4.34B (25-27% growth)
- Non-GAAP operating income: $940M-$980M (22-23% margin)
- Non-GAAP EPS: $2.36-$2.44
- **Raised from initial FY2026 guide** of $4.06B-$4.10B (18-20% growth)

Management noted "higher degree of conservatism" applied to largest customer cohort in guidance.

## GAAP vs. Non-GAAP Reconciliation (Q1 2026)

| Adjustment | Amount |
|---|---|
| Stock-based compensation | $196.8M |
| Employer payroll taxes on equity | $17.0M |
| Amortization of acquired intangibles | $1.6M |
| M&A costs | $0.7M |
| **Total adjustments** | **~$216M** |

This explains the massive gap between GAAP operating income ($7M, 1% margin) and non-GAAP operating income ($223M, 22% margin). SBC alone is ~20% of revenue.

## Notable Q&A Highlights

- AI research lab deals (7-8 figure) specifically for GPU monitoring — validates demand for observability of AI training infrastructure
- Management deliberately does NOT break out AI revenue as a percentage, calling it inseparable from core platform
- Hyperscaler adoption of Datadog (rather than building in-house) for AI workloads is a notable competitive validation
- Guidance conservatism on large customer cohort suggests awareness of lumpiness risk but also potential for upside beats
