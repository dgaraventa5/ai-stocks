# LEU (Centrus Energy) — Pre-rating context briefing, 2026-06-10

Session lens: re-rating Layer 1 power names on how DIRECTLY they own the AI power bottleneck. Direct = selling power/capacity to datacenters with disclosed terms. Indirect = fuel-cycle suppliers whose AI link runs through demand chains.

---

## Pre-research mental model (written before searching)

Centrus runs two segments: the LEU segment (SWU/uranium sales to existing-fleet utilities — lumpy, commodity, the large majority of revenue) and Technical Solutions, which houses the HALEU work. Centrus is the only NRC-licensed HALEU producer in the US, running a ~900 kg/yr demonstration cascade at Piketon, OH under the DOE HALEU Operation Contract (Phase III extension through mid-2026 as of my last knowledge). The HALEU story is option value: advanced reactors (TerraPower Natrium, X-energy Xe-100, Oklo Aurora) need HALEU, and those developers carry the hyperscaler money (Amazon→X-energy, datacenter PPAs→Oklo), but Centrus's actual revenue was government contracts plus legacy SWU. Most recent quarter I knew: Q3 2025; I had not seen Q4 2025 or Q1 2026 results, the January 2026 DOE expansion awards, or any commercial-scale HALEU funding decision.

## Findings (sources)

1. **Q1 2026 results (reported 2026-05-05):** Revenue $76.7M (+5% YoY); LEU segment $44.6M (−13% YoY: SWU volume −47%, average SWU pricing +52%); Technical Solutions $32.1M (+47% YoY, driven by a $9.8M increase in HALEU Operation Contract activity). Gross profit $31.5M; net income $10.0M; adjusted net income $23.5M. 2026 revenue guidance raised to $450–500M from $425–475M. (Q1 2026 earnings release 2026-05-05, prnewswire.com/news-releases/centrus-reports-first-quarter-2026-results-302763250.html; Q1 2026 call transcript 2026-05-07, fool.com)
2. **$900M DOE task order for commercial-scale HALEU (announced 2026-01-06):** American Centrifuge Operating (Centrus sub) selected for a $900M fixed-price base task order to expand Piketon to commercial-scale HALEU production, with DOE options up to $170M more (total $1.07B). Subject to negotiation of a definitive agreement. First tranche of new capacity expected online **2029**. Targets ≥12 MT/yr HALEU "sometime after 2030." (Centrus PR 2026-01, investors.centrusenergy.com; ANS Nuclear Newswire, ans.org/news/article-7652)
3. **Centrus is no longer the only HALEU awardee:** The same 2026-01-06 DOE round awarded **General Matter $900M** for HALEU enrichment capacity at the former Paducah, KY site; Orano got $900M for LEU (Oak Ridge) and GLE $28.5M (laser enrichment). Centrus remains the only company that has actually produced and delivered HALEU (>1.6 MT cumulative to DOE per CEO Vexler, Q1 2026 call), but the prospective monopoly is now a duopoly-by-policy. (ans.org/news/article-7652, 2026-01)
4. **Oklo–Centrus planned JV (announced 2026-03-09):** Agreement to *pursue discussions* (not a definitive JV) on HALEU deconversion services at Piketon, co-located with Centrus enrichment and **adjacent to Oklo's planned 1.2 GW power campus** in Pike County, OH. This is the closest physical/commercial link between Centrus and AI-datacenter power demand. (Oklo/Centrus joint PR 2026-03-09, prnewswire.com/news-releases/oklo-centrus-announce-planned-joint-venture-to-advance-nuclear-fuel-services-in-ohio-302708075.html)
5. **Backlog $3.9B through 2040 (Q1 2026):** LEU segment $3.1B, of which $2.4B is *contingent* LEU sales commitments under definitive agreements — contingent on Centrus building new Piketon LEU capacity and securing public/private funding. Technical Solutions $0.8B (predominantly DOE). Buyers of the contingent LEU backlog are described as US and international customers (utilities) — no hyperscaler buyer disclosed. (CEO Vexler, Q1 2026 call 2026-05-07, fool.com; Q4 2025 release 2026-02, investors.centrusenergy.com)
6. **Balance sheet / build-out:** ~$1.9B unrestricted cash at Q1 2026; 2026 total capital spend guided $350–500M; $560M Oak Ridge centrifuge-manufacturing expansion initiated; Fluor (EPC), Palantir (ops/AI software, ~$300M identified potential cost savings per CFO Tinelli), and Geiger Brothers signed in Q1. Piketon 2026 net-new-hire target raised 50→100+. (Q1 2026 call 2026-05-07, fool.com)
7. **HALEU remains the SMR bottleneck:** Industry coverage in 2026 consistently identifies HALEU enrichment capacity as the binding constraint on Oklo/X-energy/Kairos/TerraPower deployments; TerraPower's Natrium schedule has slipped toward 2030+ partly on HALEU fuel availability. (jaredwatkins.com behind-the-meter datacenter research, 2026; wwt.com SMR overview, 2026)

## DIFF (mental model vs. findings)

- **New since my model:** Q4 2025 + Q1 2026 results; the January 2026 $900M DOE commercial-scale task order (this moves commercial HALEU from pure option to funded project, first capacity 2029); General Matter as a second funded HALEU entrant (weakens the "sole source forever" framing); the Oklo deconversion JV discussions and Oklo's 1.2 GW campus adjacent to Piketon; $3.9B backlog number; Palantir partnership.
- **Confirmed:** Revenue today is still ~100% utilities (LEU segment) + US government (Technical Solutions). HALEU production still ~900 kg/yr demo scale. Advanced-reactor HALEU demand is real but a 2029-2030s revenue event for Centrus.
- **Wrong/stale in my model:** I thought of the HALEU monopoly as durable; DOE deliberately funded a competitor in the same award round. Also Phase III/option-year structure: Q1 release cites a funded $108.2M option year under the HALEU Operation Contract.

## Evidence for D1 (% of revenue genuinely AI-tied TODAY)

- Q1 2026: LEU segment $44.6M = SWU + uranium to existing-fleet nuclear utilities (commodity fuel; AI link only via long-run electricity demand). Technical Solutions $32.1M = overwhelmingly the DOE HALEU Operation Contract and other government work (Q1 2026 release 2026-05-05). **Government ≠ AI**: the DOE program is national-security/fuel-supply policy, funded regardless of datacenter demand.
- No disclosed revenue in Q1 2026 (or FY2025) from an SMR developer, datacenter operator, or hyperscaler. CEO Vexler on the Q1 call referenced "engagement with advanced reactor developers and hyperscalers" — engagement, not contracts (fool.com transcript 2026-05-07).
- **Conclusion supported by evidence: direct AI-tied revenue today ≈ 0%.** The AI linkage is forward-looking (post-2029 commercial HALEU) and intermediated (SMR developers stand between Centrus and hyperscalers).

## Evidence for D2 (HALEU near-monopoly / pricing power)

- Centrus is the only company that has produced and delivered HALEU in the US: >1.6 MT cumulative UF6 to DOE (Vexler, Q1 2026 call); Phase III contract extended to 2026-06-30 with up to 8 option years (DOE extension 2025-06, world-nuclear-news.org; Centrus 8-K 2025-06-25).
- BUT: pricing on the HALEU Operation Contract is government cost/fixed-price task-order structure — the 900 kg/yr output goes to DOE, not auctioned to fuel-hungry SMR developers. No observable HALEU market-pricing power yet.
- The +52% YoY average SWU price in Q1 2026 (Q1 release) is **LEU-segment commodity SWU**, driven by the post-Russian-suspension SWU market — real pricing power, but not AI-specific and on collapsing volume (−47%).
- Monopoly erosion: DOE's 2026-01-06 awards funded General Matter ($900M, Paducah) as a second HALEU enricher (ans.org). Centrus retains a multi-year operating head start (only licensed, only operating cascade), but the moat is now explicitly time-limited by policy design.
- Demand-side support: HALEU scarcity is documented as the cap on first-wave SMR deployments (~2-4 units per developer by 2030 per industry research, jaredwatkins.com 2026) — scarcity is real, Centrus just can't monetize it at market prices yet because its output is contracted to DOE.

## Evidence for D5 (direct hyperscaler linkage via SMR customers)

- **No direct hyperscaler contract, investment, or offtake with Centrus found.** Searches for Centrus + Microsoft/Amazon/Google/Meta agreements return only DOE material.
- Closest links, all indirect and mostly non-binding:
  - **Oklo JV discussions (2026-03-09):** deconversion services at Piketon adjacent to Oklo's planned 1.2 GW datacenter-oriented power campus. Status: "agreed to pursue discussions" — pre-definitive (joint PR via prnewswire).
  - **X-energy** (Amazon-anchored) and **TerraPower** are the marquee prospective HALEU buyers, but no disclosed Centrus supply contract with terms to either as of Q1 2026; X-energy is building its own TRISO-X fuel fabrication (NRC Category II license, 2026) and would still need enriched HALEU feed.
  - Palantir partnership (Q1 2026) is Centrus *buying* AI software for the build-out — reverse direction; not AI revenue.
- Hyperscaler money currently reaches the HALEU layer via DOE policy and via SMR-developer balance sheets, not via Centrus's P&L.
