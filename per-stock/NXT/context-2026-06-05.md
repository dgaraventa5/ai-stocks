## 6. Implications for ratings (flags, not decisions)

- **D1 AI Revenue %:** Low. Data-center/AI is a demand tailwind and a new storage vector (Prevalon), but NXT does not disclose AI-tied revenue as a material % yet. Core business is utility-scale solar. Likely 2 (some/growing exposure, not yet material). Watch for Prevalon DC-storage revenue disclosure in FY27.
- **D2 Position / D3 Moat:** #1 global tracker share, software/tracking IP, EBOS scale; 2-4 viable competitors (Array, GameChange). Firm-to-up pricing, record backlog. Mid-to-upper.
- **D4 Capacity:** Active M&A-led expansion (Zigor + Prevalon), domestic supply chain build, power-conversion entry. Strong.
- **D5 Hyperscaler Exposure:** Indirect. Customers are utilities/IPPs/developers, not hyperscalers directly. Prevalon adds 1.3 GW of contracts "supporting" hyperscaler DC, but NXT sells to project owners, not hyperscalers. Low-mid.
- **M1 EPS Revisions:** Strongly positive — beat-and-raise + multiple PT hikes (Susquehanna, UBS, etc.) in last 90 days. High end.
- **M2 Relative Strength:** Stock ~$150 near 52wk highs after strong run; benchmark XLU (or solar/clean-energy basket). Appears strong vs sector; verify exact 6-mo vs benchmark before locking.
- **M3 Insider:** Routine 10b5-1 + tax sell-to-cover; no discretionary buys → rubric default 3.
- **R1 Customer Concentration:** Customer G 17%, top-5 34%. Top customer in 15-25% band → 4.
- **R2 Geo:** 77% US, no China export-control overhang; some RoW. Low risk → 4-5. Offset: tariff (Section 232) and IRA-policy sensitivity are macro/regulatory, not export-control.
- **R3 Balance Sheet:** Net cash, undrawn revolver, ~$2B liquidity, strong FCF → 5 (note: two acquisitions will consume cash; still strong).
- **R4 Regulatory/Litigation:** Main overhang is IRA 45X step-down + Section 232 tariffs (macro policy), not company-specific litigation. No major disclosed legal threat → 4.

**Thesis-break signals:** None negative. The material updates are positive (beat-raise, record backlog, platform expansion into AI-adjacent storage). The one caution: AI/data-center exposure is still narrative/TAM, not disclosed revenue — do not over-credit D1/D5 on the Prevalon headline alone (rubric failure mode: "rating high based on a single recent customer announcement").
