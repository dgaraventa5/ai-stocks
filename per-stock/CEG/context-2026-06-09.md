# CEG — Pre-rating context briefing (2026-06-09)

Prepared for AI Thesis re-rating (Layer 01 Power — IPP / Nuclear). Session lens: how DIRECTLY does CEG own the AI power bottleneck (premium datacenter power sales, signed hyperscaler PPAs, behind-the-meter MW) vs. second-derivative commodity exposure.

## Pre-research mental model

CEG is the largest US nuclear operator (~22 GW fleet) and the marquee "sell nuclear baseload to hyperscalers" name: the September 2024 Microsoft PPA to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1 (Crane Clean Energy Center, 835 MW, 20 years) with restart originally guided to 2028, later accelerated to 2027; and a June 2025 Meta PPA for ~1.1 GW at Clinton (IL) for 20 years. The ~$16.4B Calpine acquisition (announced January 2025) was expected to close in 2026, adding the largest gas/geothermal competitive fleet and a Texas datacenter angle. Most recent quarter I know of is Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-11): a beat ($4.49 GAAP EPS) overshadowed by a 12.6% selloff on the DOE 202(c) Eddystone emergency order (per news-log entry 2026-05-19). Prior rating set D1=2 because the Microsoft PPA was forward-dated (no AI revenue flowing until restart).

## Findings

Adjusted operating EPS $2.74 (beat consensus ~$2.59), GAAP $4.49/share; FY2026 guidance affirmed at $11.00–12.00 adjusted operating EPS; targeting >20% base EPS growth 2026–2029. Sources: 8-K Item 2.02 2026-05-11 (Ex. 99.1); https://www.investing.com/news/transcripts/earnings-call-transcript-constellation-energy-q1-2026-beats-forecasts-93CH-4677180; https://www.tikr.com/blog/constellation-energy-q1-2026-earnings-revenue-doubles-on-calpine-eps-guidance-affirmed

2. **Calpine closed 2026-01-07** ("Acquisition Date"), cash-and-stock; $17,507M of common stock issued for the acquisition (10-Q 2026-05-11, Note 2 and cash flow supplemental disclosures). CFO Shane Smith: ~$2.00/share full-year Calpine accretion (Q1 2026 earnings call, 2026-05-11, https://www.fool.com/earnings/call-transcripts/2026/05/11/constellation-ceg-q1-2026-earnings-transcript/). Antitrust divestiture: agreed March 2026 to sell 4.4 GW of gas capacity (DE/PA) to LS Power for $5B (Q1 call / press reports, 2026-03).

3. **Crane restart status:** restart accelerated to 2027 (company PR 2025-02-18, https://www.constellationenergy.com/news/2025/constellation-ahead-of-schedule-for-launch-of-crane-clean-energy-center.html; ~80% staffed per company One-Year-Later release, https://constellationenergy.gcs-web.com/news-releases/news-release-details/one-year-later-crane-clean-energy-center-still-spotlight-and). Grid-deliverability risk: PJM Phase I study identified contingent transmission upgrades with in-service dates "as late as December 2030"; in March 2026 CEG filed a FERC waiver to transfer Eddystone Units 3&4 capacity interconnection rights (CIRs) to Crane, requesting a ruling by 2026-06-01 (10-Q 2026-05-11, Note 3 — Regulatory Matters). GC David Dardis on the Q1 call: response hoped "in the June–July timeframe." DOE issued a $1.0B Federal Financing Bank loan guarantee for the Crane restart in Nov 2025; no borrowings yet as of the 10-Q (10-Q 2026-05-11, Note 13). Crane listed as 835 MW under construction (10-Q 2026-05-11, projects-under-construction table). **No Microsoft PPA revenue is flowing yet.**

4. **Meta Clinton PPA:** 20-year PPA for 1,121 MW of Clinton's output **beginning June 2027**, replacing the expiring IL ZEC program; includes 30 MW uprate (company PR 2025-06-03, https://investors.constellationenergy.com/news-releases/news-release-details/constellation-meta-sign-20-year-deal-clean-reliable-nuclear). Structured as a virtual/financial PPA per Utility Dive (https://www.utilitydive.com/news/meta-constellation-ppa-could-be-first-of-many-deals-for-existing-reactors/750567/). Not flowing revenue yet.

5. **Calpine/Texas datacenter (CyrusOne) deals:** new 380 MW agreement signed Q1 2026 to serve a CyrusOne data center adjacent to Freestone Energy Center (TX), plus an exclusive agreement for a 380 MW Phase 2 (10-Q 2026-05-11, MD&A "New Data Center Facility at Freestone Energy Center"; company PR 2026-02-09, https://www.constellationenergy.com/news/2026/02/constellation-and-cyrusone-announce-agreement-to-support-new-data-center-facility-at-freestone-energy-center-in-texas.html). Combined with 2H2025 Thad Hill agreements (400 MW plus two earlier 200 MW deals), CEG has **>1,100 MW under contract with CyrusOne** in Texas (same PR). PUCT approved the Freestone net-metering agreement; delivery substation targeted for **Q4 2026 energization** (Dominguez, Q1 2026 call) — i.e., these deals are signed but largely not yet delivering power.

6. **Pricing evidence (estimates, not company-disclosed):** Morgan Stanley estimates the Microsoft Crane PPA at ~$98/MWh vs ~$50/MWh market power (https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/constellation-energy-stock-target-lifted-on-microsoft-ppa-deal-93CH-3629727); BMO estimates ~$102/MWh (https://www.sahmcapital.com/news/content/constellation-energy-reactor-restart-with-microsoft-raises-timing-and-earnings-questions-2026-03-27). 7. **Demand/pipeline signals:** Dominguez on Q1 call: hyperscaler 2026 projected capex "nearly 75% higher than last year"; CEG submitted ~5,000 MW of new capacity resources (nuclear uprates, gas, batteries) to PJM's queue; PJM colocation framework expected to go to FERC in June 2026, which management frames as the unlock for new hyperscaler deals (Q1 2026 call, 2026-05-11; 10-Q Note 3 — FERC found PJM's colocation tariff unjust/unreasonable in Dec 2025 draft order).

8. **Capital structure event:** 2026-06-01 underwriting agreement — selling shareholders sold 11.0M shares (secondary, no proceeds to CEG); CEG concurrently repurchased 2.0M shares for ~$558.0M; ~$3.5B buyback authority remaining (8-K Item 8.01, filed 2026-06-02).

## DIFF vs mental model

- **Confirmed:** Calpine closed (Jan 7, 2026) and is the main driver of revenue doubling; Crane restart on track for 2027; Microsoft and Meta PPAs both still forward-dated — D1's "no AI revenue flowing yet" premise from the prior rating is **still essentially true**, but with a near-term change coming (Freestone substation energization Q4 2026 = first direct datacenter power deliveries).
- **New since prior model:** (1) the CyrusOne relationship is bigger than I had it — >1,100 MW under contract across Thad Hill + Freestone, with another 380 MW under exclusivity; (2) the Eddystone DOE order has a silver lining — CEG is using Eddystone CIRs to de-risk Crane grid deliverability (FERC ruling expected June–July 2026); (3) PJM transmission upgrades could otherwise have pushed Crane full deliverability to as late as Dec 2030 — a bigger timing risk than my model carried; (4) 4.4 GW LS Power divestiture ($5B) trims the Calpine fleet; (5) >20% EPS CAGR 2026–2029 target is new official guidance.
- **Correction:** restart year is 2027 (accelerated), not 2028 as at the time of the prior D1 rating.

## Evidence for D1 (AI Revenue %)

Current AI/datacenter-tied revenue is approximately zero as a share of the $11.1B Q1 2026 revenue base (10-Q 2026-05-11). The Microsoft Crane PPA pays on output from a plant that restarts in 2027 (835 MW under construction per 10-Q); the Meta Clinton PPA begins June 2027 (company PR 2025-06-03); and the >1,100 MW of CyrusOne contracts deliver first power when the Freestone substation energizes in Q4 2026 (Q1 call). CEG's retail business undoubtedly serves datacenter load within C&I volumes, but no disclosure quantifies it — flag, do not credit. Math: identifiable datacenter-contract revenue flowing today ≈ $0 / $11.1B ≈ 0%. However, contracted forward AI revenue is large and dated: ~2 GW of hyperscaler PPAs estimated at ~$1.5B/yr from 2028 (third-party estimate) on a ~$45B annualized revenue base — i.e., roughly 3-4% of revenue by 2028 from the two nuclear PPAs alone, before CyrusOne/Calpine deals and before noting that PPA revenue is far higher-margin than pass-through retail revenue. Prior D1=2 on a current-revenue basis remains defensible; the forward book is now materially larger and nearer-dated than at the prior rating.

## Evidence for D2 (Supply chain position / pricing power)

Strong, with the caveat that prices are analyst estimates, not company disclosures: Morgan Stanley pegs the Microsoft PPA at ~$98/MWh vs ~$50/MWh merchant (~2x premium); BMO ~$102/MWh. The Meta Clinton deal monetizes a plant whose state ZEC subsidy expires in 2027 at what is reported to be a market premium for 20 years — converting a subsidy-dependent asset into a hyperscaler-underwritten one. Structural scarcity is real: existing nuclear baseload in PJM/MISO cannot be replicated this decade, and CEG owns the largest fleet; the Crane restart and a $1.0B DOE loan guarantee (10-Q Note 13) show buyers and government co-funding capacity specifically for this demand. The Calpine side adds a second pricing-power channel — energy + grid connectivity + site infrastructure bundles for datacenters in ERCOT (Freestone, Thad Hill), where the exclusivity grant on Phase 2 (380 MW) indicates the customer, not CEG, is competing for capacity. Counterweight: FERC/PJM colocation rules are unresolved (Dec 2025 FERC draft order; framework to FERC June 2026), which has gated new PJM deals for ~a year, and DOE 202(c) orders (Eddystone) show regulators can conscript CEG assets at non-premium economics.

## Evidence for D5 (Hyperscaler exposure)

Best-in-sector on signed, named, disclosed deals: Microsoft — 835 MW, 20 years, Crane/TMI Unit 1, disclosed in SEC filings (10-Q 2026-05-11, Note 3) with restart 2027; Meta — 1,121 MW, 20 years, Clinton, starting June 2027, disclosed via company press release with MW and duration. That is ~2 GW of 20-year committed offtake from two named hyperscalers — no other Layer 1 name has comparable disclosed terms. Third pillar is CyrusOne (>1,100 MW contracted + 380 MW exclusive Phase 2) — a datacenter developer rather than a hyperscaler, whose ultimate tenants are undisclosed (flag: do not assume hyperscaler tenancy). Management explicitly ties the next wave of deals to PJM colocation clarity expected mid-2026 (Q1 call). Structure caveat: both nuclear PPAs are reported as virtual/financial PPAs (Utility Dive on Meta; BMO on Crane), not behind-the-meter physical supply — the hyperscalers underwrite price, the power flows to the grid.

## Flags / gaps

- PPA $/MWh figures are sell-side estimates (Morgan Stanley, BMO); CEG has never disclosed contract prices. Do not treat as primary data.
- No disclosure of datacenter share of retail/C&I load — current AI revenue % cannot be computed beyond "≈0% identifiable."
- The "~$1.5B/yr by 2028" combined PPA revenue figure is from a third-party report (useluminix.com), not verified against primary sources.
- A stocktitan summary referenced "780 MW signed to-date with exclusivity for an incremental 380 MW at Thad Hill"; company PR ties the 380+380 to Freestone and 400+2x200 to Thad Hill. I used the company PR/10-Q version.
- Whether Thad Hill 400 MW (signed 2H2025) is delivering power yet is not disclosed; Freestone energization is Q4 2026.
- Crane full grid deliverability depends on the FERC CIR-transfer waiver (decision expected June–July 2026) — binary near-term catalyst; adverse outcome could leave transmission-upgrade exposure to as late as Dec 2030 (10-Q Note 3).
