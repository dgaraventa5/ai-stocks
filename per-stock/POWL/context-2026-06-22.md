## 1. Pre-research mental model

Powell makes custom-engineered electrical distribution and control equipment — medium-/low-voltage switchgear, bus duct, and integrated power-control buildings — for utility, oil & gas / petrochemical / LNG, and increasingly data-center customers. The story since FY2024 has been a margin inflection (gross margin into the high-20s/low-30s%) plus a record backlog, with a balance sheet carrying net cash and a tiny dividend. AI relevance is indirect but real: data-center electrical buildout is a fast-growing slice of orders inside the "Commercial & Other Industrial" bucket. Heritage end-market is oil & gas/petrochemical, which is the cyclicality risk and the diversification story management is leaning into.

## 6. Implications for ratings (flags only — no rating numbers pre-decided)

- **D1 / D2 (AI thesis — directness & magnitude of AI exposure):** Look up. Data-center electrical demand is now a named primary order driver, not a footnote — C&I orders +35%, a >$400M data-center mega-order (largest ever), and DC mega-orders booked in both Q1 and Q2 FY2026. Directness is still one step removed (Powell sells switchgear/bus duct into DC builds, not to hyperscalers' AI compute directly), but the magnitude inflection is material. Revisit D1/D2.
- **D5 (moat/competitive position):** Custom-engineered, project-based switchgear with ~1.7x book-to-bill and FY2028 visibility suggests pricing power / capacity-constrained demand. Worth re-examining on the order-momentum evidence.
- **M (Momentum, subjective EPS-revisions / relative strength + objective 50DMA%):** Mixed signal. Stock +~10% on the print and record orders (relative-strength positive), but EPS **missed** consensus and printed −1% YoY — EPS-revisions direction is ambiguous post-miss. Refresh the objective 50DMA% via `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` and re-examine the subjective EPS-revisions rating against the miss. Don't assume momentum is uniformly positive.
- **Growth:** Revenue +6% YoY is decelerating relative to the backlog/order growth (orders +97%) — a timing gap (backlog not yet converted) rather than demand weakness, but the scored revenue-growth input will look modest vs. the order story. EPS YoY is −1%; rule-15 does NOT apply (decline is operating-leverage/opex-driven, not a documented one-time item) so EPS YoY should stay as-is, not be blanked.
- **R1–R4 (Risk):** R1 customer concentration — watch (large mega-orders concentrate revenue/customer exposure; quantify top-customer % from 10-Q if disclosed). R-end-market: **oil & gas / petrochemical remains the single largest backlog bucket at 33%** — the cyclicality/end-market-concentration risk is real and should anchor the risk read even as utility/DC diversify the mix. No regulatory action surfaced.
- **R5 (Disruption Risk):** Defaults to **5** — POWL is physical grid/electrical infrastructure (Layer 02), no credible AI/structural disruption to the core revenue model. No change expected.
- **Rule-14 expectations flag:** POWL has re-rated hard on the AI/data-center grid theme and the stock popped ~10% post-print despite an EPS miss — multiple is likely elevated. Run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py POWL` during the scoring pass to check P/S ≥90th-percentile-of-own-range combined with sub-median revenue growth (revenue +6% is decelerating vs. the order surge — exactly the "peak multiple, decelerating reported growth" pattern the flag targets). Treat as qualitative context, not a scored metric.
- **Rule-9 objective refresh:** Required — Item-2.02 8-K filed 2026-05-04. Refresh gross margin (~29.7% Q2; note TTM will blend lower prior quarters), FCF (statement-based per CLAUDE.md), and net-cash/balance-sheet inputs in the same pass.

**Thesis-break scan:** No thesis-break signals. Backlog +33%/+12% (accelerating, not decelerating), orders +97%, book-to-bill 1.7x, all three end markets growing, guidance intact ("upper 20s" GM). The two yellow flags are (a) EPS miss / opex ramp compressing operating leverage, and (b) margin plateau — both are "watch," not "break." Oil & gas at 33% of backlog is the structural risk to monitor.
