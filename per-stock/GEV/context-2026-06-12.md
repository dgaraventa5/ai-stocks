### Step 2b — FCF conversion red-flag check
**Clean — no flag.** FY2025 statement FCF $3.71B vs net income $4.88B = ~76% conversion; FY2024 FCF $1.70B vs NI $1.55B = >100% (yfinance cashflow statement). Conversion is NOT below 60% for 2+ years, so the accrual-games condition fails at the first test. Capex is modest ($1.28B FY2025) and OCF is structurally boosted by customer prepayments/slot-reservation down payments — the working-capital signature here is a *bull* signal (customers pre-funding capacity), the opposite of the accrual-games pattern. Receivables (+14% YoY) and inventory (+23% YoY) are growing faster than the 16% revenue line, but the offsetting prepayment liabilities and the strong cash conversion mean this is build-ahead for a booked backlog, not channel stuffing. **No earnings-quality concern.**

**What is priced in:** a ~6.5–7x sales multiple on a heavy-equipment OEM is a growth-equipment multiple, not an industrial multiple — it requires the backlog-to-revenue conversion *and* the margin ramp *and* sustained pricing power to all keep delivering. The market has already pulled the $200B-backlog milestone forward to 2027 (from 2028) into the price, and forward P/E ~38x (yfinance) bakes in the EBITDA-margin climb to the mid-teens.

**Is the 4.8% YoY a genuine deceleration, or a lull before backlog converts?** Mostly a **lull / mix artifact, not deceleration of the business** — three reasons: (1) the trailing-twelve-month revenue line that the flag measures is dragged by the **Wind segment falling ~23%** and onshore delivery timing, masking Power+Electrification strength; (2) reported Q1 2026 revenue was actually **+16% YoY** (8-K) — the flag's 4.8% TTM figure straddles weaker prior quarters; (3) the leading indicator (orders +71%, backlog $163B, slots 83→100 GW) is *accelerating*, and gas turbine production is hard-capped at ~10 GW/yr, so revenue is supply-constrained, not demand-constrained — backlog converts on a multi-year schedule regardless of any single TTM print. The expectations risk is therefore **timing and multiple compression, not a broken thesis**: revenue is back-loaded behind a booked, pre-paid backlog.

**What would break the 93rd-percentile multiple:** (a) gas-turbine *order pricing* rolling over (currently +10–20% per-kW vs Q4 2025 — see Section 3); (b) hyperscaler capex air-pocket cancelling/deferring data-center slot reservations (20% of the 100 GW is explicitly DC-tied — that's the AI-levered slice); (c) Wind losses widening beyond the ~$(400)M guide or new offshore claims; (d) slot-reservation agreements (non-binding-ish reservations, 56 GW) not converting to firm equipment orders. The bull rebuttal to (a)/(d): slots are backed by down payments (the $5.3B Q1 working-capital inflow), which raises the customer's cost of walking away.
