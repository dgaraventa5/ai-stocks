# AEIS — Refresh-Context Briefing (2026-06-05)

Advanced Energy Industries, Inc. | Layer 04 Semi Equipment & Materials / power-delivery subsystems | Dual AI exposure (WFE recovery + data-center power)

---

## 1. Pre-research mental model (verbatim)

> AEIS is a precision power-conversion/control supplier. My training-era understanding: ~60-65% of revenue is semiconductor (plasma power-delivery / RF generators for etch & deposition, sold mainly to Applied Materials and Lam Research as the two anchor OEM customers), with the remainder split across data-center/enterprise computing power, industrial/medical, and telecom/networking. The WFE downturn of 2023-24 pressured the semi segment; gross margins were stuck in the mid-30s% with a management target to climb back toward ~40%. The AI angle is dual: WFE recovery lifts the semi business, and the data-center power business (high-density server/rack power) rides AI server buildouts. I don't have firm Q1 2026 numbers; my last solid quarter is likely late-2024/early-2025.

## 5. DIFF — what changed since my training

- **Data Center Computing is far bigger than I modeled.** I had it as a minority "remainder" segment. It is now $194M/qtr (38% of rev), doubling YoY, and is the primary growth driver — not semi. This is a material re-rating of the AI thesis.
- **Mix flipped:** semi is now ~43% (not 60-65%). My "semi-dominant" mental model is stale.
- **Gross margin cleared the 40% line** (non-GAAP 40.1%; GAAP 39.3%) with a credible path to >43% — better than my "stuck in mid-30s" model.
- **WFE recovery is real and accelerating:** semi guided +30%+ YoY in 2H2026, vs my "downturn pressure" framing.
- **New 800V platform + AI rack power** is a concrete product/design-win story I didn't have; second-wave DC wins push a 2027 growth leg.
- **Stock has re-rated hard** (~$320, ~$12B+ cap, Strong Buy) — valuation is now rich (Citi PT $410 but average PT well below spot per one source — dispersion).

## 6. Implications for ratings (flag only)

- **D1 AI Revenue %** — UP. DC Computing (38%, ~all AI) + a growing share of semi tied to AI/leading-edge WFE. Plausibly approaching the 4 band (25-50% clearly AI-tied), arguably higher if leading-edge semi is counted.
- **D2 Supply Chain Position** — supportive. Plasma power for etch/dep is a differentiated subsystem (few alternatives: MKS is the main rival); pricing/margin firming.
- **D3 Moat** — supportive but bounded. Plasma-power IP + OEM design-in switching costs; DC power is more contested (Vicor, Delta, Flex, Vertiv-adjacent). Guard against halo from the stock run.
- **D4 Capacity Expansion** — UP. Funded multi-geo buildout to $3.5B capacity, capex raised to $170-180M.
- **D5 Hyperscaler Exposure** — UP. Explicit hyperscale AI-rack demand; "second wave" wins. But indirect (sells power subsystems into DC OEM/hyperscale supply chain).
- **M1 EPS Revisions** — UP. Beat-and-raise, Q2 guide above consensus, PT increases.
- **M2 Relative Strength** — UP. Stock ~$320, large re-rating vs SOXX.
- **R1 Customer Concentration** — watch. Semi historically concentrated in AMAT/Lam (industry-known ~top-2 large); 10-Q does not disclose a >10% customer name this period. DC adds a different but also concentrated (hyperscaler) demand base. Mixed.
- **R2 Geo Risk** — China exposure exists ("All others" 46%); no disclosed export-control hit. Mexico now 26% of ship-to (tariff exposure flagged in MD&A).
- **R3 Balance Sheet Risk** — strong. Net cash (~$700M), convert due 2028, healthy FCF. Low risk.
- **R4 Regulatory Risk** — clean; routine only. Tariffs are a margin headwind, not a litigation/regulatory overhang.

**Thesis-break signals:** none. Direction is strongly positive. Watch-items: (a) DC demand "frequent customer changes / downstream constraints" = lumpiness risk; (b) Q2 DC guided to moderate sequentially; (c) valuation rich after re-rating; (d) tariff drag on margin.
