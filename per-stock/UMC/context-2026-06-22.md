## 1. Pre-research mental model

UMC is Taiwan's #2 pure-play foundry, focused on mature/specialty nodes (22/28nm and above) — explicitly NOT leading edge, so its AI exposure is INDIRECT: it does not fab advanced AI accelerators (that is TSMC). End-markets are consumer, communications, and computing peripherals; the bull case is specialty-process differentiation (RF, BCD, embedded NVM) plus the Intel 12nm collaboration moving it up the node ladder cheaply. Key risks are mature-node oversupply driven by Chinese capacity (SMIC/Hua Hong) and the resulting pricing/utilization pressure. Financials report in TWD; as a foreign ADR (1 ADR = 5 ordinary shares) the USD-denominated Value inputs are structurally unreliable.

## 4. Earnings/results commentary highlights

- **Tone: clearly improving / cyclical upturn.** The dominant signal is the pricing reversal: UMC entered 2026 pressing suppliers for 15% cost cuts (defensive) and by Q1 2026 was notifying customers of **H2 price hikes** — a strong demand-tightening tell for a mature-node foundry.
- **Specialty mix-up working:** 22nm at a record 14% of revenue, guided to high-teens growth — this is the "climb the value chain via specialty differentiation" thesis playing out, not chasing smaller geometries.
- **AI exposure acknowledged but peripheral:** management lists "AI-related" among demand drivers (power management, peripherals, interface chips around AI servers), but this is INDIRECT — UMC does not fab AI accelerators. Treat AI mentions as adjacency, not core.
- **Intel 12nm = optionality, not yet earnings:** on track, but production only from 2027; no 2026 revenue contribution. Capital-light path to FinFET.
- **Capital discipline:** US$1.5B 2026 capex (modest for a foundry) + new buyback = mature-node, cash-return profile rather than a growth-capex story.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not pre-decide; collaborative session required)

- **D1 (AI thesis — directness):** FLAG — AI exposure is **INDIRECT/limited**. Mature/specialty-node foundry; no advanced AI accelerators. "AI-related" demand is adjacency (power mgmt, interface, peripherals). The absolute-directness lens (rule 12) should keep D1 LOW regardless of the strong cyclical print. Do not let the earnings beat inflate directness.
- **D2 (TAM/growth runway):** improving cyclically + 22nm specialty + Intel 12nm optionality, but the AI portion of that TAM is small.
- **D5 (moat/competitive position):** specialty-process differentiation and Intel FinFET access are mild positives; offset by structural Chinese mature-node capacity (SMIC/Hua Hong) — the long-run competitive overhang.
- **Momentum (M):** positive — stock +6–7% on print, pricing turn, util/GM rising. 50DMA% (rule 11) should be refreshed via `momentum_50dma.py` for the objective leg if scoring.
- **R1 (customer concentration):** broad mature-node customer base — likely low concentration risk (verify in 20-F).
- **R2 (geography):** FLAG — **Taiwan geopolitical risk** (China-Taiwan) is a structural overhang; Taiwan-concentrated fabs. Intel Arizona partnership adds modest US diversification from 2027.
- **R3 (balance sheet):** strong cash return (buyback), modest US$1.5B capex — likely healthy; confirm net cash in 20-F.
- **R5 (disruption risk):** **defaults to 5** (rule 16) — Layer 05 fab, physical infrastructure, not a Layer-10 agentic-AI disruption target.
- **VALUE inputs (rules 3 + foreign-ADR trap):** FLAG STRONGLY — UMC reports in **TWD**; yfinance USD-derived **P/S, EV/EBITDA, FCF-yield are unreliable** (USD ÷ local-currency garbage) and must be **blanked** if/when scored, per the foreign-ADR currency trap note. **ADR = 5 ordinary shares** — watch per-share scaling on any P/E. Re-blank after any rule-9 objective refresh.
- **GROWTH / EPS YoY (rule 15):** FLAG — ~100–108% EPS YoY vs +5.5% revenue YoY suggests one-time/non-operating influence. **Verify the composition from the primary 6-K results exhibit before using EPS YoY as a scored input**; blank if non-operating-dominated, per rule 15.
