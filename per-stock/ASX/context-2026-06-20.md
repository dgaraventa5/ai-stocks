## 1. Pre-research mental model (articulated BEFORE pulling data)

> ASE is the world's largest OSAT (outsourced semiconductor assembly & test) — the back-end contract manufacturer that takes finished wafers and turns them into packaged, tested chips. Two businesses fused: legacy ATM (assembly/test, the core) plus a large lower-margin EMS arm (the old USI/electronics-manufacturing-services business). My stale view: a cyclical, low-margin (high-teens gross margin), capital-intensive packager, historically a commodity wirebond/flip-chip house competing with Amkor and JCET, that has been pivoting hard into advanced packaging (SiP, fan-out, 2.5D) to ride AI. I believe AI/advanced packaging is a fast-growing but still-minority slice of revenue; the bulk is still mainstream packaging + EMS (a lot of which is Apple-driven SiP). Taiwan-concentrated assets = real geopolitical/earthquake/power risk. Most recent quarter in my training is fuzzy — I'd guess FY2024 / early-2025 with margins recovering off a 2023 cyclical trough.

**The diff vs. fresh research (§6) is the value of this exercise.**

## 7. Sub-layer placement — confirm / critique

**Proposed:** `04 Semiconductor Equipment & Materials / OSAT & advanced packaging` (NEW sub-layer).

**Verdict: ENDORSE the new sub-layer, with a naming nuance.** The research map's Layer 4 today contains *equipment makers and materials* (ASML, AMAT, LRCX, KLAC, TER, ONTO, CAMT, **KLIC**, **BESIY**) — i.e., companies that **sell tools** into the packaging step (KLIC = packaging equipment; BESIY = hybrid-bonding/die-attach tools; CAMT = packaging inspection). **ASE sells a service, not a tool** — it is a back-end **contract manufacturer** that *operates* those tools at scale. That is a genuinely distinct value-chain position (different economics: capital-intensive capacity utilization + labor, not high-margin tool IP), so it does not belong lumped with the equipment vendors and there is no existing home for it.

**Recommendation to main loop:** create the sub-layer **"04 Semiconductor Equipment & Materials / OSAT & advanced packaging (back-end service)"** and seat ASX + AMKR (Amkor, the sibling pass spun up today) there together. Minor critique of the label: "OSAT" already implies the service; appending "(back-end service)" makes the tool-vs-service distinction explicit so it isn't confused with the KLIC/BESIY equipment names. Layer 4 is the right *layer* (it's where the back-end of fabrication lives in this map); only the sub-layer is new. (Alternative considered and rejected: a slot under Layer 5 "Fabs" — wrong, OSAT is post-fab back-end, not wafer fabrication.)

## 8. Implications for ratings (flag only — do NOT pre-decide; main loop owns the canonical scores)

**AI Thesis** — strongest category.
- **D1 AI Revenue %:** AI is the explicit growth driver but is *not yet a majority of revenue*. LEAP >$3.5B (2026E) on ~US$20B+ revenue ≈ high-teens % directly AI-attributable, plus AI-driven test and AI-peripheral packaging beyond the LEAP label. Likely a **3** (10–25% clearly AI-tied), arguably bordering 4 if AI-test + peripherals are credited. The big EMS/smartphone block and mainstream wirebond dilute the mix.
- **D2 Supply-chain position:** ASE is the **#1 OSAT globally**, an upstream-of-the-finished-chip bottleneck with limited substitutes (Amkor, JCET the only peers at scale) and *widening* lead times / capacity-pull. Strong — **4** (upstream, 2–4 viable competitors, tight capacity). Not a 5: in the marquee AI interposer step (CoWoS) it is a complement to TSMC, not the bottleneck owner.
- **D3 Moat:** scale + capital intensity + deep customer qualification/correlation switching costs, but advanced packaging is contestable (TSMC integrating forward; Amkor; substrate dependence). Mid-strong — **3–4**; be ruthless (anchor: TSMC/ASML=5; ASE's moat is scale/entanglement, not unique IP).
- **D4 Capacity expansion:** textbook **5** — multi-year, funded, customer-backed buildout; capex *raised twice to US$8.5B*; new test hub. This is the cleanest 5 in the package.
- **D5 Hyperscaler exposure (supplier framework):** indirect-to-direct. ASE packages/tests AI accelerators for the fabless/hyperscaler-silicon ecosystem (NVDA/AMD/custom-ASIC supply chains) but its *named* >10% customer is Apple (consumer), and hyperscaler revenue isn't disclosed as concentrated. Likely **3** (selling into AI/hyperscaler silicon but not a disclosed-concentrated anchor; much exposure is one step removed). Resist rating high on the AI narrative without disclosed hyperscaler concentration.

**Momentum.**
- **M1 EPS revisions:** net-positive, Q1'26 beat ~15%, targets nudged up — but not a violent beat-and-raise pattern. **~3–4.**
- **M2 Relative strength:** strong outperformer vs SOXX over 6–12 months (qualitatively); **likely 4–5** — but verify the ADR (not TWSE 3711) price series in the main loop; the "257%" figure is the local line and looks overstated for the ADR. Don't lock M2 on the unverified number.
- **M3 Insider activity:** **DATA GAP — no Form 4 / Section 16 data exists for a foreign private issuer** (yfinance heldPercentInsiders 0.0 confirms). Rubric default for "neither buying nor discretionary selling visible" = **3**, and that is the appropriate flagged default here (absence of data, not absence of buying). Note the limitation explicitly in the audit log.

**Risk (5 = lowest risk).**
- **R1 Customer concentration:** one customer ~25%, top-5 46.5%. That's squarely the rubric's "top customer 25–40%, real dependence but not existential" → **3**.
- **R2 Geographic:** the standout risk. **Asset leg present and dominant** — ~58.9% of revenue *produced in Taiwan* (non-substitutable), plus a Taiwan power-disruption risk factor; demand also U.S.-customer-concentrated (56.7%). Limited direct China-policy overhang (modest PRC ops), so it's primarily an *asset/strait* shock, not an export-control story. Per the revised R2 bands, **asset leg located in a risk geography → 2.** Worst plausible shock: Taiwan Strait conflict / major earthquake / grid failure impairing the majority of production capacity. (Anchor: TSM = 2 for fabs in Taiwan; ASE is the same asset-leg logic.) Mandatory R2 rationale = "Taiwan Strait/seismic/grid shock; ~59% of revenue produced in Taiwan (20-F)."
- **R3 Balance sheet:** ND/E 0.40, ND/EBITDA ~2x, OCF strongly positive, huge unused credit lines — but FCF currently negative on the capex ramp and a real (if moderate) debt load. **3–4** (average-to-modest leverage for a capital-intensive name).
- **R4 Regulatory/litigation:** no major disclosed regulatory threat surfaced; routine matters. **4** pending a Legal-Proceedings read (20-F Item 8) — flagged as not-yet-fully-verified.
- **R5 Disruption risk:** physical back-end manufacturing — chips must be assembled, packaged, and tested by *someone*, and advanced packaging is becoming *more* essential as Moore's-law scaling shifts value to the package. Non-Layer-10 → **default 5** (most durable). AI is a tailwind, not a disruptor, to OSAT.

**Thesis-break signals to watch:** (1) AI/LEAP demand air-pocket → stranded specialized assets (the 20-F's own named risk + the US$8.5B capex bet makes this the asymmetric downside); (2) TSMC forward-integrating advanced packaging and compressing OSAT's share of the AI package; (3) Taiwan-Strait/seismic/grid shock; (4) Apple (the ~25% customer) in-sourcing SiP or shifting volume.
