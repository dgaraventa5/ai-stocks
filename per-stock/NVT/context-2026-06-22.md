## 1. Pre-research mental model

nVent is a mid-cap electrical connection & protection company (enclosures, electrical & fastening) whose mix has tilted hard toward data-center power and liquid-cooling enclosures, making it a Layer-02 AI-infrastructure beneficiary. Management has reshaped the portfolio: divested the lower-growth Thermal Management business (to Brookfield, ~early 2025) and bolted on Trachte (utility/data-center enclosures) and Avail Infrastructure Solutions to lean into infrastructure/data-center demand. I expect strong organic growth and order momentum on AI buildout, but the open questions are (a) durability of order growth vs. a one-time backlog surge, (b) margin trajectory through acquisition integration, and (c) whether a richer multiple already prices in the AI narrative (rule-14 expectations risk).

## 2. Latest filing highlights

Primary sources: Q1 2026 Form 8-K (Item 2.02, filed 2026-05-01) attaching the Q1 press release (Exhibit 99.1), and Form 10-Q for the quarter ended 2026-03-31 (filed 2026-05-01).

- **Revenue:** Net sales $1,242.0M in Q1 2026 vs $809.3M in Q1 2025 = **+53.5% reported** (10-Q income statement, quarter ended 2026-03-31). Press release reports **organic growth ~34%** (Q1 2026 press release, 2026-05-01).
- **Gross profit / margin:** Gross profit $445.6M on $1,242.0M = **35.9% GM**, vs $313.7M on $809.3M = **38.8% GM** in Q1 2025 — **~290bps GM compression YoY** (10-Q, quarter ended 2026-03-31). Flag: compression is material; needs to be reconciled against acquisition mix (Avail/Trachte lower-margin?) vs. operating leverage — see §6.
- **Opex:** SG&A $227.2M (vs $166.2M PY); R&D $22.x M (10-Q, quarter ended 2026-03-31).
- **Segments (post-reshaping):** Two reportable segments — **Systems Protection** and **Electrical Connections** (10-Q segment note). Per press release/trade press, Systems Protection sales ~**$895M, +76% reported / +50% organic**, with infrastructure (data-center) sales **>100% organic growth** (Q1 2026 press release + tEDmag, 2026-05-01).
- **Orders / backlog:** **Record orders and backlog**; organic orders **~+40%**, primarily AI data-center buildout. Backlog cited at **~$2.6B** in Q1 (press release / Investing.com slides, 2026-05-01). Note prior-year backlog was cited ~$2.3B entering 2026 / "3x prior year" per trade press — backlog still building.
- **Cash flow:** Operating cash flow ~$90M (+41%); **free cash flow ~$54M (+21%)** (press release, 2026-05-01). FCF conversion modest in Q1 (seasonally weak quarter for NVT historically) — monitor full-year.
- **Balance sheet / M&A:** **Total debt $1,564.7M** at 2026-03-31 (10-Q fair-value note). **Avail Infrastructure Solutions ("Electrical Products Group") acquired for $979.6M cash**; goodwill of $439.6M recognized, substantially all tax-deductible (10-Q acquisition note). Thermal Management treated as discontinued/held-for-sale in prior-period comparatives (10-Q XBRL tags reference ThermalManagementMember, divestiture 2025-01-30).
- **Domicile:** Ireland-incorporated, principal offices London; files US 10-Q/10-K in USD, NYSE-listed (NVT). Treat as US filer for scoring (no ADR currency trap).

## 6. Implications for ratings (flags only — no rating numbers set here)

- **D1 / D2 (AI demand directness & magnitude):** Strongly positive signal. Infrastructure/data-center sales >100% organic; Systems Protection +50% organic; orders +40% organic into 2027 backlog. NVT sells directly into both gray-space power and white-space liquid cooling — high AI directness for a Layer-02 name. Revisit D1/D2.
- **D5 (catalysts / new contracts):** Record orders + backlog into 2027 + new liquid-cooling/power portfolio launches in 2026 are forward catalysts. Revisit D5.
- **Momentum (M):** Beat + double guidance raise + accelerating Q2 guide + strong relative strength (+53% 3-mo). M (EPS revisions, relative strength) flags positive. Refresh 50DMA% objective input alongside (rule 11) — likely high.
- **Growth category (objective):** Revenue growth metrics will screen very high (organic 21–23% guide, +53% reported). Watch the **EPS YoY** input: GAAP figures are distorted by the Thermal divestiture (discontinued ops) and Avail acquisition accounting — confirm whether YoY EPS change is one-time-dominated before scoring (rule 15). Adjusted EPS +63% is operational; GAAP comparability is muddied by portfolio reshaping.
- **Quality / margins:** GM **compressed ~290bps YoY** — a genuine flag, not yet a thesis break. Likely acquisition-mix dilution (Avail/Trachte) + integration, partially offset by operating leverage (adj EPS still +63%). Needs a margin bridge from the transcript/10-Q MD&A before trusting any quality-trend read. **Do not assume margin expansion.**
- **R1 (customer concentration):** Hyperscaler-driven backlog raises concentration questions — not quantified this pass (data gap). Flag R1 for the transcript/10-K read.
- **R3 (balance sheet):** Total debt $1,564.7M after $979.6M cash Avail deal; goodwill build (~$439.6M from Avail alone). Leverage stepped up for M&A — check net debt/EBITDA before R3. Not alarming on its face given EBITDA ramp, but quantify.
- **R5 (disruption risk):** Physical electrical/cooling infrastructure → **defaults to 5** per rule 16 (non-Layer-10, durable hardware). No change expected.
- **Rule-14 expectations angle:** Multiple is elevated (premium valuation, +53% 3-mo run, +12% on the raise). If P/S is ≥90th percentile of its own 3-yr range, the expectations red flag may fire — but here revenue growth is *accelerating*, not decelerating, so the rule-14 condition (peak multiple + decelerating growth) likely does **not** fully trigger. Note for the briefing; let `expectations_flag.py` adjudicate at scoring time.
- **Portfolio reshaping note:** Thermal divest + Trachte/Avail acquisitions materially changed the revenue base and segment structure (two segments, DC-led). Cross-period objective inputs (margins, growth, leverage) must be read with this in mind — TTM data spans the transition (rule-9 TTM limitation applies).

**THESIS-BREAK scan:** None triggered. No order deceleration (orders +40% organic, Q2 guide accelerating), no guidance cut (raised twice), no acquisition-integration blowup disclosed. The one yellow flag is **GM compression (~290bps YoY)** — watch, not break. No regulatory action noted.
