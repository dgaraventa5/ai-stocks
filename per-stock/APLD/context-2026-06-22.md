## 1. Pre-research mental model

Going in: APLD is an AI-datacenter developer pivoting from a money-losing GPU-cloud business toward a datacenter-REIT-style triple-net leasing model. Flagship is the Ellendale, ND "Polaris Forge 1" campus, anchored by a large CoreWeave critical-IT lease (~400 MW). Heavy GAAP losses, serial dilution, and large debt/financing needs are the defining risks; the bull case is that secured gigawatts of power + signed long-term hyperscaler leases convert a junk balance sheet into contracted, financeable cash flow. Income-statement Value metrics are garbage on GAAP losses, so secured MW is the real Value object (rule 13). The capacity-mw.json entry (1,036 MW, as-of 2026-01-07) was already flagged ~6 months stale — expect a material upward MW revision and a wave of new lease + financing activity.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do not pre-decide)

Rule-13 cohort reminders: secured MW is the Value object; GAAP-loss income-statement Value inputs (Fwd P/E, EV/EBITDA-standard, FCF yield, EPS-based PEG) are garbage and should be blanked/handled per cohort convention. EV/EBITDA column = **EV per secured gross MW**.

- **EV/MW (Value, rule 13) — REFRESH NEEDED.** capacity-mw.json shows 1,036 MW gross-equiv as-of 2026-01-07; the 2026-06-08 Delta Forge 2 PR discloses ~1.4 GW contracted critical IT (~1,750 MW ×1.25 gross-equiv) and ~2.15 GW grid-connected utility power. The denominator is roughly +70%–110% higher than the stale value — a stale MW denominator inflates EV/MW and understates Value. **Decide secured-MW basis carefully:** contracted critical-IT (×1.25) vs grid-connected utility power vs energized-only — the json's existing basis mixes 600 MW critical-IT×1.25 + 286 MW legacy gross. Note much of the 1.4 GW is committed/under-construction (energized today is only the 100 MW PF1 building + Jamestown 106 + legacy Ellendale 180); apply the json's "energized + committed/under-construction, speculative excluded" rule. (Cite Delta Forge 2 PR 2026-06-08 + transcript ~900 MW under construction when refreshing.)
- **D1/D2/D5 (AI Thesis directness, demand, contracts):** Strongly supported by signed long-term leases — CoreWeave 400 MW + an IG hyperscaler across 3 campuses, ~$36B contracted base-term revenue, ~1.4 GW critical IT. Contracts are real and growing; this is direct AI-infrastructure exposure. Re-examine D-dimensions given the lease wave (rule 12 earnings/contract trigger).
- **M (Momentum):** Stock +20% on the May lease wave (news-log); refresh 50DMA% via `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` (rule 11). Revenue beat + serial positive lease catalysts support momentum; EPS miss is a counter.
- **R1 (customer concentration):** Improving but still material — CoreWeave is the largest counterparty (~$11B / 400 MW). Mitigant: an IG hyperscaler now anchors 3 campuses, diversifying the book. Watch the **CoreWeave MOU** — lease assignment is contingent on the CoreWeave sub reaching IG rating; CoreWeave's own credit is the live counterparty risk.
- **R3 (balance sheet / financing):** >$4B new secured debt in one quarter ($2.15B Mar + $1.59B Jun notes at 7.000%) + $350M/$550M revolver. Cash $1.73B at 2/28. Project-level secured-notes structure (ComputeCo SPVs) is more lender-friendly than past dilution, but leverage and interest burden are rising fast and execution/construction risk is high (Delta Forge 1 ops mid-2027, ELN-04 under construction). Heavy build-ahead-of-cash-flow.
- **R5 (Disruption Risk):** Layer 09 physical infra — **defaults to 5** (rule 16). No sub-5 R5 outside Layer 10.
- **Rule 14 (expectations red-flag):** `expectations_flag.py APLD` returned **clean** — P/S 34.88 = 83.5th pctile (below the 90th-pctile trigger), rev YoY 250.2% vs 3y median 257.3%. Not flagged as peak-multiple/decelerating, but P/S is elevated; note for context.
- **Growth:** Revenue +139% YoY (Q3) / +250% TTM-ish per expectations script. EPS YoY is a GAAP loss widening — likely not a clean Growth input; consider whether rule-15 (one-time-dominated) or simply loss-to-loss makes EPS YoY unusable, and average revenue metrics instead. (Decide on documented evidence — do not pre-decide.)

**THESIS-BREAK watch:** No break detected. Positives dominate (lease wave, revenue beat, capacity scaling, customer diversification, large cash balance). Watch-items, not breaks: (a) CoreWeave counterparty credit (MOU contingent on IG rating); (b) rising leverage / 7% interest burden + construction execution to 2027; (c) continued GAAP losses + any return to equity dilution; (d) construction delays at PF1 building 2 (mid-2026) / Delta Forge 1 (mid-2027). No going-concern signal — $1.73B cash + project financing closed.
