# MPWR (Monolithic Power Systems) -- Context Briefing 2026-05-25

## 1. Pre-research mental model

What I currently believe about MPWR:
- Monolithic Power Systems designs high-performance analog/mixed-signal power management ICs
- Key products: voltage regulators, DC-DC converters, power modules used in servers, automotive, telecom, industrial
- AI thesis: each GPU tray (H100/B200) requires sophisticated power delivery -- MPWR supplies the voltage regulation modules (VRMs) that sit on the GPU board
- Customers likely include major server OEMs (Dell, HPE, Supermicro) and potentially hyperscalers for custom designs
- Competes with Texas Instruments, Analog Devices, Infineon, Renesas in power management
- Has been growing rapidly -- AI server power demand is a major tailwind
- Market cap ~$30B, less followed on the AI angle than pure-play GPU names

### Finding 3: Blackwell share loss, but Vera Rubin recapture

**Critical competitive dynamic**: MPWR lost significant market share on NVIDIA's Blackwell platform (GB200/B200) due to reported PMIC overheating issues. Infineon is expected to be the primary Blackwell supplier (60-70% share), with Renesas taking secondary share. MPWR's allocation on Blackwell is "drastically limited or eliminated."

However, MPWR is expected to **recapture ~70% market share on NVIDIA's Vera Rubin architecture** (VR200 NVL144 and R200 HGX platforms). This is projected to add >$100M to 2026 revenue estimates and ~$420M annualized by 2027, plus >$4 EPS.

MPWR's 800-volt direct-current (VDC) solutions are a technical prerequisite for Vera Rubin, giving it a competitive edge on next-gen platforms.

## 3. DIFF: what changed vs. training data / mental model

1. **Market cap is ~$78-81B, NOT ~$30B.** The stock has roughly tripled. My mental model was severely outdated. This changes the Value dimension significantly -- the stock is far more expensive than I assumed.

2. **Enterprise Data segment nearly doubled YoY (+97.7%).** I knew AI was a tailwind; I underestimated the magnitude. This is not incremental -- it is transformative for the revenue mix.

3. **Blackwell share loss is real and material.** I had MPWR as a clear GPU VRM supplier. In reality, MPWR lost Blackwell to Infineon (60-70% share) and Renesas due to PMIC overheating issues. This is a significant competitive setback that my mental model missed entirely.

4. **Vera Rubin recapture at ~70% share.** The Blackwell loss is partially offset by expected dominance on NVIDIA's next-gen Vera Rubin platform via 800V VDC solutions. This creates a "valley then recovery" dynamic rather than permanent share loss.

5. **Insider selling is extreme.** $255M from the CEO alone in 6 months; $417M+ total across insiders with literally zero purchases. While the CLAUDE.md rubric says insider selling is ambiguous, the scale here is exceptional even by high-growth semi standards.

6. **Revenue growth is accelerating, not decelerating.** Q2 guidance implies 35-36% YoY growth vs. Q1's 26%. The Enterprise Data growth floor keeps getting raised (30-40% -> 50% -> 85%). This is the opposite of what you'd expect from a stock that's already run.

7. **Full-year 2025 revenue was $2.79B** (up 26.4% YoY), making $3.4-3.6B a reasonable 2026 estimate. At $78-81B market cap, that's ~22-24x revenue -- extremely rich even for a high-growth semi.
