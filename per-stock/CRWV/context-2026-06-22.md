## 1. Pre-research mental model

CoreWeave is the flagship "neocloud" — a GPU-as-a-service operator that rents NVIDIA accelerators at scale, NVIDIA-backed (equity + allocation), IPO'd March 2025, with revenue historically dominated by a Microsoft anchor (~62-67% of 2024-25 revenue) plus a large OpenAI contract. The model is debt-heavy: GPUs are financed with contract-backed/asset-backed term loans (DDTL facilities) and OEM vendor financing, so leverage and interest expense scale with the buildout. My last data point (May 2026 news log) had Q1 2026 revenue $2.08B (+112% YoY), an EPS miss, backlog $99.4B, interest expense doubling to $536M, and a stack of new debt issuance (DDTL 5.0 $3.1B in May, a $3.5B senior unsecured notes launch announced June 11). The central tension: explosive contracted demand and ~3.5 GW secured power vs. mounting GAAP losses, heavy interest burden, and concentration in a small number of (partly non-investment-grade) AI-lab counterparties.

## 4. Earnings transcript highlights

Source: CoreWeave Q1 2026 earnings call, 2026-05-07 5:00pm ET; transcript via Motley Fool (2026-05-08) and FactSet/Q4 PDF. Speakers: Mike Intrator (CEO), Nitin Agrawal (CFO).

- **Backlog conversion timing (important for "is it real revenue"):** 36% of backlog expected recognized within **24 months**, 75% within **4 years** (CFO). So the $99.4B is near-term-weighted, not a 10-year mirage — but still requires the buildout to land on schedule.
- **Margin trough framing:** Adjusted Operating Income $21M (~1% margin) explicitly called the low point before sequential expansion; Adjusted EBITDA margin 56%. The gap between 56% adj-EBITDA and 1% adj-operating-income / -36% GAAP net margin **is depreciation + interest** — i.e., the financing/asset-intensity is the whole story.
- **Concentration de-risking:** non-IG counterparties <30% of backlog; pivot to investment-grade names (Meta, Microsoft, OpenAI-via-larger-deals).
- **No going-concern or covenant stress language**; management emphasized $3.3B liquidity and oversubscribed financings. (Absence of disclosure ≠ absence of risk, but no red flag in the call.)
- **Capacity constraint, not demand constraint:** management framed the bottleneck as power/buildout speed, not bookings — consistent with the raised capex.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — no rating numbers pre-decided)

**AI Thesis (D1-D5):**
- **D1/D2 (demand / contract directness):** Strongly reinforced — $99.4B backlog, >$40B new Q1 commitments, four preeminent labs + Meta/Anthropic/Jane Street/OpenAI/Microsoft. This is among the most direct AI-demand exposures in the watchlist. Look for confirmation that ratings already credit this.
- **D5 (counterparty quality / durability of demand):** Materially **improved** — non-IG labs now <30% of backlog, shift to investment-grade. If a prior rating penalized CRWV for non-IG-lab concentration, that input is now stale and worth revisiting.

**Momentum (M):**
- 50DMA % is objective (rule 11) — refresh via `scripts/momentum_50dma.py` at scoring time; not computed here. Subjective momentum: Nasdaq-100 inclusion + raised exit-ARR floor + backlog growth are positive narrative; offset by an EPS miss and stock volatility around earnings. Flag M for a fresh look.

**Risk (R1-R5):**
- **R1 (customer concentration):** Improving (10 customers >$1B; non-IG <30%) — but **MSFT/OpenAI still dominant and exact current concentration % is a data gap.** Revisit; don't over-credit without the MRQ customer-mix disclosure.
- **R3 (balance sheet / leverage):** This is the live risk. Interest expense $536M/qtr and growing; **new unsecured notes at 9.625%/8.5%** signal rising marginal cost of capital; FY capex $31-35B vs $12-13B revenue means continued reliance on oversubscribed-but-expensive external funding. No going-concern/covenant language, $3.3B liquidity, no material maturities until 2029 (ex self-amortizing) — so not a near-term solvency flag, but R3 should reflect the leverage trajectory. **Revisit R3.**
- **R5 (disruption risk, rule 16):** CRWV is Layer 09, not Layer 10 — defaults to **5 (most durable)**. GPU compute capacity is physical infra; AI is a tailwind, not a disruptor. No change expected; confirm it's at 5.

**Value — EV/MW (rule 13, this is the cohort's Value-metric substitute for EV/EBITDA):**
- capacity-mw.json secured_gross_mw = **3,500 MW** (as-of 2026-03-31) is **current** per Q1 disclosure — no MW refresh needed this pass. The EV/MW value will move only with EV (price/share + the growing debt load). Note the rule-13 division-of-labor: EV/MW prices the *capacity*; whether it converts to AI revenue is the AI-Thesis category's job — and here conversion probability is high (capacity is largely contracted), unlike the bitcoin-miner cohort.
- **Caveat on the EV side of EV/MW:** EV is rising fast via debt issuance (DDTL 5.0 $3.1B + ~$3.4B-equiv new notes). A stale EV (share count/net debt) would understate EV/MW. Ensure EV reflects the post-June debt stack when scored.

**Rule-14 "what's-priced-in" / expectations flag (high-multiple name):**
- CRWV trades on backlog/ARR narrative, not current earnings (GAAP loss-making). Run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py CRWV` at scoring time — but note the script may self-skip (recent IPO → <3yr P/S history; possible XBRL revenue-tag mapping issues for a young filer). The qualitative version of the flag clearly applies: peak-of-cycle AI-infra multiple, consensus crowded into the neocloud trade (recall SALP cut its CRWV *call* position -83% in Q1 while keeping equity — de-levered the bet). The genuine downside scenario is not a demand miss but a **funding-cost / buildout-execution** miss: capex raised, marginal debt now ~9-10% unsecured, and 36%-of-backlog-in-24-months means execution must stay on schedule.

**Earnings-triggered refresh (rule 9):** Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-07) already drove a >15%-type move and large sequential changes — objective inputs (revenue growth, margins, EV/MW) should be refreshed from this Q1 data if the watchlist row predates 2026-05-07. Flag for the scoring pass.
