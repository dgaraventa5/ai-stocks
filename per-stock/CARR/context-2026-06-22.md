## 1. Pre-research mental model

Carrier is a pure-play HVAC/climate company after reshaping its portfolio — acquired Viessmann Climate Solutions (2023, European heat pumps) and divested Fire & Security and Commercial/Transport Refrigeration (2024). The AI angle is data-center cooling (precision chillers, liquid cooling under the QuantumLeap brand), a fast-growing but still-minority pocket inside a broad, cyclical HVAC franchise that is mostly residential + light-commercial + European heat pumps. Known overhangs: weak European heat-pump demand (post-subsidy hangover) and soft residential. Valuation is mid-cycle industrial multiple; the data-center story is the bull case but the base business is cyclical.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — no numbers pre-decided)

- **D1 (AI directness) — CRITICAL BLENDED-EXPOSURE FLAG.** Carrier is mostly broad HVAC (residential/light-commercial cyclicality + European heat pumps); data-center cooling is only **~7% of revenue** (~$1.5B of ~$22B). D1 should reflect *blended* exposure, NOT the DC headline. This is a thermal-supplier into AI data centers — indirect/enabling, and a minority of the franchise. Do not let +500% DC order optics inflate directness.
- **D2 (revenue/demand pull from AI buildout) — positive but small-base.** Real, accelerating AI-driven DC demand (orders +500% Q1, backlog covers $1.5B FY) — but on a ~7% base. Flag as genuine secondary pull, capped by mix.
- **D5 (catalyst/contract momentum) — active.** New DC products (AquaEdge 30CF chiller, CDU for liquid cooling), DC backlog visibility, sixth year of double-digit commercial HVAC. Offset by resi/China drag. Re-examine D5 in light of both.
- **M (Momentum) — mixed.** Q1 beat + stock pop, but adj-EPS −12% YoY and FY guide only reaffirmed (no raise). Refresh objective 50DMA % (`scripts/momentum_50dma.py`) before rating; do not infer momentum from the order-growth headline alone.
- **R5 (Disruption Risk) — defaults to 5.** Physical thermal infrastructure (chillers, HVAC), not Layer 10 software — no agentic-AI disruption to the core revenue model. Default max durability per rule 16.
- **R-other (R1–R4): R2 geography** — meaningful China + Europe exposure (China segment −low-teens; European heat-pump softness); **R3 balance sheet** — Viessmann leverage (interest $90M) plus a steep Q1 FCF swing (−~$28M continuing) and ~$500M capital return — watch cash quality; **R4 regulatory** — refrigerant transition (low-GWP mandates) is both tailwind (replacement cycle) and execution risk. None look like a break yet.
- **Rule-14 (expectations) angle:** Run `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py CARR` before scoring. CARR is NOT an obvious crowded-peak name (cyclical industrial, mid-multiple, GAAP earnings depressed), so the typical "peak P/S + decelerating growth" flag is less likely to fire — but the AI-DC narrative could elevate P/S; check explicitly.
- **Rule-15 (EPS YoY):** The $96M one-time tax benefit distorts GAAP EPS YoY. If using EPS YoY as a Growth input, blank or verify it's not one-time-dominated; cite the tax item in the Rating Audit if blanked.
- **Rule-9 objective refresh:** Q1 2026 (Item 2.02) reported 2026-04-30 — objective inputs (gross margin, FCF margin, growth) should be refreshed from this quarter. TTM will blend the margin compression; flag MRQ vs TTM divergence.

### THESIS-BREAK scan
**None confirmed.** Watch items, not breaks: (1) residential/light-commercial + China softness deepening margin compression; (2) European heat-pump recovery still not materializing; (3) Q1 FCF swing to roughly −$28M (continuing) — seasonal but steep, monitor through H1; (4) FY adj-EPS only reaffirmed, not raised, despite the order beat. Data-center orders are *accelerating* (+500% Q1, +50% FY guide, backlog covers $1.5B) — no DC deceleration signal. No guidance cut.
