## 1. Pre-research mental model

AAOI is a small-cap, volatile, historically-unprofitable Layer-07 optical name: datacenter transceivers (400G/800G hyperscaler ramp) plus a legacy CATV/broadband business. The bull case is the AI-driven 800G/1.6T datacenter transceiver ramp; the bear case is its long track record of weak/negative margins, lumpy results, extreme customer concentration (Microsoft, Amazon historically), and serial dilutive capital raises. Expectation going in: Quality/EPS inputs negative or garbage (still loss-making), high R1 customer-concentration and R3 dilution/balance-sheet risk. Calendar fiscal year.

**CRITICAL flags:**
- **Quality / EPS inputs = negative/garbage.** GAAP net loss $(14.281)M, op loss $(13.0)M, negative ROIC/operating margin. Quality metrics (margins, returns) will floor-pin or read negative; EPS YoY is loss-to-wider-loss (−$0.18 → −$0.19) — not a meaningful growth signal. **Flag for whoever scores: confirm Quality treatment and whether yfinance GAAP figures distort.** Layer-07 (not Layer-10), so standard EV/EBITDA bands apply — but EBITDA may be near-zero/negative; **flag possible negative-EBITDA blanking** (same garbage-input principle as rules 10/13).
- **Rule-15 (EPS YoY) check:** The YoY EPS change is NOT one-time-item dominated — it is a genuinely wider *operating* loss (op loss widened, revenue up but margin down and opex up). So rule-15 blanking does **not** apply on a one-time basis. However, EPS YoY as loss→loss is still low-information; surface for judgment rather than mechanical scoring. The Q1 2025 base also carried a $16.7M non-cash Amazon warrant item — comparability caveat worth noting.
