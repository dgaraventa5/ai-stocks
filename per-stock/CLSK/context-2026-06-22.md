## 1. Pre-research mental model

The open question this pass must answer: has CleanSpark — which had publicly resisted an AI/HPC pivot — actually *changed course* in 2026? If a credible AI/HPC pivot or contract has emerged, the rule-13 EV/MW-high / AI-Thesis-low divergence narrows and D-dimensions deserve a fresh look. Also watch the bitcoin-price and financing/dilution risk legs.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — no rating decided here)

Per CLAUDE.md rule 2/12 the human owns the rating call. Flags for the collaborative session:

- **Rule-13 EV/MW (Value) vs AI-Thesis divergence — STILL THE POINT.** EV/MW continues to score CLSK *well* (1,809 secured gross MW of cheap utility power against a depressed equity), while **AI-Thesis D-dimensions should stay LOW because there are still zero signed AI/HPC contracts and zero AI revenue** (100% BTC mining). This divergence is *by design* per rule 13 — do NOT fold "pivot optionality" into the EV/MW Value metric. The capacity is real; conversion to AI revenue is unproven.
- **D1/D2/D5 (AI Thesis) — watch, do not yet upgrade.** The 2026 pivot is now a *documented strategic intent* (10-Q language, Texas sites, PSU incentive, lead tenant in negotiation) — a genuine change from "resisted." But it is **negotiation-stage, pre-revenue.** A signed hyperscaler/neocloud AI lease (e.g., Sandersville tenant converting) would be the trigger to revisit AI-Thesis upward; until a lease is *executed*, D-dimensions should reflect zero contracted AI exposure. Flag for the rater: distinguish "credible pivot underway" from "AI revenue secured."
- **R5 Disruption Risk = 5 (default).** CLSK is Layer 09 (BTC miner / power infra), not Layer 10 — rule 16 default of 5 (most durable / no agentic-AI disruption to the core model) applies. No change.
- **R3 (balance sheet) / financing risk — flag.** LT debt jumped to ~$1.8B; $260M cash but ~$1.2B total liquidity incl. $400M BTC-backed credit. Leverage rising into a capex-heavy AI buildout that has no contracted offtake yet. Management claims share-count *reduction* over 18 months (dilution risk currently low), but the AI buildout could change the financing posture. Watch.
- **Bitcoin-price exposure — primary thesis-break vector.** Q2 net loss was overwhelmingly a −$224.1M BTC fair-value mark; revenue −25% QoQ purely on BTC price. Earnings and treasury value are directly geared to BTC price — both a Risk and a Growth/Momentum input. Any scoring read of "revenue declining" should be sourced to BTC price, not operational deterioration (hashrate/BTC-mined were flat-to-up).
- **Rule-14 (expectations) — note.** Not a scored metric; consider running `python3 scripts/expectations_flag.py CLSK` before a scoring pass. Equity is depressed (BTC drawdown), so the "peak multiple / decelerating growth" flag is unlikely to fire, but document the result.
- **capacity-mw.json (rule 13) — NOT stale.** Entry: 1,809 MW, asof 2026-03-31, "High" confidence, basis "total contracted gross utility power; zero AI/HPC MW contracted to date." This exactly matches the 2026-03-31 10-Q. The >5 GW speculative pipeline and the 285+600 MW Texas sites are correctly excluded (no executed AI lease; Texas energizing 2027). **No update needed**, though refresh again at fiscal Q3 (period ~2026-06-30) earnings.

**THESIS-BREAK watch:** (1) Bitcoin-price drawdown — already pressuring revenue/treasury (live risk). (2) Financing/dilution stress from a debt-funded AI buildout with no contracted offtake. (3) Power-cost squeeze (not evidenced this quarter; flat unit economics). (4) **(Positive break) a SIGNED AI/HPC lease** (Sandersville lead tenant) — would be the event that finally narrows the rule-13 EV/MW-high / AI-Thesis-low divergence; none signed yet.
