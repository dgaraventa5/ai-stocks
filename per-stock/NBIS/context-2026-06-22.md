## 1. Pre-research mental model

NBIS is the renamed/restructured former Yandex N.V. (Netherlands holdco), now an AI GPU-cloud / neocloud (infra-as-a-service) sitting on a large cash pile from the Yandex-Russia divestiture, plus minority stakes (Toloka, Avride, ClickHouse). Prior mental model: rapid ARR ramp, very heavy GPU/data-center capex, a Microsoft anchor deal, and as of the last log a $2.6B Bloom Energy fuel-cell agreement with capex guided to $20–25B. Foreign filer (20-F / 6-K, reports in USD). Expected the key debate to be capacity-buildout funding stress vs. the secured-backlog/contract story.

## 2. Latest filing highlights

Primary: Q1 2026 results furnished via **6-K filed 2026-05-20** (Exhibit 99.1 Operating/Financial Review, 99.2 unaudited financials; cover sheet confirms USD, FPI 20-F filer). Underlying numbers below sourced from the Q1 2026 results press release / shareholder letter (Nebius, businesswire, 2026-05-13) and the 6-K exhibits. The standalone **20-F (FY2025) was filed 2026-04-30**.

**Revenue / ARR (lead):**
- Q1 2026 group revenue **$399.0M**, up **684% YoY** from $50.9M (Q1 2025); ~+75% QoQ vs Q4 2025 (Q1 2026 results PR, businesswire 2026-05-13; Seeking Alpha Q1 breakdown).
- Core AI-cloud **ARR $1.92B**, up **54% QoQ** (Q1 2026 shareholder letter, Nebius, 2026-05-13).
- FY2026 targets reiterated: **ARR $7–9B**, group revenue **$3.0–3.4B** (Q1 2026 results, 2026-05-13).

**Secured MW capacity (the rule-13 Value object):**
- Targeting **>3 GW contracted power by end-2026** (raised from prior >2.5 GW); >2 GW already contracted; **total power capacity surpassed ~3.5 GW** per mid-2026 commentary (DCD 2026-03; Yahoo/Finance capacity coverage 2026).
- **Connected power** (live in built DCs) target **800 MW–1 GW by end-2026**; ended 2025 with **~170 MW active** (vs 100 MW target) (capacity coverage 2026). NOTE: "contracted" (secured land+power) >> "connected" (energized) — only ~170 MW was actually energized at YE2025. The capacity-mw.json 3,500 MW is a *contracted/secured* figure, not energized.
- Q1 2026 PR also cited securing **1.2 GW for a new U.S. AI facility** (Globe and Mail, 2026-05-13).

**Capex:**
- 2026 capex guidance **raised to $20–25B** (from $16–20B) to pre-fund 2027 capacity that contributes revenue H1 2027 (Q1 2026 results, 2026-05-13).

**Cash / balance sheet:**
- **Cash & equivalents $9.30B** as of 2026-03-31, up sharply — driven by **$4.34B convertible notes** issued and **$2.0B prefunded warrants** (Q1 2026 financials, 6-K 2026-05-20 Ex 99.2; stocktitan/businesswire 2026-05-13).
- Non-current debt rose to **~$8,432M** (from ~$4,103M) reflecting the note issuance (6-K Ex 99.2, 2026-05-20).
- Subsequent to quarter: **>$6B of new capital raised** to support forthcoming debt + equity funding (Q1 2026 results commentary, 2026-05-13).

**Profitability (caution — see rule 15):**
- **Adj. EBITDA +$129.5M** (from −$53.7M YoY) (Q1 2026 results, 2026-05-13).
- **Net income from continuing ops $621.2M** (from −$104.3M) — **but ~$780.6M is a one-time gain from revaluation of equity investments** (stocktitan/Futurum, 2026-05-13). Underlying **adjusted net loss WIDENED to −$100.3M** — the GAAP profit is non-operating-dominated.

## 6. Implications for ratings (FLAGS ONLY — do NOT pre-decide; collaborate per rules 2/12)

**Rule-13 EV/MW cohort — Value:**
- NBIS Value object is **secured gross MW**, NOT income-statement multiples. capacity-mw.json: **3,500 MW**, basis "company 'contracted power', IT-vs-gross unspecified (treated gross, flagged); >75% owned," asof **2026-03-31**, source Q1 2026 6-K + May 13 2026 call, confidence Medium. **This is the EV/EBITDA-column input for scoring.**
- **MW staleness flag:** json is asof 2026-03-31 (Q1) and updated 2026-06-12 — **within the quarter, NOT stale.** BUT mid-2026 commentary says total capacity "surpassed 3.5 GW" and the end-2026 *contracted* target was raised to >3 GW (already >2 GG / 3.5 GW total) — the 3,500 figure may now be conservative if "contracted" has grown since 3/31. Worth a refresh check at Q2, but defensible as-is.
- **Garbage income-statement Value inputs (rule 13 rationale):** GAAP EBITDA/EPS/FCF are distorted — Q1 GAAP net income is dominated by a ~$780.6M equity-revaluation gain (rule 15: EPS YoY should be **blanked** as one-time-dominated if it were being scored). Standard rule-13 cohort treatment applies: do NOT let income-statement Value metrics drive the score; EV/MW carries Value.

**Growth (rule 15):** Revenue growth is genuine and enormous (+684% YoY, ARR +54% QoQ). EPS YoY is non-operating-dominated → **blank it** if used; Growth averages revenue metrics.

**AI Thesis (D1–D5):** D1/D2 strongly positive — directly an AI-infra pure-play; available capacity "sold out"; two hyperscaler anchors (MSFT, Meta) + NVIDIA Vera Rubin platform. Moat/D-dims: owned-DC, capital-efficient claim; but neocloud differentiation vs CRWV/peers is the open question. Re-examine D-dims in same pass (rule 12, earnings trigger).

**Momentum (M / rule 11):** Strong — Nasdaq-100 inclusion 2026-06-11, stock ~+21% since, near $300; Citi PT +70% post-Q1. Refresh 50DMA % via `momentum_50dma.py`.

**Risk:**
- **R1 (customer concentration) — ELEVATED:** Microsoft + Meta = ~$46B of ~committed backlog, the vast majority from **two** counterparties. Strong credits, but extreme concentration. Flag for R1.
- **R3 (balance sheet / capex funding) — KEY WATCH:** $20–25B capex vs $9.30B cash; funding model is converts ($4.34B) + prefunded warrants ($2.0B, dilutive) + asset-backed loans collateralized by MSFT/Meta contracts + >$6B more raised. Debt doubled to ~$8.43B. Self-funding gap is large; execution depends on continued capital-markets access. Flag for R3.
- **R5 (disruption) — defaults to 5** (Layer 09, physical AI infra/compute; non-Layer-10). No change.

**Rule-14 expectations red-flag:** P/S at/near peak with Nasdaq-100-driven multiple expansion and consensus enthusiasm — qualitative caution that a lot is priced in (run `expectations_flag.py NBIS`; foreign filer may self-skip on XBRL revenue tag). Not a scored metric.

**THESIS-BREAK watch (none triggered):** No going-concern, no guidance cut (FY26 reiterated/raised), no customer loss (added Meta). The live risks are (a) capacity-buildout **funding stress** given the capex/cash gap and reliance on capital markets, (b) **dilution** (prefunded warrants + likely future equity), and (c) extreme **two-customer concentration**. None breaking now.
