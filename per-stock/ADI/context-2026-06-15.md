## AI / Datacenter exposure & directness — the key finding

ADI is a **diversified analog/mixed-signal name with a fast-rising but still minority datacenter/AI exposure**, accelerating hard:

| End market (FQ2'26) | Revenue | YoY |
|---|---|---|
| Industrial | $1,800M | +56% |
| Automotive | $871.6M | +2% |
| Communications | $554.7M | +79% |
| Consumer | $397.8M | +23% |

Source: ADI Q2'26 results / earnings call, 2026-05-20.

**Datacenter specifics (management, Q2'26 call):**
- Communications = **~15% of total revenue**; **data center = >75% of communications** → datacenter is roughly **~11-12% of total ADI revenue** (derived; not directly disclosed — flag).
- Data center **up >90% YoY**, "driven by both our optical and power portfolios" — growth split roughly **equally between power and optical**.
- Quote: *"Data center, which now accounts for more than 75% of our communications revenue, was up more than 90% year over year driven by both our optical and power portfolios."*
- Datacenter projected ADI's **fastest grower** into Q3, "up low- to mid-teens sequentially."

**Directness of AI thesis — two real signal-chain content vectors:**
1. **Optical / networking signal integrity** — ADI supplies high-speed signal-chain content into optical interconnect (the SerDes/optical-DSP-adjacent analog content scaling with 800G/1.6T datacenter networking). Direct AI-cluster networking exposure.
2. **Datacenter power** — vertical power delivery for AI compute. The **Empower Semiconductor acquisition** (announced ~May 2026) targets high-density point-of-load power: claims to shrink power footprint up to 4x and cut DC compute power consumption an estimated 10-15%. Management expects "significant revenue contribution in 2027" pending regulatory approval; "a lot of design-ins in train."

**Assessment:** Real, accelerating, two-vector AI exposure (power + optical), but **diluted by a large industrial/auto base** (~73% of revenue). Not a pure-play. Directness is moderate-to-good for a diversified analog name — content is genuine AI-datacenter signal/power chain, not just "GDP-of-tech" tailwind. Industrial recovery (+56%) is the bigger near-term swing factor.

## Red flags / anomalies

1. **3Y rev CAGR is negative (−2.8%)** — pure artifact of cycle timing: FY22 was an ADI peak (post-Maxim integration), FY23-24 were the analog downturn trough. ADI is now in a sharp V-recovery (MRQ +37%, GM +360bps adj). The 3Y CAGR materially understates current trajectory; Growth category should lean on MRQ YoY, not the 3Y number. Consider this when weighting.
2. **TTM vs MRQ divergence (rule 9):** GAAP GM TTM 64.5% vs MRQ 67.3% (+280bps); adj. GM MRQ 73.0%. TTM averages drag in the lower trough quarters. High-momentum name — TTM biases scores DOWN. Surface MRQ in any rating.
3. **EPS YoY is operational, NOT one-time-dominated (rule 15 check):** the +110.5% GAAP EPS jump is driven by revenue +37% and op-margin expansion off a depressed base. No divestiture/fair-value/tax one-off identified in the period. **Do NOT blank EPS YoY.** (One-time-item scan of last 4 quarters surfaced nothing material; the Empower acquisition closes later and is a cash use, not a gain.)
4. **Valuation is rich / expectations elevated (rule 14 spirit):** P/S 16.3, EV/EBITDA 33.9, FCF yield only 2.2%, fwd P/E 28.9. Stock fell on the Q2 print despite a beat ("results beat, stock falls") — consistent with high embedded expectations. PEG 0.91 looks cheap but rides optimistic forward EPS growth ($6.73 trailing → $14.77 fwd implies ~120% EPS growth, off a trough); treat PEG with skepticism here.
5. **ROIC is approximate** — equity derived from yfinance ROE (0.0964) rather than balance-sheet read; ~9.1% is indicative only. For a final score, pull stockholders' equity directly from the 10-Q balance sheet. Flag as Medium confidence.
6. **Datacenter % of total is derived (~11-12%), not directly disclosed** — built from "comms = 15% of rev" × "DC = >75% of comms." Directional, not exact.

## Proposed subjective view (do NOT write to xlsx — for collaborative session)

- **AI Thesis directness: moderate-to-good (call it a 3-4/5 range).** Two genuine AI-datacenter content vectors (high-density power via Empower; optical/networking signal integrity), datacenter +90% YoY and accelerating. But ~73% of revenue is industrial/auto — the name is a diversified analog play with a fast-growing AI minority, not a pure AI infrastructure name. Industrial recovery is the larger near-term earnings driver. Empower DC-power upside is a 2027 story.
- **Key risks:** (1) cyclicality — analog is famously cyclical; the +37% is partly a recovery snapback off a 2-yr trough, not pure secular AI. (2) Valuation/expectations — rich multiple, stock sold off on a beat. (3) Datacenter is still only ~11-12% of revenue, so AI optionality is real but not yet dominant. (4) Auto only +2% — weakest segment, watch for further softness. (5) Empower integration/regulatory and 2027 timing risk on the headline DC-power story.
