Company expects power generation capacity to reach **~2,200 MW by early 2028** based on committed deliveries; "majority currently committed to customers under contracts" (10-K).

**Q1 2026 (10-Q):** Revenue **$196.2M** (+55.3% YoY — yfinance rev_yoy confirms 55.3). **Customer concentration (10-Q, Note 16 — material):** For Q1 2026, **two customers accounted for 52% and 11% of revenues** (top-2 = 63%). PY Q1 2025: 37% and 14%. AR concentration at 3/31/26: 45% and 14%. Concentration is *increasing* as the power business scales on a few anchor data-center contracts.

**Stateline VIE (10-K):** Consolidated VIE formed Apr 2025 to provide ~**900 MW** off-grid power to a single data-center customer under a long-term rental arrangement — ~41% of Power Solutions' generation assets. SEI sold a minority interest to MZX (affiliate of CTC); SEI continues to consolidate. Single-project, single-customer dependency flagged in 10-K risk factors.

**Capex / financing (10-Q, 8-Ks):** Capex "heavily weighted toward Solaris Power Solutions." Stateline term loan: $186.0M drawn at YE2025, $332.5M additional capacity to be drawn through 2026. Bridge Term Loan $300M + $200M additional commitments. Convertible senior notes due 2030 and 2031 issued May & Oct 2025. In May 2026 priced **$1.3B 6.375% Senior Notes due 2031** + upsized **$650M 0.25% convertible notes** (with $88.00 capped-call cap) — proceeds to repay borrowings + fund growth capex. This is a heavily-levered, externally-funded buildout.

### 2b. FCF-conversion red flag
TTM FCF is sharply **negative** (statement FCF ≈ −$583M; FCF margin −84%) against positive net income — but this is **textbook heavy growth-capex**, not an accrual-games signature. The rule-47 condition (conversion <60% for 2+ yrs AND receivables+inventory outgrowing revenue as a *working-capital* tell) does **not** cleanly apply: the cash gap is turbine fleet capex (investing), not ballooning working capital. **Interpretation: not an earnings-quality flag; it IS a balance-sheet/funding-risk flag** (see R3). Note the separate short-seller allegations re: MER accounting (Section 3) as the genuine earnings-quality overhang to watch.

## 5. DIFF — what changed since my training

- **Scale is far larger than I modeled.** FY25 revenue $622M (+99%); Q1'26 $196M (+55% YoY); operating >900 MW with a contracted path to ~2,200 MW by 2028. I thought of this as an early-pivot story; it is now a multi-GW contracted data-center power platform.
- **Three named long-term AI/data-center contracts** with investment-grade tech counterparties (>2 GW), incl. a 600 MW 10-yr deal signed Apr-2026 and a ~900 MW Stateline project. My model had vague "data center exposure"; it's now concrete, contracted, and the dominant growth engine (Power Solutions = 65% of rev).
- **Customer concentration is severe and rising** — one customer 52% of Q1'26 revenue (top-2 = 63%). I flagged concentration as an open question; it resolves to a clear R1 negative.
- **The funding picture is aggressive and a live risk** — $1.3B senior notes + $650M converts + Stateline term loans; FCF deeply negative on turbine capex. "Funded?" yes, but via heavy debt + dilution, not internally.
- **Short-seller (Morpheus) + securities class action** over the MER acquisition — a regulatory/litigation + earnings-quality overhang I had no awareness of. Directly relevant to R4 and to skepticism on reported numbers.

## 6. Implications for ratings (flagging only — not pre-deciding)

- **D1 (AI rev %):** Power Solutions = 65% of rev and effectively all AI/data-center-contracted on the marginal MW; but ~46% of total rev (Logistics + non-DC power) is still legacy oilfield. Net: solid-but-mixed → likely 3–4, debatable.
- **D2 (supply-chain position) / D4 (capacity expansion):** D4 is a clear high read — funded multi-GW buildout under contract. D2 is the debate: mobile/modular gas turbines have competitors (GEV, CAT/Caterpillar, ProEnergy, VoltaGrid, rental gensets) and the moat is contract/lead-time/turbine-supply access, not a structural bottleneck.
- **D3 (moat):** halo-prone; the durable edge is long-term take-or-pay contracts + scarce turbine supply access (MER relationship), not proprietary tech → modest.
- **D5 (hyperscaler exposure):** three "leading technology company" counterparties, >30% of rev clearly tied to data-center buildout → high read, but counterparties are un-named affiliates (verify "investment-grade global tech" claim vs. disclosure).
- **M1/M2/M3:** beat-and-raise + guidance up (M1 strong); strong relative strength / near all-time highs (M2 strong, but see expectations flag); CEO+CFO open-market buys (M3 leans high per rubric's buy-asymmetry).
- **R1:** severe single-customer concentration (52%) → low score. **R2:** US-domestic assets/revenue, no China/export legs → high score (low risk). **R3:** heavy leverage + negative FCF + maturity/draw schedule → low score. **R4:** Morpheus short report + securities class action over MER → low-ish score.

**Thesis-break signals to watch:** (1) any data-center contract cancellation/slippage given 52% single-customer concentration; (2) outcome/expansion of the MER securities class action and Morpheus allegations (earnings-quality risk); (3) funding execution if capital markets tighten mid-buildout.
